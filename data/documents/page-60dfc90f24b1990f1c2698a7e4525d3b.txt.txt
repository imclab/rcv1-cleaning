(Courtesy Photo)  Posted: Thursday, February 23, 2012 2:00 am Off-brand of humor showcased in Aspen Laff Festival Staff Report Montrose Daily Press  |  0comments  An intelligent, thought-provoking brand of humor that straysinto nervier, darker territory will be on tap at 8 p.m. Friday asthe Aspen Laff Festival comes to the Center for the Arts, 606 SixthSt. in Crested Butte. The Aspen Laff Festival showcases three comics who havedistinguished themselves on the club and competition circuit. TomSimmons is not afraid to strip long-held beliefs of theircredibility with an unprecedented blend of searing logic and greatjokes. He questions everyone and everything. Winner of the 2009 SanFrancisco Comedy Competition, Simmons is also a two-time finalistat the Boston Comedy Festival and has been seen on Comedy Central,Showtime and at the Montreal Just for Laughs International ComedyFestival. Danny Bevins' audacious, irreverent, charring and sarcasticmouth has gotten him arrested, fined and fired from every job he'sever had. The son of a Jesus freak and a Green Beret, he straddlesthe line between temptation and authority. Bevins is the winner ofthe 2000 San Francisco International Comedy Comedy Festival, hasbeen featured on Comedy Central, Paramount's "The World Stands Up,"HBO's U.S. Comedy Arts Festival and the Jameson Comedy Tour.
