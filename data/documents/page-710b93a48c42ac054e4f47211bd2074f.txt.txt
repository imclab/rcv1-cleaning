Copenhagen's famed mermaid gets a male counterpart Fast, hot and classic cars featured at major new auto museum By Jayne Clark , USA TODAY Updated Comments Harold LeMay never met a car he didn't like. Over his lifetime, he owned a Guinness World's Record-breaking 3,300 vehicles or more. "No one knows the exact number. I don't think Harold even knew," says David Madeira, president of the new LeMay – America's Car Museum , whose grand opening is today in Tacoma, Wash. LeMay, who died in 2000 at the age of 81, was an up-from-the-bootstraps entrepreneur who made his fortune in trash collection. His early acquisitions were junkers he got paid to haul away. Later, as he became wealthier, he began serious collecting – Franklins, Cords, Pierce-Arrows, a $1.5 million red convertible Duesenberg! PHOTOS:   Tour America's Car Museum In the last 25 years of his life, it's estimated LeMay bought an average of five cars a week, Madeira says. But the new $60 million, 165,000-square-foot museum on nine acres is more than just a tribute to one man's obsession. It'll showcase vehicles from his collection on a rotating basis. But it's also meant to take a broader look at American automotive history and future, plus serve as a venue for auto-centric happenings, from drive-in movies and beach parties to Corvette and vintage motorcycle fests. The reason many car museums struggle as regional, niche attractions is because they too often focus on a single collection, says Madeira, who has been involved for a decade in this museum's development. "We want to keep it fresh by telling stories Americans can relate to," he says. "(You'll) recognize the car from a family road trip, or the car you first owned, or the car you first made out in." Special exhibits pay homage to the British Invasion (MG, Triumph, Jaguar,); the Indianapolis 500; and alternative propulsion methods, from the Stanley Steamer to the electric car. Admission is $14 adults; $8 ages 5-12. Will tourists swallow a NYC ban on super-size sugary drinks?
