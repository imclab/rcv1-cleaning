Use of car safety seats is required for the protection of your child when riding in a vehicle. Car safety seat should be selected carefully. Several factors must be considered when selecting car safety seat for your child. Theses factors include the child's age, size, and type of vehicle. To be sure your child is using the right car seat, you may read NHTSA (National HighwayTrafficSafety Administration) recommendations at safecar.gov: Car Seats &amp; Booster Basics . Another reference, provided by American Academy of Pediatrics, can be found on Car Seats: Information for families 2012 . Infant Car Safety Seats and Rear-Facing Seats NHTSA recommends that infants must be seated in the back seat, in rear-facing child safety seat, as long as possible up to the height and weight limit of the car seat. Convertible Car Safety Seats , Rear-facing and Forward-facing Convertible car seats can be used in rear-facing for infants and then converted to foward-facing for older children. The use of convertible car safety seat means that your child can use the seat for a longer time. However, expect them to be bulkier than infant seats.
