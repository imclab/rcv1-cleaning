Detroit boasts thegreatest variety of visual and performing arts, as well as being thebirthplace of Motown. Another Michigan arts mecca is the charmingcity of Ann Arbor. Of course, outside of these cities, you'll stillfind theaters, art galleries, independent movie houses, and many morecultural offerings. Arts andCrafts For artlovers, Michigan boasts the Detroit Institute of Arts, which is oneof the biggest art museums in the world, as well as the Grand RapidsArt Museum. Plenty of small galleries can be found in all of thestate's major cities. Ann Arbor hosts several arts festivals,including the annual Ann Arbor Street Fair, an eclectic,family-friendly event with art, music, crafts, and more. Similarannual events can be found in East Lansing, Marquette, and elsewhere. Craftyvisitors to Michigan will find an incredibly abundant world of arts,crafts, and antiques. Nearly every small town in the state is hometo at least one shop selling handmade goods and antiques. Plus,numerous crafts fairs take place year-round. Such events are popularand well-attended statewide. A few examples are Royal Oak's Clay andGlass Festival, the Ypsilanti Heritage Festival, and the UpperPeninsula's Wild Blueberry Festival. For a funkier, non-traditionalevent, the Detroit Urban Craft Fair specializes in a progressive,urban, hand-crafted aesthetic. PerformingArts For danceenthusiasts, Michigan offers a variety of both performances andclasses in many genres, including ballet, jazz, tap, hip-hop, swing,ballroom, and tango. Detroit and Ann Arbor are two major performingarts centers, as well as good places to go out to dance clubs. Meanwhile, smaller towns and cities across the state are home toseveral smaller folk dancing companies. Theaterthrives in Michigan, too. Detroit alone boasts the Fisher Theatre,the Masonic Temple Theatre, the Detroit Repertory Theatre, theAfrican American centered Plowshares Theatre Company, and the improvcomedy group Second City Detroit. Theater companies can also befound in Ann Arbor, Chelsea, Grand Rapids, Kalamazoo, and Calumet. Of course,Michigan has made great contributions to American music, and itsmodern music scene reflects this. Even though Motown Records movedaway long ago, Detroit's jazz, rock, R&amp;B, and rap scenes are nowthriving more than ever. Plus, the city hosts the popular DetroitInternational Jazz Festival. As for the rest of the state, small,thriving music communities can be found all over, as well as musicfestivals such as the Michigan Womyn's Music Festival and the PineMountain Music Festival. Film andLiterature Michiganfosters an active film community, thanks in part to nativeindependent filmmakers like Sam Raimi, of The Evil Dead fame. And documentary filmmaker Michael Moore first garnered acclaimwith his debut Roger and Me ,which dealt with the decimation of his hometown, Flint, after GMclosed its auto factories in the city. Inaddition to serving as a shooting location, Michigan hosts severalwell-known film festivals. One of the state's major filmevents is the Ann Arbor Film Festival, a progressive-orientedfestival specializing in small, independent films from a diversearray of artists. Other major film festivals include the CentralMichigan International Film Festival, the East Lansing Film Festival,the Reel Pride Film Festival, and the Waterfront Film Festival. Plus, cities like Detroit, Kalamazoo, Grand Rapids, and Ann Arbor arehome to small art theaters, where travelers can catch independent andforeign films. Literarybuffs, too, will not be disappointed. Lauded authors Elmore Leonardand Jeffrey Eugenides have both immortalized the Motor City in theirpages, while Ernest Hemingway spent boyhood summers in northernMichigan, where he began his writing career. Visitors can tourErnest Hemingway's former home, attend a poetry reading, or browse anindependent book shop. The state also hosts a variety of literaryevents, such as the Ann Arbor Book Festival, the Ludington PoetryFest, and the Detroit Festival of the Arts. At these events, visitorscan attend readings, workshops, book sales, and more. Travelerswho enjoy the arts and entertainment will find plenty to choose fromin the Wolverine State. From Shakespeare to sculpture, Michigan'scultural attractions offer a range of ways to inspire, amuse, andexpand your artistic horizons.
