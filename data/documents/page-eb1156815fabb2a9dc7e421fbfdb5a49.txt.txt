Most of the “Eat &amp; Greet” events will take place before the book festival, which will be held Sept. 7 in the Downtown Arts District. Debbie Macomber, a No. 1 New York Times bestselling fiction writer, will attend a lunch at Old Salem Visitors Center at 11:30 a.m. Sept. 6. She will give a presentation and sign books. Tickets are $45, which includes a boxed lunch and a hardcover copy of Macomber’s new book, “Rose Harbor in Bloom.” Sherri Brooks Vinton, the author of “Put ’em Up!”, “Put ’em Up! Fruit” and “The Real Food Revival,” will talk and sign books at Whole Foods at 4 p.m. Sept. 6. Admission is free. Tickets are not required. Adrian Miller, a culinary historian and barbecue judge, will give a presentation and sign books at a breakfast at Sweet Potatoes Restaurant at 8:30 a.m. Sept. 7. Breakfast will be prepared from Miller’s forthcoming book, “Soul Food: The Surprising Story of an American Cuisine, One Plate at a Time.” Tickets are $52, which includes breakfast and a copy of Miller’s book. Elana Amsterdam, the author of “Gluten-Free Cupcakes” and the forthcoming “Paleo Cooking from Elana’s Pantry,” will give a presentation and sign books at 4:30 p.m. Sept. 7 at Mary’s Gourmet Diner. Tickets are $48, which include a copy of “Paleo Cooking from Elana’s Pantry” and the chance to sample recipes. Tickets are on sale now at http://bookmarks.roundtablelive.org .
