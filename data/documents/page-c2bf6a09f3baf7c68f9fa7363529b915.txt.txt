                                <h2>March 29th, 2013 Update</h2>
<div></div>
<div>
<div></div>
<div>
<div>Thank you riders and clubs for a really good 2013 snowmobile season.&nbsp; Good spring riding still exists in higher elevations and in northern NH; specifically areas around Pittsburg, Diamond Pond, Errol and Jericho Mountain State Park.&nbsp; Check local club websites for updates through April.&nbsp; Grooming will likely end in all areas after this weekend but riding will be open in some places as long as snow remains.&nbsp; Riding at Boundary Pond and Deer Mountain areas in Pittsburg anticipated through most of April. <br  />
</div>
Thank you again for registering your snowmobile and supporting the local clubs.&nbsp; Your registration sticker is the only source of funding for trail grooming and construction.<br  />
<br  />
<strong>Pittsburg</strong>- Trail system is 90% open. Grooming is ongoing as weather and conditions permit. Trails to the north are good to great. Trails to the south are poor with spring riding conditions. Corridor 20 below Indian Stream Eatery is cloed. Inlets to First Connecticut Lake and Lake Francis have begun to open up. Stay off lakes and ponds. For specific trail information, please visit http://www.snowmobilepittsburgnh.com/<br  />
<br  />
<strong>Colebrook</strong>- Some riding in the Diamond Peaks and Diamond Pond area. All gates into Colebrook and in East Colebrook are closed. Rail bed between Colebrook and Beecher Falls is closed. Gates in the Bungy/Columbia areas will close this morning. No grooming at this time.<br  />
<br  />
<strong>Groveton/Nash Stream</strong>- 2-6&#8221; base. Trails are groomed and riding is fair to good.<br  />
<strong><br  />
Diamond Pond/Coleman State Park</strong>- 1-2&#8221; of new snow on a 5-8&#8221; base. Trails are groomed and riding is good to great.<br  />
<br  />
<strong>Errol</strong>- 2-6&#8221; base. Trails are groomed and riding is moderate to good. <br  />
<br  />
<strong>Cambridge</strong>- 3-6&#8221; of new snow. Trails are groomed and riding is good. <br  />
<br  />
<strong>Milan</strong>- Trails are groomed and riding is fair. <br  />
<br  />
<strong>Berlin</strong>- 2-8&#8221; base. Trails are groomed and riding is fair to good. &nbsp;<br  />
<br  />
<strong>Berlin/Success</strong>- Trails are not groomed and riding is fair to moderate. &nbsp;<br  />
<br  />
<strong>Gorham</strong>- Trails are groomed and riding is fair to good. &nbsp;<br  />
<br  />
<strong>Jefferson</strong>- Trails are groomed and riding is good. <br  />
<br  />
<strong>Lancaster</strong>- Trails are not groomed and riding is fair.<br  />
<br  />
<strong>Whitefield</strong>- Trails are not groomed and riding is fair.<br  />
<br  />
<strong>Franconia Notch</strong>- 1-4&#8221; base. Trails are groomed and riding is fair to good. <br  />
<br  />
<strong>Campton/Thornton</strong>- Trails are groomed and riding is available in higher elevations.<br  />
<br  />
<strong>Bear Notch</strong>- 6-8&#8221; base. Trails are groomed with spring riding conditions. Railroad tracks exposed. Gates will be closed at Sawyer River Road and at Newts Brook.</div>
<br  />

 ARTS &amp; ENTERTAINMENT 
 New Hampshire has a thriving local music and arts scene. Here you can catch emerging acts in small venues, see a play in a historic theater or browse small shops for inspiring artwork.  
 Thanks to its larger venues and institutions you can also see world class shows and art in New Hampshire. The Verizon Wireless Arena, the Currier Museum of Art, the Capitol Center and the Stone Church all bring music, art and theater from around the world to New Hampshire. 
 Use the links to the left to discover your creative outlet. 
 Or, visit the  New Hampshire State Council on the Arts  for more information on the arts in New Hampshire.
