Advertisement  Posted: Sunday, July 14, 2013 12:00 am Richmond area arts and entertainment event calendar Getting Started FESTIVAL OF ARTS Summer outdoor performing-arts series on the main stage (unless noted) in Dogwood Dell and family friendly entertainers on the Ha’Penny Stage, Byrd Park. Free. 646-3355 or 646-1031. Today: “Clowning Around with Shakespeare,” 4 p.m. on the Ha’Penny Stage; Melena , Cuban-born percussionist in music and dance, 8 p.m. Wednesday: “Recycling Music,” 11 a.m. on the Ha’Penny Stage. WEEKDAY HIGHLIGHTS Innsbrook After Hours , 5 p.m. (gates) at Snagajob Pavilion, 4901 Lakebrook Drive. www.innsbrookafterhours.com . Wednesday: Blues guitar legend B.B. King , $30-$55. Thursday: Country duo Thompson Square with Zach Lockwood, $20-$38. WEEKEND WATCH VCU Guitar &amp; Other Strings: Quatro na Bossa in Brazilian music, 7:30 p.m. Friday in Vlahcevic Hall, VCU Singleton Center, 922 Park Ave. $15. 828-6776. Shockoe Tomato: Festival at the height of tomato season, packed with market vendors, zany contests, kids’ stuff, music and tomatoes any way you want them, 11 a.m.-4 p.m. Saturday at 17th Street Farmers’ Market, 17th and East Main streets. Free. 646-0477. JoyFest: LeCrae, Kirk Franklin , Israel Houghton, Le’Andria Johnson, Isaac Carree, Humble Tip and lots more star in the gospel-music event, 4 p.m. Saturday at Kings Dominion, Doswell. $49.99 in advance, $54.99 at gate (includes park admission); $19.99 for season passholders; VIP packages available. www.kingsdominion.com or 876-5000. ART OPENINGS Virginia Museum of Fine Arts , 200 N. Boulevard, 340-1400. Opens today: “Unreal: Conceptual Photographs from the 1970s and ‘80s,” group show of manipulated images. Through Dec. 29. CONCERTS Summer Sounds , outdoor concerts Thursdays-Sundays through Sept. 2 (Labor Day) at Stony Point Fashion Park, 9200 Stony Point Parkway. Free. 560-7467. Today: Dixie Power Trio in blues and rock, 1-5 p.m. Thursday: Deuce in rock and pop, 4-8 p.m. Friday: Casper in Motown, oldies and soul, 4-8 p.m. Saturday: Susan Greenbaum in pop, rock and blues, 4-8 p.m. The Continentals in big-band favorites, 7-9 p.m. Tuesday at Hermitage High School, 8301 Hungary Spring Road. Free. 501-5859. Pop singer Susan Greenbaum performs a lunchtime concert (bring your own) at noon Thursday in Library Park, behind the main Richmond Public Library, 101 E. Franklin St. Free. 646-7223. Summer Music: Sportsbar Rockstar , noon-1 p.m. Thursday at James Center Plaza, 1051 E. Cary St. Free. 344-3232. Jazz Café: Ban Caribe plays Caribbean soul, jazz and R&amp;B at 6 p.m. Thursday at Best Café, Virginia Museum of Fine Arts, 200 N. Boulevard. Free. 340-1400. Short Pump Summer Concert Series: Power trio E3 , 6-8 p.m. Thursday at Short Pump Town Center’s main plaza, 11800 W. Broad St. Free. 360-1700. Thursdays in Old Towne: Casper plays outside at 7 p.m. (gates at 6) and a DJ spins dance tunes inside beginning at 9 at Old Towne Civic Center, 136 River St., Petersburg. $10. 733-8344. Volume Three music festival benefitting WRIR indie radio, various venues Thursday through July 21, $5-$10 each show, except for July 21, which is free; 622-9747. Thursday: Zac Hryciak &amp; the Jungle Beat , Clair Morgan and others, 10 p.m. at Strange Matter, 929 W. Grace St. Friday: The Diamond Center and DJ sets, 10:30 p.m. at Balliceaux, 203 N. Lombardy St.; Saturday, MUTWAWA , Herro Sugar, Warren Hixon and more, 4 p.m. at Gallery5, 200 W. Marshall St. July 21: Heavy Midgets , Hoax Hunters and more, 10 p.m. at Bandito’s Burrito Lounge, 2905 Patterson Ave. Pocahontas Premieres: Eagles tribute band Hotel California , 7 p.m. Friday at Pocahontas State Park, Chesterfield County. $10 in advance (includes parking); day of show $10 plus $5 parking. 796-4255. African Children’s Choir sings at 7 p.m. Friday at West End Baptist Church, 6506 Boydton Plank Road, Dinwiddie County, 733-9331; and 7 p.m. July 21 at Duncan Memorial United Methodist Church, Randolph-Macon College, Ashland, 798-7224. Donation requested. RVA Grooves: R&amp;B singer-songwriter Anthony David , 8 p.m. Friday (doors at 7) at The Hippodrome Theater, 528 N. Second St. $25. www.rvagrooves.com . THEATER OPENINGS “bare: a pop opera,” Richmond Triangle Players’ production of a rock musical about two gay high school seniors at a Catholic boarding school, previews at 8 p.m. Wednesday and opens at 8 p.m. Thursday at RTP Theater, 1300 Altamont Ave. $18 preview; $32 opening night; $28-$32 thereafter. 346-8113. Through Aug. 17. DIVERSIONS Richmond Floodwall Walking Tour: Richmond Railroad Museum conducts a guided 1½-hour walk, meeting at 2 p.m. today at the museum, 102 Hull St. Free. 353-9363. Family Day: Kid-focused house tour, Colonial games and activities and more, 1-3 p.m. Wednesday at Wilton House Museum, 215 S. Wilton Road. $5; reservation required. 282-5936. Flowers After 5: Extended hours, wines from South America ($12 extra), music by Bruce Evans, food for purchase, Water Play (until 8 p.m.) and more, 5-9 p.m. Thursday at Lewis Ginter Botanical Garden, 1800 Lakeside Ave. $7-$11. 262-9887. Movies in the Park: “The Amazing Spiderman,” PG-13-rated action adventure based on the comic book hero, screened outdoors at 8:30 p.m. Friday (activities begin at 8) at Hotchkiss Park, 701 E. Brookland Park Blvd. Free; bring blanket or lawn chair. www.richmondgov.com/parks or 646-5733. Screen on the Green: “Soul Surfer,” inspirational PG-rated film about a teen surfer who lost an arm in a shark attack, screened outdoors at 8:30 p.m. Friday at Crump Park, 3400 Mountain Road. Free; bring blanket or lawn chair; concessions available. 501-5808. Richmond’s Historic Theaters: Walking tour of movie palaces and theaters in the Broad Street arts district, including a “behind the scenes” look at Virginia Rep Center’s November Theatre, meeting at 10 a.m. Saturday at Virginia Rep Center, 114 W. Broad St. $5-$10. 649-0711, ext. 319. Richmond 48 Hour Film Project: Premiere showings of indie films shot in and around Richmond July 12-14, 1:30 and 4 p.m. Saturday and July 21 at the Byrd Theatre, 2908 W. Cary St. $10 per session, $15 weekend pass. www.48hourfilm.com/en/richmond .
