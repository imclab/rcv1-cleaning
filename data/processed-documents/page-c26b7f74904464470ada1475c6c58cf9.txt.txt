Convertible Top Accessories There are important components of a convertible top installation that are not part of the actualconvertible top. The important ones are the top hold down cables, the frame bow tacking strips,the convertible top pads (or webbing), the top's rear well liner and possibly other items. We have assembled, for many convertibles, top installation packages. In these convertible toppackages there will usually be hold down cables and, where often required, new top bow tackingstrips. If available for your convertible an instruction manual will be included for free in that toppackage. In the following sections we'll describe some important convertible top accessories. Hold Down Cables Most tops are held to the folding top frame, along the sides, with special cables. Replacementhold down cables are recommended when the top frame has been frequently raised and lowered. Some tops have rear base attachment cables (Jaguar, Volkswagen) but most do not. And a fewtops have short rear side cables for the corner flaps of the convertible top. Tacking Strips Some tops, notably the BMW and some very recent modular top assemblies, require no staplingin the installation of the convertible top. Almost all American tops prior to 2005, and otherclassic two part top designs, require that staples be driven through fabric into the tack stripswhich are embedded in the top frame. Tack strips are not difficult to remove and replace. Tackstrips are most often employed in both the header bow (the convertible top frame bow that isover the windshield) and the rear top bow (the top frame bow that is over the rear window.) Most cars with tacking strips also employ complex rear "trim stick" assemblies. These are alsotacking strips, but they are embedded along the rear belt line of the body of the car. The baseof the top is stapled there. Replacement trim sticks are separate from the tacking strips and aremuch less often required for convertible top replacement. Convertible Top Pads Many convertible tops have, under them, convertible top pads. These may be reinforced nylonwebbing or foam and fabric assemblies. New pads are often required but only after the top hasbeen in use for significant time or has been raised and lowered many times. We include padsin our packages as it is a good idea for the do-it-yourself installer to perform a conservativerestoration. Installation Manuals These are our own published top installation manuals.
