  In 1986 Nissan phased out the Datsun brand after 55 years of providing the World with such cars as the Datsun 510, the&nbsp;Fairlady Roadster,&nbsp;<br><br><img alt="" src="http://lovelyrides.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Datsun-510-Bluebird-Modified.jpg"><br><img alt="" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/SPL212.jpg/1024px-SPL212.jpg"><br><br>and later on the 240Z &amp; 280Z coupes<br><br><img alt="" src="http://cdn.stanceworks.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/datsun-240z-slammed-red.jpg"><br><br>Which in mind mind is one of the Prettiest sports cars to come out of the land of the rising sun, along with the 1971 Nissan Skyline and Toyota 2000GT<br><br><img alt="" src="http://image.automotive.com/f/featuredvehicles/sstp_1003_1971_nissan_skyline/26937443+soriginal/sstp_1003_04_o+1971_nissan_skyline+front.jpg"><br><img alt="" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-k4BBBYbf3VI/T9l3hvm4LOI/AAAAAAAAJL0/OYgpLaC86JE/s1600/toyota+2000gt+autoart.jpg"><br><br>In 2012 Nissan announced that they were to revive the Datsun brand, citing that it was returning "under the same spirit" and that "<span>The up-and-coming generation deserves a new kind of car â€“ a car built just for them." This is all sounds great, right up until you see the concept drawings of this "car for a new generation"...<br><br><img alt="" src="http://resources.datsun.com/assets/images/car-large.jpg?c=MAAuADAALgAxADcAMgAuADEANAAyADEAMQA"><br><br>.. Now correct me if I'm wrong but this looks a lot like a squashed Nissan Qashqai? Is this really what the brains at Datsun see the new generation driving? a family car?!? I mean I know that the number of teenage pregnancy has sky rocketed in recent years but is this really what they see the new generation driving?&nbsp;<br><br>I'm truly surprised that a brand better known for their classic sports cars are relaunching with something as un-ambitious&nbsp;as this.</span>
