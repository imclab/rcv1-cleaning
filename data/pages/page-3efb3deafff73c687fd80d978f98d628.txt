</div></div><!--END: Paragraph Fact Box--><p>&#x201c;Well, it&#x2019;s four magicians that are all here in the [Washington] D.C. area,&#x201d; Phillips said. &#x201c;We have been performing at the Gaithersburg Arts Barn &#x2014; I believe this is our seventh year &#x2026; seventh or eighth year that we&#x2019;ve been partnering with the city and the Arts Barn. We love being there and we&#x2019;re happy that they keep having us back.</p><p>&#x201c;It&#x2019;s nice that we seem to fill a little niche. It&#x2019;s difficult for them to schedule into because we typically come in on a Friday before a new theatrical production starts. So the last show, whatever that has been, has just cleared out and the next show has not yet taken over the stage with lighting and so forth. It&#x2019;s a neat little window of opportunity we get to sneak in on once a month and do a show.&#x201d;</p><p>The group, which has worked together for several years now, started out working primarily at private parties or large business events. Since their family and friends weren&#x2019;t able to see these shows, the Society jumped at the chance to play in front of a regular audience.</p><p>&#x201c;We were looking for some place where we could get together, have a good time because this is obviously something that we love to do,&#x201d; Phillips said. &#x201c;It&#x2019;s a great opportunity for us to work with each other on new material because we&#x2019;re all such good friends that, when we&#x2019;re trying something new, we&#x2019;ll get the other guys and say &#x2018;I&#x2019;m trying something new tonight. Can you watch it and tell me if this looks good or if you could tell me a funny joke that I might be able to put in here?&#x2019; &#x2026; So it&#x2019;s a good way for us to develop additional material as well.&#x201d;</p><p>Folks who are expecting the theatricality of David Copperfield should probably be warned there will be no monument disappearances or &#x2018;wireless&#x2019; flying about the theater.</p><p>&#x201c;The type of magic that we do is not the grand spectacle kind of thing ...&#x201d; Phillips said. &#x201c;So the size of the magic we do lends itself much more to comedy. The four of us happen to be specialists in that type of magic in what is typically considered standup comedy magic. Our style fits the venue and the venue fits our style. It&#x2019;s a nice little matchup.&#x201d;</p><p>Part of the fun during the show is audience participation. Usually during a trick or comedy bit, the Society will call upon someone in the audience for their assistance. Of course, working with novices can be tricky. It can also have hilarious results.</p><p>&#x201c;[Working with the audience is] something that we&#x2019;ve done for years and years,&#x201d; Phillips said. &#x201c;All of us have been professional entertainers for, gosh, I mean Brian is the youngest and he&#x2019;s been doing it for at least 15 years. So it&#x2019;s something that you become very good at and it&#x2019;s actually often times the most entertaining part of the show because it&#x2019;s certainly something that&#x2019;s happening right there in the moment.</p><p>&#x201c;It&#x2019;s basically improvising. The spectators themselves don&#x2019;t know where we&#x2019;re going with our interactions and we tend to know specifically what we&#x2019;re trying to accomplish with the spectator up on stage &#x2014; whether it&#x2019;s a magic trick or a comedy situation or something like that. Their responses to what&#x2019;s going on are often the funniest parts of the show.&#x201d;</p><p>Phillips takes pride in the show&#x2019;s family-friendly nature. So it&#x2019;s often going to have adult themes, it&#x2019;s going to have adult language and there are certainly comedians who are well known for that type of material. It&#x2019;s going to be fun, it&#x2019;s going to be funny but it&#x2019;s also going to be family friendly, as we say.&#x201d;</p><p>At the end of the day, Phillips said he hopes audiences walk away from their shows with a better understanding of professional magic. Of course, hysterical laughter would be accepted as well.</p><p>&#x201c;I think that [the audience will] get to see magic on a level of quality that they&#x2019;ve probably never seen live before,&#x201d; Phillips said. &#x201c;Most people&#x2019;s experience with live magic is probably a kids&#x2019; show entertainer for their 6-year-old daughter&#x2019;s birthday or one of their friends and there&#x2019;s as good a chance as not that the magician performing there is not a professional. That person might just be doing magic as a hobby and they really like it, or they just might be a friend of the family or something like that. So most people&#x2019;s experience with live magic is not on the level of quality as when they see our show.</p><p>&#x201c;I think they are quite surprised, generally, at how fun it is, how funny it is and how badly they get fooled by the magic &#x2014; how impossible the things that we do look. I think that&#x2019;s probably the best thing in that they don&#x2019;t realize that magic can look this good and be this fun.&#x201d;</p><p><br/><br/>wfranklin@gazette.net</p>
