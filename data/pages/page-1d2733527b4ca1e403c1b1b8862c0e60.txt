<p>Add another important concept to the list of debuts at next month’s Detroit<a target="_blank" title=" Auto Show" href=" http://www.automobilemag.com/auto_shows/"> Auto Show</a>: Honda has announced that it will debut the 2013 Honda Accord Coupe concept at the North American International Auto Show in January.</p>
<p>As for what we know for sure about the concept, we know that it’ll have two doors…and that’s about it. Honda is predictably tight-lipped on the new concept.</p>
<p>What we can predict, however, is that the Coupe Concept is the first in a line of Honda Accord concepts we’ll see in the future, culminating with a new Accord production model. It’s about time: the current generation of Accord has been sold since 2008. That might not seem like a lot of time, but the past few years have seen some stiff competition.</p>
<p>The Accord must compete against the usual suspects in the form of the Toyota Camry, Nissan Altima, and Ford Fusion, but also fend off fights by the Hyundai Sonata/Kia Optima. It must also deal with the emerging Volkswagen Passat, <a title="Chevrolet Claims the 2013 Malibu Tops One Million Test Miles" href="http://rumors.automobilemag.com/chevrolet-claims-the-2013-malibu-tops-one-million-test-miles-93569.html">the new Chevrolet Malibu</a>, and the dark horse of the mid-size sales market, the Chrysler 200/Dodge Avenger. Keep in mind the Accord <a title="November 2011 Mid-Size Sedan Sales: Camry is King, Accord Slips to Fifth" href="http://rumors.automobilemag.com/november-2011-mid-size-sedan-sales-camry-is-king-accord-slips-to-fifth-92113.html">came in fifth place</a> among these competitors last month, and all but the Altima have been recently updated or will be soon.</p>
<p>We’ll begin to see just where the storied Accord nameplate is headed when the Coupe Concept bows in just a few weeks. Stay with <em>Automobile Magazine</em> for all the latest news surrounding the 2012 Detroit auto show.</p>
<p>Source: Honda</p>
