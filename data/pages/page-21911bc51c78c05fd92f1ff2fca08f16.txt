<p><font face="Verdana" size="4" color="#808080">But the granddaddy of the 3.0 
coupes -- the BMW 2000CS -- is not as well-known as its winged progeny.</font></p>

<p><font face="Verdana">The story of the <a href="../cs_cars.htm">CS cars</a> -- the C stands for Coupe and 
the S stands for Sport -- begins with the <a href="../vintage/500.htm">Baroque 
Angels</a> and other <a href="../vintage/50s.htm">cars of the '50s</a>.</font></p>

      <blockquote>

<p>
            <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3">
            <img border="0" src="../../images/r_pointer.gif" width="7" height="11"> </font>
<font face="Verdana">C, CS and CSL coupe models:</font></p>

        <ul>
          <li><font face="Verdana" size="2">2000CS 1990cc, four-cylinder, 
          135bhp, 0-60mph in 11.3 sec., 23 mpg</font></li>
          <li><font face="Verdana" size="2">2.5CS 2494cc, six-cylinder, 150bhp, 
          0-60mph in 10 sec., 22 mpg</font></li>
          <li><font face="Verdana" size="2">2800CS 2788cc, six-cylinder, 170bhp, 
          0-60mph in 8.5 sec., 19 mpg</font></li>
          <li><font face="Verdana" size="2">3.0CS 2985cc, six-cylinder, 180bhp, 
          0-60mph in 8.0 sec., 18 mpg</font></li>
          <li><font face="Verdana" size="2">3.0CSi 2985cc, six-cylinder, 200bhp, 
          0-60mph in 7.5 sec., 20 mpg</font></li>
          <li><font face="Verdana" size="2">3.0CSL 3003cc, six-cylinder, 200bhp, 
          0-60mph in 7.2 sec., 17 mpg</font></li>
          <li><font face="Verdana" size="2">3.0CSL &quot;Batmobile&quot;, 3153cc, 
          six-cylinder, 206bhp, 0-60mph in 6.8 sec., 22 mpg</font></li>
        </ul>
</blockquote>

<p><font face="Verdana">In 1962 the <a href="../vintage/pow/italian_design2.htm">Italian designer Bertone</a> was asked to 
produce a coupe version of the Goertz designed <a href="../vintage/503.htm">BMW 
503</a>. The result was the BMW 3200CS, the last BMW powered by the 3.2-liter V8 
first used in the <a href="../vintage/502.htm">BMW 502</a> Baroque Angel Sedan 
in 1954. Only 603 of the Bertone V8 CS coupes were produced.</font></p>

<p><font face="Verdana" size="4"><font color="#808080">For the next generation, 
enter BMW Chief designer Wilhelm Hofmeister, </font>
<a href="../newclass/index.htm">the New Class</a><font color="#808080"> and 
the Karmann coach builders.</font></font></p>

<p><font face="Verdana">To create the 2000CS, BMW first modified the New Class 
2-liter engine, put it in the Karmann CS coupe body and added some Hofmeister 
design touches.</font></p>

<p><font face="Verdana">The engine modifications included twin two throat side 
draft carburetors. Like the New Class sedans, the 2000CS coupe had MacPherson 
strut front suspension with semi-trailing arms in the rear. The 1990cc 
four-cylinder engine produced 135bhp and could motor from 0-60mph in 11.3 
seconds with a fuel rating of 23 mpg.</font></p>

<p><font face="Verdana" size="4" color="#808080">These cars are instantly 
recognizable by their distinctive front-end, especially the headlights. The 
headlight treatment placed two sets of front lights and turn signals within a 
wrap-around glass enclosure. </font></p>

<p><font face="Verdana" size="3">Advanced for its time, the design is similar to 
the front-end treatment on the <a href="../e39.htm">E39 5 Series sedan</a> and 
other contemporary BMW models. But the motoring press was critical of the design 
and after 11,720 C/CS cars were made (2837 2000C and 8883 2000CS) between 1965 
and 1969, the 2000CS gave way to the BMW 2800CS (and the Europe-only 2.5CS), 
with a more conventional front-end treatment and a six-cylinder engine.</font></p>

<p><font face="Verdana" size="4" color="#808080">The interior of the 2000CS was 
very luxurious. BMW ads at the time said the car &quot;handles like a Grand Prix 
champion&quot; yet &quot;feels like a long, silky limousine&quot;. </font></p>

<p><font face="Verdana" size="3">This 'limousine' had a sleek console and 
steering wheel, lots of wood trim and leather or two-tone plush cloth seats.</font></p>
