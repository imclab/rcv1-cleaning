<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-2100371269743751947' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.freeautomechanic.com/overheating.html" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-c0pJsbXcbLI/UdMVNK0RxSI/AAAAAAAABf0/X-v5_h5rSPE/s440/ford_escape_hybrid_2009.jpg" /></a></div>
Dave was driving it home from work about a week ago. He exited the interstate, and when he reached the stop sign at the end of the ramp the car died. I went out to give him a jump, and we ended up adding a bunch of water to the car.

He says the "hot" light never came on, but I never know whether to believe him.

When I was following him home, at one point I aw a lot of either smoke or steam coming from the exhaust. But not all the time. 

When we got home we found a plastic Y connector that connected two hoses in front had broken. We got that replaced, but the car still isn't right. It was idling rough, wanting to die, and running hot.

I know you're thinking "head gasket" because I am too. But there's no smoke coming from the exhaust, and he claims the oil doesn't look milky or anything.

He replaced the thermostat today, and it seemed to be ok. He took it on a test drive - about 30 miles away, and what would have been 30 miles back. But at about the 27 mile marker on the return trip, he got to a stop sign, and it stalled again. I had to go out and give him a jump. But it was not low on water. He claims the "Hot" light did not come on.

He drove it home, and it died on us again about 1/4 mile marker, and would not start. It acted like it was hot, but no light. I pushed him home and here we are.

What should we check?

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://www.auto-repair-questions.com/2013/06/ac-question-on-whether-to-add-some-pag.html'>A/C question on whether to add some PAG oil or not?</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-2970927800099699954' itemprop='description articleBody'>
I don't think the make/model is important, but we have a 1998 Mazda<br />Protege 
that we just love. I went to <a href="http://www.freeautomechanic.com/airconditioning.html" target="_blank">recharge the R134A refrigerant in my air conditioning system</a> (I<br />don't think it 
ever had a top-up since it was manufactured) but I<br />forgot to purge the line 
from the kit I bought. I got air into the<br />system and it's running strange. If 
I had done it right I would not<br />have had any issues, I'm certain. <br /><br />I 
tried bleeding some of it off then adding some a few times, and it<br />didn't get 
any better. A licensed A/C professional (he installed my<br />home A/C and heating 
system) said that air can make the compressor<br />cycle on and off and when it's 
on it can pull very hard on the engine<br />when the air gets to certain parts of 
the system. So I decided to<br />remove the R134A and the air and start over. 
<br /><br />I am not going to remove the compressor to drain and measure the 
oil<br />removed from it. If you knew how cramped the area is where 
the<br />compressor is located you would understand why. <br /><br />I now have an 
evacuation pump that will pull a vacuum down to 75<br />microns (at sea level, I 
guess), and a set of gauges. I am familiar<br />with the refrigeration cycle and 
process, and have viewed some decent,<br />well-explained YouTube videos on how to 
evacuate the system and<br />recharge it. <br /><br />As far as I know, we have no 
leaks. I should know for sure after I see<br />that the vacuum holds for 1/2 to 1 
hour after I shut the valves and<br />remove the pump.<br /><br />Assuming that I 
don't have any leaks, I want to know if I should add<br />any PAG oil to the 
system or not. I've read that the oil will<br />evaporate under vacuum. I've also 
read that the oil will stay in the<br />compressor and lines.<br /><br />I bought a 3 
oz can of PAG oil that gets added through the gauge set<br />in case I need it. 
The under hood sticker does not tell how much oil<br />to use, just the amount of 
refrigerant. <br /><br />Can I safely assume that the oil is still in the system or 
should I<br />add some? Too much oil can be detrimental to the system. <br /><br />I 
guess what I really need to know from someone more experienced is if<br />the oil 
evaporates with the refrigerant and any moisture in the system<br />(there 
shouldn't be any moisture in my system) when a vacuum is pulled<br />for 1/2 hour 
or so. <br /><br />If someone here knows whether I should add the 3 oz can and 
assume<br />it's not too much or if I should avoid adding any since I 
haven't<br />opened the system nor am I aware of any leak, please let me know. 
<br /><br />I don't need to know about the general procedure, how to use 
the<br />reclaimer, etc., just some information about the oil. However, 
I'm<br />open to any tips you might have.
