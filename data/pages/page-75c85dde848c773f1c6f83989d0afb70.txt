    <h3>Art Works: Prepping Teens for the Job Market</h3>
    <p>Lifeguarding at the pool, mowing lawns, working at a fast food restaurant, retail store or as a cashier at the local grocery store—these are just a few of typical summer job for teenagers.  For adults who are old enough, it’s difficult to imagine not being able to find a summer job back then.  But according to the <a href="http://www.bls.gov/cps/demographics.htm">Bureau of Labor Statistics</a>, the current summer unemployment rate for Americans between the ages of 16 and 24 hovers around 26 percent.</p>
