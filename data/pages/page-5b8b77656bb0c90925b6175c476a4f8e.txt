                <p class="content">By teaching these skills in the context of

                learning in a highly effective manner that basal programs do not

                <td width="236">
                <p class="content"><i>Learning Language Arts Through Literature</i> 
                is an exciting and different approach to language arts learning. 
                Best of all, it's been proven highly effective. Students and 
                teachers love it.</td>
              </tr>

            </center>
            &nbsp;<p class="content">Learning Language Arts Through Literature 
            is a fully integrated language arts program that teaches grammar, 
            reading, spelling, vocabulary, writing mechanics, creative writing, 
            thinking skills and more. <br>
            <br>
            For example, a basal program may teach adjectives separately from 
            other skills. The student may be asked to fill in blanks with 
            adjectives and circle them in a paragraph. This may give your 
            student a clue about adjectives, but does not translate into his 
            practical use of adjectives in his writing. <br>
            <br>
            In LLATL, your student may read literature passages that use 
            adjectives. He may be asked to read the passage without the 
            adjectives and decide how important they are to the understanding of 
            a story. Another activity may include writing a descriptive 
            sentence/paragraph about a picture using adjectives or rewriting the 
            passage using different adjectives. These activities not only teach 
            language arts skills in addition to adjectives, but your student 
            will incorporate the skills into his everyday writing. <br>
            <br>
            The goal of any language arts program should be to equip the student 
            for a lifetime of communication through the written and spoken word. 
            With LLATL, your student will acquire a deeper understanding and a 
            greater interest in the language arts subjects. <br>
            <br>
            Real books are used in the reading lessons, complete with vocabulary 
            words, discussion questions and a variety of activities. Reading 
            real books instead of basal stories makes reading a joy and the 
            beginning of a lifelong adventure for your student. <br>
            <br>
            All levels are written for a 36 week school year. Grades 1- 9 
            include a Teachers Book written in a conversational format making 
            it easy to use with little or no preparation time for the teacher. 
            Student Activity Books are available for the 1st - 8th grade. All 
            levels include review activities and assessments. </p>
