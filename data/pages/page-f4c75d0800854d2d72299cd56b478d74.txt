<div><b>Definition: </b>Convertible Seats can be used in both the Rear-facing and Forward-facing position. They are especially useful for big babies who have outgrown their infant only seat, but still need to be rear-facing until they are two years old.<p>

Convertible Seats When Used <b>Rear Facing</b>: 
<ul>
<li><li>According to the latest <a href="http://pediatrics.about.com/od/carseats/a/car-seat.htm">car seat guidelines</a>, infants and toddlers should ride in a rear-facing car seat (infant-only rear facing car seat or rear-facing convertible car seat) until they are two years old or until they have reached the weight and height limits of their car seat. 
<li>If a younger infant or toddler meets the weight or height limits of an infant only seat before age two years, it is likely safest to move the child into a convertible seat which is recommended for a child up to 35-40 pounds in the rear-facing position. This seat is also placed rear-facing.
<li>Harness straps should be at or below infant's shoulders when used rear facing. 
<li>Harness chest clip should be at infant&#8217;s armpit level.
</ul>
  
Convertible Seats When Used <b>Forward Facing</b>: 
<ul>
<li>All are rated for children up to 40 pounds, although some newer convertible seats have higher 65 to 80 pound weight limits.
<li>Once children are two years old (or, regardless of age, have outgrown their rear-facing car seat) children should sit in a forward-facing car seat with harness straps as long as possible and until they reach the weight and height limits of their car seat.
<li>Harness straps should be at or above child's shoulders. Use top harness slots of safety seat.
<li>Harness chest clip should be at child&#8217;s armpit level. 
</ul>
