	<div class="entry-category-interview entry-author-brittany entry-type-post entry" id="entry-6a00e552403d2f8833019104ff7a86970c">

					<h3 class="entry-header"><a href="http://www.nashvillegab.com/2013/08/jaida-dreyer-10-random-questions.html">Jaida Dreyer - 10 Random Questions</a></h3>
		
		<div class="entry-content">
                    <input type="hidden" name="zemanta-related" val="" />
			<div class="entry-body">
				<p>
<a class="asset-img-link" href="http://nashvillegab.typepad.com/.a/6a00e552403d2f88330192acc8c474970d-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, &#39;_blank&#39;, &#39;width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0&#39; ); return false" style="display: inline;"><img alt="JaidaD3" class="asset  asset-image at-xid-6a00e552403d2f88330192acc8c474970d" src="http://nashvillegab.typepad.com/.a/6a00e552403d2f88330192acc8c474970d-320wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="JaidaD3" /></a><br />Jaida Dreyer is a very special individual. It&#39;s not often you find someone as talented as she is that also has a personality of a rainbow. She is sweet, funny and amazingly beautiful. The best part? She can sing &amp; write songs like no one else. Jaida was born in Ontario and raised up in Iowa. At the young age of 19, Jaida was offered a publishing deal with Byron Gallimore. I figured it was time for the world to know a little bit more about Jaida Dreyer. </p>
<p>&#0160;1. <strong>At what age did you know you wanted to pursue music?</strong></p>
<p>It&#39;s something I have always wanted to pursue. I grew up singing in church, choir and along with the radio. I was always a writer of sorts, but coming from my background it wasn&#39;t something that ever seemed tangible or realistic. I was 17 when I moved to Nashville and I kind of just threw myself into it. I think it was the best thing I ever done. </p>
<p>2. <strong>What artists did you listen to growing up?</strong></p>
<p>Dolly Parton, Kris Kristofferson, Lorette Lynn, Waylon Jennings, Wanda Jackson, Steve Earle etc* I was raised on traditional country music. I have always been most intrigued by the songwriters that sang. There&#39;s just a certain kind of magic in listening to someone sing out their own stories and heartache.</p>
<p>3. <strong>What artists do you currently listen to?</strong></p>
<p>Corb Lund, Hayes Carll, Fred Eaglesmith, James McMurtry, Jessie Baylin, Mary Gauthier, Justin Townes Earle, Gillian Welch. It&#39;s an endless list for me. There&#39;s so much great music out there for you to discover if you just open your ears. </p>
<p>4. <strong>If you could duet with any male artist who would you pick?</strong></p>
<p>At this exact moment, I&#39;d say Hank Williams III. Tomorrow would probably be Kris Kristofferson. Yesterday it was Jack White. </p>
<p>5. <strong>If you could have any superpower in the world, what would it be?</strong></p>
<p>I&#39;d be pretty keen on being able to teleport myself. </p>
<p>6. <strong>One food you can&#39;t live without?</strong></p>
<p>Mexican</p>
<p>7. <strong>Sweet or spicy?</strong></p>
<p>All of the above. Depends on the circumstance. </p>
<p>8. <strong>Do you have a life motto?</strong></p>
<p>Keep on keepin&#39; on</p>
<p>9. <strong>Do you think country music should evolve?</strong></p>
<p>Everything evolves and changes, whether we want it to or not. That being said, I feel like to know where we&#39;re going in country music, we need to know and respect the foundation of what it was built on. I think a lot of artists choose to neglect that these days. </p>
<p>10. <strong>Any new projects in the works for you?</strong></p>
<p>This summer I&#39;ve spent a lot of time playing shows on the road and when I&#39;m home have really focused on writing again. I recently had a few songs placed in the ABC series Nashville. In the fall, we&#39;ll be going back into the studio to cut the first batch of songs for the new record and will hopefully have fresh ear candy released early next year. </p>
<p>You can follow Jaida on twitter @jaidadreyer </p>
<p>As always, for more country music updates, news &amp; gossip follow me @brittwv</p>
<p>&#0160;</p>

<a class="asset-img-link" href="http://nashvillegab.typepad.com/.a/6a00e552403d2f883301901f03204c970b-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, &#39;_blank&#39;, &#39;width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0&#39; ); return false"><img alt="Taylor-swift-selena-gomez-shut-the-fuck-up-vmas-2013" class="asset  asset-image at-xid-6a00e552403d2f883301901f03204c970b" src="http://nashvillegab.typepad.com/.a/6a00e552403d2f883301901f03204c970b-500wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Taylor-swift-selena-gomez-shut-the-fuck-up-vmas-2013" /></a><br />Clear back in 2009, the world was outraged by Kanye West&#39;s brazen microphone kidnapping while Taylor was accepting the award for Best Female Video.&#0160;</p>
<p>However, it appears that four years later, Taylor has become the official villain of the VMAs.&#0160;</p>
<p>During tonight&#39;s show, Taylor was once again awarded the Moonman for Best Female Video for <em>I Knew You Were Trouble</em>, but all anyone is talking about is how crappy she was to her ex, One Direction member Harry Styles, who was at the show.&#0160;</p>
<p>The first crappiness came when One Direction took the stage to announce the winner of the Best Pop Video. Taylor turned to her bestie Selena Gomez, who happened to be nominated, and said shut the fuck up, presumably aiming the sentiment to her ex up on stage. Selena ended up winning the award. Kind of sucked the wind out of Selena&#39;s win I&#39;m thinking.&#0160;</p>
<p>Then when Taylor took the stage to accept the award for Best Female Video, she couldn&#39;t help but throw a little shade towards an ex, presumably Harry, by saying&#0160;“I also want to thank the person who inspired this song…who knows EXACTLY who he is.”</p>
<p>Um, okay.&#0160;</p>
<p>Seriously, I think it may be time for Taylor to grow up just a bit and get over this whole ex thing. I think the world might be ready to break up with the whole woe is me bit - or maybe it&#39;s just me who is over it.&#0160;</p>
<p>On a side note, Taylor looked absolutely stunning. Her hair was fabulous and her dress was amazing. Check out her whole look <a href="http://stylenews.peoplestylewatch.com/2013/08/25/vmas-2013-performers-presenters-star-style/" target="_blank">here</a>.&#0160;</p>

		<div class="entry-content">
                    <input type="hidden" name="zemanta-related" val="" />
			<div class="entry-body">
				<p>Let&#39;s face it, no matter how talented you are there will always be someone out there more talented. That goes for looks, money, skills, and many other things - no matter how much you have of something, someone else out there will have more.&#0160;</p>
<p>That&#39;s when being creative and having a good understanding of thinking outside of the box sometimes comes in handy.&#0160;</p>
<p>Yesterday, Jake Owen tweeted a funny picture (or scary depending on how you look at it) of something unexpected he came across in his fuel tank.&#0160;</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Only in Nashville will someone leave you their CD and biz card in your fuel tank... Just found this.. Smooth move!! <a dir="ltr" href="http://t.co/OIUUD7V5QM">pic.twitter.com/OIUUD7V5QM</a></p>
</blockquote>
<p>
<a class="asset-img-link" href="http://nashvillegab.typepad.com/.a/6a00e552403d2f8833019104e914c6970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, &#39;_blank&#39;, &#39;width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0&#39; ); return false"><img alt="Jake owen CD and card" class="asset  asset-image at-xid-6a00e552403d2f8833019104e914c6970c" src="http://nashvillegab.typepad.com/.a/6a00e552403d2f8833019104e914c6970c-320wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Jake owen CD and card" /></a><br />Too funny! I enlarged the photo and the business card is for Tyler and The Little Mountain Band. A quick Google search turned up a <a href="https://www.facebook.com/TylerAndTheLittleMountainBand" target="_blank">Facebook</a>&#0160;for the band and a <a href="http://www.youtube.com/user/tylerhat9" target="_blank">YouTube account</a>. It also turned up Tyler Hatley&#39;s <a href="https://twitter.com/TylerHatley" target="_blank">Twitter account</a>&#0160;(<a href="https://twitter.com/TylerHatley">@TylerHatley</a>). I followed him and quickly got a message directly from Tyler. </p>
<p>I asked him about the card and CD and he says that he and his drummer were at Jake&#39;s block party on Monday in Nashville and they thought it might be a good way to get a foot in the door.&#0160;</p>
<p>As for how he knew it was Jake&#39;s pickup ... that&#39;s easy, he had spotted pictures of it that Jake had posted in the past.</p>
<p>Twitter ... helping stalkers and aspiring artists since 2006.&#0160;</p>
<p>Tyler says that he hasn&#39;t gotten a phone call from Jake yet, but watching the video below for <em>Good Life,</em> I have a feeling someone will be calling in the very near future.&#0160;</p>

				<p>
<a class="asset-img-link" href="http://nashvillegab.typepad.com/.a/6a00e552403d2f88330192acb1764c970d-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, &#39;_blank&#39;, &#39;width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0&#39; ); return false" style="display: inline;"><img alt="Luke-Bryan-2013-300-02" class="asset  asset-image at-xid-6a00e552403d2f88330192acb1764c970d" src="http://nashvillegab.typepad.com/.a/6a00e552403d2f88330192acb1764c970d-320wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Luke-Bryan-2013-300-02" /></a></p>
<p>SHAKE-UP ALERT!</p>
<p>Can we all just take a moment to appreciate that Florida
Georgia Line have dropped from #1 on Hot Country Songs and Country Digital
Songs? ‘Cruise’ is down to #2 on both, while ‘Here’s To The Good Times’ has also
been pushed to #2 on Top Country Albums. In addition, ‘Round Here’ has suffered
a fall to #8 on Hot Country Songs and #10 on Country Digital Songs. It remains
at #7 on Country Airplay.</p>
<p>The culprit for Florida Georgia Line’s slide down the charts
is Luke Bryan (kind of unfortunately), with ‘That’s My Kind of Night’, which
has shot to #1 on Hot Country Songs and Country Digital Songs (plus #16 on
Country Airplay) this week alongside the release of his 4<sup>th</sup> album ‘Crash
My Party’, which of course is also at #1. Amazingly, his last single of the
same name is also at #3 on Hot Country Songs and two songs from his new album
have hit Country Digital Songs; ‘Play It Again’ at #22 and ‘Dirt Road Diary’ at
#20. In addition, his past album ‘Tailgates &amp; Tanlines’ is at #4 on Top
Country Albums.&#0160;
