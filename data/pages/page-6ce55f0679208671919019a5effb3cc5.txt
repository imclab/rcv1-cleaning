<h1 class="title">Private Used Car Sales</h1>
<span class='st_email_hcount' displaytext='Email'></span><span class='st_sharethis_hcount' displaytext='ShareThis'></span>
<div style="float:right"><a href="http://files.findlaw.com/pdf/consumer/consumer.findlaw.com_lemon-law_private-used-car-sales.pdf" rel="nofollow"><img src="http://flimages.findlaw.com/design/public/images/pdficon_small.png" alt="Download article as PDF"/> Download article as a PDF</a></div>

<section id="article_content">
<div id="calloutmodule">
<!-- insert call out ad -->

<p>Newspapers, bulletin boards, and classified listing websites are often awash
with advertisements for used cars. While buying a used car from an individual is
a viable alternative to buying from a
<a title="Dealer Used Car Sales and Warranties" href="http://consumer.findlaw.com/lemon-law/dealer-used-car-sales-and-warranties.html">used
car dealership</a>, you should do so with caution. That’s because used cars
purchased from individuals are often not subject to the same protections as
those purchased from a dealership. Below, you’ll find explanations of the key
legal differences between buying from a licensed dealership and buying from a
private party.</p>
<p><strong>The Used Car Rule</strong></p>
<p>Private sellers generally are generally not covered by the Used Car Rule and
don't have to use the Buyers Guide (discussed in
<a href="http:///lemon-law/dealer-used-car-sales-and-warranties.html">Buying a
Used Car: Dealer Sales</a>). However, you can use the Guide's list of an auto's
major systems as a shopping tool. You also can ask the seller if you can have
the vehicle inspected by your mechanic before committing to a sale.</p>
<p><strong>State Law Implied Warranties</strong></p>
<p>Private sales usually are not covered by the
"<a title="What is an Implied Warranty?" href="http://consumer.findlaw.com/consumer-transactions/what-is-an-implied-warranty-.html">implied
warranties</a>" of state law. That means a private sale probably will be on an
"as is" basis, unless your purchase agreement with the seller specifically
states otherwise. If you have a written contract, the seller must live up to the
promises stated in the contract. The car also may be covered by a manufacturer's
warranty or a separately purchased service contract. However, warranties and
service contracts may not be transferable, and other limits or costs may apply.
Before you buy the car, ask to review its warranty or service contract.</p>
<p><strong>State Inspection Rules</strong></p>
<p>Many states do not require individuals to ensure that their vehicles will
pass state inspection or carry a minimum warranty before they offer them for
sale. Ask your state Attorney General's office or local consumer protection
agency about the requirements in your state.</p>
