<h2>Arkansas is thriving with southern music, art centers, independent film festivals and cultural expression.</h2>
<p><a target="_blank" href="/arts-crafts/"><strong>Art centers</strong></a>, museums and galleries in <a href="/places-to-go/cities-and-towns/city-detail.aspx?city=Eureka+Springs">Eureka Springs</a> and throughout the state display works of both the masters and up-and-coming artists. Located in the scenic Ozark Mountains, Mountain View is the "Folk Music Capital of the World" where visitors can enjoy authentic <strong>southern music</strong> traditions. The nighttime verve of the <a href="/places-to-go/nightlife/little-rock-nightlife.aspx">River Market District in Little Rock</a> and <a href="/places-to-go/nightlife/nwa-nightlife.aspx">Dickson Street in Fayetteville</a> make them prime <a href="/things-to-do/nightlife/">nightlife spots</a> to take in <a target="_blank" href="/dining/">trendy restaurants</a> and to enjoy live <strong>southern music</strong> from hard rock and jazz to country and the blues.</p>
<p>Performances by Broadway touring companies frequently pass through our cities, and the state is host to several successful <strong>independent film festivals</strong>, including the world-class <strong>Hot Springs documentary film festival</strong>. For classical music buffs, the <a href="/attractions/detail.aspx?id=18108&amp;r=Central&amp;city=Little%20Rock">Arkansas Symphony Orchestra</a> performs year-round, and if the blues are your thing, Helena in the historic Arkansas Delta serves as the backdrop for the Arkansas King Biscuit Blues Festival.</p>
