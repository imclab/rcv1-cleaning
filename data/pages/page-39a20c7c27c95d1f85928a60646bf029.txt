<!-- ws:end:WikiTextTocRule:92 --><!-- ws:start:WikiTextHeadingRule:3:&lt;h1&gt; --><h1 id="toc1"><a name="Overview of Social Networking's Impact on the Arts, Entertainment and Leisure"></a><!-- ws:end:WikiTextHeadingRule:3 -->Overview of Social Networking's Impact on the Arts, Entertainment and Leisure</h1>
 <hr />
<strong><em>Social Networks</em></strong> <!-- ws:start:WikiTextRemoteImageRule:105:&lt;img src=&quot;http://www.dmr.state.ct.us/images/srrespite102002-1.gif&quot; alt=&quot;&quot; title=&quot;&quot; align=&quot;left&quot; /&gt; --><img src="http://www.dmr.state.ct.us/images/srrespite102002-1.gif" alt="external image srrespite102002-1.gif" title="external image srrespite102002-1.gif" align="left" /><!-- ws:end:WikiTextRemoteImageRule:105 --><strong><em>are &quot;platforms for self-expression.&quot;</em></strong><br />
<br />
<br />
Social Networking websites are social structures created for people of all audiences. There is an array of different social networking websites featured on the Internet. People are creating their own spaces which allows them to <strong>connect and communicate</strong> with others of the same interest. Social Networks give people the <strong>freedom</strong> to voice their opinions and allows people to teach and learn from others. Everyone is looking for a place to fit in and social networks are giving that opportunity to anyone with access to the Internet. People spend their time searching for materials that grab their <strong>attention</strong>. Social networks gives individuals their <strong>own community</strong> to belong to. More and more people are getting involved in the arts, and Social Networks are one way that people are gathering information about books, music, photography, film making, etc. Networking websites are connecting people from around the globe because of the <strong>worldwide passion for the arts</strong>.<br />

<!-- ws:start:WikiTextRemoteImageRule:106:&lt;img src=&quot;http://www.fontshop.de/news/fontshop_web02/myspace_logo.gif&quot; alt=&quot;&quot; title=&quot;&quot; align=&quot;right&quot; /&gt; --><img src="http://www.fontshop.de/news/fontshop_web02/myspace_logo.gif" alt="external image myspace_logo.gif" title="external image myspace_logo.gif" align="right" /><!-- ws:end:WikiTextRemoteImageRule:106 --><br />
<a class="wiki_link_ext" href="http://www.myspace.com/" rel="nofollow">MySpace</a> is a popular networking site where many musical artists today are getting their &quot;big break.&quot; These musicians have easy access to fans all over the world without having to go on tour or spend money on recording and selling a demo. Musicians can easily post their music, blogs, information about merchandise, and upcoming shows. Posting music on Networking sites helps make independent musicians' music go &quot;<strong>viral</strong>.&quot; The number of users of MySpace increases by <strong>250,000 new members daily</strong>, which is definitely a promising number of new music enthusiasts.<br />

Purevolume gives musicians an easy and <strong>effecient</strong> way to share their information and start spreading the word about their music. Musicians can also post ringtones of their music for people to download to their telephones on purevolume and MySpace which is just another simple way of getting fans to introduce their favorite music to their own friends.<br />

<!-- ws:start:WikiTextHeadingRule:31:&lt;h2&gt; --><h2 id="toc15"><!-- ws:end:WikiTextHeadingRule:31 --> </h2>
 <!-- ws:start:WikiTextHeadingRule:33:&lt;h2&gt; --><h2 id="toc16"><a name="Overview of Social Networking's Impact on the Arts, Entertainment and Leisure-What needs to happen to produce employees who are ready for the business world in the future:"></a><!-- ws:end:WikiTextHeadingRule:33 -->What needs to happen to produce employees who are ready for the business world in the future:</h2>
 Most schools frown upon using Social Networking sites such as MySpace, Facebook, Xanga, and YouTube because of the distraction these websites can cause for students. Schools, colleges, and other places of education need to realize that Social Networks on the Internet allow people to connect and <strong>come together</strong> around one main topic or idea. Schools need to teach students how to be &quot;social&quot; and how to be effective <strong>collaborators</strong> because of the<!-- ws:start:WikiTextRemoteImageRule:114:&lt;img src=&quot;http://www.amigobc.com/business-people.jpg&quot; alt=&quot;&quot; title=&quot;&quot; align=&quot;right&quot; /&gt; --><img src="http://www.amigobc.com/business-people.jpg" alt="external image business-people.jpg" title="external image business-people.jpg" align="right" /><!-- ws:end:WikiTextRemoteImageRule:114 --> social world they are growing up in. Social Networks will not only change students' social outlook on things, but will help them see different ways to look at <strong>education</strong>. Using Networking sites in the corporate world gives businesses a running start to new ideas, <strong>expand</strong> their knowledge, and collaborate with others in the same working field, but that aren't even in the same country.<br />
<br />
