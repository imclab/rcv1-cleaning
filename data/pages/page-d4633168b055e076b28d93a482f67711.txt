            	<h2 class="section-bg section-bg-arts-entertainment section-front-display"><a href="/arts-entertainment">Arts + Entertainment</a></h2>
				<h1>College humor</h1>
								<div class="item image-main"><img src="/images/made/images/uploads/buffalo_com/content/arts/melissa_wanzer_718_400_80_s_c1.jpg" alt="Melissa Wanzer" title="Melissa Wanzer"></div><!--end image-->
                	<p class="post-details">blog by 
                	<a href="/staff/list/Kathryn%20Przybyla/1822/">
	
                	Kathryn Przybyla</a> 
                	&nbsp;&bull;&nbsp; <span class="dateline">January 28, 2012 @ 8:00am</span></p>
				<div class="item body">
<p> 
<p>Constructive uses of humor. It&#8217;s a real college course. Not kidding.</p>

<p>Is she a standup comedian with a love for teaching? Is she just a funny teacher? Not exactly, but <a href="http://www.canisius.edu/communication-studies/wanzer.asp" target=”_blank”>Melissa Wanzer </a>has more than enough credentials to teach you a thing or two about humor. </p>

<p>&#8220;Most of my research was on humor and how it could be used effectively,&#8221; Wanzer said. </p>

<p>Don&#8217;t think she&#8217;s watching Adam Sandler movies all day either. Her focus through education is on humor within the medical profession, among other things. </p>

<p>How nurses cope with stress in the work environment and how grieving adults cope with death are topics she has studied. Sometimes, humor is the cure. </p>

<p>&#8220;Students are sometimes disappointed that we&#8217;re not just watching funny movies, comedians and cartoons. It&#8217;s very researched-based. As funny as it sounds, I&#8217;m trying to get people to take humor seriously.&#8221;</p>

<p>Humor has always played a part of Wanzer&#8217;s life at a personal level and not just a professional one. &#8220;I started studying and reading it when my father was sick. He used it to deal with his illness,&#8221; she explained. &#8220;I use it with my husband too. Should I be upset at the fact that he forgot to get butter on his way home? No. He&#8217;s still a great husband, and I can laugh about it.&#8221; Wanzer feels like no one should take themselves too seriously when it comes to the little things like that. </p>

<p>The students in the constructive uses of humor class have an interesting option when it comes to the final grade for the semester. They can choose to take a written final, like most of their classes, or they can opt for a standup routine as their final grade. Most students choose the latter of the two, but that doesn&#8217;t mean there&#8217;s less preparation involved. </p>

<p>&#8220;They are all frightened,&#8221; Wanzer explained. &#8220;With 40 students in the class, it can be a little face threatening. But the coolest part about it is, that even though they all think they are going to bomb it and no one will laugh, most of the time they are really funny. They get up there and have their classmates laughing. It&#8217;s a confidence boost for sure.&#8221;</p>

<p>Sometimes it takes students a little while to warm up to the class, but there&#8217;s one story that Wanzer always tells to make them more comfortable in a constructive humor environment. </p>

<blockquote><p>When I was pregnant&#8212;very pregnant&#8212;I had a chair in my classroom that had been questionable all semester. Questionable in the ability to hold a person in it. The students were just commenting on how it looked like I hadn&#8217;t gained any weight from the baby, which was incredibly flattering after having gained 60 pounds. The showering of compliments clouded my perception for a minute and I proceeded to sit in the questionable chair. Iit broke to pieces underneath me. A couple of male students jumped up in the back, highly concerned and shouted, &#8220;Ms. Wanzer, are you going to give birth?&#8221; Slightly embarrassed, I responded, &#8220;if only it were that easy.&#8221;</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Even though she was convinced the males in her classroom that day were determined to gather towels and boil water, it&#8217;s a story that also gets her students thinking. They tell humorous stories about their names, their families, dating and all sorts of topics. In the long run, it really is about coping&#8212;coping with the way someone reacts to your upbringing, coping with the way your parents react to bringing home a new boyfriend and other comping outlets are dealt with through humor. </p>

<p>Sometimes it&#8217;s not just about being funny, it&#8217;s about being funny for the right purpose and situation. Humor can truly heal when used correctly, and why shouldn&#8217;t it? As they always say, laughter is the best medicine. </p>

<p><img src="/images/uploads/buffalo_com/content/arts/melissa_wanzer_2_thumb.jpg" alt="Melissa Wanzer" width="550" height="121"  style="border: 0;" alt="{title}" title="{title}" /></p>

<p>Photo courtesy of <a href="http://www.canisius.edu/communication-studies/wanzer.asp" target=”_blank”>Canisius</a><br /></p>
