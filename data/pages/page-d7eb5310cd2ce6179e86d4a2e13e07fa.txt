	 		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody"><span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">LONDON</span></span></span>, <span class="xn-chron">November 9, 2012</span> /PRNewswire/ --</p>

<p style="FONT-STYLE: italic"><span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Munich</span></span></span>, synonymous with German auto culture, races toward the future of electric cars</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">Read more on <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Munich's</span></span></span> economic development agency, City of Munich Department of Labor and Economic Development here:&#160;<a onclick='linkOnClick(this)' target='_blank' href="http://www.locations4business.com/europe/germany/bavaria--bayern-/munich-munchen/munich-city-department-for-work-and-business/">http://www.locations4business.com/europe/germany/bavaria--bayern-/munich-munchen/munich-city-department-for-work-and-business/</a></p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody"><span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Munich, Germany</span></span></span> and luxury car culture are concepts so intertwined, it is difficult to imagine one without the other. With <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Munich</span></span></span> as the capital of the Bavarian home of BMW and Audi, among many other automotive-sector leaders, it is not surprising to discover that <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Munich</span></span></span> is also leading the way in innovating on electric car technology and is introducing consumer-ready electric cars to the market.</p>

<p style="FONT-WEIGHT: bold"><span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Munich</span></span></span>: Electronic Mobility Pioneer</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">Designated as a 2009-2011 model region for electronic vehicles by the German government, <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Munich</span></span></span> participated in a federal funding initiative promoting and supporting electromobility. Some of the programmes implemented as a part of the project included <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Munich</span></span></span> public transport experimenting with hybrid buses, and most notably, automakers BMW and Audi launching fleet tests of their prototype BMW Mini E and Audi A1 e-tron vehicles.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">The current BMW ActiveE is a demonstration electric car based on the BMW 1-series and the second after the Mini E to be developed under BMW's Project i. BMW is set to launch its first electric car, the long-awaited i3 Megacity Vehicle in 2013.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">The Audi project puts the carmaker into partnership with <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Munich's</span></span></span> municipal utility company Stadtwerke M&#252;nchen (SWM) and the Technical University of <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Munich</span></span></span> (TUM) who have worked together to launch 20 Audi A1 e-tron car models onto <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Munich's</span></span></span> roads. In addition to getting these electric cars on the road, the project also dealt with data transfer and smartphone interface technology to make the human-machine interface issues more seamless. As a part of the infrastructure to support the scheme, E.ON and SWM have installed the charging stations required to keep the electric auto fleet up and running.
