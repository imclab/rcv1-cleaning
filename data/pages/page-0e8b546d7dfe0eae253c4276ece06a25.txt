								<h2>Mazda Launches Certified Used Car Program</h2>
								<small>by Steve Cypher on Monday, July 5th, 2010</small>
								<div class="entry">
									<p>Consumers in the market for a used car to finance with a bad  credit car loan got some good news this week when Mazda announced that it was  launching its own Certified Pre Owned program.<span id="more-1415"></span></p>
<h2>Affordable and  reliable used cars</h2>
<p>At Auto Credit Express we know that with the cost of new  vehicles rising every year, most credit-challenged customers that fill out our  bad credit car loan application and get approved at one of the dealers in our  nationwide network will decide that it makes more sense to go with a 2 or 3-year-old  pre-owned vehicle.  With the quality and  durability of vehicles at an all-time high, this decision makes sense. Now  they&#8217;re faced with another decision: should they buy a regular used car or a  certified used car?</p>
<h3>News from Mazda</h3>
<p>On Friday, Mazda North American Operations announced the  launch of a new Certified Pre-owned Vehicle (CPO) program with designed to give  buyers even more peace of mind as well as &#8220;lower customers&#8217; cost of ownership.&#8221;</p>
<h3>CPO vehicles</h3>
<p>If you&#8217;ve read any of the previous blog articles on Auto  Credit Express, you know how we feel about certified used cars &#8211; generally  known in the industry as certified pre-owned or CPO vehicles. The fact is, we  believe that, for the most part, certified used cars are a good choice for  consumers who plan on financing a used car using car loans with bad credit.</p>
<p>Most certified used car programs work this way: if a vehicle  meets certain age, mileage and inspection requirements, dealers can use a  trade-in vehicle or purchase back a lease turn-in and sell either one with an  extended service contract that is very similar to the warranties found on new  vehicles &#8211; the only difference being that the total mileage and number of years  can be extended past the original warranty date to cover additional months and  miles the amount of which depends on the manufacturer and the program involved.</p>
<p>Because of this built-in, manufacturer-backed warranty  coverage and other perks (such as roadside assistance), the dealer can (and  usually has to because of the additional cost involved) sell these CPO vehicles  at a premium over non-certified used cars &#8211; something that bad credit buyers  should keep in mind when considering the affordability factor of these  vehicles.</p>
<p>In the case of Mazdas new program, this means that every  certified Mazda goes through a multi-point inspection and reconditioning  program and will be identified on dealer lots by informational materials that  will outline the details of the warranty program as well as other benefits. </p>
<p>All Mazda CPO vehicles will offer either the remaining  balance of the factory warranty or an additional 12 month/12,000 mile  comprehensive warranty (covering, we&#8217;re assuming, systems similar to the  vehicle&#8217;s new car warranty) as well as a 7 year/100,000 mile powertrain  warranty. One other bonus &#8211; there is no deductible or do-pay associated with  any warrantable repair.</p>
<h3>Additional CPO perks</h3>
<p>In addition, all CPO vehicles will qualify for roadside  assistance at no charge for the balance of the powertrain warranty period &#8211;  which includes such amenities as flat tire changing, battery jump, vehicle lock  out service and fuel delivery.</p>
<h3>A reminder</h3>
<p>Remember that the program we&#8217;re discussing here is part of  the manufacturer&#8217;s (in this case, Mazda&#8217;s) certification programs. Just because  your local Mazda dealer advertises a car as certified does not necessarily mean  that it is certified by the manufacturer. Any dealer can &#8220;certify&#8221; a car, since  the word, itself, really has no meaning. So make sure that the &#8220;certified&#8221; car  you are buying is certified by Mazda. If not, you may be required to pay for  any repairs up front and submit the receipts to the warranty company for  reimbursement &#8211; a process that is much less desirable.</p>
<h3>Choose the right model</h3>
<p>One other thing that we&#8217;re more than a little bit nit-picky  with around here is keeping your bad credit auto loan payment affordable and,  fortunately, a number of Mazda vehicles fall into this category.</p>
<p>Although the Tribute, CX-9, CX-7, RX-8, Miata and Mazdaspeed3  are beyond the budgets of most bad credit buyers, the Mazda3, Mazda5, new  Mazda2, and possibly even the Mazda6 are all vehicles that may very well work.</p>
<p>All 2006-2010 vehicles with 60,000 miles or less qualify for  the program &#8220;as long as they meet the rigorous standards for quality.&#8221;</p>
<h3>In Summary</h3>
<p>Keep in mind that certified pre-owned vehicles are usually  more expensive than their non-certified counterparts. The costs of an  inspection, the required replacement of warn and defective parts as well as a  fairly expensive service contract all add to the price of a CPO vehicle. Before  buying one, it makes sense to do some research and compare the selling prices  of both certified pre-owned vehicles and their non-CPO counterparts (priced it with  a comparable warranty). Ultimately, you will have to decide for yourself if a  CPO car is worth the difference in price.</p>
