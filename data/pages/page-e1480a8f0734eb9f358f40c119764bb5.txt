<H2>
<P>Honey Bee Language Arts and Literature
</H2>
<P>
<P>Grades:               7-8
<P>Essential Skills:       Language Arts, Literature 
<P>Duration:                 1-2 class periods
<H3>
<P>OVERVIEW
</H3>

<P>Lesson Plan
</H2>
<H3>
<P>Introduction activity (30 minutes)
</H3>
<P>Discuss literature that includes references to honey bees Focus discussion on different ways
 bees have been portrayed. Read aloud from Hubbell's <I>
A Country Year </I>and sections of <I> Following the Bloom:  Across America with the Migratory Beekeepers </I>
 and contrast to children's stories such as<I> Winnie The Pooh.</I>
<P>
<P>Post enlarged copies of bee poems around the room. Give the students copies. Have the students read aloud a few poetry selections with bee themes, especially the poems for two
 voices. Ask the students to explain what they think each poem means. 
<H3>
<P>Activity 2 Writing about bees (30 minutes)
</H3>
<P>Explain different forms of poetry, such as Haiku, Cinguain, or Diamante. Have the
 students find and read poetry that include references to bees. Have them write their own poetry or Haiku.
<H3>
<P>
<P>Activity 3 Diary of an Africanized honey bee (45 minutes)
</H3>
<P>Give a copy of Diary of an Africanized Honey Bee (<A HREF="inf25.html">
Information Sheet 25</A>) to each student to take home and read as homework. Tell them they will need their copy for class the next
 day. Have them circle any words that are unfamiliar and discuss them in class the following
 day. Make a vocabulary list on the chalkboard.
<P>
<P>Let students take turns reading aloud the Diary. Discuss the bee biology contained in it.
 Compare it with what they learned in <A HREF="lsn31.html">
Lesson 3.1</A>. Facilitate a class discussion about what
 it means to anthropomorphize animals.
<P>
<P>Have the students write a story from a bee's point of view about the migration of
 Africanized honey bees from Brazil to Arizona. Provide a copy of <A HREF="act32.gif">
Activity Sheet 32</A> (Movement of AHB from Brazil) for reference.
<H3>
