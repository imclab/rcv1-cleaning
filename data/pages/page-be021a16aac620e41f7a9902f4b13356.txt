	 		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody"><span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">LOS ANGELES</span></span></span>, <span class="xn-chron">May 13, 2013</span> /PRNewswire/ -- Seven Arts Entertainment Inc. (OTCQB: SAPX) ("Seven Arts") today announced the appointment of <span class="xn-person" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name">Anthony G. Barlo</span></span> as President of Seven Arts Music and Television, a new subsidiary of Seven Arts.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">As founder of leading independent music company Microhits, Inc., Mr. Barlo has a proven track record of identifying and acquiring iconic music assets. He is also an industry expert in copyright protection, having resolved contractual and copyright disputes involving the Beastie Boys, <span class="xn-person" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name">Lenny Kravitz</span></span>, <span class="xn-person" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name">Tupac Shakur</span></span>, Lynyrd Skynyrd, <span class="xn-person" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name">John Mellencamp</span></span>, Public Enemy, Cat Stevens, <span class="xn-person" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name">Jaco Pastorius</span></span>, <span class="xn-person" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name">Sidney Bechet</span></span>, Igor Stravinsky and others. </p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">Seven Arts Music expects that it will immediately begin acquiring music publishing and recording catalogs in the <span class="xn-money">$1 million to $5 million</span> range, financed through Microhits' existing partners. New funding will also be available through its subsidiary Seven Arts Music and Television Inc. </p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">Since 2000, the Microhits catalog has grown to over 27,000 songs and recordings, including numerous gold and platinum records by artists such as Lady Antebellum, <span class="xn-person" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name">Rod Stewart</span></span>, <span class="xn-person" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name">Harry Connick, Jr.</span></span>, <span class="xn-person" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name">Donny Hathaway</span></span>, <span class="xn-person" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name">Christina Aguilera</span></span>, and many others. Microhits catalog also includes the single "Girls," from the current Billboard #2 album by Kid Cudi.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">As part of a long-term agreement, Microhits Music Ltd., which represents Microhits Music Corp.'s catalog in the UK/EIRE territories, will be sub-published by Seven Arts Music and Television. This also includes the catalog of China Music Publishing, the official music publisher for <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">the People's Republic of China</span></span></span>.</p>
