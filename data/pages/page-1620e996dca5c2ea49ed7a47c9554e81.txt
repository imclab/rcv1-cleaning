			<p>What famous American's 1952 autobiography is entitled From Under 
			My Hat?<br>
			A: Hedda Hopper's -- she was known for her exotic hats and juicy 
			Hollywood gossip.</p>
			<p>Somerset Maugham's 1919 novel The Moon and Sixpence is a roman a 
			clef about what great French artist?<br>
			A: Paul Gauguin.</p>
			<p>What is the origin of the phrase, &quot;United we stand, divided we 
			fall&quot;?<br>
			A: Aesop's fables. It's from the Four Oxen and the Lion, written in 
			the sixth century B.C.</p>
			<p>The first Encyclopedia Britannica, published in 1771, devoted its 
			first volume to &quot;A&quot; and &quot;B.&quot; How many additional volumes did it take 
			to cover &quot;C&quot; through &quot;Z&quot;?<br>
			A: Only two.</p>
			<p>Whose marriage in March 1952 was featured in a &quot;Life&quot; magazine 
			cover story?<br>
			A: Comic strip characters Daisy Mae Scragg and Li'l Abner of 
			Dogpatch. Marryin' Sam performed the ceremony for $1.35, ending 
			their 17-year courtship.</p>
			<p>The work of what famous French artist hung upside down in the 
			Museum of Modern Art in New York for 47 days before someone realized 
			it?<br>
			A: Henri Matisse, in 1961. The painting was Le Bateau.</p>
			<p>What comic-strip character started out in 1932 as a middle-aged 
			woman who sold apples on street corners?<br>
			A: &quot;Mary Worth,&quot; who was originally known as &quot;Apple Mary.&quot;</p>
			<p>Who used the pen names Acton, Ellis and Currer Bell?<br>
			A: Anne, Emily and Charlotte Bronte.</p>
			<p>What book did Aristotle Onassis keep on his desk in his 
			multimillion-dollar yacht Christina?<br>
			A: A copy of J. Paul Getty's How to be Rich.</p>
			<p>Who wrote the first modern ghost story in the English language?<br>
			A: Daniel Defoe. It was True Relation of the Apparition of One Mrs.
