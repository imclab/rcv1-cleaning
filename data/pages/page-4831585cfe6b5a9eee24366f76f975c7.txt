<p><b>Mario Armando Lavandeira, Jr.</b> (born March 23, 1978), known professionally as <b>Perez Hilton</b> (a play on "Paris Hilton"<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup>), is an American <a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">blogger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Presenter" title="Presenter">television personality</a>. His blog, <i>perezhilton.com</i> (formerly <i>PageSixSixSix.com</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> is known for posts covering <a href="/wiki/Gossip" title="Gossip">gossip</a> items about <a href="/wiki/Celebrity" title="Celebrity">celebrities</a>. He is also known for posting <a href="/wiki/Tabloid_(newspaper_format)" title="Tabloid (newspaper format)">tabloid</a> <a href="/wiki/Photograph" title="Photograph">photographs</a> over which he has added his own <a href="/wiki/Caption_(comics_convention)" title="Caption (comics convention)">captions</a> or "doodles". His blog has garnered negative attention for its attitude, its former <a href="/wiki/Outing" title="Outing">outing</a> of alleged closeted celebrities and its role in the increasing coverage of celebrities in all forms of <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">media</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_and_current_life">Early and current life</span></h2>
<p>Hilton was born in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami, Florida</a>, to parents from <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. He graduated in 1996 from Miami's <a href="/wiki/Belen_Jesuit_Preparatory_School" title="Belen Jesuit Preparatory School">Belen Jesuit Preparatory School</a> and received his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Fine_Arts" title="Bachelor of Fine Arts">BFA</a> in <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">drama</a> from <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>. In 2002, Hilton moved to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles, California</a>, where he resided for eleven years prior to relocating to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in 2013 stating, "I love New York and that is where me and my growing family want to call home right now".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Perez was referring to his first child, Mario Armando Lavandeira III, born on February 16, 2013, conceived with a donor egg and carried by a surrogate mother.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="PerezHilton.com">PerezHilton.com</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PerezHilton2009.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/PerezHilton2009.jpg/220px-PerezHilton2009.jpg" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/PerezHilton2009.jpg/330px-PerezHilton2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/PerezHilton2009.jpg/440px-PerezHilton2009.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PerezHilton2009.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf13/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Official publicity photograph used on PerezHilton.com, 2009.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>After graduation from New York University in 2000, and before beginning his blogging career, Hilton attempted a career as an actor.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> He briefly worked as a media relations assistant for <a href="/wiki/LGBT_social_movements" title="LGBT social movements">LGBT rights</a> organization <a href="/wiki/Gay_%26_Lesbian_Alliance_Against_Defamation" title="Gay &amp; Lesbian Alliance Against Defamation" class="mw-redirect">GLAAD</a>, was a <a href="/wiki/Freelancer" title="Freelancer">freelance</a> writer for gay publications, worked as a receptionist for <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">NYC</a> gay events club Urban Outings, and was briefly the managing editor of <i><a href="/wiki/Instinct_(magazine)" title="Instinct (magazine)">Instinct</a></i>, a gay men's magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-gaywitchhunt_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gaywitchhunt-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> He says he started blogging "because it seemed easy."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Hilton's angle on celebrity gossip includes an unapologetic desire to mingle with and be a part of <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_culture" title="Celebrity culture">celebrity culture</a>. He often describes celebrity <a href="/wiki/Awards_show" title="Awards show" class="mw-redirect">awards shows</a>, clubs, and private events he has attended, and posts photographs of himself with the celebrities he writes about under the "Personally Perez" category of his blog. Although Hilton has an affinity for some celebrities, such as <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sophia_Bush" title="Sophia Bush">Sophia Bush</a>, he also has a "vendetta" against certain stars, such as Disney Channel star <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Hudgens" title="Vanessa Hudgens">Vanessa Hudgens</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Gossip_Girl" title="Gossip Girl">Gossip Girl</a></i> teen actress <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Momsen" title="Taylor Momsen">Taylor Momsen</a>. Teen phenomenon <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a> publicized her personal disapproval of Hilton over <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> to which he replied.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Some have suggested, however, that Hilton's proximity to the celebrities about whom he writes has led to <a href="/wiki/Media_bias" title="Media bias">biased</a> coverage on his blog. He purports to have befriended <a href="/wiki/Paris_Hilton" title="Paris Hilton">Paris Hilton</a>, the source of his nickname and frequent subject of his posts. It has been noted, for example, that he rarely reports on stories or rumors casting Paris Hilton in a negative or unflattering light,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> and that, unlike most gossip blogs, he often acknowledges and praises her positive achievements.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Additionally, Hilton has been known to speak out publicly against the <a href="/wiki/Discrimination" title="Discrimination">discriminatory</a> behavior of celebrities and other public figures. For instance, he called for the firing of <a href="/wiki/Isaiah_Washington" title="Isaiah Washington">Isaiah Washington</a> from <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i> for making <a href="/wiki/Homophobia" title="Homophobia">homophobic</a> remarks and called for his readers to do the same.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> However, in early 2007, he was criticized by the blog <i>The Hollywood Gossip</i> for ignoring racist and homophobic remarks made by Paris Hilton.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Hilton often promotes his favorite up-and-coming musicians by posting streams of their songs under the "Listen to This" category of his blog.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>-based singer <a href="/wiki/Mika_(singer)" title="Mika (singer)">Mika</a>'s 2007 rise to popular success in North America has been partially attributed to Hilton's frequent support.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> Hilton and Mika also claim to be friends.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blogging_success">Blogging success</span></h3>
<p>During an interview in <a href="/wiki/Clich%C3%A9_Magazine" title="Cliché Magazine">Cliché Magazine</a>'s June/July 2010 issue, Hilton said that he only started blogging as a hobby. "What set my site apart is that prior to PerezHilton.com, most blogs were mainly online journals and diaries, but that never interested me. I wanted to talk about celebrities because they're far more entertaining."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> On March 11, 2005, within the first six months of Hilton's blogging career, <i>PageSixSixSix.com</i> was named "Hollywood's Most-Hated Website" by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Insider_(TV_series)" title="The Insider (TV series)" class="mw-redirect">The Insider</a></i>, catalyzing an initial surge in its popularity which temporarily crippled its <a href="/wiki/Web_server" title="Web server">server</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> Hilton claimed that on July 30, 2007, a seemingly "ordinary day," <i>Perezhilton.com</i> had over 8.82 million page views in a 24 hour period.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> Other sources dispute the reliability of Hilton's traffic claims.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Pop singer <a href="/wiki/Fergie_(singer)" title="Fergie (singer)">Fergie</a> has confirmed that she is referring to Hilton in her 2006 song "<a href="/wiki/The_Dutchess" title="The Dutchess">Pedestal</a>", in which she criticizes an unidentified person for making negative remarks about her on the internet.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On August 17, 2007, citing exclusive sources, Hilton announced the death of <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuban</a> President <a href="/wiki/Fidel_Castro" title="Fidel Castro">Fidel Castro</a>, and claimed that he was the first media outlet in the world to break the news.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> Although Hilton claimed that U.S. officials would be making an announcement within hours, no announcement was made, and no major media outlets verified Castro's death.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a> later determined that rumors were sparked by a meeting of Miami officials who were to discuss the city's plans when Castro dies. Rumors were further fueled by a road closure in the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">Florida Keys</a> that was due to a police standoff.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> Castro appeared in an interview on Cuban television on September 21, 2007, "looking frail but sounding lucid," and mocking rumors of his death.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> On September 9, 2009, Perez appeared on <a href="/wiki/The_Tyra_Banks_Show" title="The Tyra Banks Show">The Tyra Banks Show</a> and cited his premature reporting of Castro's death as his "one regret."</p>
<p>On September 15, 2008, Terra.com named Perez Hilton as the Hispanic of the Year in Honor of Hispanic Heritage Month.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>As of April 2009, PerezHilton.com was ranked by <a href="/wiki/Alexa_Internet" title="Alexa Internet">Alexa</a> as the 491st most <a href="/wiki/Web_traffic" title="Web traffic">trafficked</a> website on the Internet (143rd within the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>) with 2/3 of users being <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> and strongest <a href="/wiki/Demographics" title="Demographics">demographic</a> being females between the ages of 18 and 24.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_litigation">Civil litigation</span></h2>
<p>A suit against Hilton for <a href="/wiki/Copyright" title="Copyright">copyright</a> infringement was filed by <a href="/wiki/RCA/Jive_Label_Group" title="RCA/Jive Label Group">Zomba Label Group</a> on October 11, 2007, stating that Hilton illegally posted recordings by <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a> on PerezHilton.com. The illegal postings include at least 10 completed songs and unfinished recordings leaked over a period of three months. The suit asks for real and punitive damages in an unspecified amount as well as legal costs. Spears is not a party to the suit.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> In March 2008, Hilton announced on his blog that he would no longer blog about artists signed to <a href="/wiki/Sony_BMG" title="Sony BMG">Sony BMG</a> (which owns Zomba). However, when the lawsuit was settled in November of that year, Hilton ended his boycott and resumed regular blogging about Sony BMG artists.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On October 11, 2007, a judge cleared the way for Hilton to be deposed in an on-going defamation suit brought against him by <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a> <a href="/wiki/Samantha_Ronson" title="Samantha Ronson">Samantha Ronson</a>, after a post on PerezHilton.com claimed that she had planted cocaine in friend <a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Lohan" title="Lindsay Lohan">Lindsay Lohan</a>'s car and set Lohan up to be photographed while under the influence of alcohol and drugs. Hilton's report was a repetition of gossip initially posted on CelebrityBabylon.com. The judge was informed in court that the owner of that site has already settled the case with Ronson.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> On January 23, 2008, Perez Hilton was awarded $85,000 by <a href="/wiki/Superior_court" title="Superior court">Superior court</a> Judge Elihu Berle in this lawsuit filed by Ronson.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Hilton has attracted lawsuits due to his use of video footage of celebrities on his blog. He was named as a defendant in a <a href="/wiki/Lawsuit" title="Lawsuit">lawsuit</a> filed by attorneys for <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> actor <a href="/wiki/Colin_Farrell" title="Colin Farrell">Colin Farrell</a> on July 18, 2005, after posting a link to Farrell's <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_sex_tape" title="Celebrity sex tape">sex tape</a> with then-girlfriend <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Narain" title="Nicole Narain">Nicole Narain</a> on his site<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> and on February 20, 2007, a lawsuit filed against him by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal City Studios Productions LLP</a> for posting a <a href="/wiki/Toplessness" title="Toplessness">topless</a> image of actress <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Aniston" title="Jennifer Aniston">Jennifer Aniston</a> that was allegedly "misappropriated and illegally copied" from unreleased footage from her 2006 motion picture <i><a href="/wiki/The_Break-Up" title="The Break-Up">The Break-Up</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Bloggers, journalists, news agencies and photographers alike have charged that Hilton posts <a href="/wiki/Paparazzi" title="Paparazzi">paparazzi</a> photographs and other <a href="/wiki/Copyright" title="Copyright">copyrighted</a> content from their sites.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> On November 30, 2006, celebrity photo agency X17Online filed a lawsuit against L Hilton in <a href="/wiki/United_States_district_court" title="United States district court">federal court</a>, seeking over US$7.5 million in damages for <a href="/wiki/Copyright_infringement" title="Copyright infringement">copyright infringement</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> X17's co-owner Robin Navarre told the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> that the sale value of their photographs has been significantly reduced because the photos have appeared on PerezHilton.com before they could be published in magazines to which exclusivity is important. Navarre said,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"X17 can make as much as tens of thousands of dollars from one magazine on an exclusive story. In the case of the Spears smooch shot, X17 sold a two-page spread to Us Weekly, but the magazine decided to shrink the photo play (which lowered the price by $10,000, to $15,000)...because the images had already been on Hilton's site and others."<sup id="cite_ref-latimes_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Hilton defends his use of this material by claiming it falls under the <a href="/wiki/Fair_use" title="Fair use">fair use</a> exception to the <a href="/wiki/Copyright_law_of_the_United_States" title="Copyright law of the United States">Copyright Act</a>; that is, according to the <i>Los Angeles Times</i>, the photos are altered "to achieve a satiric or humorous end."<sup id="cite_ref-latimes_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> On March 9, 2007, a judge ruled that Hilton could continue operating his website while the lawsuit remained before the courts.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> On April 23, 2007, a consortium of five celebrity photo agencies filed a joint lawsuit in <a href="/wiki/United_States_district_court" title="United States district court">federal district court</a> in California against Hilton, claiming more than US$7 million in damages from 25 instances of alleged copyright infringement.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup> Just days later, on April 26, 2007, upon arriving at the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Airport" title="Sydney Airport">Sydney Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney, Australia</a> to attend the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Australia_Awards" title="MTV Australia Awards">MTV Australia Video Music Awards</a>, Hilton was served with a lawsuit by celebrity photo agency PhotoNews claiming <a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">C$</a>4,200 in damages for his alleged unauthorized use of a single copyrighted paparazzi photograph of <a href="/wiki/John_Mayer" title="John Mayer">John Mayer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Simpson" title="Jessica Simpson">Jessica Simpson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On June 20, 2007, <i>Variety</i> reported that Hilton's web host had dropped his site upon threats of <a href="/wiki/Legal_liability" title="Legal liability">liability</a> in the cases outlined above. Francois Navarre, co-owner of X17, told Variety, "It's the first victory, and we put a lot of work into trying to get this to happen." He added, "It's a precedent that's huge. When we were talking to Crucial Paradigm they were saying they were not responsible, dragging their feet. We had to threaten them and show them they were liable. His new host is Blogads, and we're contacting them already."<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On June 26, 2007, Hilton posted an open call on his blog to all of X17's photographers, both past and present, to contact him if they have not been adequately compensated for working overtime or pictures submitted in the past.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> Hilton has not used photos by X17 on his website since the suit.</p>
<p>According to an August 2009 <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> profile of Hilton, X17's lawsuit against Hilton was ultimately settled out of court. The terms of the agreement were not disclosed.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Outing_celebrities">Outing celebrities</span></h2>
<p>On his blog, Hilton is open about his homosexuality and about his desire to <a href="/wiki/Outing" title="Outing">out</a> those who he claims are closeted gay celebrities. When former <a href="/wiki/%27N_Sync" title="'N Sync">'N Sync</a> member <a href="/wiki/Lance_Bass" title="Lance Bass">Lance Bass</a> officially <a href="/wiki/Coming_out" title="Coming out">came out</a> as gay on July 26, 2006, Hilton received criticism for having been partially responsible in the outing. "It upsets me that people think what I'm doing is a bad thing," Hilton told <i><a href="/wiki/Access_Hollywood" title="Access Hollywood">Access Hollywood</a></i>. "I don't think it's a bad thing. If you know something to be a fact, why not report it? Why is that still taboo?"<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> On November 2, 2006, another celebrity often questioned by Hilton for remaining closeted, actor <a href="/wiki/Neil_Patrick_Harris" title="Neil Patrick Harris">Neil Patrick Harris</a>, revealed that he is gay.<sup id="cite_ref-MonstersCritics_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MonstersCritics-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Prominent members of the <a href="/wiki/LGBT_community" title="LGBT community">gay community</a> who have criticized Hilton's outing tactics include Arts &amp; Entertainment Editor of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Advocate" title="The Advocate">The Advocate</a></i> Corey Scholibo, <a href="/wiki/AfterEllen.com_and_AfterElton.com" title="AfterEllen.com and AfterElton.com" class="mw-redirect">AfterElton.com</a> editor Michael Jensen, and Damon Romine, spokesperson for the <a href="/wiki/Gay_%26_Lesbian_Alliance_Against_Defamation" title="Gay &amp; Lesbian Alliance Against Defamation" class="mw-redirect">Gay &amp; Lesbian Alliance Against Defamation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gaywitchhunt_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gaywitchhunt-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Kim Ficera, contributing writer for <a href="/wiki/AfterEllen.com_and_AfterElton.com" title="AfterEllen.com and AfterElton.com" class="mw-redirect">AfterEllen.com</a>, wrote,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"I have to question the character of a man who attacks others on such deeply personal levels, without provocation and for self-benefit, monetary or otherwise...If he's emotionally incapable of exhibiting even the tiniest bit of compassion for closeted people, if he can't be sensitive to the fact that coming out is a very personal decision and that the process can be difficult for some—especially celebrities—I feel sorry for him. If his juvenile behavior is his shtick, I think it makes him a much more pathetic figure, and one the gay and lesbian community should not support...If we support behavior like Hilton's, we applaud shallowness, arrogance, rage and invasion of privacy, and risk becoming what we despise."<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Some of Hilton's fellow gossip bloggers have also objected to his approach. <a href="/wiki/Trent_Vanegas" title="Trent Vanegas">Trent Vanegas</a>, who runs "Pink Is the New Blog," told <a href="/wiki/Salon_(website)" title="Salon (website)">Salon.com</a>, "I do not outright call people gay. I do not feel it is my place, or anyone else's place, to make people come out of the closet. Being shockingly hurtful just to get attention is not my style." David Hauslaib of gossip blog <a href="/wiki/Trent_Vanegas" title="Trent Vanegas">Jossip.com</a> stated, "The rationale that he's doing this for the good of that gay community is tantamount to saying that there is a <a href="/wiki/Homosexual_agenda" title="Homosexual agenda">gay agenda</a>. Is this a positive for the gay community? I'd say, 'No.'" Author, screenwriter, and former friend Japhy Grant has also questioned his motives, writing on Salon.com, "Spreading gossip is just your average pedestrian variety of immorality. Claiming that you're doing it to further civil rights is an outright sham."<sup id="cite_ref-gaywitchhunt_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gaywitchhunt-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>When questioned on <i><a href="/wiki/The_David_Pakman_Show" title="The David Pakman Show">Midweek Politics</a></i> about whether reporting on celebrities' <a href="/wiki/Sexual_orientation" title="Sexual orientation">sexual orientation</a> incites homophobia by making it news, Hilton indicated that he did not believe so.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> He said that coming out in Hollywood is not necessarily a bad thing, citing <a href="/wiki/Ellen_DeGeneres" title="Ellen DeGeneres">Ellen DeGeneres</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rosie_O%27Donnell" title="Rosie O'Donnell">Rosie O'Donnell</a> as examples: "I know there is some controversy about outing people, but I also believe the only way we're gonna have change is with visibility. And if I have to drag some people screaming out of the closet, then I will. I think that lots of celebrities have an archaic fear that being gay will hurt their career but look at Rosie. Look at Ellen."<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Some prominent gay rights advocates disagree. GLAAD spokesperson Damon Romine told Salon.com, "Media speculation about a celebrity's orientation is not something we support. This kind of gossip can lead some people to the decision to come out, as we've seen recently, or it may drive others further into the closet. People are going to become more guarded and secretive and not less, because they don't want to create any opportunities [for anyone to out them]." Actor <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Vilanch" title="Bruce Vilanch">Bruce Vilanch</a> said, "What purpose does it serve? These [people like Perez] are professional homosexuals. They are gay people for a living. They have to respect the rights of homosexuals who aren't professional."<sup id="cite_ref-gaywitchhunt_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gaywitchhunt-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> In an article entitled "Just How Dangerous is Perez Hilton?", AfterElton.com suggested that Hilton's actions put people's careers at risk, because anti-gay bias is still a prominent part of American culture. He continued, "Both as a gay man and a journalist, I question whether the gay community should approve of Hilton's actions...Being associated with someone who publishes photos of panty-less starlets and scribbles dirty notes...makes us look infantile and ridiculous."<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Miss_USA_2009">Miss USA 2009</span></h3>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Miss_USA_2009_controversy" title="Miss USA 2009 controversy">Miss USA 2009 controversy</a></div>
<p>Hilton served as a judge for the <a href="/wiki/Miss_USA_2009" title="Miss USA 2009">Miss USA 2009</a> pageant in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a> on April 19, 2009.</p>
<p>During the Q&amp;A portion of the contest, Hilton asked the <a href="/wiki/Miss_California_USA" title="Miss California USA">Miss California USA</a> representative, <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Prejean" title="Carrie Prejean">Carrie Prejean</a>, whether she believed every state should legalize same-sex marriage. She responded that she believes marriage is between a man and a woman due to her religious upbringing. After the pageant Perez Hilton made derogatory comments about the contestant and told ABC news "She lost it because of that question. She was definitely the front-runner before that,"<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup> leading some to believe that the answer directly had caused her to lose the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-fox_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fox-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup> Prejean stated that Miss California USA officials had pressured her to apologize for her statement and "not talk" about her Christian faith.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>After the pageant, Hilton posted a video blog on his website, where he called Prejean a "dumb bitch" and said her answer was the worst in pageant history.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup> Several politicians and commentators, including gay rights activists, condemned both Hilton and Prejean for making bigoted comments. However, some people, including a few gay rights activists, defended Prejean for exercising her right to free speech, saying that expressing one's views, no matter how offensive they may be, does not justify the use of misogynistic slurs against that person.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-MMVA_2009_incident_in_Toronto">Post-MMVA 2009 incident in Toronto</span></h3>
<p>In June 2009, Hilton alleged in a video blog<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup> that he was assaulted by <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas" title="The Black Eyed Peas">The Black Eyed Peas</a>' operation manager, Polo Molina, following the <a href="/wiki/2009_MuchMusic_Video_Awards" title="2009 MuchMusic Video Awards">2009 MuchMusic Video Awards</a> after party, where the group's lead vocalist, <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a>, was DJing; Hilton reported that he was "clocked" in the eye by Molina.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup> will.i.am also released a video statement<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup> in which he claimed he had approached Hilton regarding derogatory comments made by the blogger concerning bandmate <a href="/wiki/Fergie_(singer)" title="Fergie (singer)">Fergie</a> and the group's latest release <i><a href="/wiki/The_E.N.D." title="The E.N.D." class="mw-redirect">The E.N.D.</a></i>. During the incident, Hilton called will.i.am a "faggot" (Hilton later admitted in an interview with <a href="/wiki/The_Advocate" title="The Advocate">The Advocate</a> that he originally considered using the word "nigger" instead)<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64"><span>[</span>64<span>]</span></a></sup> in order to get a reaction out of him, and it was at this point that Molina allegedly attacked Hilton. will.i.am claimed the attacking party was a fan, although Molina was detained and appeared in a Toronto court concerning the incident. Hilton later filed a civil lawsuit against Molina, seeking an unspecified amount over $25,000 US to cover medical costs and as compensation for the "humiliation, mental anguish, and emotional and physical distress" that Hilton suffered as a result of the attack.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65"><span>[</span>65<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Gay_%26_Lesbian_Alliance_Against_Defamation" title="Gay &amp; Lesbian Alliance Against Defamation" class="mw-redirect">Gay &amp; Lesbian Alliance Against Defamation</a> called upon Hilton to apologize for using an anti-gay slur against will.i.am during the incident, saying "We have reached out to Hilton and asked him to apologize . . . and we would ask media outlets to avoid repetition of the slur in their coverage of this story."<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66"><span>[</span>66<span>]</span></a></sup> Hilton initially refused to apologize, saying, "I am saddened GLAAD chose to victimize me further by criticizing me for how I non-violently dealt with a very scary situation that, unfortunately, turned violent." On June 25, 2009, however, Hilton did apologize, stating "I am NOT apologizing to GLAAD...I am apologizing to the gay community, to anyone who was hurt by my choice of words, and to all the people who have ever emailed me to thank me for all that I have done to fight for gay rights over the last few years."<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67"><span>[</span>67<span>]</span></a></sup> In addition, he pledged to donate any money won in the lawsuit against Molina to the <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Shepard_Foundation" title="Matthew Shepard Foundation">Matthew Shepard Foundation</a>. In a statement released on its website, Judy Shepard, Chair of the MSF, declined the gift, saying that "because the lawsuit presumably involves the physical attack prompted by Mr. Hilton's admitted use of an anti-gay slur, the Foundation will be unable to accept any funds obtained in such a manner."<sup id="cite_ref-apology_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apology-68"><span>[</span>68<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Hilton received little sympathy in the media over the incident,<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69"><span>[</span>69<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70"><span>[</span>70<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71"><span>[</span>71<span>]</span></a></sup> a fact he addressed in his video blog.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72"><span>[</span>72<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_Mayer" title="John Mayer">John Mayer</a> made comments ridiculing Hilton and the incident on his <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> page, resulting in a volley of insults between the two.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73"><span>[</span>73<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gawker" title="Gawker">Gawker</a> viewed Hilton's public apology with skepticism, being of the opinion that it was just an attempt to rebuild his "brand" and a possible attempt to prevent Canadian authorities from filing criminal charges against him for his use of hate speech during the Toronto incident.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74"><span>[</span>74<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Michael_Jackson.27s_death">Michael Jackson's death</span></h3>
<p>On June 25, 2009, shortly after <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a> had gone into <a href="/wiki/Cardiac_arrest" title="Cardiac arrest">cardiac arrest</a>, Hilton posted an article about the star's illness, claiming it was a <a href="/wiki/Publicity_stunt" title="Publicity stunt">publicity stunt</a>. Hilton's original post featured sarcastic comments such as "Get your money back, ticket holders!!!!" and "Jacko pulled a similar stunt when he was getting ready for his big HBO special in '95 when he 'collapsed' at rehearsal!", in response to Jackson's recently sold out tour through March 2010. Upon realizing Michael Jackson's death was not a hoax, Hilton immediately deleted the original news post and replaced it with a shorter message that linked to a story at entertainment news site <a href="/wiki/TMZ_(website)" title="TMZ (website)" class="mw-redirect">TMZ</a>, which said the singer had in fact gone into cardiac arrest.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75"><span>[</span>75<span>]</span></a></sup> Hilton has received backlash for the incident, becoming entangled in a Twitter argument with <a href="/wiki/Pete_Wentz" title="Pete Wentz">Pete Wentz</a> about the situation.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76"><span>[</span>76<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Miley_Cyrus_upskirt_picture">Miley Cyrus upskirt picture</span></h3>
<p>On June 13, 2010, Hilton posted a <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> message linking to an <a href="/wiki/Upskirt" title="Upskirt">upskirt</a> picture of <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>, which allegedly showed the singer without underpants. Since Cyrus was still <a href="/wiki/Minor_(law)" title="Minor (law)">underage</a> at the time, questions were raised as to whether or not <a href="/wiki/Child_pornography" title="Child pornography">child pornography</a> charges could be raised.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77"><span>[</span>77<span>]</span></a></sup> The site that he had linked to removed the picture in question. Perez responded to the accusations by claiming that the controversy was "fake" and that Cyrus did wear underwear in the photo in question. Hilton did not re-post the photo for proof. Instead, he linked to another image of Cyrus, supposedly taken on the same occasion, to prove that she had been wearing underwear.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78"><span>[</span>78<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anti-bullying_campaign">Anti-bullying campaign</span></h3>
<p>In October, 2010, Hilton became involved in the <a href="/wiki/It_Gets_Better_Project" title="It Gets Better Project">It Gets Better Project</a> started by <a href="/wiki/Dan_Savage" title="Dan Savage">Dan Savage</a>, urging celebrities to support gay teenagers who were being driven to suicide by anti-gay bullying.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup> This prompted <a href="/wiki/Khlo%C3%A9_Kardashian" title="Khloé Kardashian">Khloé Kardashian</a> to call out Hilton as her "personal bully", claiming that it seemed hypocritical to make an anti-bullying message at the request of a man who had repeatedly bullied her.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80"><span>[</span>80<span>]</span></a></sup> On October 13, 2010, Hilton posted a video on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> entitled "I'm Going To Be Doing Things Differently," in which he addressed his actions over the previous years, and apologized for bullying celebrities. Hilton vowed to change the occasionally malicious tone of his website, even if it meant losing readers and revenue. Hilton also announced the launch of three new websites: CoCoPerez.com, which focuses on fashion, FitPerez.com, which encourages <a href="/wiki/Physical_fitness" title="Physical fitness">physical fitness</a>, and TeddyHilton.com, an <a href="/wiki/Animal_rights" title="Animal rights">animal rights</a> site.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81"><span>[</span>81<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Hilton expressed that his own son, Mario Armando Lavandeira III, will likely be bullied as well. Hilton said, "He'll probably be bullied out and about because people might say things to me in front of him when we're in public... That's even one of the reasons why, even though his name is Mario, I call him Perez Jr. I want him to have an alter ego as well so when people do say things that are hurtful about his dad or hurtful about him hopefully it won't hurt as much because they don't really know him. Those people know you as Perez Jr. but you're not Perez Jr. so it doesn't matter what they say." <sup id="cite_ref-Conversations_with_Maria_82-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Conversations_with_Maria-82"><span>[</span>82<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lady_Gaga">Lady Gaga</span></h3>
<p>In 2013, the long friendship Hilton had with <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a> came to an end. The initial cause of their feud is still unconfirmed. In February 2013, Lady Gaga cancelled her <a href="/wiki/Born_This_Way_Ball_Tour" title="Born This Way Ball Tour" class="mw-redirect">tour</a> due to a hip injury caused by repetitive movements in her show. According to claims made by Lady Gaga on twitter, Perez responded by sending her a picture featuring a wheelchair with the word Karma written across and Madonna pointing a gun.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83"><span>[</span>83<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84"><span>[</span>84<span>]</span></a></sup> In August, with the release of Lady Gaga's new single, <a href="/wiki/Applause_(song)" title="Applause (song)">Applause</a>, Perez began posting illegal download links on twitter and commenting frequently on the single's lack of success.</p>
<p>On 18 August, a twitter user reported to Gaga that Perez Hilton had been spotted in the singer's New York City apartment building. Gaga asked the follower to enter the building and collect photographic evidence, before claiming that Hilton was stalking her.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85"><span>[</span>85<span>]</span></a></sup> Later that day, Perez published a statement on his website in which he denied stalking Lady Gaga. He claimed that he was looking for a place to live in New York after the birth of his son, and that he was shown the apartment without knowing Lady Gaga lived in the building.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86"><span>[</span>86<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The feud between the pair led many of Lady Gaga's fans to send antagonistic tweets to Hilton, threatening both the blogger and his infant son.<sup id="cite_ref-dailymail.co.uk_87-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailymail.co.uk-87"><span>[</span>87<span>]</span></a></sup> Hilton retaliated by sending tweets to Lady Gaga's parents, saying " If anything happens to me or my son, YOU will have blood on your hands. Hopefully you get your daughter the help she needs!"<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88"><span>[</span>88<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89"><span>[</span>89<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup> Gaga has since asked her fans not to send death threats to Hilton, although the blogger was not referenced by name in her statement.<sup id="cite_ref-dailymail.co.uk_87-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailymail.co.uk-87"><span>[</span>87<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
