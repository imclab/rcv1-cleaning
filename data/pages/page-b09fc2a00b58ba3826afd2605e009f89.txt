                          <p>Covered in light brown mess and holding one of her young daughters, Jill Kaplan stood in front of the mountain of laundry, looking worn and defeated.</p><p>Before kids and marriage, the work-at-home mom had laundry. Now she has a laundromat. She used to have a purse. Now she has a bag of used lollipops, tissues, other junk and - if you look hard enough - a wallet.</p><p>Although tired and slightly disheveled, however, Jill wouldn't dream of trading in the family she loves - even for the "perfect" life of her picturesque neighbors.</p><p>Jill is the main character in The Pajama Diaries, Kingston native Terri Libenson's syndicated comic strip found every day in the funny pages of The Citizens' Voice and other newspapers.</p><p>Now, though, Libenson has put together a collection of her strips into a new book, The Pajama Diaries - Deja To-Do. </p><p>The book is available from major retailers online. It's also on the shelves of the Barnes and Noble in Wilkes-Barre Township, where Libenson will be signing copies from 7 to 9 p.m. Friday.</p><p>Libenson's strip aims to capture the hectic, crazy and often humorous aspects of modern family life.</p><p>"I thought that there really wasn't a comic strip that portrays this," Libenson said, "especially with a female protagonist."</p><p>In the strip, Jill and her family mirror Libenson and her family - albeit with a lot of creative license, she said.</p><p>Jill also draws comics for a living but seems to spend much of her time managing her home and the lives of her two precocious daughters. Libenson highlights the adjustment to becoming a work-at-home mom and the daily oddities of raising a family, which of course produce a number of humorous situations.</p><p>While her family is one of the bigger influences on the characters, though, Libenson said she doesn't ink her life on paper verbatim.</p><p>"It varies. Sometimes our family is a jumping off point. A lot of times there's some creative license," she said. "I'm starting to get slight indications from my oldest daughter that I shouldn't mirror her life too closely."</p><p>Though the strip often focuses on stuff such as Jill walking into a room 10 minutes after cleaning it and finding total chaos with her two daughters standing with big grins on their faces, Libenson isn't afraid to feature some of the more realistic aspects of family life that often don't find their way to the funny pages, including sex and intimacy.</p><p>"That's something I did intentionally from the start," Libenson said. "I think it's a really honest part of family life and a relationship."</p><p>"Talk about an opportunity for humor," she added with a laugh, "especially with having kids."</p><p>Jill and her husband Rob - who mirrors Libenson's husband, Michael Davis - struggle to find time to spend alone. In one panel, the couple are sitting in bed and Rob is showing Jill a chart that looks like it's measuring the diving stock market.
