                        <h2 class="with_sub">Convertible car seats</h2>
                        <h3 class="subhead">A well selected and properly installed seat can keep a child safe for several years</h3>
                     
                     <!--ginsu_info babies-kids/baby-toddler/convertible-car-seats/overview/convertible-car-seats-ov.htm | 256909,rev12 | srcfile_mod_time: Fri Oct 12 10:19:54 2012 | ginsu_time: Mon Aug 26 12:49:49 2013 ginsu_info-->
                        <div class="last_rev">
                           Last reviewed: November 2011
                        </div>
                     
                     <!--ginsu_info babies-kids/baby-toddler/convertible-car-seats/overview/convertible-car-seats-ov.htm | 256909,rev12 | srcfile_mod_time: Fri Oct 12 10:19:54 2012 | ginsu_time: Mon Aug 26 12:49:49 2013 ginsu_info-->
                        <div class="floatRightMedia">
                           <div id="video-box">
                              <div class="box-no-tabs-photo">
                                 <div class="img-border"><img alt="Little boy in a car seat" border="0" src="http://static2.consumerreportscdn.org/cro/cdn-resources/images/babies-kids/baby-toddler/convertible-car-seats/overview/cro_car_seats.jpg" width="240px"></div>
                                 <div class="photo-text"><strong>Convertible car seats</strong></div>
                                 <div class="description">
                                    <div xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word" class="description">Rear-facing longer is better.</div>
                                 </div>
                              </div>
                           </div>
                        </div>
                        <p xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word" class="pSpan">Car seats should be at the top of every new parent's to-buy list. Not only will you need one to take your baby home from the
                           hospital, but for every car ride thereafter. Every state requires that children up to 4 years old be secured in a car seat
                           while riding, and most require booster seats for older children.
                        </p>
                        <p xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word">Motor vehicle crashes remain the leading cause of death for children ages 3-14 in the U.S. In 2009, of the 322 child passengers
                           under age 4 who died in motor-vehicle crashes, 31 percent were riding unrestrained (see <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=giYQE1Hskjc" target="blank" title="NHTSA crash test video of unrestrained infant">NHTSA crash test video of unrestrained infant</a>).
                        </p>
                        <p xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word">Car seats can reduce fatalities of infants younger than one year by 71 percent and by 54 percent for children aged one to
                           four years. (See our full coverage of <a href="/cro/babies-kids/baby-toddler/car-seats/index.htm" inlinetype="rxhyperlink" rxinlineslot="103" sys_dependentid="229080" sys_dependentvariantid="833" sys_folderid="218084" sys_relationshipid="2593070" sys_siteid="308" title="all car seats">all car seats</a>.)
                        </p>
                        <div xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word" style=" border: 1px solid rgb(221, 221, 221); padding-right: 19px; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 19px; background-color: rgb(248, 248, 248); padding-bottom: 4px;">
                           <h3 class="article"><em>CR</em> convertible-seat test key findings
                           </h3>
                           <ul class="bullet-list-space">
                              <li>There was a wide disparity in how easy the seats were to properly install and fit into a variety of vehicle types.</li>
                              <li>Research shows keeping children two years&nbsp;old and younger in a <a href="/cro/babies-kids/baby-toddler/convertible-car-seats/rear-facing-longer-is-better/convertible-car-seats-rear-facing.htm" inlinetype="rxhyperlink" rxinlineslot="103" sys_dependentid="256915" sys_dependentvariantid="845" sys_folderid="256899" sys_relationshipid="2593074" sys_siteid="308" title="rear-facing position">rear-facing position</a> helps prevent severe injury.
                              </li>
                              <li>There are more seat models available that have higher harness weights to accomodate larger kids both rear-and forward-facing.</li>
                              <li>Our technicians found rear-facing car seat installations more difficult than forward-facing.</li>
                           </ul>
                        </div>
                        <h3 xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word" class="article">Infant vs. convertible car seats</h3>
                        <p xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word">A rear-facing infant seat is the first stage. A built-in harness secures the infant, reclined at an angle typically between
                           30 and 45 degrees, to provide optimum protection in a crash without interfering with breathing. Infant seats can accommodate
                           most children from birth up to about 22 lbs. or more. (See our infant seat <a href="/cro/babies-kids/baby-toddler/car-seats/infant-car-seat-ratings/ratings-overview.htm" title="Ratings">Ratings</a>.)
                        </p>
                        <p xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word">With its removable carrier and swing-up handle, an infant seat lets you move your baby in and out of the car without disturbing
                           him. Though it might be a better value to jump into a convertible seat first, infant seats, by their design, tend to be more
                           compact and secure infants better when compared to larger convertible models, which is why we recommend them as the first
                           step.
                        </p>
                        <p xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word">Though convertible seats can be used for a newborn, they will likely fit better in an infant seat. Convertible seats are best
                           for when a baby's weight reaches the infant seat's limit, which may be as early as 6 to 9 months old. Orienting the convertible
                           seat in a rear-facing position until your baby is at least 1 year old and over 20 lbs. is a must. But research shows that
                           babies up to age two&nbsp;are better protected rear-facing (see <a href="/cro/babies-kids/baby-toddler/convertible-car-seats/rear-facing-longer-is-better/convertible-car-seats-rear-facing.htm" inlinetype="rxhyperlink" rxinlineslot="103" sys_dependentid="256915" sys_dependentvariantid="845" sys_folderid="256899" sys_relationshipid="2593073" sys_siteid="308" title="Rear-facing longer is better">Rear-facing longer is better</a>). Eventually, you can "convert" the seat to face forward, and use it that way until your toddler reaches the seat's forward-facing
                           height and weight limits. Overall, height requirements or limits for the convertible seats we tested ranged from 19 inches
                           to 53 inches, and weight limits from 5 pounds to 80 pounds, so they can be used for several years. (Learn more about <a href="http://news.consumerreports.org/baby/2009/05/car-seats-safe-transportation-for-overweight-kids--were-all-aware-that-many-of-us-in-the-us-are-heavier-than-we-should.html" title="convertible car seats for kids up to 100 pounds">convertible car seats for kids up to 100 pounds</a>.)
                        </p>
                        <h3 xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word" class="article">When in doubt, throw it out</h3>
                        <p xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word">The results from many of our tests reinforce the message that it's best not to re-use any car seat that has been in a crash,
                           whether or not it shows visible cracks. After our tests, even models that are not visibly cracked can still show signs of
                           being potentially compromised upon close inspection: Plastics are visibly stressed, internal structures and bars are bent
                           or have changed position, and parts that help create a taut, secure fit became pinched or difficult to use.
                        </p><a xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word" id="1" name="1" style=" color: #000; text-decoration: none;"></a> 
                        <div xmlns:o="urn:www.microsoft.com/office" xmlns:st1="urn:www.microsoft.com/smarttags" xmlns:w="urn:www.microsoft.com/word" style=" border: 1px solid rgb(221, 221, 221); padding-right: 19px; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 19px; background-color: rgb(248, 248, 248); padding-bottom: 4px;">
                           <h3 class="article">The federal crash standard: Realistic to today's vehicles?</h3><br><div class="rx_ephox_inlinevariant" contenteditable="false" style=" padding-left: 10px; float: right; display: inline;"><img alt=""Bench" style seat" border="0" contenteditable="false" height="125" inlinetype="rxvariant" rxinlineslot="105" rxselectedtext="" src="http://static2.consumerreportscdn.org/cro/cdn-resources/images/babies-kids/baby-toddler/convertible-car-seats/overview/sled_bench.jpg" sys_dependentid="257016" sys_dependentvariantid="302" sys_folderid="256893" sys_relationshipid="2593069" sys_siteid="308" unselectable="on" width="150"> 
                              <div class="description" style=" width: 150px;">The flat "bench"-type car seat required by the federal standard for child seat testing, at the facility where we tested our
                                 convertible models.
                              </div>
                           </div>
                           <p class="pSpan">Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard 213, which governs requirements for child restraints, was introduced in 1971. Though
                              it was updated in the last few years, our technicians have found that it isn't representative of current vehicles. Our engineers
                              note that in order to be representative, the "bench"-style seat used should be more contoured and have a firmer cushion. The
                              standard also uses two-point belts instead of the three-point lap-and-shoulder belts found in contemporary passenger vehicles.
                              Finally, the standard lacks a side-impact test. While improvement in the standard is needed, car seats are still effective
                              when properly installed. According to <a href="http://www.usa.safekids.org/skbu/cps/index.html" target="blank" title="Safe Kids Worldwide">Safe Kids Worldwide</a>, properly used safety seats decrease the risk of death by 71 percent for infants and 54 percent for toddlers. They are often
                              installed improperly, however. (Go to the American Academy of Pediatrics Web site for <a href="http://www.healthychildren.org/English/safety-prevention/on-the-go/Pages/Car-Safety-Seats-Information-for-Families.aspx" target="blank" title="car seat installation tips">car seat installation tips</a>, and see <a href="/cro/babies-kids/baby-toddler/convertible-car-seats/installation-tricks-of-the-trade/convertible-car-seats-installation.htm" inlinetype="rxhyperlink" rxinlineslot="103" sys_dependentid="256912" sys_dependentvariantid="845" sys_folderid="256907" sys_relationshipid="2593067" sys_siteid="308" title="car seat installation tricks of the trade">car seat installation tricks of the trade</a> for more information.)
                           </p>
                        </div>
