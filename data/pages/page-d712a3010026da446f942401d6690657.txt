</tr></table></div><a name="full"></a><br><h2>The new 2013 <a href="/cars/topics/cadillac" class="alinks-link" title="Cadillac">Cadillac</a> CTS-V Coupe </h2>
<p>The Cadillac CTS-V Coupe is the most dramatic model in the V-Series range of high-performance luxury models, blending a personal 2+2 layout with a <a href="/cars/reference/car-dictionary-glossary-automotive-termsd/drivetrain" class="alinks-link" title="Drivetrain: Glossary definition">drivetrain</a> vetted on Germany’s famed Nürburgring.</p>
<p><span class="inline inline-left"><img src="http://www.autospectator.com/cars/files/images/2013-Cadillac-CTS-V-Coupe-007.jpg" alt="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo" title="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo"  class="image image-_original" width="500" height="333" /><span class="caption" style="width: 498px;"><strong>2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo</strong></span></span></p>
<p>Like the Sedan model that established the CTS-V legacy, the V-Series Coupe is powered by a supercharged 6.2L V-8 that delivers 556 <a href="/cars/reference/car-dictionary-glossary-automotive-termsh/horsepower-hp" class="alinks-link" title="Horsepower: Glossary definition">horsepower</a> (415 kW) and is backed by either a six-speed manual transmission or a six-speed automatic. A collection of technical and performance elements complement the powertrain, including Magnetic Ride Control, Brembo brakes and Michelin Pilot Sport PS2 performance tires mounted on 19-inch forged aluminum wheels.</p>
<p><span class="inline inline-left"><img src="http://www.autospectator.com/cars/files/images/2013-Cadillac-CTS-V-Coupe-008.jpg" alt="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo" title="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo"  class="image image-_original" width="500" height="333" /><span class="caption" style="width: 498px;"><strong>2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo</strong></span></span></p>
<p>A unique grille that doubles front-end airflow to help enable its high-performance capabilities is one of the V-Series’ distinguishing features, along with an aggressive front fascia and raised center section of the aluminum hood that provides clearance for the <a href="/cars/reference/car-dictionary-glossary-automotive-termss/supercharger" class="alinks-link" title="Supercharger: Glossary definition">supercharger</a>.</p>
<p><span class="inline inline-left"><img src="http://www.autospectator.com/cars/files/images/2013-Cadillac-CTS-V-Coupe-010.jpg" alt="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo" title="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo"  class="image image-_original" width="500" height="333" /><span class="caption" style="width: 498px;"><strong>2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo</strong></span></span></p>
<p>For 2013, the CTS-V family adds new, two-piece front brake rotors and Glacier Blue Metallic exterior color. The two-piece rotors replace co-cast components, offering greater resistance to warping and potentially longer rotor life, as well as reduced weight. The design features a separate rotor mounted to a “hat” that attaches the brake assembly to the wheel hub. The hat and rotor heat and cool at different rates, which helps the rotor, dissipate heat more effectively to prevent warping.</p>
<p><span class="inline inline-left"><img src="http://www.autospectator.com/cars/files/images/2013-Cadillac-CTS-V-Coupe-005.jpg" alt="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo" title="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo"  class="image image-_original" width="500" height="332" /><span class="caption" style="width: 498px;"><strong>2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo</strong></span></span></p>
<p>CTS-V Coupe design and exterior features</p>
<p>Compared with non-V Series variants, the CTS-V exterior incorporates a number of unique cues that blend function with aesthetics. The dihedral Cadillac grille, for example, is larger to enable more air intake and features a satin finish, while the raised hood encompasses the supercharged engine beneath it. The front and rear fascias, as well as a rear spoiler that houses the center high-mounted stop lamp, are specific to the car, as are its standard 19-inch wheels.</p>
<p><span class="inline inline-left"><img src="http://www.autospectator.com/cars/files/images/2013-Cadillac-CTS-V-Coupe-009.jpg" alt="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo" title="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo"  class="image image-_original" width="500" height="375" /><span class="caption" style="width: 498px;"><strong>2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo</strong></span></span></p>
<p>Additional exterior features include:</p>
<p>    High-intensity discharge headlamps with Adaptive Forward Lighting System are standard<br />
    Heated and power-adjustable outside mirrors are standard<br />
    Rainsense automatic windshield wipers are standard<br />
    Nineteen-inch painted aluminum wheels are standard<br />
    Nineteen-inch high-polished aluminum wheels are optional<br />
    Nineteen-inch aluminum wheels with satin graphite finish are optional and are packaged with yellow brake calipers<br />
    Exterior colors: Radiant Silver Metallic, Black Raven, Black Diamond Tricot, Crystal Red Tint coat, White Diamond Tricot, Thunder Gray Chroma Flair, Opulent Blue Metallic and Glacier Blue Metallic.</p>
<p><span class="inline inline-left"><img src="http://www.autospectator.com/cars/files/images/2013-Cadillac-CTS-V-Coupe-002.jpg" alt="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo" title="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo"  class="image image-_original" width="500" height="338" /><span class="caption" style="width: 498px;"><strong>2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo</strong></span></span></p>
<p>CTS-V Coupe interior features</p>
<p>The CTS-V Coupe interior features streamlined instrumentation, LED lighting and hand-cut-and-sewn accents. It includes performance seats featuring perforated sueded microfiber inserts designed to hold the driver and front passenger in place during cornering. Recaro performance driving seats are optional and provide excellent support during performance driving. These 14-way adjustable performance seats include pneumatic bolster controls in the seat cushions and backrests.</p>
<p>Complementing the performance seating are a thick steering wheel and a gauge package designed for easy, at-a-glance reference during performance driving. Large instruments have bold readouts, including an LED-lit “tracer” function in the tachometer that flashes as the engine nears the red line. The gauge layout includes a boost gauge and a lateral acceleration display.</p>
<p>Obsidian Black accents grace the center stack, console, steering wheel and door panels. The production steering wheel and shifter are available in Alcantara, providing the luxurious character of suede. Additional interior features include:</p>
<p>    Audio system with navigation, including AM/FM stereo, CD/DVD player, MP3 playback, Bose 5.1 Cabin Surround Sound with 10 speakers, 40-gig hard drive and USB with audio connectivity, is standard<br />
    Bluetooth phone connectivity is standard<br />
    Dual-zone automatic climate control is standard<br />
    Driver information center is standard<br />
    Keyless entry and keyless start features are standard<br />
    Ambient lighting package is standard<br />
    Remote vehicle start is standard<br />
    Front bucket seats with power eight-way driver’s adjustment and articulating head restraints are standard on all trim levels<br />
    Recaro performance seats are optional and include heated/cooled feature and leather trim with microfiber inserts<br />
    Steering wheel with microfiber-trimmed rim is available and is packaged with microfiber-trimmed shift knob and Ricardo performance seats<br />
    Power sunroof is available<br />
    Interior color and trim choices: Ebony seats with Ebony trim, Light Titanium seat with Ebony trim, Cashmere seats with Cocoa trim, Ebony seats with Ebony microfiber inserts and Light Titanium seats with Light Titanium microfiber inserts.</p>
<p><span class="inline inline-left"><img src="http://www.autospectator.com/cars/files/images/2013-Cadillac-CTS-V-Coupe-001.jpg" alt="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo" title="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo"  class="image image-_original" width="500" height="333" /><span class="caption" style="width: 498px;"><strong>2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo</strong></span></span></p>
<p>Supercharged performance</p>
<p>The CTS-V’s 6.2L supercharged V-8 engine is rated at 556 horsepower (415 kW) and 551 lb.-ft. of torque (747 Nm). It is the most powerful engine in Cadillac’s history.</p>
<p>The engine features an intercooled Eaton Twin Vortices Series™ (TVS™) supercharger, which employs twin four-lobe rotors that are twisted 160 degrees. Typical superchargers feature three lobes twisted 60 degrees. The fourth lobe and greater helix profile, when combined with unique air inlet and outlet ports, create smoother, more efficient airflow into the engine. In addition to improved overall efficiency, this supercharger has superior noise and vibration characteristics compared to more ordinary designs.</p>
<p>A pair of six-speed transmissions is offered in the CTS-V Coupe – a Tremec TR6060 six-speed manual with a dual-disc clutch and a Hydra-Matic 6L90 six-speed automatic with steering wheel-mounted shift controls. The steering wheel-mounted controls require no shift lever movement and allow immediate tap up/tap down gear selection. Performance Algorithm Shifting provides the automotive transmission with a performance-oriented shift pattern during sustained high-performance driving. As with many Cadillac models, the driver needs only to slide the shifter into the manual “gate” to put the transmission into a performance mode.</p>
<p>Torque is channeled through the six-speed transmissions to a high-performance rear axle that features a limited-slip rear differential within a cast iron housing for greater thermal management and asymmetrical half-shafts to smoothly manage the supercharged engine’s incredible torque. The unique design includes alternately sized half-shafts that also help dynamically balance the oscillation of torque from side to side, effectively eliminating “power hop.”</p>
<p><span class="inline inline-left"><img src="http://www.autospectator.com/cars/files/images/2013-Cadillac-CTS-V-Coupe-004.jpg" alt="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo" title="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo"  class="image image-_original" width="500" height="375" /><span class="caption" style="width: 498px;"><strong>2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo</strong></span></span></p>
<p>CTS-V Coupe driving experience</p>
<p>The V-Series is designed for great road-holding performance, while maintaining the poise of a luxury car in average driving conditions. This is enabled by Cadillac’s landmark Magnetic Ride Control, the world’s fastest-reacting suspension technology. It uses shocks controlled by advanced magneto-rheological technology, rather than mechanical valves, to greatly accelerate response time and precision. Electronic sensors at all four wheels literally “read the road” every millisecond, making constant adjustments to damping to create virtually instantaneous and extremely precise control of body motions.</p>
<p>The Magnetic Ride shocks also enable a broader range of damping control to optimize the ride and handling for all driving conditions. In the CTS-V, an extra measure of control is offered, with two selectable modes (Tour and Sport), enabling the driver to tailor the suspension for grand touring or more spirited performance driving.</p>
<p>The CTS-V Coupe also incorporates sophisticated braking and tire systems, in partnership with two companies renowned for high-performance technology: Brembo and Michelin. Brembo brakes are at all four corners and include powerful, six-piston calipers in the front and four-piston calipers in the rear. Large, vented rotors enable strong initial braking force while optimizing heat resistance and eliminating fade.</p>
<p>Michelin partnered with Cadillac engineers to develop an exclusive Pilot Sport PS2 tire specifically engineered for the CTS-V. The tires support high levels of grip in track conditions, while also affording excellent performance on the road.</p>
<p>Additional chassis and suspension features include:</p>
<p>    An independent short/long arm (SLA) front <a href="/cars/reference/car-dictionary-glossary-automotive-termss/suspension-system" class="alinks-link" title="Suspension System: Glossary definition">suspension system</a> that features low-mass aluminum for the upper and lower control arms, as well as the knuckles<br />
    A multi-link rear suspension with a fully isolated sub frame<br />
    StabiliTrak electronic chassis control system is integrated with the car’s standard four-channel <a href="/cars/reference/car-dictionary-glossary-automotive-termsa/abs-antilock-brake-system" class="alinks-link" title="ABS (Antilock Brake System): glossary definition">ABS</a> with the full-function traction control, as well as the hydraulic brake assist and engine control systems.<br />
    A limited-slip differential<br />
    A 3.73 axle ratio on manual-transmission models<br />
    A 3.23 axle ratio on automatic-transmission models<br />
    A power-assisted rack-and-pinion steering system.</p>
<p>CTS-V Coupe safety and crash-avoidance features</p>
<p>CTS-V Coupe is designed to enhance Cadillac’s reputation for providing safe occupant environments in luxury vehicles. Features include:</p>
<p>    Dual-stage driver front air bag<br />
    Segment-first dual depth front passenger air bag<br />
    Driver and front passenger side seat-mounted pelvic/thorax side air bags<br />
    Roof-rail side curtain air bags with rollover protection technology that cover front and rear seating rows<br />
    Front safety belt pretensions<br />
    Tire pressure monitoring system<br />
    Body structure with strategically placed high-strength steel<br />
    Active head restraints for front seat occupants<br />
    Ultrasonic rear parking assist<br />
    Rearview camera system<br />
    Side blind zone alert.</p>
<p><span class="inline inline-left"><img src="http://www.autospectator.com/cars/files/images/2013-Cadillac-CTS-V-Coupe-006.jpg" alt="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo" title="2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo"  class="image image-_original" width="500" height="332" /><span class="caption" style="width: 498px;"><strong>2013 Cadillac CTS-V Coupe photo</strong></span></span></p>
<p>OnStar details</p>
<p>OnStar is standard on CTS-V Coupe and includes one year of Directions and Connections service. OnStar uses <a href="/cars/reference/car-dictionary-glossary-automotive-termsg/global-positioning-system-gps" class="alinks-link" title="GPS: Glossary definition">GPS</a> and cellular phone technology to automatically call for help in the event of crash. OnStar subscribers also can use the service to be Good Samaritans by sending help to other motorists, and to call for help in other non-crash emergencies. Additional features:</p>
<p>    Directions and Connections service with Turn-by-Turn Navigation for easier route guidance on all models. The feature is easy to use because all directions are sent to the car by an advisor, without the need to stop.<br />
    Stolen Vehicle Assistance helps police track the vehicle.<br />
    My Cadillac and OnStar my Link mobile apps offer vehicle information and OnStar services via the customer’s smartphone.</p>
