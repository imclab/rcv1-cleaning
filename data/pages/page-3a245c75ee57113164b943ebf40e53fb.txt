<p>The Ford Fiesta is turning out to be the subcompact for choice for younger drivers. This model has outsold both Toyota and Scion brand vehicles for the first half of 2013. In fact, it may well be on its way to being the number one choice for young drivers according to an analysis of Polk retail registration information. The small car is the choice of 46% of the generation Y and generation X buyers. Toyota is coming in second place in that market by capturing 36% of sales.</p>
<p>More than half of the Ford Fiesta buyers state that the Ford SYNC technology was what won them over to wanting to drive one home for themselves. Also, the fuel economy package is appealing as the Fiesta Is the highest rated subcompact with ratings set to 41 highway miles per gallon.</p>

<p>Amy Marentic, marketing manager, large cars and crossovers, said, “Fiesta started the momentum we are seeing across our car and crossover lineups, which is helping us attract younger buyers and grow in coastal markets where the imports long have dominated. We are adding the EcoBoost engine and MyFord Touch to the new Fiesta because we have younger buyers switching to us for unique features such as SYNC that cannot be found on other small cars.”</p>
<p>“Gone are the days when loading up a B-segment vehicle meant adding power windows, door locks and air conditioning,” adds Marentic. “Today’s millennials and gen Xers demand the kinds of technology features that allow them to stay connected while behind the wheel, and to fully integrate the mobile devices they depend on every day.”</p>
<p>The current Ford Fiesta features MyFord Touch driver connect technology, and later this year it will offer Ford’s acclaimed 1.0-liter EcoBoost engine. The subcompact also offers a rear view camera, leather seats and sunroof. Ford is quick to point out that these features are not available on Toyota Yaris or Scion iQ or xD.</p>

		<h1 class="main_title"><a href="http://automotivediscovery.com/bmw-gearing-up-for-frankfurt-auto-show/9217837/">BMW Gearing Up for Frankfurt Auto Show</a></h1>
		<p class="meta">Posted  by <a href="http://automotivediscovery.com/author/writer4/" title="Posts by Ron Halpert" rel="author">Ron Halpert</a> on Aug 27, 2013 in <a href="http://automotivediscovery.com/category/automotive-news/" title="View all posts in Automotive News" rel="category tag">Automotive News</a>, <a href="http://automotivediscovery.com/category/brands/bmw/" title="View all posts in BMW" rel="category tag">BMW</a>, <a href="http://automotivediscovery.com/category/press-releases/" title="View all posts in Press Releases" rel="category tag">Press Releases</a> | <span class="comments-number"><a href="http://automotivediscovery.com/bmw-gearing-up-for-frankfurt-auto-show/9217837/#respond">0 comments</a></span></p>		<div class="post-content clearfix">
										<div class="featured_box">
					<a href="http://automotivediscovery.com/bmw-gearing-up-for-frankfurt-auto-show/9217837/">
						<img src="http://automotivediscovery.com/wp-content/uploads/et_temp/BMW-at-Frankfurt-Auto-Show-33378_481x230.jpg" alt='BMW Gearing Up for Frankfurt Auto Show' width='481' height='230' />					</a>	
				</div> 	<!-- end .featured_box -->
						
			<div class="entry_content">
				<p>The famous Frankfurt Auto show is being held next month beginning on the 12<sup>th</sup> through the 22<sup>nd</sup> and BMW is one automaker that is gearing up to make a big showing. Not only will BMW be showing off a few tried and true lineups, the German auto manufacturers will be premiering the brand new BMW i3. This is the first series produced models from the BMW I brand and has a passenger cell made from carbon fiber reinforced plastic (CFRP) and BMW eDrive technology designed to deliver zero-emission driving.</p>
<p>The BMW i3 model’s electric motor offers up 170 horsepower from the lithium ion battery pack that is mounted in a low central position of the vehicle’s underbody. For the standard version you can get between 80 and 100 miles of driving time. If you choose the two cylinder range extender combustion engine option your mileage will be around 180 miles per charge.</p>
<p>The press release states, “The LifeDrive concept underpins the car’s visionary form, which is defined by a standalone design language and generous levels of space for all four seats. A signature theme of the BMW i brand is the thread of sustainability running throughout the value chain. For example, the carbon fibres for the passenger cell are manufactured at Moses Lake in the USA using hydro power alone, while the energy required for production of BMW i cars at BMW Plant Leipzig is generated 100 per cent by purpose-built wind turbines.”</p>
<p>The BMW 4 Series Coupe is another model that will make an appearance at the Frankfurt Auto Show along with the BMW X5 Sports Activity Vehicle (SAV), the BMW Concept X5 eDrive, the BMW Concept Active Tourer Outdoor and the The BMW 5 Series. Stay tuned for more information about these innovative new vehicles from BMW along with all the other exciting vehicles that will make an appearance in Frankfurt in September.</p>

			<div class="entry_content">
				<p>From here on out you will no longer have to choose which smartphone platform you buy based on whether it’s compatible with your car or not, if you are driving a General Motors made vehicle that is. The OnStar RemoteLink Mobile App is not only the first of its kind to allow drivers to remotely access their vehicles, it is now the only one that works on every smartphone application including iPhone, Android, Blackberry and Microsoft Windows phones. This app is available at your smartphone app market. For Windows Phone, it is available for 7 and 8 operating systems and can be downloaded from the Windows Phone Store on mobile devices.</p>
<p>Steve Schwinke, GM director, Application Development, Global Connected Consumer, said, “RemoteLink remains one of our subscribers’ most desired OnStar services, so to be able to offer it across all of the major smartphone platforms is a great step for us. RemoteLink is one of the most-used automotive manufacturer apps in the marketplace and is a great example of what you can do with built-in vehicle connectivity.”</p>
<p>Along with RemoteLink being available on all smartphone platforms, it has new key fob features that include remote start, lock, unlock and horn and lights activation. All new General Motors made vehicles will offer this as a standard option for five years. That does not include the additional OnStar services though you can pay for those extra features if you choose to.</p>
<p>Currently the RemoteLink mobile app has been downloaded more than 1.7 million times and is used most often to check vehicle data such as oil life, tire pressure and fuel levels. By most often, that is about 600,000 times every month for those who are making use of the app. It is available for OnStar subscribers in the United States, Canada and Mexico and supports four language options which are US English, Canadian English, French Canadian and Spanish.</p>
<p>Source General Motors</p>

Many are calling it a refreshed Lotus 414 E, and it may well be, but no one really cares, they just notice that this is an attention getting vehicle that walks it talk in regard to offering high performance and exceptional fuel efficiency by way of the two place hybrid gasoline-electric configuration.</p>
<p>In regard to high performance, drivers can expect to get around 402 horsepower and 738 pound feet of torque, making this one of the fastest of the hybrid gas electric breeds. The bucket racing seats are built for comfort and to keep your body hugged in during even the most assertive curving. The dashboard, instrument cluster, seats and steering wheel are highly innovative and offer a simple and convenient driving experience. Overall, if the production version sticks pretty close to the concept design, it should quickly become a favorite, not only for Infiniti drivers, but for the green community and sports car fans alike.</p>
