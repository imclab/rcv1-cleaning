<h1>Seven Arts Entertainment Announces Television and Digital Releases of "The Pool Boys" and "Drunkboat" </h1>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p>
<div class="mw_release">
 <p>LOS ANGELES, CA--(Marketwire - May 1, 2012) -  Seven Arts Entertainment Inc. (NASDAQ: SAPX) ("Seven Arts" or the "Company") announced today that it has licensed United States pay television rights to its film "<span style="text-decoration: underline">The Pool Boys"</span> to HBO, through the Company's distributor Entertainment One, and is licensing the English pay television rights to "<span style="text-decoration: underline">The Pool Boys"</span> to BSkyB. The Company also announced the digital premiere of its film "<span style="text-decoration: underline">Drunkboat"</span> through Warner Bros. Digital on July 18, 2012 and is licensing the English pay television rights to "<span style="text-decoration: underline">Drunkboat</span>" to BSkyB. "<span style="text-decoration: underline">Drunkboat"</span> will be theatrically released by the Company in June, 2012.</p>
        <p><span style="text-decoration: underline">"The Pool Boys"</span> is the hilarious new comedy from the makers of "<span style="text-decoration: underline">American Pie</span>." Starring Matthew Lillard, Brett Davern, Efren Ramirez, Rachel LeFevre and Tom Arnold, the film chronicles the playful mischief that results when a pool boy and a gardener and some women practicing the world's oldest profession throw a massive party in the empty home of one of their clients. </p>
        <p><span style="text-decoration: underline">"Drunkboat"</span> is the new tragi-comedy from playwright/director Bob Meyer, starring John Malkovich, John Goodman and Dana Delany. "<span style="text-decoration: underline">Drunkboat"</span> tells the story of Mort Gleason (Malkovich), who returns to the family home where his sister (Delany) is raising her young son, who is in the thrall of an unscrupulous boat salesman (Goodman). Both powerful and touching, this film tells the story of a young man's dreams of sailing the world and an older man's reconciliation with his family.</p>
        <p>Seven Arts' CEO Peter Hoffman stated: "We continue to build the digital and television licensing of our films, directly and through our distributors. We believe this revenue will be an important contribution to our financial results in future periods."</p>
        <p><strong>About Seven Arts Entertainment Inc.:<br />
        </strong>Seven Arts Entertainment Inc. is the successor to Seven Arts Pictures Plc, which was founded in 2002 as an independent motion picture production and distribution company engaged in the development, acquisition, financing, production and licensing of theatrical motion pictures for exhibition in domestic (i.e., the United States and Canada) and foreign theatrical markets, and for subsequent worldwide release in other forms of media, including home video and pay and free television.</p>
