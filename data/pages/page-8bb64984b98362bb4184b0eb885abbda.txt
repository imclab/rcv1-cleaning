<h3><a name="genarts">General and Visual Arts</a></h3>

<p class="pur">See also <a href="domestic.htm">Domestic Fiction</a> for novels featuring crafts, 
	and <a href="mystery.htm#mystart">Art in Mystery</a> for mysteries featuring art.</p>

<hr align="center" width="60%">

<p><a href="http://victoria.tc.ca/~mattison/ficarch/index-books.htm">The Fictional World of Archives, Art Galleries and Museums</a> (David Mattison):
Author, title, publication year, some links and some summaries, notation of Canadian authors, notation for books about 
     art/artists, archives, and museums. Total of more than 320 books, including mysteries. Also: <a href="http://www.victoria.tc.ca/%7Emattison/ficarch/index-stories.htm">Short Stories</a> 
     about art, archives, museums. 
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.lansing.lib.il.us/webfiles/RA_bibs/artists.pdf">Artists and Artworks</a> (Lansing PL, IL):
14-page <strong>PDF</strong>. Author, title, and summary for about 60 novels about painting and visual arts, from 
     <cite>The Portrait</cite> by Charles Atkins to <cite>Beauty</cite> by Susan Wilson. Compiled March 2005, updated Feb. 2006.    
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.madisonpubliclibrary.org/booklists/artists.html">Art, Artists and the Art World </a> (Madison Public Library, WI): 
Author, title, publication year, webcat link and summary for about 60 works of fiction 
	and another 15 <a href="http://www.madisonpubliclibrary.org/booklists/artists.html#mystery">mystery 
	titles</a>. Compiled 1998, updated March 2008.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.northbrook.info/lib_fiction_arts.php">Fiction about the Arts</a> (Northbrook Public Library, IL) : 
Listed by art form: Painting, Sculpture, Photography, Theater, and Music. 
	Title, author, summary for about 45 novels, including mysteries. 
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.mhl.org/read/lists/Art%20in%20Fiction.pdf">Art in Fiction</a> (Memorial Hall Library, Andover MA): 
2-page PDF list. Author, title, pub. year, and summary for 17 books, 
	from Dan Brown's <cite>The Da Vinci Code</cite> to Larry Watson's <cite>The Orchard</cite>. Compiled Nov. 2003, updated Oct. 2005.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.statelibrary.tas.gov.au/whatdo/reading/novelapproach/art">Art in Fiction</a> (State Library of Tasmania): 
About 55 books/series listed, with authors, some with summaries, all featuring art or artists. Updated March 2008.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.skokie.lib.il.us/s_read/rd_find/rd_lists/artful.asp">Artful Fiction</a> (Skokie Public Library, IL): 
Author, title, webcat link, pub. year for about 30 books, from Barbara Taylor Bradford's <cite>A Sudden Change of Heart</cite>
 	to Jeanette Winterson's <cite>Art and Lies: a Piece for 3 Voices and a Bawd</cite>. Updated Feb. 2006.
</p>

<p><a href="http://sachem.suffolk.lib.ny.us/advisor/artists.htm">A Broad Canvas: Novels About Artists</a> (Sachem Public Library, Holbrook NY):
Author, title, pub. info and summary for about 30 books arranged in order by artist's name, from Audubon to Vermeer and 
   including Georges de la Tour, Oskar Kokoschka, Artemisia Gentileschi, Rockwell Kent, Mary Cassatt, Emily Carr and others.  
    Updated April 2007.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.prattlibrary.org/findanswers/pratt_recommends.aspx?id=4562">Novels from the Studio: Artists in Fiction </a> (Enoch Pratt Free Library - Baltimore): 
Compiled 2006. More than 20 books, with author, title, summary, webcat link, and publication year. 
	From <cite>Luck</cite> by Joan Barfoot to <cite>The Forest Lover</cite> by Susan Vreeland. Books published 2003-2006.	
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.overbooked.org/booklists/subjects/art/art.html">Art</a> (Overbooked): 
'Novels about art, the art world and artists.' General fiction and mystery and crime fiction lists. These books have received starred 
	reviews from the major reviewing sources. Author, title, publication info, brief 
	reviews, and sometimes external links for more than 15 fiction titles, plus a few non-fiction. 
	Updated Aug. 2008.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.answerpoint.org/reading_room/book_list.asp?sort=101&list=1267">Lives of the Painters</a> (Central Rappahannock Public Library, VA): 
Author, title, summary, webcat link for about 15 books, from <cite>Artemisia</cite> by Alexandra Lapierre to 
	<cite>The Tears of the Madonna</cite> by George Herman.
</p>


<p><a href="http://thelibrary.org/booklist/titles_jackets.cfm?catid=517">Fiction About Art and Artists</a> (Springfield-Greene County Library, MO): 
Author, title, webcat link, and summary for more than 15 novels. 
</p>


<p><a href="http://www.st-charles.lib.il.us/arl/booklists/artistsinfiction.htm">Artists in Fiction</a> (St. Charles Public Library, IL): 
Author, title, summary for each of 11 novels featuring art, from <cite>The Day Laid on the Altar</cite> by Adria Bernardi 
	to <cite>Paradise Farm</cite> by Brenda Webster, with a list of another 18 books without summaries. Compiled May 2001.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.wilmette.lib.il.us/readersservices/bibliographies/staffbib.php?bib_title=Novels%20about%20Paintngs&bib_title_id=233">Novels About Paintings</a> (Wilmette Public Library, IL): 
Author, title, publication year, webcat link, and summary for 8 books, from Tracy Chevalier's <cite>Girl 
	With a Pearl Earring</cite> to Katherine Weber's <cite>The Music Lesson</cite>. Last revised July 2006.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.readersadvice.com/readadv/000329.html">Struggling Artists in Fiction</a> (Reader's Advice): 
Author, title, and Amazon link for about 55 titles, 
	from John Ed Bradley's <cite>Restoration</cite> to Michele Zackheim's <cite>Broken Colors</cite>. 
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.readersadvice.com/readadv/000343.html">Painting and Artists</a> (Reader's Advice): 
'Notable books involving specific famous paintings and/or painters, either historical or set in the present.' 
	Author, title, and Amazon link for about 50 titles, 
	from Aliette Armel's <cite>Love, the Painter's Wife and the Queen of Sheba</cite> to 
	Gerard Woodward's <cite>A Curious Earth</cite>.
</p>


<p><a href="http://www.webrary.org/rs/flbklists/Art.html">Novels about Art, Artists, and the Art World</a> (Penny Stevens/Jennifer Hance, Fiction-L): 
Author/title for over 65 novels listed, many with short summaries. 
	Compiled Sept. 1998, updated June 2001. 
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.webrary.org/rs/flbklists/Fineart.html">Fine Art Fiction</a> (Sarah Herlache, Fiction-L): 
Author/title for about 90 fiction titles featuring fine art, from Margaret Atwood's <cite>Cat's Eye</cite> 
	to Virginia Woolf's <cite>To the Lighthouse</cite>. Compiled May 2002. 
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.trlib.org/booklist.aspx?listname=53&searchtype=LnameTitle&listtype=adult&noimage=n">Art in Fiction</a> (Timberland Regional Library, WA):
Author, title, some cover images, some summaries for about 25 titles. July 2007.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.stcharleslibrary.org/arl/booklists/picture.htm">Picture Perfect</a> (St. Charles Public Library, IL): 
Author and title only for 'fictional accounts of prominent artists paired with nonfiction overviews of the artists' lives and work,' 
	listed by artist from Audubon to Vermeer. January 2007.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.st-charles.lib.il.us/arl/booklists/artfictn.htm">Novels about Artists</a> (St. Charles Public Library, IL): 
Author and title only (no summaries) for about 35 fictional 
	works about artists. From <cite>Portrait of the Artist's Wife</cite> by Barbara Anderson 
	to <cite>Art and Lies</cite> by Jeanette Winterson.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.kpl.org/books_more/booklists/art.html">Arts and Artists in Fiction</a> (Kitchener Public Library, Ontario, Canada): 
Authors and title(s) for about 40 books featuring the arts or artists, 
	from Margaret Atwood's <cite>Cat's Eye</cite> to Niall Williams' <cite>Four Letters of Love</cite>.  
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.pollardml.org/artfic.html">Art in Fiction</a> (Pollard Memorial Library, Lowell, MA): 
Lists author, title, publication year, and artist for over 20 novels featuring art or 
	artists, from Kate Braverman's <cite>The incantation of Frida K.</cite> (Frida Kahlo) to 
	Katherine Weber's <cite>The music lesson</cite> (Vermeer).
</p>



<!-- Vermeer -->


<p><a href="http://www.hclib.org/pub/bookspace/booklistaction.cfm?list_num=505">If you liked ... The Girl in Hyacinth Blue by Susan Vreeland</a> (Hennepin County Library, MN): 
Author, title, publication year, cover image, summary, and webcat link for about a dozen books similar to Vreeland's. 
	From Robert Begiebing's <cite>The Adventures of Allegra Fullerton</cite> to another book by Vreeland, 
	<cite>The Passion of Artemesia</cite>. Updated Feb. 2006.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.overbooked.org/booklists/subjects/art/vermeer.html">Vermeer</a> (Overbooked): 
Books related to Vermeer and his art. These books have received starred reviews from the major reviewing sources. 
	Author, title, publication info, brief reviews, and sometimes external links for 
	6 fiction titles and 2 non-fiction. Updated Aug. 2008.
</p>



<!-- Photography -->


<p><a href="http://www.arrtreads.org/bookmarksonavarietyoftopics.htm#through">Through the Lens: Novels Featuring Photography and Photographers</a> (AART, Rolling Meadows Library, IL): 
Author, title, publication year, and page count for 13 novels, from 
	   Robert Barnard's <cite>Bodies</cite> to Robert James Waller's <cite>The Bridges of Madison County</cite>.
</p>

</p>

<p><a href="http://www.winfield.lib.il.us/winfield/ghosts.html">Ghosts and Haunted Houses</a> (Winfield PL, IL):

<h3><a name="movies">Books Made Into Movies / Based on the Book</a></h3>

<p><a href="http://www.imdb.com/keyword/based-on-book/?sort=date">Internet Movie Database: Date Sorted</a>: 
Movie name and date, with link to IMDB details for each movie. Thousands of movies listed. Sortable by 
      title and IMDB ranking, too.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.mcpl.lib.mo.us/readers/movies/">Based on the Book</a> (Mid-Continent Public Library, MO): 
A compilation of over 1,200 book titles, short stories, and plays 
	that have been made into motion pictures. Utilizing the Internet Movie Database as 
	the authority on release dates, all movies in this collection have been released 
	since 1980. View by Movie Title, Movie Release Year, Book Title, or Book Author. 
</p>

<p><a href="http://library.christchurch.org.nz/Guides/BooksIntoFilm/">Books Into Film and TV</a> (Christchurch City Libraries, NZ): 
Huge resource. Movie and book title, and webcat link to book, for hundreds (maybe thousands) of films and TV movies made from books, listed alphabetically by movie title. 
	For movies just recently released, there's <a href="http://library.christchurch.org.nz/Guides/ReadTheBook/">Read the Book - then see the film</a>, 
	which lists another 75 or so.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.madisonpubliclibrary.org/booklists/movies.html">You've Seen the Movie, Now Read the Book, 1986-1996</a> (Madison Public Library, WI): 
Listed in alphabetical order by movie title, includes release date of movie and book title and author. 
	From Accidental Tourist to The Witches Of Eastwick. Also see their list 
	of <a href="http://www.madisonpubliclibrary.org/booklists/movies_2.html">You've Seen the Movie, Now Read the Book, 1997-2004</a>.
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.lcl.lib.ne.us/depts/bookguide/lists/oscarwinners.htm">You've Seen the Movie ... Now Read the Book!</a> (Lincoln City Libraries, NE): 
Winners of Academy Awards (Oscar winners) for best picture that are based on novels, plays 
	and short stories. Listed in chronological order of Oscar win. Name of film, name of book if different, 
	year of film, publication year of book, number of Oscar wins, author of book, and webcat links for most. 
	From All Quiet on the Western Front (1928/1930) to Million Dollar Baby: Stories From the Corner (2000/2004).
</p>

<p><a href="http://www.epl.ca/EPLMaster.cfm?ID=BOOKMOVIES">Books Made Into Movies</a> (Edmondton Public Library, Canada): 
Author, movie/book title, catalog link for books made into movies in these categories: 
	Now Playing and Coming Attractions; Action and Adventure; Classics; Comedies; 
	Courtroom; Drama; Family; Fantasy; Historical; Horror; Mystery 
	and Suspense; Reality-Based; Romance; Science Fiction; Shakespeare; 
	War; Westerns.
</p>


<p><a href="http://www.wakegov.com/libraries/readersservices/lists/nonfiction/sawmovie.htm">No, But I Saw the Movie</a> (Wake County Public Libraries, NC): 
<strong>Nonfiction</strong> books made into movies. Author, title, one-line summary, webcat link 
	for about a dozen books made into movies, from <cite>Friday Night Lights</cite> by H.G. Bissing to 
	<cite>Jarhead: A Marine's Chronicle of the Gulf War</cite> by Anthony Swofford. 
</p>
