<!-- WEEBLY_START_CONTENT --><h2  style=" text-align: left; ">Bangladesh Music<br /></h2>
<p  style=" text-align: left; display: block; ">The styles of music are classical, folk, and modern.&nbsp; The best known forms of folk music are Bhatiali, Baul, Marthti, Murshidi, and Bhawaiya.&nbsp; The most popular is Baul.&nbsp; Some classical composers are Ustad Alauddin Khan and Ustad Ayet Ali Khan, who are both internationally known.&nbsp; Modern music is also practiced widely.&nbsp; Contemporary patterns are most in the west, and pop songs and band groups are coming up mainly in Dhaka City.<br /></p>

<h2  style=" text-align: left; ">Art<br /></h2>
<span  style=" z-index: 10; float: left; "><a><img src="/uploads/3/4/9/6/349658/1610180.jpg" style="margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; margin-right: 10px; border: 1px solid black; z-index: 10;" /></a></span>
<p  style=" text-align: left; display: block; ">The most common types of art are terracotta, pottery, painting, and literature.&nbsp; <br /><br />The painting is of two people.&nbsp; The picture below is of a man making a pot.&nbsp; </p>

<h2  style=" text-align: left; ">Food<br /></h2>
<p  style=" text-align: left; display: block; ">Rice is the main staple of their diet, and with it they regularly eat fish and <EM>dal</EM> (spicy lentil-based soup).&nbsp; Except for carrots and cucumbers, all vegetables are fried in oil.&nbsp; Bangladeshis prefer foods spiced with cumin, ginger, coriander, tumeric, and pepper.&nbsp; Friuts that are available are tomatoes, mangoes, litchis, jackfruit, guavas, watermelon, bananas, and papaya.&nbsp; Food is usually marinated in <EM>shu'ra</EM> (chopped onions and spices marinated in warm cooking oil), and a good <EM>shu'ra</EM> indicated fine cooking.&nbsp; They don't usually eat dessert, but on special occasions they will have dough fried and boiled in syrup.&nbsp; A light breakfast is typically eaten around 6 or 7 am, followed by snacks (mostly leftovers).&nbsp; Lunch is around 1 pm, an afternoon snack is eaten around 5 or 6 pm, and dinner is at 10 pm.&nbsp; At restaurants, the wealthiest person usually pays for everyone's meal.&nbsp; Eating out is rare for most people, but Chinese food is popular with the middle and upper classes.&nbsp; Food is more traditional.<br /></p>

<h2  style=" text-align: left; ">Main Dish: Korma (Fish)<br /></h2>
<span  style=" z-index: 10; float: left; "><a><img src="/uploads/3/4/9/6/349658/4524691.jpg" style="margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; margin-right: 10px; border: 1px solid black; z-index: 10;" /></a></span>
<p  style=" text-align: left; display: block; ">Note: Chicken, beef, and mutton are commonly used in place of fish.<br /><br /><STRONG>Ingredients</STRONG>:<br /><br />1 kilogram fish, such as carp<br /><br /> 1/2 cup yogurt<br /><br /> 1/2&nbsp;cup onion paste <br /><br /> 2 teaspoons ginger paste<br /><br /> 1 teaspoon garlic paste<br /><br /> 1 tablespoon coriander paste<br /><br /> 6 cardamoms<br /><br /> 2, 1 inch pieces of cinnamon sticks<br /><br /> 2 teaspoons salt<br /><br /> 3/4 cup oil<br /><br /> 6 green chilies<br /><br /> 1 tablespoon sugar<br /><br /> 1 tablespoon lemon juice<br /><br /> <STRONG>Directions</STRONG>:<br /><br /> Cut fish in large pieces (a small type of fish will not work for this recipe).&nbsp; Add all ingredients except for green chilies, into a cooking dish.&nbsp; Mix well.&nbsp; Cover and cook on low heat.&nbsp; Turn the fish once to make sure that both sides are thoroughly cooked.&nbsp; When the water has almost evaporated, add the green chilies.&nbsp; Cook for a half an hour on very low heat.&nbsp; Serve after the oil begins to float on top.<br /><br /></p>
<hr  style=" clear: both; visibility: hidden; width: 100%; "></hr>

<h2  style=" text-align: left; ">Dessert: Rasgullas (Fried dough with syrup)</h2>
<span  style=" z-index: 10; float: left; "><a><img src="/uploads/3/4/9/6/349658/3662628.jpg" style="margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; margin-right: 10px; border: 1px solid black; z-index: 10;" /></a></span>
<p  style=" text-align: left; display: block; ">Note: This is a favorite dessert, eaten on special holidays in Bangladesh.<br /><br /><STRONG>Ingredients</STRONG>: <br /><br />1 liter milk<br /><br />Juice of 1 medium sized lemon<br /><br />1 teaspoon refined flour<br /><br />2 cups water<br /><br />1 cup sugar<br /><br />1 teaspoon rose essence<br /><br /><STRONG>Directions</STRONG>:<br /><br />Boil the milk and let it cool to room temperature.&nbsp; Skim the cream off the milk.&nbsp; Return milk to boil.&nbsp; When it reaches boil, add lemon juice, and continue boiling until the whey separates.&nbsp; Cover pot for 15 minutes.&nbsp; Take a muslin cloth, and strain the curdled milk.&nbsp; Lightly press to strain out the water.&nbsp; Place a heavy weight on the muslin cloth for about an hour.&nbsp; The end of this product is the dough.&nbsp; Place the dough on a dry surface, and kneed well for about 3 minutes.&nbsp; Add refined flour, and kneed for another 5 minutes.&nbsp; Meanwhile, boil sugar, rose essence, and water in a pressure cooker to form a thin syrup.&nbsp; Make small balls of the dough mixture, and place them in the sugar syrup.&nbsp; Cover and cook for 7 minutes on a medium flame.&nbsp; Refrigerate and serve chilled.</p>
<hr  style=" width: 100%; clear: both; visibility: hidden; "></hr>

<h2  style=" text-align: left; ">Entertainment<br /></h2>
<p  style=" text-align: left; display: block; ">Men enjoy playing soccer, field hockey, cricket, table tennis, and badminton.&nbsp; Televisions, VCRs, and movie theatres are popular in cities, and people enjoy Hindi movies and films from the US.&nbsp; There are no nightclubs in Bangladesh, and families do not travel for pleasure, but they go on picnics.&nbsp; Girls are strongly discouraged from playing sports except for handball.&nbsp; The most popular boys game is <EM>ha-dudu </EM>or <EM>ka-baddi</EM>.&nbsp; It's played on a square court.&nbsp; While continually saying "hadud-du-du" one team's player enters the arena and tries to tag as many people as he can.&nbsp; If he returns to his side and has repeated the words without taking a breath, the players he tagged are out and he gains some of his team back.&nbsp; If he is trapped by the opposing team and is forced to take a breath, he is out and the other team gains one member back.&nbsp; The first team to eliminate all the players of the opposing team wins.</p>
