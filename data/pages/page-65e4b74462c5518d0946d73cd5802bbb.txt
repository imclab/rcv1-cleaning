          <td height="35"> <div class="shoppingadvice">Suzuki Repair Service Advice</div></td>
        </tr>
        <tr> 
          <td><div class="grey10bold">How to Choose a Great Auto Mechanic</div>
            <div class="grey10">Look for an <strong>Suzuki car repair</strong> shop before you need 
              one and consider the following important factors: 
              <p> Reputation: Ask friends or family members to recommend Suzuki mechanics 
                or repair shops that they like and trust. The local Better Business 
                Bureau will give you information on whether complaints have 
                been filed against a specific <strong>Suzuki service shop</strong>, the total number and whether 
                or not they have been resolved.</p>
              <p>Suzuki Mechanic Qualifications: Ask the service manager if the 
                shop's mechanics have been certified by Suzuki or a professional 
                organization, such as Automotive Service Excellence (ASE), and 
                in which areas they have been certified, such as brakes, electrical, 
                etc. Look for <strong>Suzuki mechanics</strong> with good training 
                and credentials, updated knowledge and skill, proper tools and 
                equipment and experience working on your particular model automobile.</p>
              <p>Warranty: If your Suzuki is covered by warranty, be certain that 
                the facility you select is authorized to perform <em>Suzuki repairs</em> 
                under the warranty. Work done by an unauthorized Suzuki repair 
                shop may void you warranty and be costly to you.</p>
              <p>Appearance of Service Area: The work area should be clean, well 
                lit, organized and stocked with modern equipment and professional 
                quality tools.</p>
              <p>Customer Service: Ask if the <strong><em>Suzuki repair shop</em></strong> 
                provides the customer services you desire, such as transportation 
                to and from work while repair work is being done, a loaner car, 
                evening and weekend hours or a discount for work done on Sunday.</p>
              <p>Suzuki Car Repair Pricing: The cost for the same repair may vary greatly 
                from one shop to another. If you know what type of work is needed, 
                ask several Suzuki service shops for a written estimate.</p>
              <p class="grey10bold">What should you review when repairs are complete?</p>
              <p>&#8226; Carefully review the completed invoice before you pay 
                the bill to be certain that all requested work was done, and ask 
                for an explanation of any changes or discrepancies.</p>
              <p>&#8226; Request a written warranty or guarantee, if applicable, 
                and know what it covers.</p>
              <p>&#8226; Ask for all replaced parts and save them until you are 
                sure that the <em>Suzuki car repairs</em> are satisfactory.</p>
              <p>&#8226; Inspect and/or test drive the car in the parking lot 
                or on the highway, if that is where prior symptoms arose, to make sure the problem has been corrected.</p>
              <p>&#8226; If possible, pay for <em><strong>Suzuki car repairs</strong></em> 
                with a credit card since, under certain conditions, you can withhold 
                payment if the repairs were not done correctly.</p>
              <p>&#8226; Bring any Suzuki repair service problem to the shop's attention 
                as soon as possible, and give them a chance to correct any deficiencies.<br>
              </p>
              </div></td>
