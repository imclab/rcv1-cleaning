<p>Paul McCartney refuses to let his super-stardom limit where he can go out in public and likes nothing better than mingling with fans while taking public transportation.</p>
<p>The Beatles legend admits he is no stranger to hopping on a bus on occasion and he&#8217;s learned that the best way to deal with his starstruck fellow travelers is to engage them in casual conversation.</p>
<p>He tells talk show host Jimmy Fallon, &#8220;There&#8217;s a lot of places you can go to where people kind of understand that you&#8217;re on private time. But I kinda go anywhere. I don&#8217;t like the idea that you get famous and it ruins it for you, it&#8217;s kind of a sad idea.</p>
<p>&#8220;Two years ago I was on a bus in New York&#8230; and people were (staring). They don&#8217;t let on. Except this African-American woman. I sat down quietly and she turns around and goes, &#8216;You Paul McCartney?&#8217; So I said, &#8216;Yes I am&#8217; and you can see all these other people (giggling). And she goes, &#8216;What are you doing on a bus?&#8217; I said, &#8216;I&#8217;m going uptown, like you are. Come sit with me.&#8217; So she came round and sat next to me and I said, &#8216;Where are you going?&#8217; &#8216;I&#8217;m going to see my sister&#8230;&#8217; We were just (chatting). But in that situation, you just do that and people are cool.&#8221;</p>
