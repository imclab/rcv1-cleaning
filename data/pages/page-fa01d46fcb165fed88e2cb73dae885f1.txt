<span class="byline"><b><A HREF="mailto:ishaya@crain.com">Isabella Shaya</A></b></span>     <br /><b>Automotive News</b><br />
<span class="date">June 25, 2013 - 4:13 pm ET 
</span></div><p>BMW says the redesigned 2014 X5 crossover will start at $53,725 for the new rear-wheel-drive sDrive35i model.</p><p>The least expensive all-wheel-drive X5 model -- the xDrive35i -- will be priced from $56,025. The diesel X5 xDrive35d will be priced from $57,525 and the awd X5 xDrive50i, with a V-8 engine, will be priced from $69,125. All prices include a $925 destination charge.</p><p>Pricing on the base 2013 X5 xDrive35i started at $48,425, including destination. The 2013 base, premium and sport activity models have been streamlined into one new model for 2014, a BMW spokesman said.</p><p>Pricing for the 2014 xDrive50i increased $4,000 from $65,125 for the 2013 model.</p><p>The X5, available for the first time in rwd, is scheduled to go on sale in the fourth quarter.</p><p>The new sDrive35i and the xDrive35i will be powered by BMW's 3.0-liter twin-turbo inline six-cylinder engine.</p><p>All models include an eight-speed automatic transmission.</p><p>The redesigned X5 has a new front end, is slightly longer and will have new communications and safety features available.</p><p>New standard equipment on the X5 includes BMW's iDrive4.2 navigation system with a touch pad, 10 years of BMW Assist eCall and BMW TeleService, BMW Apps, Servotronic speed-sensitive power steering and BMW's auto stop-start system.</p><p>The X5 was introduced in 1999 and U.S. sales peaked in 2012 at 44,445 units, according to the Automotive News Data Center.</p><p>This year through may, U.S. sales of the X5 rose 13 percent to 18,053 units.</p>
