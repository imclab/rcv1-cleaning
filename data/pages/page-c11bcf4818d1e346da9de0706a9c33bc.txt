	<p class="wiki">

	
	
	
	
	
		<p><p>
	No matter what kind of vehicle you drive, you know that <a href="http://carolinas.aaa.com/MaintainVehicles/OilChangesAndMaintenance/PreventativeMaintenance/Pages/Oil-and-Filter-Changes.aspx" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Oil &amp; Filter Changes - carolinas.aaa.com">having your oil changed</a> every few thousand miles is essential to efficient, long-lasting engine performance. But keeping up with oil changes is not enough. Today, drivers can choose from two major kinds of oils for their vehicle: synthetic and conventional. To know which is right for your car, you first need to know the difference between these two common automotive oil varieties.</p>
</p>
	
	

	
	
	
		

	

	<div id="postPhotos_4097278" class="postPhotoHolder">

		</div>

		<p><p>
	<strong>Where Automotive Oil Comes From</strong></p>
<p>
	The major difference between <a href="http://autos.aol.com/article/synthetic-motor-oil/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Is Synthetic Oil Better? - autos.aol.com">synthetic and conventional car oils</a> is how they are made. Conventional oils are derived from naturally occurring crude oil in the Earth. Additives are mixed in to enhance specific properties of the oil to help it perform better in specific types of vehicles. Synthetic oils, on the other hand, are produced entirely in the laboratory and are not petroleum based.</p>
<p>
	<strong>Performance Differences</strong></p>
<p>
	Synthetic oils are marketed as a complete alternative to conventional motor oils, and offer some benefits that make its use more appealing. Synthetic oils are less sensitive to temperature changes, so you&rsquo;ll never have to worry about engine performance in cold weather. Synthetic oils are also longer lasting than conventional oils, with change intervals as long as 7,000 miles. This is more than double the 3,000 change interval recommended for most conventional oils.</p>
<p>
	<strong>Weighing the Costs</strong></p>
<p>
	While synthetic oils may outperform conventional oils, they come at a slight premium. The sticker price for synthetic oil is usually twice that of conventional oils. However, factoring in improved efficiency shrinks the difference to only about 60%.</p>
<p>
	<strong>Choosing Synthetic Oils for Your Vehicle</strong></p>
<p>
	Although your local automotive repair specialist can give you more specific advice, always consult your owner&rsquo;s manual before altering your oil change regimen. Manufacturers typically recommend a specific grade, brand, or type of oil for each vehicle, and deviating from this recommendation may affect your warranty.</p>

We serve North Carolina and South Carolina drivers with the very best oil change and automotive repair services available. Visit AAA.com/CarCare find a AAA Car Care Center near you in the Carolinas.</p>
</p>
	
	

</p>

	<p class="wiki">

	
	
	
	
	
		<p><p>
	Although having your car professionally maintained by an automotive repair expert is important, being able to spot signs of engine trouble on your own is perhaps even <em>more </em>important. Knowing when things are just not right with your engine can help you to stay safe on the road, avoid getting stranded, and minimize any necessary repair costs. Here are a few signs that your engine might be in need of <a href="http://carolinas.aaa.com/MaintainVehicles/Coupons/Pages/default.aspx" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Maintenance Coupons from AAA - carolinas.aaa.com">professional auto repair</a> right away:</p>
</p>
	
	

	
	
	
		

	

	<div id="postPhotos_4063658" class="postPhotoHolder">

		
		
		

		<div id="mainPhoto_739858_4063658">
			<a href="" class="noPointer" id="mainPhotoLink_739858_4063658" onclick="return false;" target=" ?: '_blank'">

			</a>

			</div>

		<p><p>
	<strong>Sign 1: Check Engine Light</strong></p>
<p>
	If you&rsquo;ve been ignoring that bright yellow light behind your steering wheel, head to a car care center right away. The check engine light is the first warning sign that something under the hood needs attention from an auto repair expert. It&rsquo;s hard to tell what the problem is on your own, so take it to a professional who can diagnose your engine problems for you.</p>
<p>
	<strong>Sign 2: Shaking, Jerking, or Stalling</strong></p>
<p>
	Another sign of engine trouble is weird behavior from your vehicle. If you notice sudden shaking or jerking, or if your engine starts to stall, take your car to an <a href="http://auto.howstuffworks.com/under-the-hood/diagnosing-car-problems/mechanical/5-signs-engine-trouble.htm#page=4" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Top 5 Signs of Engine Trouble - auto.howstuffworks.com">auto repair shop</a> right away. Delaying can only intensify this malfunctioning, which can make driving very dangerous.</p>
<p>
	<strong>Sign 3: A Suddenly Noisy Engine</strong></p>
<p>
	If your vehicle gives off a nice, gentle purr, then your engine is probably fine. But once it starts roaring or screeching, it may be crying for help. Strange noises from the engine are a sure sign that something is wrong and needs attention from an experienced auto repair technician.</p>
<p>
	<strong>Sign 4: Strange Odors</strong></p>
<p>
	If you smell oil, gas, coolant, or unidentified foul odors in your car, they may be originating from your engine or exhaust system. This is very dangerous, since engine fumes can contain dangerous gases like carbon monoxide and carbon dioxide.</p>
<p>
	If <a href="http://www.aaa-carcarecenter.com/679236/2013/04/10/signs-its-time-for-a-tune-up.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Signs It's Time for a Tune Up - aaa-carcarecenter.com">your engine needs professional attention</a>, bring your car to one of the many AAA Car Care Centers across North Carolina and South Carolina. We are the region&rsquo;s premier auto repair service, specializing in all makes and models. Visit AAA.com/CarCare to find the AAA Car Care Center near you.</p>
</p>
