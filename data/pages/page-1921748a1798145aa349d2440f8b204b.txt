      <span property="v:itemreviewed sioc:Item foaf:Document" content="BMW 4 Series Coupe review"></span><div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><p> Next year, the <a href="/bmw/3-series">BMW 3 Series</a> line-up will grow to include four models, comprising the saloon, Touring estate, 3 Series GT and, from next year, the 3 Series Gran Coupe. So that’s meant the old 3 Series Coupe has been replaced by the 4 Series, a very stylish three-door coupe, based on a wider version of the current 3 Series saloon’s platform. The 4 Series features bespoke bodywork, hi-spec interiors and the six most powerful engines from the 3 Series range, plus suspension that has been stiffened, lowered and retuned to give a sportier drive. The car makes its official debut at the Frankfurt Motor Show. From launch in October, BMW’s xDrive four-wheel drive system will be optional on three of the six engines offered. Five trim levels will be available, with the 4 series M Sport also available right from launch.</p>
<iframe width="420" height="236" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/SKnLJr4HzII" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe></div></div></div><div class="field-group-format group_styling field-group-div group-styling clearfix speed-fast effect-none"><h3><span>Styling</span></h3><div class="field field-name-field-styling-rating field-type-fivestar field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><div class="clearfix fivestar-average-stars"><div class="fivestar-static-form-item"><div class="form-item form-type-item">
 <div class="fivestar-autoexpress standard"><div class="fivestar-widget-static fivestar-widget-static-vote fivestar-widget-static-5 clearfix"><div class="star star-1 star-odd star-first"><span class="on">4.3</span></div><div class="star star-2 star-even"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-3 star-odd"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-4 star-even"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-5 star-odd star-last"><span class="on" style="width: 30%"></span></div></div></div>
<div class="description"><div class="fivestar-summary fivestar-summary-"></div></div>
</div>
</div></div></div></div></div><div class="field field-name-field-styling-description field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><p> Coupes are all about style, and the 4 Series has it by the bucket load. Although it bears quite a close resemblance to the 3 Series saloon, only the bonnet is shared between the two cars. The 4 gets more swept-back lights, which can be had with adaptive LEDs, and a more aggressive front bumper, while models have BMW’s Air Breather in their front wing to help improve aerodynamics. Flared rear wheelarches give the car a more planted stance, while LED tail-lights with horizontal bars help make the car seem wider. Inside, the 4 Series uses much of the 3 Series dashboard, but features plusher materials and frameless doors. </p>
</div></div></div></div><div class="field-group-format group_driving field-group-div group-driving clearfix speed-fast effect-none"><h3><span>Driving</span></h3><div class="field field-name-field-driving-rating field-type-fivestar field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><div class="clearfix fivestar-average-stars"><div class="fivestar-static-form-item"><div class="form-item form-type-item">
 <div class="fivestar-autoexpress standard"><div class="fivestar-widget-static fivestar-widget-static-vote fivestar-widget-static-5 clearfix"><div class="star star-1 star-odd star-first"><span class="on">4</span></div><div class="star star-2 star-even"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-3 star-odd"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-4 star-even"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-5 star-odd star-last"><span class="off"></span></div></div></div>
<div class="description"><div class="fivestar-summary fivestar-summary-"></div></div>
</div>
</div></div></div></div></div><div class="field field-name-field-driving-description field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><p> The 4 Series uses the same part-aluminium platform as the 3 Series. But while both cars feature the same wheelbase, the 4 Series is wider and has wider tracks. The suspension is 10mm lower than the 3 Series, and the seats are mounted 9mm lower too, giving a sporty driving position. The suspension and body have been stiffened over the 3 Series, giving the car sharp responses, although the difference between the 4 Series and 3 Series isn’t quite as marked as we’d have liked. BMW includes the Drive Performance Control, which lets you switch the setup of the car depending on preference, and, for keen drivers, BMW offers a host of M Performance upgrades for suspension, brakes and extra power.  </p>
</div></div></div></div><div class="field-group-format group_reliability field-group-div group-reliability clearfix speed-fast effect-none"><h3><span>Reliability</span></h3><div class="field field-name-field-reliability-rating field-type-fivestar field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><div class="clearfix fivestar-average-stars"><div class="fivestar-static-form-item"><div class="form-item form-type-item">
 <div class="fivestar-autoexpress standard"><div class="fivestar-widget-static fivestar-widget-static-vote fivestar-widget-static-5 clearfix"><div class="star star-1 star-odd star-first"><span class="on">3.8</span></div><div class="star star-2 star-even"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-3 star-odd"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-4 star-even"><span class="on" style="width: 80%"></span></div><div class="star star-5 star-odd star-last"><span class="off"></span></div></div></div>
<div class="description"><div class="fivestar-summary fivestar-summary-"></div></div>
</div>
</div></div></div></div></div><div class="field field-name-field-reliability-description field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><p> The 4 Series uses much of the same mechanical and electronic equipment that has been used by the 3 Series range for the past couple of years, so that should mean that reliability is decent. There’s plenty of safety kit available, but much of it is on the options list. This includes the Driving Assistant that uses a camera on the rear-view mirror to detect rear impacts, Active Cruise control and a Dynamic Safety monitor to watch the driver for signs of fatigue. </p>
</div></div></div></div><div class="field-group-format group_practicality field-group-div group-practicality clearfix speed-fast effect-none"><h3><span>Practicality</span></h3><div class="field field-name-field-practicality-rating field-type-fivestar field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><div class="clearfix fivestar-average-stars"><div class="fivestar-static-form-item"><div class="form-item form-type-item">
 <div class="fivestar-autoexpress standard"><div class="fivestar-widget-static fivestar-widget-static-vote fivestar-widget-static-5 clearfix"><div class="star star-1 star-odd star-first"><span class="on">3.8</span></div><div class="star star-2 star-even"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-3 star-odd"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-4 star-even"><span class="on" style="width: 80%"></span></div><div class="star star-5 star-odd star-last"><span class="off"></span></div></div></div>
<div class="description"><div class="fivestar-summary fivestar-summary-"></div></div>
</div>
</div></div></div></div></div><div class="field field-name-field-practicality-description field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><p> Despite being a two-door coupe, the 4 Series is surprisingly practical. All models come with an automatic bootlid release – as long as you have the car key in your pocket, a simple wave of your foot under the rear bumper will open the boot. The boot is large too, and just 35-litres smaller than the 3 Series saloon. The rear seat has space for two adults, although headroom is compromised slightly by the curve of the roof. Up front, there’s plenty of room and good all-round visibility despite the lower seating positon when compared to a 3 Series. BMW’s optional ConnectedDrive is available, allowing the car to connect to the internet and give you accurate real-time traffic alerts. </p>
</div></div></div></div><div class="field-group-format group_running_costs field-group-div group-running-costs clearfix speed-fast effect-none"><h3><span>Running Costs</span></h3><div class="field field-name-field-running-costs-rating field-type-fivestar field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><div class="clearfix fivestar-average-stars"><div class="fivestar-static-form-item"><div class="form-item form-type-item">
 <div class="fivestar-autoexpress standard"><div class="fivestar-widget-static fivestar-widget-static-vote fivestar-widget-static-5 clearfix"><div class="star star-1 star-odd star-first"><span class="on">4</span></div><div class="star star-2 star-even"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-3 star-odd"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-4 star-even"><span class="on"></span></div><div class="star star-5 star-odd star-last"><span class="off"></span></div></div></div>
<div class="description"><div class="fivestar-summary fivestar-summary-"></div></div>
</div>
</div></div></div></div></div><div class="field field-name-field-running-costs-description field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><p> The 4 Series costs around £3,000 more than the equivalent 3 Series to buy, but each coupe claims marginally better performance and claimed fuel economy and CO2 figures. As ever, mpg will fall and costs will increase if you drive hard, but the 4 Series’ aerodynamic body will help cut fuel bills on a long motorway cruise. It’s worth considering the optional eight-speed automatic gearbox. Not only is it smooth and quick to change gear, cars with the auto claim better fuel economy. Every engine in the range already meets the forthcoming Euro 6 emissions standards. </p>
