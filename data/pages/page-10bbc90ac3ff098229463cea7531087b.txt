<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adjective">Adjective</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=automotive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Adjective">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en" xml:lang="en">automotive</strong> (<i>not <a href="/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#comparable" title="Appendix:Glossary">comparable</a></i>)</p>
<ol>
<li>Having the ability to <a href="/wiki/move" title="move">move</a> by itself; <a href="/wiki/self-propelled" title="self-propelled">self-propelled</a> or <a href="/wiki/self-propelling" title="self-propelling">self-propelling</a>.</li>
<li>Of, or relating to <a href="/wiki/motor" title="motor">motor</a> <a href="/wiki/vehicle" title="vehicle">vehicles</a>.
<ul>
<li><span class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><span class="book"><b>2006</b>,  <a href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edwin_Black" class="extiw" title="w:Edwin Black">Edwin Black</a>,  chapter 1, <cite>Internal Combustion</cite><sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://openlibrary.org/works/OL4103950W">[1]</a></sup></span>:</span></span>
<dl>
<dd><span class="cited-passage">But electric vehicles and the batteries that made them run became ensnared in corporate scandals, fraud, and monopolistic corruption that shook the confidence of the nation and inspired <b>automotive</b> upstarts.</span></dd>
</dl>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
