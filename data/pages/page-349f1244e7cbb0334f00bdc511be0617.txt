<b><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">1 Introduction</span></b>
</h1>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">On the 3rd of July 1886 the first motorcar was built in Mannheim by Carl Benz.</span>
<sup><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">1</span></sup><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">From that point of time the mobility for human beings was about to change. Today motor vehicles are a part of the everyday life of most Europeans. They are also responsible for the income of many, as the invention about 120 years ago was the beginning of a mature, global and very competitive industry.</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">&#x201C;The automotive industry is often described as &#x201C;the engine of Europe&#x201D; because of its economic and social importance and the historical role it has played in the development of our continent&#x201D;(Mission Statement of the European Automobile Manufacturers Association).</span>
<span style="font-size: 0.8em;">2</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">The European car industry employs directly about 1.1 million people and supports another 11-12 million jobs.</span>
<sup><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">3</span></sup><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">Further it provides activity for other industries such as components, electronics, informatics, telecom and logistics.</span>
<sup><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">4</span></sup><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">The industry is regarded as a key player in the European Union of the 21</span>
<sup><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">st</span></sup><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">century and as an essential part of the economy.</span>
</p>
<h1 id="page_6-chapter_3">
<b><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">1.1 Problem</span></b>
</h1>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">Since the beginning of the new millennium the European car industry is facing the problem of leveling and decreasing sales. With the markets being very crowded and saturated, competition is great.</span>
<span style="font-size: 0.8em;">5</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">In general European manufacturers have to deal with a decrease in sales as Japanese and Korean manufacturers are gaining strength in the steady market.</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">Companies recognize large losses of profits and revenues for example the bestselling European cooperation Volkswagen Group has announced a 48% fall in its 2003 profits and also a decline in production.</span>
<span style="font-size: 0.8em;">6</span>
</p>
<p>
<sup><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">1</span></sup><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">http://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auto#Wirtschaft</span>
</p>
<p>

</p>
<p>
<sup><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">3</span></sup><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">http://www.acea.be/ASB/ASBv1_1_new.ns</span>

<p>

<span style="font-size: 0.8em;">1</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">With loss of profits and demand, companies have to cut down excess capacity, in order to reduce production and relive the problem of too many cars chasing to few buyers. For example Skoda Auto redirected 4700 workers of the best selling Fabia plant for a two day break in order to act on the current sales slump.</span>
<sup><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">2</span></sup><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">Labour reduction at Skoda of polish workers went from 4000 at the highest level to 200-300 today.</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">Another example for the significant of the problem is that last year only in Germany about 1200 industry related enterprises went bankrupt and about 7000 people lost their employment..</span>
<span style="font-size: 0.8em;">3</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">Basically many European manufacturers are facing a recession and have entered a period of profound restructuring, which has a major impact on the European economy.</span>
</p>
<h1 id="page_7-chapter_4">
<b><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">1.2 Aims and Objective</span></b>
</h1>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">In this project I aim to identify the factors that affect sales in the European automotive market and will try to rank them in their importance.</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">The three European markets France, Germany and the UK will be case studies as they are three of major markets in the European Union and match with the European internationality of this project.</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">By looking at Major players and their success in their markets I will spot market leaders and manufacturers that are doing well. I will try to identify the reasons for their success and compare it to others.</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">Also by examining past sales figures industry I will identify trends get a better idea how markets and producers are performing in order to support my arguments.</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">My aim is to name and define the major internal and external factors that could have an influence on the sales of vehicles.</span>
</p>

<h1 id="page_9-chapter_6">
<b><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">2.1 Introduction to the European Industry and Market including Sales</span></b>
</h1>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">Western Europe is besides the US the strongest automobile manufacturing area. The 166 parts and assembly plants in the EU build about 16 million automobiles each year which is about 30% of the world car production (Appendix1). With 14 to 15 million sales in Western Europe per year most vehicles that are produced are also consumed on the continent. Main producers are Germany(22%), GB(15%), France (15%), Italy(16%) and Spain(10%) (Appendix 2).1</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">The European car market consists of 40 marques offering about 250 different models.</span>
<sup><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">2</span></sup><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">Since mergers and takeovers defined the industry in the 80s and 90s a couple large corporations now own several brands, sharing technology, parts, R&amp;D, costs and profits. As sales in Western Europe had their peak in 1999 with 15&#x2019;060&#x2019;847 units</span>
<sup><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">3</span></sup><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">and were not able to reach this mark ever since, economists say that the European car markets are slowly reaching saturation what is known to the US markets since the mid 70s.</span>
<span style="font-size: 0.8em;">4</span>
</p>

This may support the argument that the European car industry is reaching saturation.</span>

With their own car industry supplying their own market, the French tend to buy vehicles that are produced in France. The Market leaders are Renault, Citroen and Peugeot. <h1 id="page_13-chapter_10">
<b><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">2.3.2 German Market including Sales Analysis</span></b>
</h1>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">The German car market is the largest in Europe. With Sales over 3 million new passenger cars a year it is regarded as one of the most important but also one of the most difficult markets in the world, especially for importers. The marques VW, Audi, BMW and Mercedes are regarded superior and are well liked by German customers. A reason for that according to Franz-Josef Paefgen (Bentley Boss) is that &#x201C;German customers have a strong affinity to German car marques&#x201D;.</span>
<sup><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">1</span></sup><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">This can be supported by statistics showing that about 1,8 million cars that are sold in are originally German marques, which is more than half of the total market (Appendix4). Another way of looking at it is that about 2 million cars that are sold in Germany are also produced there which gives buyers a relation to the marques.</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">Looking at the German sales statistic of the past ten years one can see that producers are having great difficulties in the German market as sales decreased. As well as the European sales the Germans had their peak in demand for passenger cars in 1999 with 3,8 million vehicles sold. The current sales figures of 2004 at 3,25 million lie even below the sales ten years ago.</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">In the German market, there is definitely a negative trend which does not seem to change, as sales in January 2005 fell by another 3% compared to the year before (Appendix6). A reason for that might be that might be the recession of the economy in Germany.</span>
</p>

<b><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">2.3.3 UK Market including Sales Analysis</span></b>
</h1>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">The UK market has grown to the second largest market in Europe overtaking Italy in 2001, having a yearly demand of about 2,5 million passenger cars. Other than the French and the Germans the British do not have a preference when it comes to the origin of their vehicles. They buy in first place American vehicles with about 1 million and French, German and Japanese vehicles with all about half a million sales.</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">A reason for this is that GB has hardly a own car industry left, apart from foreign producers manufacturing in the country for example, Nissan and Vauxhall. The original prestige brands like Land Rover, Jaguar, Bentley and Rolls Royce still have a great attraction on the island but do now belong to American and German Car Groups. MG Rover and some smaller sports car producers is what is left in British ownership.</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">Looking at the sales figures the British market is one of the only markets that show a positive trend although the general trend on the continent is reversing. Over the past 10 years demand in the UK has risen by about half a million cars which is about 32%. This shows that there is no sign of saturation on the island although the infrastructure is about to hit its limit. This is a very positive development, which might be related to the good economic performance of Britain over the last years. It can definitely be regarded as a leader and good example in the depressing industry at the moment.</span>
</p>
<h1 id="page_14-chapter_12">
<b><span style="font-size: 1.0em;">2.4 The Major Players in the European Market including Sales</span></b>

<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">Producers from all over the world define the European Car market. With the cooperation and integration of manufacturers, several very powerful car groups developed which now own and control a wide variety of brands. The most important car organizations are from America, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, Korea and the UK.</span>
</p>
<p>
<span style="font-size: 1.0em;">An introduction to the major players in the European mass market of passenger cars will be made by country of origin, group and managed brands.</span>
