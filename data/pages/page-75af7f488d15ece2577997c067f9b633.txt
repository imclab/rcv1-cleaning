<h1 class="posttitle">Literature about art and entertainment analysis?<br>
<span class="smallcopy">September 10, 2011 <span>6:37 AM</span> &nbsp; <a href="http://ask.metafilter.com/195702/Literature-about-art-and-entertainment-analysis/rss"><i class="feedicon"></i></a> <a href="http://ask.metafilter.com/195702/Literature-about-art-and-entertainment-analysis/rss">Subscribe</a></span></h1>
<div class="copy">What are the important works of literature that analyze art and entertainment (a&amp;e) from an interdisciplinary, critical or literary perspective?<br>

<div style="border-top:1px dotted #777;margin-top:6px;padding-right:20px;">

<br> I'm particularly interested in literature that advocates action or change in how a&amp;e are practiced.<br>
<br>
The intent being to better understand and appriciate but also getting inspiration to create more meaningful a&amp;e.</div>

<span class="smallcopy">posted by <a href="http://www.metafilter.com/user/46739" target="_self">Foci for Analysis</a> to <a href="/media-arts" target="_self">media & arts</a> (4 answers total)  <span id="favcnt3195702"><a href="http://ask.metafilter.com/favorited/3/195702" style="font-weight:normal;" target="_self">2 users marked this as a favorite</a></span>

<a name="2816871"></a><div class="comments" id="c2816871"><em>I'm particularly interested in literature that advocates action or change in how a&amp;e are practiced.</em><br>
<br>
The history of a<a href="https://secure.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/wiki/Art_manifesto">rt manifestos</a> in the modern era is really fascinating.  They may seem dogmatic at first, but a lot of their rules are based on some very real and fascinating theory.<br>
<br>
You may have to get a little more specific in the kind of work you're looking for, because it seems like you've kinda just asked for the entire genre of art history and criticism.<br><span class="smallcopy">posted by <a href="http://www.metafilter.com/user/88408" target="_self">Think_Long</a> at <a href="/195702/Literature-about-art-and-entertainment-analysis#2816871" target="_self">7:51 AM</a>  on September 10, 2011 </span></div><br><br>

<a name="2816950"></a><div class="comments" id="c2816950">Hmmm...that's a pretty broad question.  Do you mean art and entertainment as one entity, or the intersection of the two?<br>
<br>
Things like art/aesthetic critique come to mind, people like:<a href="http://plato.stanford.edu/entries/adorno/"> Adorno</a> (specifically Philosophy of Modern Music, Aesthetic Theory and Culture Industry), Benjamin (specifically<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Work_of_Art_in_the_Age_of_Mechanical_Reproduction"> Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction</a>), <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Danto">Danto</a> (specifically The End of Art and Transfiguration of the Commonplace), Cavell's <a href="http://books.google.com/books/about/The_world_viewed.html?id=Ro23ozNGdzQC">The World Viewed</a> (for film specifically), perhaps<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michel_Foucault"> Focault</a> (like Order of Things, Archeology of Knowledge).<br>
<br>
That's just the tip of the iceberg, and most of that is pretty abstract.  Literary theory is a wide reaching topic and I'm no expert.<br><span class="smallcopy">posted by <a href="http://www.metafilter.com/user/62231" target="_self">Lutoslawski</a> at <a href="/195702/Literature-about-art-and-entertainment-analysis#2816950" target="_self">9:14 AM</a>  on September 10, 2011 [<a href="/favorited/4/2816950" style="font-weight:normal;" title="1 user marked this as favorite">1 favorite</a>] </span></div><br><br>

<a name="2817031"></a><div class="comments bestleft" style="margin-left:60px;" id="c2817031">Think_Long, yeah, the question became really broad but that's because I need to educate myself on the basics.<br>
<br>
Lutoslawski, I was thinking of art and entertainment as one entitiy but intersection sounds equally interesting (too broad, I know!). Thanks for the suggestions, I think I'll start looking there.<br><span class="smallcopy">posted by <a href="http://www.metafilter.com/user/46739" target="_self">Foci for Analysis</a> at <a href="/195702/Literature-about-art-and-entertainment-analysis#2817031" target="_self">10:33 AM</a>  on September 10, 2011 </span></div><br><br>

<a name="2817579"></a><div class="comments" id="c2817579">Try Hugo's &quot;Hunchback of Notre-Dame.&quot;  Make sure it's a translation that hasn't pruned all of Hugo's magnificent tangents.  Go to Book V Chapter II (in the Signet Classics version, anyway).<br>
<br>
Superficially, it doesn't &quot;advocate change,&quot; but it sort of does.  <br>
<br>
&quot;The book will kill the edifice!&quot;  It's fascinating, and if your goal is to create A&amp;E which is truly meaningful, this may at least help in defining &quot;meaningful.&quot;<br><span class="smallcopy">posted by <a href="http://www.metafilter.com/user/107647" target="_self">deep thought sunstar</a> at <a href="/195702/Literature-about-art-and-entertainment-analysis#2817579" target="_self">8:35 PM</a>  on September 10, 2011 </span></div><br><br>
