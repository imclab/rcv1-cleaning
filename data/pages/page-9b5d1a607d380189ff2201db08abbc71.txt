		<h1 class="entry-header">Top 10 Best-Selling Cars: January 2012</h1>
	
	<div class="entry-content">
		<div class="entry-body">
			<p><a href="http://blogs.cars.com/.a/6a00d83451b3c669e20163008a7cf4970d-pi" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="RM012_040FN" border="0" class="asset  asset-image at-xid-6a00d83451b3c669e20168e681da54970c" src="http://blogs.cars.com/.a/6a00d83451b3c669e20168e681da54970c-800wi" title="RM012_040FN" /></a></p>
<p>After capping 2011 with four decent months, the auto industry hit 2012 running. Analysts expect auto sales to outpace 2011 figures <a href="http://blogs.cars.com/kickingtires/2012/01/why-2012-could-be-the-year-of-the-us-auto-industry.html" target="_self" title="anywhere from 6% to 10%">anywhere from 6% to 10%</a>, which could make 2012 the best sales year since 2007.</p>
<p>January enticed consumers with total incentives — including financing offers, automaker and dealer cash, and trade-in bonuses — amounting to 16% off the average car’s MSRP, CNW Marketing Research reports. That beats January 2011, when shoppers saw only 13.5% off the average MSRP.</p>
<p>Shoppers took advantage. January sales jumped 11%, beating <a href="http://blogs.cars.com/kickingtires/2012/01/carscom-news-briefs-jan-31-2012.html" target="_self" title="Tuesday&#39;s expectations">Tuesday’s expectations</a>. This marks the best annualized month since 2009, Automotive News reports. Chrysler, Nissan and Hyundai-Kia saw double-digit sales gains, with GM the only top-seven automaker to post a loss.</p>

		</div>
		
			<a id="more"></a>
				
Pickups were all over the place. Helped by higher incentives versus year-ago levels, sales for the Ram increased 46.8%, making it the strongest January for Chrysler’s popular pickup since 2008. The Ford F-Series improved 7.5%, but Chevrolet can’t wait for <a href="http://news.pickuptrucks.com/2012/01/2014-silverado-sierra-has-big-changes/comments/page/2/" target="_self" title="the next-generation">the next-generation</a> Silverado to arrive. The outgoing truck, which has been around <a href="http://www.cars.com/chevrolet/silverado-1500/2007/expert-reviews/?revid=50283" target="_self" title="since late 2006">since late 2006</a>, fell 4.7% versus a strong January 2011.
<p>With higher incentives to start the year, Nissan Altima sales ballooned 35.9% off of a slow January 2011. The Altima would have been January’s top-selling sedan, had the redesigned Toyota Camry not jumped <em>55.9% </em>—<em> </em>a Mitt Romney-size smack-down to keep the title of best-selling sedan, though we doubt the Camry demographic includes <a href="http://content.usatoday.com/communities/onpolitics/post/2012/01/mitt-romney-releases-taxes-/1" target="_self" title="guys like Mitt">guys like Mitt</a>. (For the record, though, Romney’s cars during his ’08 campaign <a href="http://blogs.cars.com/kickingtires/2008/01/cars-of-the-can.html" target="_self" title="don&#39;t exactly smack of privilege">don’t exactly smack of privilege</a>.)</p>
<p><strong></strong>We digress. Small cars are selling well: <a href="http://blogs.cars.com/kickingtires/2011/08/consumer-reports-not-recommending-2012-honda-civic-.html" target="_self" title="A lukewarm reception">A lukewarm reception</a> has had little effect on the redesigned Honda Civic, whose sales are up nearly 50%. The Ford Focus and Chevrolet Cruze fell less than 1,700 units off the Top 10, with the Ford up some 60%. Focus ended January just 649 sales short of the Cruze, a car <a href="http://blogs.cars.com/kickingtires/2011/12/why-the-chevrolet-cruze-beat-the-ford-focus.html" target="_self" title="that trounced">that trounced</a> Focus last year. Ford may close the gap yet, but it comes at a cost. Shoppers in January enjoyed up to $1,000 off the 2012 Focus — a big discount for a redesigned compact — while Chevrolet kept its narrow lead with no cash incentives on the Cruze.</p>
<p>Both are looking up at the Toyota Corolla, which is down 12.6%. Rounding out the list are the redesigned Honda CR-V, the ever-popular Ford Escape and the age-old Chevrolet Impala, a popular alternative to the defunct Ford Crown Victoria for police fleets.</p>
<p>Despite sales falling 6.1%, GM keeps its 2011 crown as America’s best-selling automaker. Ford, Toyota, Chrysler and Honda follow. Hard-charging Hyundai-Kia improved 20.3% in January sales to come within 1,102 cars of Nissan, but the Japanese automaker gained 10.4% to keep sixth place.</p>
<p>Here are January’s 10 best-selling cars.</p>
