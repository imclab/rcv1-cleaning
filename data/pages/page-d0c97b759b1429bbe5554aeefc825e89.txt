<p><span class="dropcap2 ie6fix">1</span>We have been serving our customers since 1993. Our customers choose Fixers Automotive because they trust us to provide quality service at an affordable price.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.fixersautomotive.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/crina_breanna_tarfin1.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-284" title="crina_breanna_tarfin" src="http://www.fixersautomotive.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/crina_breanna_tarfin1-150x150.jpg" alt="" width="150" height="150" /></a></p>
</div>
<div class="one_fourth "><h3>Free Loaner Car</h3>
<p><span class="dropcap2 ie6fix">2</span>We understand that being without your car can be stressful. That&#8217;s why we offer our customers a <strong>FREE</strong> loaner car if major repairs are needed.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.fixersautomotive.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/free-loaner1.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-286" title="free loaner" src="http://www.fixersautomotive.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/free-loaner1-150x150.jpg" alt="" width="150" height="150" /></a></p>
</div>
<div class="one_fourth "></div>
<h3></h3>

<h3></h3>
				</div>
				
												
				<h1 class='fptab '><!--<a href="http://www.fixersautomotive.com/why-avisio"> -->Car Care Tips<!--</a>--></h1>
				<div class='fptab_content tab2 '>
				<div class="one_fourth "><h3>Regular Oil Change</h3>
<p><span class="dropcap2 ie6fix">1</span><span style="font: 12px/16px arial, helvetica, clean, sans-serif; text-align: left; color: #333333; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; float: none; display: inline !important; white-space: normal; orphans: 2; widows: 2; background-color: #ffffff; -webkit-text-size-adjust: auto; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">Checking and changing oil is critical to keep today’s engines running properly and efficiently. Follow manufacturer guidelines for changing the lubricant – generally, every 3,000 miles or three to six months.</span></p>
<p><a href="http://clientpreviewpage.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/oil_change_coupons.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-thumbnail wp-image-178" title="Oil Change" src="http://clientpreviewpage.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/oil_change_coupons-150x150.jpg" alt="" width="150" height="150" /></a></p>
</div>
<div class="one_fourth "><h3>Engine Coolant</h3>
<p><span class="dropcap2 ie6fix">2</span><span style="font: 12px/16px arial, helvetica, clean, sans-serif; text-align: left; color: #333333; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; float: none; display: inline !important; white-space: normal; orphans: 2; widows: 2; background-color: #ffffff; -webkit-text-size-adjust: auto; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">Replace most engine coolant or antifreeze every 30,000 miles – or every two to three years. Newer formulas, however, may last up to 50,000 miles. To check coolant level, turn the car off and wait for the engine to cool. Locate the coolant reservoir (usually a translucent plastic tank) and eyeball the level of the coolant against the full and low indicators.</span></p>
<p><a href="http://clientpreviewpage.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/coolant.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-thumbnail wp-image-181" title="coolant" src="http://clientpreviewpage.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/coolant-150x150.jpg" alt="" width="150" height="150" /></a></p>
</div>
<div class="one_fourth "><h3>Brakes and Fluid Check</h3>
<p><span class="dropcap2 ie6fix">3</span><span style="font: 12px/16px arial, helvetica, clean, sans-serif; text-align: left; color: #333333; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; float: none; display: inline !important; white-space: normal; orphans: 2; widows: 2; background-color: #ffffff; -webkit-text-size-adjust: auto; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">Check to ensure that brake fluid levels are full. How often you need to replace brake pads or other components depends on how you drive and typical driving conditions. Warning signs of a brake problem include noise, vibration or “grabbing” when you apply the brakes. Working on your brakes is a job best left to our certified mechanics.</span></p>
<p><a href="http://clientpreviewpage.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/brakes.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-thumbnail wp-image-185" title="brakes" src="http://clientpreviewpage.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/brakes-150x150.jpg" alt="" width="150" height="150" /></a></p>
</div>
<div class="one_fourth last"><h3>Air Filter</h3>
<p><span class="dropcap2 ie6fix">4</span>C<span style="font: 12px/16px arial, helvetica, clean, sans-serif; text-align: left; color: #333333; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; float: none; display: inline !important; white-space: normal; orphans: 2; widows: 2; background-color: #ffffff; -webkit-text-size-adjust: auto; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">hange your air filter each year or every 12,000-15,000 miles. A clean air filter can help your engine “breathe” better and improve gas mileage and reduce harmful emissions.</span></p>
<p><a href="http://clientpreviewpage.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/air-filter.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-thumbnail wp-image-188" title="air filter" src="http://clientpreviewpage.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/air-filter-150x150.jpg" alt="" width="150" height="150" /></a></p>
