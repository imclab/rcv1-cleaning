<font size="1">the lexus 'LF-LC' concept sports coupe</font><br/>
<br/><br/>
<a href="http://www.lexus.com/" title="" target="_blank">lexus</a> has unveiled its 'LF-LC' concept sport coupe, showcasing the company's emphasis on 'drive-focused vehicles' <br/>
with its combination of performance elements and integrated technology. the car is on exhibition through january 22nd <br/>
at <a href="http://www.naias.com/" title="">detroit auto show</a> 2012.<br/>
<br/>featuring a glass roof, the 'LF-LC' is a front-engine, rear-wheel drive hybrid four-seater in 2+2 configuration <br/>
(two in the front and two in the rear). daytime running lights, as well as integrated tail and fog lamps, <br/>
resemble the form of an inverted 'L'; while air intakes at the base of the car's mesh spindle grille offer an element <br/>
both functional and sculpture.<br/>
<br/>the vehicle's interior features a central touchscreen control board, with optionally raisable touchscreen keyboard <br/>
between the front seats, utilizable for control of audio, climate functions, and navigation. smaller touch panels <br/>
are built into the sides of each door to control side mirrors, doors and windows, and seat adjustments. <br/>
the instrument panel is a 12.3-inch LCD display that layers analog and digital elements to showcase temperature, <br/>
fuel economy, tachometer, and speedometer.<br/>
