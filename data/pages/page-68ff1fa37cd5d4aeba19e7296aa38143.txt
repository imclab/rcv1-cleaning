<p>Ricky Martin admits he was homophobic as he struggled to deal with his own sexuality.</p>
<p>  The superstar came out in 2010 after years of speculation. He has since become an outspoken advocate of gay rights around the world, but has revealed he would aim abuse at homosexuals while he tried to secretly deal with his feelings.</p>
<p>  In an interview with Australian GQ, he says, “I was very angry, very rebellious. I used to look at gay men and think, ‘I’m not like that; I don’t want to be like that, that’s not me.’ I was ashamed.</p>
<p>  “When you’re told you’re wrong by everyone, from society, from your faith – my self-esteem was crushed. I took my anger out on those around me.</p>
<p>  “I look back now and realize I would bully people who I knew were gay. I had internalized homophobia. To realize that was confronting to me. I wanted to get away from that.” </p>

<p>  The 20-year-old stripped down to her rubber underwear and donned a giant foam hand to perform “Blurred Lines” with Robin Thicke, who seemed more than a little bemused as his stage partner danced provocatively in front of him and grinded her backside into his crotch, while making inappropriate gestures to the audience.</p>

<p>  She says, “I don’t understand what Miley Cyrus is trying to do. I think she’s misbegotten in this attempt of hers. And I think it was not beneficial. I didn’t get what her point was. It was so over the top as to almost be a parody of itself.” </p>

<p>Funnymen Seth Rogen, Jonah Hill and Bill Hader led the bawdy tributes to their pal James Franco as the actor was “roasted” in California on Sunday night.</p>
<p>The star was the butt of all jokes at the Comedy Central Roast as his pals mocked his movie flops, his sexuality and his much-maligned co-hosting gig with Anne Hathaway at the 2011 Oscars.</p>
<p>Rogen, who served as master of ceremonies, held nothing back as he picked on his “Pineapple Express” co-star, quipping, “He once told me he worked for 36 hours straight, which I didn’t believe. The straight part, of course.”</p>
<p>According to TheWrap.com, the host also joked, “Look at me doing all the talking while you sit there doing nothing. I feel like I’m hosting the Oscars with you!”</p>
<p>But Franco was given some respite at the TV taping in Culver City when the comedy stars let rip at one another.</p>
<p>Poking fun at chubby actor Hill, Jeff Ross said, “When Jonah Hill’s agent told him Quentin Tarantino wanted him to be in a spaghetti western, Jonah said, ‘You had me at spaghetti.’”</p>
<p>Hill got his own back when he took aim at stand-up star Sarah Silverman, telling her, “You were one of my favorite comedians as a kid. I don’t get why people say she’s hot for a comic. She’s not hot for a comic, she’s hot for her age.”</p>
<p>Franco’s roast will air as a special on Comedy Central on Labor Day.</p>

<p>Broadway star Kristin Chenoweth was left stunned during a show at the Hollywood Bowl in Los Angeles on Saturday when a fan joined her onstage for a “Wicked” duet and became an instant star.</p>
<p>  The actress/singer asked Sarah Horn to join her for a rendition of “For Good,” which Chenoweth sang on Broadway as a member of “Wicked”‘s original cast, and was blown away by her talents.</p>
<p>  Horn, a California voice coach, belted out the duet and even harmonized with the star, leaving Chenoweth lost for words and beaming with glee.</p>
<p>  Declaring Horn’s performance “inspiring” as she left the stage to a standing ovation following her stint in the spotlight with her heroine, Chenoweth added, “That voice is teaching our young people.”</p>
<p>  Horn says her moment of glory was destined because she actually swapped seats with another concertgoer and then offered to take the place of Chenoweth’s first volunteer when she revealed she did not know the words to “For Good.”</p>
<p>  She tells LAist.com, “I lifted my microphone to my mouth, looked Kristin straight in the eye and sang the first of my lines. She leaned back and dropped her jaw. She knew I was a voice teacher but didn’t know if I did well in the execution or not. As far as she knew, I could have been tone deaf.” </p>
