			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://japanesenostalgiccar.com/2013/08/26/qotw-what-non-car-japanese-nostalgic-vehicle-would-you-love-to-drive/" title="12:56 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">August 26, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://japanesenostalgiccar.com/author/admin/" title="View all posts by Ben">Ben</a></span>							</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

<p>We just spent two days driving every new Nissan vehicle produces, from the Moco kei car to the Cabstar truck (more on this soon). Which got us thinking:</p>
<p>What non-car Japanese nostalgic vehicle would you love to drive?</p>
<p>By 1974 Mazda was fully invested in the rotary engine, putting them in everything from sports coupes to luxury sedans to, yes, even a bus. The <strong>Mazda Parkway 26</strong> could carry you and 25 friends in premium comfort, with such amenities as &#8220;a soft ceiling trim&#8221; and &#8220;three-stage heating.&#8221; Not would would you be driving the world&#8217;s only 13B-powered bus, but one of the rarest old Japanese vehicles around. Only 44 were every built over four years of production.</p>
