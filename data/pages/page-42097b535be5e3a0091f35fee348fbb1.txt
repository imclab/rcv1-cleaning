					<p>I can literally hear you groan as you read this. I must be the only person in the world, besides Busy, that thinks that is an adorable name.</p>

<p>There was a Cricket on a soap opera I watched when I was a teenager and home for long, boring summers before I could drive..and I always thought it was a perfect girl name. But apparently no one else in the entertainment world thinks so.</p>
<p>Busy has been busy defending her decision to name her child after a bug. And it makes me like her more for it. I also like her a hellalot because she looks like a normal woman after having a baby. Not a freak-of-nature back to normal in 4 weeks Hollywood stepford mom.</p>

</em></p>

<p><em>Cricket Silverstein, the newborn daughter of Busy Philipps and husband Marc Silverstein, joins her big sister Birdie, four, who has a pretty and humdrum name in comparison.</em></p>

					<p>You think you were raised in a messed up church that preached fire and brimstone? At least you didn&#8217;t grow up in the Church of Scientology like Leah Remini. The King of Queens actress joined the church with her mother in the 70&#8242;s and has always been a strong supporter of their crazy.</p>
<p>Until now. Apparently, Leah has left the church because for the past five years she has been under some crazy ass mind-programming thing.</p>
<p>Seriously. And I&#8217;m starting to get a little worried about David Miscavige&#8217;s wife.</p>
<p><em>Remini is believed to be dropping out of the organization as she is against its policy of barring members from questioning the authority of Scientology leader David Miscavige.<br />
</em></p>
<p><em>The rift began, a source told the <a href="http://www.nypost.com/p/pagesix/remini_quits_scientology_kPy6Yw4WymA6dSg9VQYVEN" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">New York Post</a>, at the wedding of Tom Cruise and Katie Holmes in 2006.<br />
</em></p>

<p><em>According to a blog by ex-Scientology member <a href="http://www.mikerindersblog.org/leah-remini-has-left-the-building/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Mike Rinder</a>, Davis said: &#8216;You don’t have the [bleeping] rank to ask about Shelly.&#8217;</em></p>
<p><em>Miscavige&#8217;s wife has not been seen in public for six years.<br />
</em></p>
<p><em>The actress was then subsequently subjected to five years of &#8216;thought modification&#8217;, according to the Post. Church friends, many of whom Remini had known for years, allegedly wrote reports on her and had her family investigated. </em></p>
