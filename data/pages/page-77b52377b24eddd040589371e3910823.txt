Trade In Value vs. Retail Value</h3>

<p>Car Trade in value VS. wholesale value, what&#8217;s the
best way to find out
your car's trade in value, learn the secret to sell your car to dealer
for
top dollars!
</p>
<p>Car trade in value and wholesale value are
essentially
the same. The only difference would be the car&#8217;s trade in value CAN BE
higher than the wholesale value. To better understand the scenario. We
will use a case study to illustrate both terms.</p>
<p>This page is fairly long as I'm trying to
explaining the
subject matter as precise as possible, so please be patient and read it
from the top to the bottom. Then you will have a good understanding of <a href="http://www.used-cars-advice.com/used-car-value.html">Used
Car Values</a> as well as <a href="http://www.used-cars-advice.com/used-truck-value.html">
Used Truck Value</a>.</p>
<p>Let&#8217;s say you own a year 2003 Toyota Camry LE 4
cylinder
base model and you are in the market of buying another vehicle, you
went to this dealer and you like to buy the year 2008 Toyota 4Runner
Sports Edition that they have on the used car lot.</p>
<p><a href="#Should_I_Trade_In_or_Sell_On_My_Own"><img style="border: 0px solid ; width: 270px; float: right; height: 239px;" alt="" src="http://www.used-cars-advice.com/image-files/used-car-values.jpg"></a>The
term trade in
normally implied that you are
selling
your current vehicle back to the dealership as a TRADE, for whatever
the value this dealer appraised for and you intend to purchase another
vehicle at the same dealership.</p>
<p>Since you intend to purchase another inventory
vehicle
of the dealer you visit, meaning the dealership has financial interest
on you (as a customer) and your vehicle (as a trade in vehicle and
another used vehicle inventory to sell for profit). Therefore, your
car&#8217;s trade in value will vary based on:</p>
<ul>
<li>Profit margin of the used car that you are
interested in buying (2008 Toyota 4Runner) </li>
<li>Trade in vehicle condition, marketability,
potential
(2003 Toyota Camry)</li>
</ul>
<p>To make the transaction happen, the dealer would
either
offer higher car trade in value for your 2003 Toyota Camry which is
common practice, or lowered the retail price of the 2008 Toyota 4Runner
that you would like to own. Or they will do both to earn your business.</p>
<p>The dealer will say it out loud that they pay you
above
the <a href="http://www.used-cars-advice.com/black-book-car-values.html" title="black book car values page"> Black Book Car Values</a>
for your trade. Now you know their secret agenda about trade in value
PERIOD! Well of course, if you are from Canada deals will go with <a href="http://www.used-cars-advice.com/canadian-black-book.html">
Canadian Black Book</a>.<br>

<h3><br>
Wholesale Value Is Book Value</h3>
<p><img style="width: 288px; float: left; height: 273px;" alt="" src="http://www.used-cars-advice.com/image-files/black-book-used-car-values.jpg" hspace="10" vspace="5">Now let&#8217;s define the term
wholesale value. If you
decided just to sell your car straight to the dealer without buying
another one, the dealer will pay your car with itswholesale value.</p>
<p>Unlike car trade in value, a car&#8217;s wholesale value
is
how much the car is sold on auctions (auction price), or the value that
wholesalers would pay for.</p>
<p>Since this is a single vehicle transaction and
less
avenue for dealer to make profit. Sometimes a dealer would just print
out an auction report to show you exactly how much was the car sold in
recently auctions.</p>
<p>Depends on the mechanical and physical condition
of your
car, the wholesale value can be even lower than the auction report
print out. Because there are reconditioning cost, repairs and safety
inspections etc.</p>
<h3><a name="Should_I_Trade_In_or_Sell_On_My_Own"></a>Should
I Trade In or Sell On My Own</h3>
<p>Obviously selling it on your own will get you the
most
money out of your car. If you are not comfortable dealing with private
parties, or just not interested in selling by yourself.</p>
<p>Sell the car back to dealer would be your second
choice,
sure you leave some money on the table, but that&#8217;s a quick and easy
transaction. Especially for people don&#8217;t have lots of time and need to
sell the car fast.</p>
<p>If you own an older model cars like 15 years old
or
older, the car trade in value can be as little as $500 to $1,000.</p>
<p>If the car is still in reasonable driving
condition,
it&#8217;s better for you to sell it privately than trade it in.</p>
<p>If on the other hand you own a late model car less
than
5 years old, trade it in for a new car would much easier for you. </p>
