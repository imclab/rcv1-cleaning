<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-65602" src="http://www.automobilesreview.com/uploads/2013/05/2013-Mercedes-Benz-E-Class-Coupe-651.jpg" alt="" width="651" height="434" /></p>
<p><strong>Mercedes-Benz UK</strong> has released the pricing of the new <strong>2013 E-Class Coupe</strong>. It starts from £35,095 for the entry level E 220 CDI SE and goes up to £42,435 for the top-of-range E 350 BlueTEC AMG Sport.</p>
<p>The 2013 E-Class Coupe comes with significantly enhanced standard specification and a stylish new look as part of the most extensive model revision ever undertaken by <a href="http://www.automobilesreview.com/category/car-makes/mercedes-benz/">Mercedes-Benz</a>.</p>
<p>The luxurious Coupe is now up to 21 % more efficient and feature the most advanced technology ever created for the Mercedes-Benz range of vehicles, including the introduction of the new Stereo Multi-Purpose Camera, which features two ‘eyes’ to produce a 3D view of up to 50-metres in front to aid many of the new or upgraded safety systems.</p>
<p>The engine range includes a 2.2 liter diesel with 170HP (125 kW) for <strong>E 220 CDI</strong>, a 2.2 liter with 204HP (150 kW) for <strong>E 250 CDI</strong>, a 3.0 liter twin-turbo diesel with 252HP (185 kW) for <strong>E350 BlueTEC AMG Sport</strong>. Last, but not least the petrol 2.0 liter with 184HP (135 kW) in <strong>E 200</strong> AMG Sport.</p>
<p>The standard specification includes COMAND Online with Media Interface, DAB digital radio, a new speed-sensitive electric power Direct Steering system that improves CO<sub>2</sub> and fuel consumption, new partial LED headlights, Active Park Assist, Attention Assist and Collision Prevention Assist.</p>
<p>In addition, the SE benefits from 17” five twin-spoke alloy wheels, a new front apron with single lamella Sport grille, leather upholstery with heated front seats and dark aluminium trim.</p>
<p>The AMG Sport models are enhanced by 18” twin-spoke alloy wheels, upgraded front brakes featuring branded calipers and perforated discs, new AMG bodystyling, visible twin-pipe exhaust, leather upholstery with multi-contour heated front seats, an AMG steering wheel in nappa leather with flattened bottom section and AMG floor mats.</p>
<p>For new AMG Sport models, there is the option of upgrading to the AMG Sport Plus package (available from October 2013, priced at £1,795), consisting of 19” AMG bi-colour alloy wheels, an AMG boot spoiler in body colour, red seat belts, AMG Sports seats in Artico/Dinamica with red stitching, AMG steering wheel with red stitching, AMG floor mats with red edging and red stitching on the door panels, centre console and armrest.</p>
<p>The option list includes the new full LED Intelligent Light System (£1,280), new Aragonite Silver metallic paint (from October 2013, priced at £645) and new leather upholstery colours in Espresso Brown, Deep Sea Blue and Bengal Red.</p>
