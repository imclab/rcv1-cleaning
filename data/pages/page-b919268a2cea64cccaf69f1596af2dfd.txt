				<h1>What Is a Fine Arts Degree?</h1>

				<p class="wikiDesc">
					A degree in Fine Arts can prepare students for careers in the visual arts or the performing arts. Programs at colleges, universities, art academies and technical schools often provide an overview of Fine Arts before requiring students to choose a concentration.

						<h4 id='section--FineArtsCoursework'>Fine Arts Coursework</h4>
<p>Fine Arts programs are as varied as the students who enroll in them. Students may choose to concentrate in a visual art, such as painting photography and sculpture, or they may conentrate in a performing art like dance, voice and theater. Examples of topics a Fine Arts student can expect to study are listed below:
</p>
<ul>
<li> Art history
</li>
<li> Art theory and criticism
</li>
<li> Performing arts
</li>
<li> 2D art (painting, drawing, photography)
</li>
<li> 3D art (sculpture, pottery)
</li>
<li> Digital art
</li>
</ul>
<h4 id='section--AssociateOfFineArtsAFA'>Associate of Fine Arts (AFA)</h4>
<p>An associate's degree program in Fine Arts from a two-year or four-year college or university provides a foundation for upper class work leading to a bachelor's degree. Two-year technical schools and art academies may provide more specialized training in a shorter amount of time. Some career opportunities available to Associate of Fine Arts graduates are listed below:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Private music or art teacher
</li>
<li>Theater production assistant
</li>
<li>Freelance photographer
</li>
<li>Jewelry designer
</li>
</ul>
<h4 id='section--BachelorOfFineArtsBFA'>Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA)</h4>
<p>Bachelor of Fine Arts programs help artists define their creative expression and develop professional skills that will help them promote their art as freelancers or obtain jobs with arts-related companies or nonprofit organizations. This undergraduate degree qualifies graduates for entry-level positions with galleries, museums and theater companies, including such job positions as the following:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Creative writer
</li>
<li>Actor/director
</li>
<li>Art teacher
</li>
<li>Art therapist
</li>
<li>Dance instructor
</li>
</ul>
<h4 id='section--MasterOfFineArtsMFA'>Master of Fine Arts (MFA)</h4>
<p>A Master of Fine Arts can lead to management and directorship positions in arts-related enterprises. Graduate degree programs offer both the creative and the management skills needed to become a museum director or a theatrical producer, for example. Master of Fine Arts candidates specialize in their chosen area of concentration. Career opportunities include the following:
</p>
<ul>
<li> Curator
</li>
<li> Appraiser
</li>
<li> Costume designer
</li>
<li> Archivist
</li>
<li> Nonprofit administrator</li>
</ul>
