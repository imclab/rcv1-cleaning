Weekly's film critic Scott Foundas, frequent Voice contributor Melissa Anderson, and former Voice film editor Dennis Lim, the New York Film Festival&mdash;officially presented by the Film Society of Lincoln Center&mdash;is obviously the best fest in town. (Sorry, De Niro.)&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-film-festival-1436229/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Combination Movie Theater, Archive, and Community Center</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-combination-movie-theater-archive-and-community-center-1436293/'>Maysles Institute</a></h3>
In 2005, cinema v&eacute;rit&eacute; godfather Albert Maysles founded (with his son, Philip) Harlem's Maysles Institute, charging his fledgling organization with the task of teaching documentary filmmaking to community youth. In 2008, the organization finished work on a movie theater, making it Manhattan's northernmost repertory cinema. Since then, the Institute has programmed everything from "Country Rap Tunes," a Texas/Louisiana hip-hop documentary&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-combination-movie-theater-archive-and-community-center-1436293/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<img class='framed' src='http://media.villagevoice.com/4009803.45.jpg' alt="Eugene Mirman" /><h4>Best Stand-Up Comedian</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-stand-up-comedian-1436298/'>Eugene Mirman</a></h3>
When Brooklyn funnyman Eugene Mirman does his stand-up, he sometimes tells the story about why his teacher in Massachusetts placed him in special-ed in the sixth grade. Instead of giving a book report, the Russian-born Mirman came to class dressed in a cowboy costume and lip-synched Bill Cosby's stand-up routine for five minutes straight. Oh, if only he'd attended a&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-stand-up-comedian-1436298/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best After-Hours Museum</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-after-hours-museum-1436316/'>Guggenheim</a></h3>
We can't recall the last time we attended the Guggenheim during normal business hours, but we have reveled in many of the museum's after-hours soirees, flask in jacket pocket. It's not that their collections, as of late, haven't been stimulating&mdash;it's just that we rather enjoy interpreting Thannhauser and Kandinsky while bumping tunes blare through the white-spiraled wormhole of Frank Lloyd&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-after-hours-museum-1436316/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Multimedia Artificial-Intelligence Arts Group</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-multimedia-artificial-intelligence-arts-group-1436317/'>OpenEnded Group</a></h3>
"A.I." usually conjures images of evil, world-dominating machines, but not for a trio of multimedia artists. In the mid-'90s, Paul Kaiser and Shelley Eshkar worked together on computer-animation pieces and were later joined by Marc Downie in 2001 to form the OpenEnded Group. That year, they collaborated with the late choreographer Merce Cunningham for Loops: Cunningham's moving hands were fitted&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-multimedia-artificial-intelligence-arts-group-1436317/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Classical Radio DJ</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-classical-radio-dj-1436318/'>Jeff Spurgeon</a></h3>
Oh, hell, let's just call him the city's best radio DJ, period. Or at least the most amusing. The classical music scene can sometimes take itself a bit too seriously&mdash;no news there&mdash;but there's little danger of stuffiness every weekday morning on WQXR from 5:30 a.m. to 10 a.m., when Haydn, Mendelssohn, and the rest of that strings-loving crew find themselves&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-classical-radio-dj-1436318/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Grown Men Who Still Play With Dolls</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-grown-men-who-still-play-with-dolls-1436319/'>Nick Jones and Raja Azar</a></h3>
"When I was a child," intones First Corinthians, "I spake as a child, I understood as a child, I thought as a child: but when I became a man, I put away childish things." Well, thank God Nick Jones and Raja Azar haven't read the Bible. Though they're all grown up, playwright Jones and composer Azar have yet to put&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-grown-men-who-still-play-with-dolls-1436319/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Queens Sweet Spot</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-queens-sweet-spot-1436320/'>Chocolate Factory</a></h3>
Not a month goes by that we don't receive news of another New York theater shuttering. Stalwarts fall to high rent and creeping gentrification, classic stages become trendy boutiques, black boxes give way to yoga studios, and warehouses are transformed into condominiums. So we like to order a standing ovation when we hear of a new space: The Chocolate Factory&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-queens-sweet-spot-1436320/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best New Music Venue That Has a Ring to It</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-new-music-venue-that-has-a-ring-to-it-1436321/'>The Bell House</a></h3>
In Motherless Brooklyn, Jonathan Lethem described the Gowanus Canal as "the only body of water in the world that is 90 percent guns." But recently, that benighted waterway has become home to an art and music scene that greatly improves the canal's sights and sounds&mdash;if not its particular stench. The latest to arrive is The Bell House, a bar and&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-new-music-venue-that-has-a-ring-to-it-1436321/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Brooklyn Gallery</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-brooklyn-gallery-1436322/'>Brooklynite Gallery</a></h3>
The only stuffy thing at the Brooklynite Gallery are the loads of people who come in droves on opening nights, lured by handmade invitations that always promise a memorable musical experience (examples have included sets by Soulsonic Force and DJ Kool Herc). The gallery opened in 2008, as curator Hope McGrath tells us, "after three long years of construction and&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-brooklyn-gallery-1436322/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Booking Coup</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-booking-coup-1436323/'>Housing Works Used Bookstore Caf&eacute;</a></h3>
May was a great month for the little guy&mdash;Housing Works Used Bookstore Caf&eacute; saw to it. Their "Live From Home" concert series staff pulled the incredible victory of nabbing Bj&ouml;rk and the Dirty Projectors for an exclusive, cosmos-colliding benefit concert in the Soho store; the brilliant Icelandic sprite and the Brooklyn art-rockers performed a suite of original compositions by head&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-booking-coup-1436323/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best New Brooklyn Band</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-new-brooklyn-band-1436324/'>Cymbals Eat Guitars</a></h3>
Have the students become the teachers? Cymbals Eat Guitars, originally a Weezer cover band when they formed in their native Staten Island, have dramatically shifted their stride since relocating to Brooklyn. Their sonic soup still contains a bit of their idols' nebbish self-effacement, but it melds deftly into psych-pop and post-punk, abetted live by singer/guitarist Joseph D'Agostino's twitching, jarring solos&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-new-brooklyn-band-1436324/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Gay Theater Fest</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-gay-theater-fest-1436326/'>The HOT! Festival</a></h3>
It's fitting that The HOT! Festival turned 18 in 2009. Just when Dixon Place moved into a fabulous, state-of-the-art space from the fleabag furnished Bowery room it used to inhabit, HOT! grew up, too. Six weeks of LGBT programming always offer a diverse lineup of new and exciting works, but this year's festival really took the cake, showing some of&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-gay-theater-fest-1436326/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best World Music Venue</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-world-music-venue-1436328/'>Peter Norton Symphony Space</a></h3>
One of the most grandiose neon marquees in New York can be found far from the perennial noon of Times Square&mdash;it's in the Upper West Side, nestled among the strollers and Starbucks&mdash;hawking the Peter Norton Symphony Space, a beautiful, modern concert hall that hardly needs the introduction. The broad room is best known as the home of the InterSchool Orchestras&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-world-music-venue-1436328/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Sketch Comedy Ensemble</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-sketch-comedy-ensemble-1436330/'>Elephant Larry</a></h3>
Remember Minesweeper: The Movie? No, it wasn't produced by Jerry Bruckheimer. Actually, it was a mock movie trailer by New York's very talented sketch group Elephant Larry. Done with ridiculous panache and excellent production, the short concerned a hilariously unskilled team of minesweepers (think The Hurt Locker, but with way more accidents) and quickly became one of CollegeHumor.com's most popular&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-sketch-comedy-ensemble-1436330/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Political Comic</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-political-comic-1436331/'>Randy Credico</a></h3>
Timing is everything in stand-up comedy, and it helps in politics as well. This spring&mdash;as a cabal of shady state senators were preparing to hijack the entire legislature with shakedown demands&mdash;comedian/political agitator Randy Credico showed up in the Albany State Capitol building wearing a white robe, a fake, wispy white beard, and a rubber face mask depicting a bald guy&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-political-comic-1436331/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<img class='framed' src='http://media.villagevoice.com/4202857.45.jpg' alt="Screaming Females" /><h4>Best Guitar-Shredder</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-guitar-shredder-1436333/'>Screaming Females</a></h3>
In the history of rock music, there have been exactly zero even tolerable covers of "Cortez the Killer," so please understand how improbable it is that New Brunswick, New Jersey's Screaming Females are now offering a stupendous version of the Neil Young weeper on their MySpace page, sped up and imbued with a menacing, bizarrely erotic swagger. The shred-punk trio's&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-guitar-shredder-1436333/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Small Press</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-small-press-1436334/'>Melville House</a></h3>
Melville House's 2008 leap across the Hudson River from Hoboken to DUMBO means they're fully ours now, and Amen. There is no shortage of small and independent presses in New York City, but Melville House&mdash;whose stable includes both an avant-fiction wing, of the Tao Lin/Stephen Dixon variety, and a lively political nonfiction roster that includes Mark Danner, Renata Adler, and&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-small-press-1436334/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Music Critic</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-music-critic-1436335/'>Rapidshare</a></h3>
With all apologies to our own Rob Harvilla, as well as every other human on the planet, this one we have to give to Rapidshare, the inanimate Internet technology that allows users to host and trade files online without the interference of antiquated middlemen like, say, the employees of this newspaper. File-sharing isn't news on the Net, of course&mdash;Napster debuted&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-music-critic-1436335/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Reason to Read 'The New Yorker'</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-reason-to-read-the-new-yorker-1436336/'>Kelefa Sanneh</a></h3>
Seymour Hersh has the impeccable stats, the arts critics are, by and large, a murderers' row, and Hertzberg's got a certain gentlemanly leftist rage on lock, but the best reason to read The New Yorker these days is Kelefa Sanneh, the youthful refugee from The New York Times who was scooped up in a savvy move early last year. His&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-reason-to-read-the-new-yorker-1436336/'>More >></a>
</li>
<li>
<h4>Best Young Writer</h4>
<h3><a href='/bestof/2009/award/best-young-writer-1436338/'>Wells Tower</a></h3>
Young talent, however beleaguered and outer Bushwick it gets, remains one of New York's selling points. And, in the mostly non-electrified literary community, youthful debuts are about as exciting as things get, give or take a new Philip Roth novel. So far, this year's debut to beat is Wells Tower's Everything Ravaged, Everything Burned, an eerily assured, unsettling story collection&hellip; <a class='more' href='/bestof/2009/award/best-young-writer-1436338/'>More >></a>
</li>
</ul>
