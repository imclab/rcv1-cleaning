                                  <h1>Automotive Repair in Greensboro, NC</h1>
                 
                 <p class="editable" data-editmode-options="{position:  true,       path:  'proadtexteditor.htm?proAdId=151361&proAdAccountId=321655&function=Description',  text:  'Edit  Description'}">
                   <p><span style="font-size: small;"><img style=
"float: left; margin: 5px 10px;" title="IMG_7861" src=
"http://images.talkingphonebook.com/images/timesunion/media_library/202/057/media39.jpg"
 alt="IMG_7861"> We are an independent auto service facility that
provides an economical alternative to expensive dealer service of
your automobile. Our automotive repair center is a state-of-the-art
facility prepared for any type of servicing.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">We are equipped to handle work
from the simplest oil change to rebuilding or replacing the engine
or transmission in your vehicle. We offer a complete range of
diagnostic programs, including the foreign diagnostics, to deal
with today&rsquo;s complicated emissions control systems. We also
sell tires of a wide variety brands.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">Expect only prompt and courteous
personal service at very reasonable rates. We look forward to
earning your trust &amp; working with you for a long time! We are
your go to experts in foreign and domestic car repair.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">HOURS Monday-Friday 7:30 to
5:30pm</span><br>
 <span style="font-size: small;">Brinsfield Automotive 707 Edwardia
Drive Greensboro, NC 27409 Phone: 336-292-6499 Email:
service@brinsfieldautomotive.com</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">We specialize in:</span></p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Volkswagen,
Audi, Mini Cooper, Honda, Toyota, Jaguar, Lexus, Infiniti, Volvo,
Saab, Acura, Mazda, and Nissan.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">Alignments Air Conditioning
Repair and Service</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">Brake repair
Clutches&nbsp;</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">Cooling System Repair and
Maintenance CV Axles</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">Diagnostic Services Electrical
(Alternators, Batteries, Starters)&nbsp;</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">Engine Repair
Exhaust</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">Engine Replacement
Mufflers</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">Engine Performance Check Wheel
Alignment</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">Fuel Injection Service and
Repair Oil Changes&nbsp;</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">Steering repair Suspension
(Shocks and Struts) repair</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">Timing Belt Replacement
Tires&nbsp;</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">Transmission Service and Repair
Tune Ups&nbsp;</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">New and Rebuilt Transmissions
Pre-Purchase Inspections&nbsp;</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: small;">30, 60, 90,000 Mile Maintenance
Services And More...</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">Oil Change Service</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">Short drives and infrequent oil
changes are two factors that lead to formation of sludge in an
engine. This gelling of the oil can cause premature engine failure.
Oil change intervals depend on the type of driving done. If most of
the driving is open highway with little stop and go the interval
could be 5000 miles. But if the driving done is short trips and or
stop and go, the oil should be changed every 3000 miles or 6 months
whichever comes first. Some vehicles use a monitoring program that
lets the computer say when to change the oil. Every oil change
includes a 12 point inspection, top up of fluids and set tire
pressures.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">Automatic Transmission
service</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">Remove transmission oil pan and
replace internal filter (if equipped), top off fluid and test
drive. This service is designed for regular maintenance and should
be performed with a transmission flush if the fluid is extremely
dirty or burnt. This service only replaces approximately 50-60% of
the fluid.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">Cooling system
service</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">Engine coolant loses some of its
protective qualities after a period of time. This is why you should
replace the engine coolant at 30,000 mile intervals if your engine
has the traditional coolant (the green stuff) and 60,000 mile
intervals for most extended life coolants (the red stuff). Some
manufacturers will state 100,000 mile intervals for extended life
coolant but in reality it should be replaced more often. When this
service is performed, the entire cooling system is inspected to
determine if hoses or other components need replacement. Coolant
hoses are recommended to be replaced every 4 years, regardless of
how many miles they have on them.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">Timing belt
replacement</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">Many of today&rsquo;s engines
utilize a timing belt. If this belt breaks, serious engine damage
could occur. This is why the vehicle manufacturer will specify
certain intervals at which this belt should be replaced. The
mileage will vary from 60,000 to 90,000 miles with some
recommending replacement at 105,000 miles. We recommend timing belt
replacement at 90,000 miles or your manufacturer&rsquo;s specified
interval, whichever is less.</span></p>
<p><img style="float: right; margin: 5px 10px;" title=
"Brinsfield Import Service4" src=
"http://images.talkingphonebook.com/images/timesunion/media_library/202/057/media37.jpg"
 alt="Brinsfield Import Service4"></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">Minor maintenance
service</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">This service includes an oil
change, full vehicle inspection including the brakes and
suspension, inspection of all exterior lights, windshield wipers
and a battery service. This service should be performed in between
your major maintenance services to help keep your car in top
running order.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">Major maintenance
service</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">This is typically your
30/60/90,000 mile service. Each manufacturer has their own
recommendations on what services should be performed at this
interval. Ask your service advisor for more information on keeping
your car maintained.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">Brakes</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">We first perform a complete
inspection of the braking system. Without doing this, we cannot
adequately determine what your car really needs or how much it will
cost. Our technicians can repair all braking systems including the
newer anti-lock brake systems. Each car requires different
components to repair the braking system so please schedule an
appointment to have your brakes inspected.</span></p>
<p><span style=
"font-size: medium;">Suspension/shocks/struts</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">By replacing the shocks and
struts on your vehicle you can maintain the ride quality, handling
characteristics and performance of your automobile. Shocks and
struts wear out very gradually and most people can&rsquo;t feel the
difference until they are replaced and the ride and handling are
firm and crisp. Any time a shock or strut is leaking it is
impossible for it to do its job properly and should be replaced. On
most vehicles, when the front struts are replaced an alignment
should also be performed.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">Engine diagnostics</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">If your car is not running
properly, won&rsquo;t start or has a &lsquo;check engine&rsquo;
light on, you will need some diagnostics. This procedure first
communicates with your car&rsquo;s computer to find out what the
computer thinks is wrong. We then verify component failure to make
sure the computer is not simply confused. After determining the
cause of your problem, we will recommend the necessary repairs. Our
cars are becoming mobile computer systems and the diagnostics
process is becoming more complicated with every new model. Our
experienced technicians and state-of-the-art equipment will help to
fix your problem.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">Clutches</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">The clutch is what engages the
transmission to the engine. Where and how you drive will have a
huge impact on how long this part will last. On some vehicles,
additional parts replacement may be required or recommended at the
time of clutch replacement. Ask us about clutch replacement at the
first sign of problems.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;"><img style=
"float: left; margin: 5px 10px;" title="IMG_7857" src=
"http://images.talkingphonebook.com/images/timesunion/media_library/202/057/media38.jpg"
 alt="IMG_7857"> Batteries/Charging System</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">The battery is the lifeblood of
your vehicle. It is responsible for starting your car and running
many of the accessories on the vehicle. The battery must endure
temperature extremes and still be able to perform. The natural
venting of fumes from the battery will cause corrosion on the
battery terminals. This must be periodically cleaned. The
alternator is responsible for operating all the electrical
accessories while the engine is running. Everything from the turn
signals to the engine computer to the fuel pump is powered by the
battery and alternator. The alternator also recharges the battery
after it is used to start the engine. Electrical system problems
can be slight or major. We sell batteries and offer electrical
system repairs and alternator replacement using quality
parts.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">Heat/Air
Conditioning</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small;">The heating and air conditioning
system, also known as climate control, is what keeps us comfortable
when we drive our cars. The coolant should be replaced
periodically. Proper service and diagnosis of the air conditioning
system and related repairs should only be done by a qualified
technician. There are certain EPA regulations and procedures that
must be followed for proper repair and recovery of the refrigerant.
An improper repair can result in damage to the rest of the system.
Please allow our qualified technicians to service your heating and
air conditioning system.</span></p>
