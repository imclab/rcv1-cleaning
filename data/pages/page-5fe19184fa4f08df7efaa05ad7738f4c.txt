			<h2>Language Arts: Writing, Literature, and Creativity</h2>

  <div class="content clear-block">
								<!--<img class="header_image" src="" />-->
		    <p>
	<i>Be sure to see our <a href="/lang_lit/">Language &amp; Literature Subject Center</a><br />
	for more great lesson ideas and articles.</i></p>
<center>
	<p>
		&nbsp;</p>
	<p class="rteleft">
		<strong><a href="http://www.educationworld.com/a_lesson/guy-fawkes-night-and-multiculturalism.shtml"><strong><span class="redheader"><strong>Cross-Cultural Dialogue Writing: Guy Fawkes&rsquo; Gunpowder Plot </strong></span></strong></a></strong><br />
		Expand students&rsquo; cultural horizons by shaping a fun dialogue-writing exercise around Guy Fawkes Night, a November 5 historical observance that&rsquo;s popular in England.</p>
	<p class="rteleft">
		<strong><a href="http://www.educationworld.com/a_lesson/harry_potter_learning_activity/design-a-wizard-sports-team.shtml">Harry Potter Inspires: Design a Wizard Sports Team</a></strong><br />
		In this lesson for grades K-2, students use creativity, reasoning and language skills to develop a new sports team.</p>
	<div class="views-field-field-description-meta-value rteleft">
		<strong><a href="http://www.educationworld.com/a_lesson/lesson_plan_booster/harry_potter/surprising_origins_of_famous_fiction.shtml">Lesson Plan Booster: Surprising Origins of Famous Fiction</a></strong></div>
	<div class="views-field-field-description-meta-value rteleft">
		Students draw inspiration from J.K. Rowling&#39;s<em> Harry Potter</em>, Mary Shelley&#39;s <em>Frankenstein</em> and Lewis Carroll&#39;s <em>Alice in Wonderland</em> in order to jump-start their own creative writing process.</div>
	<p class="rteleft">
		<strong><a href="http://www.educationworld.com/a_lesson/harry_potter_learning_activity/character_sketch_for_a_new_wizard.shtml">Harry Potter Inspires: Character Sketch for a New Wizard</a></strong><br />
		In this writing and literature lesson for grades 5-8, students create a new character that would fit into the wizard world.</p>
	<p class="rteleft">
		<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp065.shtml"><strong>Creating a Poetry Collection</strong></a><br />
		In this lesson, which encourages students to think about the sounds of poems, students use lip-syncing software to produce an animated talking head.</p>
</center>
<p>
	<a href="/a_admin/admin/admin507.shtml"><strong>Community Scavenger Hunt Teaches Research Skills, Much More</strong></a><br />
	When armies of students descend on local libraries, it has to be time for the State of Jefferson Scavenger Hunt. The three-day event challenges kids to track down answers to a series of questions. The results include improved research skills, priceless memories.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_lesson/dailylp/dailylp/dailylp042.shtml"><b>100 Years Ago in History </b></a><br />
	Start the new year by investigating events that happened 100 years ago this year. (Grades 5-12)</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp059.shtml"><b>From the Land, Of the Land: An Interdisciplinary Lesson on Indigenous Peoples</b></a><br />
	Energize a geography or language arts lesson with this online activity for grades 7-12. Students research the concept of indigenous people then write a diamante poem about what they&#39;ve learned. Both the research and the poem creation are done online.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp057.shtml"><b>Jet-Setting Pets</b></a><br />
	In this lesson, K-5 students select a pet and a travel destination, then find four things the pet could do at that locale. Designed for use with Inspiration/Kidspiration, the lesson can be adapted for use with other programs.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp055.shtml"><b>This Bird Can Blog!</b></a><br />
	Want to improve your students&#39; quality and quantity of writing? Check out this lesson plan for grades 3-5! Students assume the persona of a real or imagined classroom pet and write a blog describing daily activities in the classroom.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_lesson/dailylp/dailylp/dailylp068.shtml"><b>Daily Language Practice Builds Skills, Test Scores</b></a><br />
	Turn daily language practice into a game, build test scores too. (Grades 3-12)</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp054.shtml"><b>Scriptwriting with a Wiki</b></a><br />
	This middle- and high-school lesson plan uses a wiki -- a Web site that allows users to add and edit content collectively -- to write a one-act play.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp052.shtml"><b>Biography Brainstorm: Using Word and the Web to Jumpstart Research</b></a><br />
	Students use Internet resources and Microsoft Word drawing tools to brainstorm (through webbing) questions about a person they will research and write about.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp051.shtml"><b>A Picture&#39;s Worth 1000 Words</b></a><br />
	In this interdisciplinary lesson, K-8 students try to interpret what pictographs -- pictures that symbolize a word or concept -- really mean. Then they write sentences using their own pictograph system.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp048.shtml"><b>What&#39;s the Word?</b></a><br />
	In this activity for grades 3-8, students define 4-5 vocabulary words, use a free online tool to create crossword puzzles with the words, and then share their puzzles for review.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp047.shtml"><b>Portrait of a Hero</b></a><br />
	Students select and research someone they consider a hero and then use facts about that person and quotes by that person to create micrographic portraits.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/sites/sites072.shtml"><b>As Easy as A, B, C</b></a><br />
	Are your students learning their ABCs? Then check out these easy-to-use and fun Web sites filled with ideas for teaching and learning the alphabet. Whether you make the sites available in a computer center or display them on a projector, something valuable is just a click away!</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp044.shtml"><b>No Place Like Home</b></a><br />
	Students learn to &quot;read&quot; digital content as they analyze photographs of high plains sod homes and read accompanying narratives. They then choose one photograph and identify characteristics, points, differences, and questions they find in that photograph.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp042.shtml"><strong>A Lesson in Proofing</strong></a><br />
	Students in middle and high school learn to use Word&#39;s Find/Replace feature to check written work, increase accuracy, and improve their grades. Teachers might want to use the feature too.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp041.shtml"><strong>Teaching Writing on a Computer</strong></a><br />
	Students love seeing their work in print, so why not have them write some of their work on the computer? Walk students through the basics of typing and saving on a computer.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp040.shtml"><strong>Edit Essays with Word Tables</strong></a><br />
	Still grading essays, red ink pen in hand? Next time your students have an essay due, ask them to submit their work in Word, help them identify their grammar and spelling errors, and then have them correct those errors using Word&#39;s table feature.</p>
<p>
	<strong><a href="/a_lesson/lesson/lesson243.shtml">Fun Activities Get the School Year Off to a Good Start! </a></strong><br />
	Every teacher has a different approach to the first few days of school. Whatever your approach, Education World has an activity for you!</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_curr/reading/Literature/Literature001.shtml"><strong>Books Give Us Wings</strong></a><br />
	In this Letters About Literature lesson, students read and discuss letters written by young readers to the authors of books that &quot;gave them wings.&quot;</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp036.shtml"><strong>Will the Leaning Tower Fall?</strong></a><br />
	Students in grades 9-12 research the Tower of Pisa and write a report about its history. They examine the physics of why the Tower leans and whether it might fall. Finally, they plan a trip to Italy to see the Tower, developing an itinerary and budget for the trip.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp032.shtml"><strong>A Favorite Poem</strong></a><br />
	Students explore a variety of poems, find one poem they feel a particular connection to, and share that poem by reading it aloud to their classmates. Students then create videos of their readings to share with other classes.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp027.shtml"><strong>Create A Poetry Calendar</strong></a><br />
	In this lesson, students research, design, and create a school-year calendar based on the work of famous poets. The activity, which can take 4-5 class periods to complete, is a great culminating activity for a poetry unit for grades 3-12.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp025.shtml"><strong>Quilting Connections</strong></a><br />
	Help your K-8 class celebrate Women&#39;s History Month. Students research an influential woman, then create on the computer a quilt block with text and graphics. Quilt blocks are then printed and combined to form a quilt of connections.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp024.shtml"><strong>Definition Expedition</strong></a><br />
	Students in grades 9-12 learn three ways to define a term in technical writing, search the Web for scientific text, then copy and paste sections into a Word document. Finally, they use the highlighter feature of Word to highlight examples of definitions within the text. &nbsp;</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp023.shtml"><strong>Write a Number Story</strong></a><br />
	Make writing across content areas fun with this K-3 computer activity. Students use AppleWorks (or Office) to write and illustrate number stories. The stories then are used to create a Keynote or PowerPoint show and displayed for the class (or parents!) to see and share.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp022.shtml"><strong>Poetry From Photos: A Lesson on the Great Depression.</strong></a><br />
	Getting information from the Internet often is just a copy and paste operation. The challenge for teachers is to teach students to apply and extend what they learn online. In this lesson, students view photographs of migrant families during the Great Depression, try to interpret the photos to answer questions about the subject&#39;s life, and then write a cinquain poem based on their interpretations.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp018.shtml"><strong>PowerPoint Poetry Slam</strong></a><br />
	Make poetry come alive with this English lesson for grades 7-12. In a unique twist to a standard poetry reading, students select poems, create PowerPoint presentations that use graphics and text to enhance their poems, and then display those presentations as they read their poems aloud.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp017.shtml"><strong>Prehistoric Pen Pals</strong></a><br />
	Students research dinosaurs, then assume the personality of a specific dinosaur species in this lesson for grades 3-5. Each student/dinosaur writes an online &quot;getting to know you&quot; letter to another student/dinosaur in class. Students swap letters and reply to each other&#39;s questions, improving research and letter writing skills while learning fun dinosaur facts.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp016.shtml"><strong>The News Behind the (Short) Story</strong></a><br />
	Transform students from passive readers to gritty journalists. In this lesson, students read a short story, then create a one-page newspaper depicting the facts of the fictional story as real-life events. Included: Links to an online newspaper template as well as to a classic short story site are included.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_lesson/lesson/lesson344.shtml"><strong>Take the <i>Polar Express</i> to Learning</strong></a><br />
	<i>Polar Express,</i> the movie, arrives in movie theaters nationwide on November 10. Don&#39;t miss the movie <i>and </i>the opportunity to take advantage of this &quot;teachable moment.&quot; Included: Education World connects you to lessons, resources.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp008.shtml"><b>Illustrating Illustrating Student Writing in Grades 1-3</b></a><br />
	In this lesson, students type in Microsoft Word a descriptive paragraph about a person or other creature they would most like to meet. They then draw in Microsoft Paint a picture of that person and/or of their meeting, and insert the image into the Word document.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp005.shtml"><b>What&#39;s New? Translating Foreign Newspapers With Word</b></a><br />
	Engage students while practicing translation skills. This activity helps foreign language students decode newspaper articles from around the world using Microsoft Word&#39;s Text-to-Table tool.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_tech/techlp/techlp002.shtml"><b>How Does it End? A Lesson in Creativity</b></a><br />
	Put students&#39; creative talents to work by having them compose their own endings to a read-aloud story. Students use drawing software, such as Paint or AppleWorks, to draw, or write and draw, what they want to happen at the end of the story.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_lesson/lesson/lesson340.shtml"><b>Celebrating Asian and Pacific-Island Heritage</b></a><br />
	Each May -- during Asian Pacific American Heritage Month -- we recognize the special contributions of people of Asian and Pacific Islander heritage. The lessons here introduce students to famous Asian Americans and explore their origins and their literature.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_lesson/lesson/lesson336.shtml"><b>A Puzzle A Day Provides Practice That Pays</b></a><br />
	Puzzles exercise students&#39; critical thinking skills while providing practice in many curriculum areas. Puzzles make great &quot;bellringer&quot; activities. Introduce a puzzle a day: A puzzle a day provides practice that pays Included: A year of puzzles!</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_lesson/lesson/lesson334.shtml"><b>Teaching Grammar Without the Hammer: Five Fun Activities</b></a><br />
	Learning grammar has been compared to other fun things -- like having teeth pulled or being assigned detention. But it needn&#39;t be a painful experience with these five lessons that teach grammar -- without the hammer!</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_lesson/lesson/lesson328.shtml"><b>Word Wall &quot;Active-ities&quot; Build Vocabulary, Spelling, Writing Skills</b></a><br />
	A classroom word wall has many uses. A wide variety of activities and games can be used to reinforce vocabulary words on the wall -- and to build students&#39; vocabulary, spelling, and writing skills. Included: Teachers share favorite word wall activities.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_lesson/lesson/lesson326.shtml"><b>Celebrate Books: A (Book) Week of Fun!</b></a><br />
	The calendar might identify November 17-23 as Children&#39;s Book Week, but for most teachers -- always on the lookout for new ideas to promote literacy -- <i>every </i>week is Book Week. Education World offers five new lessons for a week of reading fun.</p>
<p>
	<a href="/a_lesson/lesson/lesson322.shtml"><b>Take Note: Five Lessons for Note Taking Fun</b></a><br />
	If recent surveys are any indicator, cheating and plagiarism are on the rise. As teachers, however, we might be able to reverse that trend by teaching our students to take good notes. Included: Five fun lessons that teach needed note-taking skills.</p>

How do you help your students overcome their fear of the blank page? How can you make writing an exercise in personal expression, not drudgery?
