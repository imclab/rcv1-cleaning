				<h2 class="entry-title">Vintage Parts for Your Classic Automobile</h2>
				<div class="entry-content">
<p><a href="http://www.precisionautoaccessories.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/corvette1961chevrolet.jpg"><img class="alignlcenter size-medium wp-image-8" title="corvette1961chevrolet" src="http://www.precisionautoaccessories.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/corvette1961chevrolet-300x192.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="192" /></a><br />
<strong>Are you of hunting for vintage parts for your classic automobile? </strong></p>
<p>Old car parts can be expensive and hard to find.  Just where do you look</p>
<p>for these parts which are often unavailabe at your local auto parts store?</p>
<p><strong>Here are ten places to begin your search:</strong></p>
<p>1. The first place you can look is in your local junkyard.</p>
<p>2. There are online stores that specialize in vintage car parts  and accessories.</p>
<p>3. Visit local classic car shows and get to know other owners who may be able to direct you to sources for you to explore.</p>
<p>4. Contact vendors at local classic car autions, or flea markets.</p>
<p>5. Join your local vintage car club where there are others who share your interests.</p>
<p>6. You can find classified ads in Sports car trade magazines.</p>
<p>7. And don&#8217;t forget eBay where often you can find just what you are looking for.</p>
<p>8. Attend local car races for possible leads.</p>
<p>9. Possibly you could start an online group of people with common interests who will share their experiences and knowledge.</p>
<p>10. Place ads in your local newspaper, shopper’s guides, Craigslist, bulletin boards, etc.</p>
<p><strong>After-market options and upgrades</strong> have been around for a long time. There are bolt on accessories to further individualize your car. Tuning accessories such as Twin carburetors, fancy manifolds, wild cams and improved exhaust systems are popular and readily available to juice up your car. There are companies that can provide you with all kinds of  upgraded parts and extras to really make your once-dull car fly.</p>
<p>Custom Grilles, Spoilers, Alloy wheels, Chrome Trim, mats, mudflaps, LED Taillights, fog lights and spot lights are easy additions. Plain ordinary looking steering wheels can be replaced with cool alloy wheels or wheel covers.</p>
<p>You can rebody your humble looking Ford with an exotic looking plastic shell. There is no end to the things you can do to make your vintage car special,  whether you want to restore it to it&#8217;s original condition or if you want to make it your own creation.</p>
<p>The parts you need or want may be expensive, or hard to find but if you keep looking, you will find what you need at prices you can afford.</p>
<p>There are  a few auto parts stores out there that specialize in the sales of Vintage car parts and the tips offered above will help you narrow your search.</p>
