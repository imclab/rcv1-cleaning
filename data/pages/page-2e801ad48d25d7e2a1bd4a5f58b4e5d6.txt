They face the rear of the car and carry babies up to 22 or even 35 pounds and 29 to 32 inches tall.</p><p>These seats usually have a handle for easy carrying. They can snap in and out of a base you install in the car, and in and out of your stroller.</p><p><strong>Read our complete article on <a href="/0_how-to-buy-an-infant-car-seat_5754.bc">buying an infant car seat</a>.</strong></p><div class="superImagePromoNew small floatRight standard"> <div class="postIt"> <div class="moduleContent"> <h2 class="moduleHeader"><a href="/babyCostCalculator.htm" shape="rect"> Baby Costs Calculator </a></h2> <a href="/babyCostCalculator.htm" shape="rect"><img src="http://assets.babycenter.com/ims/2010/09sep/stk2010_09_iStock_000008657620Medium_424x302.jpg?width=206" alt=""/></a> <div class="caption"><div class="captionInner"> <p>Calculate the cost of your <a href="/babyCostCalculator.htm" shape="rect">baby's first year</a>, including gear, diapers, childcare, and more.</p> </div></div> </div> </div> </div><a name="articlesection2"></a><h3>Convertible car seats</h3><p>Convertible car seats are larger and heavier than infant-only car seats. (Note: Some "3-in-1" car seats are not for babies.
