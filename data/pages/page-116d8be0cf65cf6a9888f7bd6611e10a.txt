<h5 align="center">&quot;Where Art Meets Community!&quot;</h5>

 <div>
<form name="form1" method="post" action="">
</form>
<p><FORM   METHOD=post ACTION="http://events.irm-systems.com/altuscoc/events2.nsf/main?OpenForm&Seq=1" NAME="_Home"> 
<div align="center">
<p align="left">When it&#8217;s time to get away from the hustle and bustle 
    of life in order to relax and enjoy yourself, Tuscaloosa County offers many 
    outlets&#8212;in the performing arts, museums, outdoor events, and sporting 
    events. </p>
  <p align="left"><a name=arts></a><img src="/images/jpg/performingartsheader.jpg" width="600" height="100"></p>
  <p align="left">Tuscaloosa County offers a feast for the eyes and ears, as the 
    arts are celebrated daily on stage, in the theatre and throughout the community. 
    Whatever the season or the specialty, the Tuscaloosa area community offers 
    an extraordinary year-round calendar of arts and cultural activities that 
    rank among the best in the South. Our cultural offerings are unsurpassed by 
    even some larger metropolitan areas; as a result the community&#8217;s cultural 
    assets are interwoven with our economic progress and continue to enrich our 
    exceptional quality of life.</p>
  <h1 align="center">TUSCALOOSA AMPHITHEATRE</h1>
  <p align="left">The City of Tuscaloosa&#8217;s outdoor Amphitheatre, situated 
    along the Black Warrior River, features a stage of approximately 6,000 square 
    feet and 7,500 seats and features state of the art sound, visual and electronic 
    infrastructure, and amenities. It hosts many concerts and events plus serves 
    as a platform for the cultural arts in the area.</p>
  <p></p>
</div>
<h1 align="center" class="contentart">ARTS COUNCIL OF TUSCALOOSA COUNTY</h1>
<p align="justify"><img src="/images/jpg/artscologo.jpg" width="151" height="192" hspace="5" align="left">The 
  <font color="#003399"><a href="http://www.tuscarts.org/" target="_blank">Arts 
  Council</a></font> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"> 
  and its member organizations provide a coordinated year-round calendar of events. 
  Theatre, dance, opera, visual arts, music, crafts, festivals, films and educational 
  programs......these and more provide Tuscaloosa County with exceptional cultural 
  opportunities. The Arts Council's Bluegrass, Big Bands &amp; More performing 
  arts series brings the best of bluegrass, jazz, blues, and big band music to 
  the Tuscaloosa community. The Cinema Noveau Movie Series provides the best in 
  art, foreign and independent films.</p>
<p align="justify">The <font color="#003399"><a href="http://www.tuscarts.org/" target="_blank">Arts 
  Council</a></font> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"> 
  also provides educational programs for children throughout the year. The community 
  actively encourages the involvement of children in theatre, music, dance and 
  art through programs, such as: Bama Fanfare; the SPECTRA program, which provides 
  guest artists in school cultural enrichment; Art 4 All; and the Artist-in-Residence 
  program in the City Schools. Call (205) 758-5195 for more information.</p>
<p align="justify">&nbsp;</p>
<p align="center"><a href="#top"><img src="/images/pageparts/back-to-top.gif" width="76" height="20" border="0"></a><br>
</p>
<h1 align="center"><img src="/images/jpg/bamatheatrePorfirio.jpg" width="250" height="167" hspace="5" align="right"><a href="http://www.tuscarts.org/bama%20theatre.html" target="_blank">BAMA 
  THEATRE</a> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></h1>
<p align="justify">The beautiful, restored 1930s-style Bama Theatre is a performing 
  arts center in Downtown Tuscaloosa, offering a full calendar of music, dance, 
  theatre, stage productions, and film. The Bama is home to the Tuscaloosa Children&#8217;s 
  Theatre, Tuscaloosa Community Dancers, Junior League Gallery of Art, and other 
  community-based cultural activities.</p>
<h1 align="center"><a href="http://www.theatretusc.com/" target="_blank">BEAN-BROWN THEATRE</a> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></h1>
<p align="justify">A state-of-the-art, teaching and performing theatre, the Bean-Brown 
  Theatre is located on the campus of Shelton State Community College and is home 
  to Theatre Tuscaloosa, one of the region&#8217;s most acclaimed community theatres.</p>
<h2 align="center"><img src="/images/jpg/artspicture.jpg" width="250" height="700" hspace="5" border="0" align="left"><a href="http://www.music.ua.edu/" target="_blank">THE 
  UNIVERSITY OF ALABAMA SCHOOL OF MUSIC</a><font color="#003399" size="4" face="Comic Sans MS"> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></font></h2>
<P align="justify">Opened in 1988 on the campus of The University of Alabama, 
  the Frank Moody Music Building is one of Tuscaloosa's primary cultural performing 
  arts centers. The School provides an extraordinary annual calendar of cultural 
  and entertainment experiences, featuring national and international performers 
  and artists, from classical and opera to jazz and popular.</p>
<p>With its 1,000-seat Concert Hall, considered one of the most acoustically perfect 
  facilities of its kind in the nation, the Moody Music Building is home to the 
  Tuscaloosa Symphony; the Celebrity Series; and to the famous, magnificent Holtkamp 
  Organ. Call (205) 348-1477 for information.</p>

<h2 align="center"><a href="http://www.music.ua.edu/cms/" target="_blank">COMMUNITY 
  MUSIC SCHOOL</a><font color="#003399" size="4" face="Comic Sans MS"> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></font></h2>
<p align="justify">Offers private and group music instruction to people of all 
  ages and abilities including lessons on all orchestral and band instruments, 
  piano, guitar, and voice. Early music education classes (Kindermusik) are available 
  for ages infant through 7 years. Housed in the Moody Music Building at The University 
  of Alabama. Call (205) 348-6741 for information.</p>
<h2 align="center"><a href="http://www.tsoonline.org/" target="_blank">TUSCALOOSA 
  SYMPHONY</a> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></h2>
<p align="justify">Formed in 1979, the Tuscaloosa Symphony has become a vital 
  part of the Tuscaloosa arts and cultural fabric and one of the most successful 
  regional symphonies in the Southeast. Very few communities of comparable size 
  or larger can claim its own symphony of such exceptional quality. The Symphony 
  provides an annual season of concerts in addition to performances in our schools, 
  pops concerts, and various other entertainment venues. Call (205) 752-5515 for 
  ticket information. </p>
<h2 align="center"><a href="http://www.theatretusc.com" target="_blank">THEATRE 
  TUSCALOOSA</a> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></h2>
<P align="justify">With its roots in the 1920s, Theatre Tuscaloosa has grown into 
  Alabama&#8217;s top community theatre and one of the region&#8217;s best. Offering 
  a full calendar of drama, comedy and musicals each year, Theatre Tuscaloosa 
  provides area citizens with exceptional performances at the Brown-Bean Theatre, 
  located on the campus of Shelton State Community College. Call (205) 391-2277 
  for ticket information.</p>
<h2 align="center"><a href="http://www.kentuck.org/" target="_blank">KENTUCK ARTS 
  CENTER</a><font color="#003399" size="4" face="Comic Sans MS"> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></font></h2>
<p align="justify">Kentuck is an extensive, growing network that includes the 
  Kentuck Art Center, Kentuck Museum, and the Kentuck Festival of Arts, which 
  has grown into one of the largest and most important regional arts and craft 
  shows in the Southeast. The Kentuck Arts Center, located in downtown Northport, 
  is home to some of the country's most exceptional artists and craftsmen. The 
  Kentuck Museum, also in downtown Northport, provides regular traveling and invitational 
  arts and crafts exhibits. A focal point for the Alabama Crafts Council, Kentuck 
  maintains a superb year round collection of Alabama arts and crafts. Call (205) 
  758-1257 for information.</p>
<p align="center"><a href="#top"><img src="/images/pageparts/back-to-top.gif" width="76" height="20" border="0"></a></p>
<h2 align="center"><a href="http://www.as.ua.edu/theatre/" target="_blank">UNIVERSITY 
  OF ALABAMA'S DEPARTMENT OF THEATRE AND DANCE</a><font color="#003399" size="4" face="Comic Sans MS"> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></font></h2>
<p align="justify"> University of Alabama Theatre presents excellent performances 
  regularly at the Gallaway Theatre as well as plays in the Allen Bales Studio 
  Theatre. The University&#8217;s Department of Dance offers unique performances 
  throughout the year, including Dance Alabama. For ticket information call (205) 
  348-3400 or <a href="http://www.as.ua.edu/theatre">www.as.ua.edu/theatre</a>.</p>
<h2 align="center"><a href="http://www.theatretusc.com/hof/hof.shtml" target="_blank">STAGE 
  AND SCREEN HALL OF FAME</a><font color="#003399" size="4" face="Comic Sans MS"> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></font></h2>
<p align="justify"> <img src="/images/jpg/alabamascreen.jpg" alt="Alabama Stage and Screen Hall of Fame" width="195" height="91" hspace="7" align="right">Located 
  on the campus of Shelton State Community College, the Alabama Stage and Screen 
  Hall of Fame salutes the state&#8217;s amazing collection of celebrity talent, 
  from Fannie Flagg to Jim Nabors and beyond..... and inspires new generations 
  of emerging actors to follow their dreams and opportunities.<br>
</p>
<h2 align="center">HORIZONS PROGRAM</h2>
<p align="justify">Offering popular entertainment throughout the year, The University 
  of Alabama's Horizon Program provides opportunities to enjoy Broadway musicals, 
  classical and popular music and a variety of other entertainment specials.</p>
<h2 align="center"><a href="http://www.alboychoir.org/" target="_blank">ALABAMA 
  CHOIR SCHOOL</a><img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></h2>
<p align="justify"><img src="/images/jpg/SpringConcert.jpg" width="250" height="188" hspace="5" align="left">The 
  Alabama Choir School is a community organization for boys and girls, grades 
  two and above. The school includes the Alabama Boychoir, founded in 1985; the 
  Alabama Girls Chorus, founded in 1996; and the Alabama Childrens Chorus, founded 
  in 1992. The School has five different choirs -- the resident and touring choir 
  for boys grades 4-8; the resident and touring choir for girls grades 4 and up, 
  and the chorus for children grades 2-3. Annual concerts for all choirs take 
  place in December and May at the Moody Concert Hall on the University of Alabama 
  campus, Tuscaloosa. Concerts are also scheduled in various local schools twice 
  yearly and on other occasions as requested. For ticket information call (205) 
  758-0927. </p>
  <h2><center><a href="/tuscaloosa/onlineart.php">Morrow Conference Center Art Gallery</a></center></h2>
  The Chamber of Commerce of West Alabama is proud to host artwork from artists around the community and display it in the Morrow Conference Center.  Many of these artists have their works on display in the Tuscaloosa Sketchbook.  Follow this link to see art on display: <a href="/tuscaloosa/onlineart.php">Art Gallery</a>
<p align="center"><a href="#top"><font color="#990000"><img src="/images/pageparts/back-to-top.gif" width="76" height="20" border="0"></font></a></p>
<a name=mus></a>
<img src="/images/jpg/museumsheader.jpg" width="600" height="100">
<p align="justify">Tuscaloosa County surprises visitors with our abundance and 
  variety of museums, galleries and other visitor attractions. Among some of our 
  unique offerings are:</p>
<h2 align="center"><img src="/images/jpg/CHOMart.jpg" width="200" height="305" hspace="5" align="left"><a href="http://amnh.ua.edu/" target="_blank">Alabama 
  Museum of Natural History</a> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></h2>
<p align="justify"> Alabama Museum of Natural History, featuring exhibits from 
  the Age of Dinosaurs and the Ice and Coal Ages. An Alabama treasure! Call (205) 
  348-2041. </p>
<h2 align="center"><a href="http://bryantmuseum.ua.edu/" target="_blank">Bryant 
  Museum</a> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></h2>
<p align="justify">Bryant Museum, featuring memorabilia and exhibits of Alabama 
  Crimson Tide football since 1892 and other related sports exhibits. Call (205) 
  348-4668. </p>
<h2 align="center"><a href="http://www.chomonline.org/" target="_blank">Children&#8217;s 
  Hands On Museum</a> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></h2>
<p align="justify">Children&#8217;s Hands On Museum, provides a delightful experience 
  for children of all ages with a variety of special programs and hands-on, participatory 
  exhibits. Moms and dads enjoy this, too! Call (205) 349-4235 for more information.</p>

<h2 align="center"><img src="/images/jpg/HARRISON%20GALLERY.jpg" hspace="5" align="right"> 
  <a href="http://www.harrisongalleriesllc.com" target="_blank">Harrison Galleries</a> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10">
</h2>
Harrison Galleries features paintings, mostly in American or European Romantic 
and Hudson River School traditions, and photography by prominent Tuscaloosa area 
photographers. Located in historic Tuscaloosa at 2315 University Blvd. Call (205) 
464-0054 for information. <br>
<h2 align="center"><a href="http://www.kentuck.org/" target="_blank">Kentuck Museum</a> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></h2>
<p align="justify">Kentuck Museum, features a gallery of exhibits of some of the 
  region&#8217;s finest crafts and art works provided by the South&#8217;s finest 
  artisans and craftsmen. Kentuck Museum is located in Downtown Northport, selected 
  as one of the &#8220;Top 100 Small Art Communities&#8221; in America. Call (205) 
  758-1257. </p>
<h2 align="center">
  
  <img src="/images/jpg/mervisitors.jpg" width="200" height="205" hspace="5" border="0" align="left"> 
  <a href="http://www.mbusi.com/pages/vc_home.asp" target="_blank">Mercedes-Benz Museum Visitors Center</a>  <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"> </h2>
<p>Mercedes-Benz Museum &amp; Visitors Center, one of Alabama&#8217;s top tourist 
  attractions, provides visitors with an up-close view of the star-studded history 
  of Daimler-Benz, the world&#8217;s premier automobile manufacturer. The story 
  is chronicled in exciting, interactive exhibits at the Visitor&#8217;s Center, 
  the first of its kind outside Germany; and explores the technological innovations 
  and development of Mercedes-Benz. </p>


<h2 align="center"><a href="http://www.renaissanceartgallery.com/" target="_blank">Renaissance 
  Art Gallery</a> <img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></h2>
<p align="justify">Located in historic downtown Northport, the Renaissance Gallery 
  offers original art by more than twenty regional and local artists. Work on 
  display includes some of the best traditional, folk and contemporary work available 
  in the Southeast. Each painting or sculpture is a one of a kind, hand&#8211;made 
  original, signed by the artist. Call (205) 752-4422.<br>
</p>
<h2 align="center"><img src="/images/jpg/warner.jpg"  hspace="5" align="right"><a href="http://www.warnermuseum.org/" target="_blank">Westervelt-Warner 
  Museum of American Art </a><img src="/images/jpg/icon-newwindow.jpg" width="14" height="10"></h2>
<p align="left"> Located at the beautiful Westervelt Corporate Headquarters, the 
  museum features works from several prominent American artists. The impressive 
  collection of paintings, sculptures, and antiques provides a unique perspective 
  on our country's history. For more information call (205) 343-4540. </p>
