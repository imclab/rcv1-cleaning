							<p>  	So much for <a href="http://www.themotorreport.com.au/tag/bmw-i4">the so-called i4</a>: BMW has revealed a new &#39;coupe&#39; version of the upcoming<a href="http://www.themotorreport.com.au/tag/bmw-i3"> i3 electric vehicle</a>, ahead of its unveiling at this week&rsquo;s <a href="http://www.themotorreport.com.au/tag/la-auto-show">Los Angeles Auto Show.</a></p>  <p>  	Aside from losing two doors, the i3 Concept Coupe features several detail changes compared with previous iterations.    </p>  <p>  	The Coupe picks up a more production- and safety-friendly side treatment, ditching the earlier glass-heavy design for a more conventional beltline.</p>  <div class="inarticle-ad" style="text-align: center;">		<div id="ad-521d2a2080159" style="height: 250px;">
			<noscript><a href="http://ad.au.doubleclick.net/jump/tmr.news/details;car=bmw;sz=300x250;tile=3;ord=15433667?" target="_blank"><img src="http://ad.au.doubleclick.net/ad/tmr.news/details;car=bmw;sz=300x250;tile=3;ord=15433667?" border="0" alt=""/></a></noscript>
		</div>
</div><p>  	A closer-to-production interior design is also revealed, featuring more realistic controls throughout, although the center stack differs notably to that revealed in <a href="http://www.themotorreport.com.au/tag/bmw-i3+spy-photos">recent spy photos.</a></p>  <p>  	The rear valance has also been reshaped, with production-necessary reflectors now integrated.</p>  <p>  	
	<div class="inline-image-left" style="float: left; width: 200px">
		<a href="/content/image/b/m/bmw_i3_concept_coupe_14-1128.jpg" class="lightbox" title=""><img src="/content/image/b/m/bmw_i3_concept_coupe_14-1128-200x200.jpg" alt="bmw i3 concept coupe 14" class="small"/></a>
			</div>
</p>  <p>  	Built on the same 2570mm wheelbase as the earlier five-door concepts, the Concept Coupe maintains the five-door&rsquo;s 2+2 seating configuration.</p>  <p>  	The Concept Coupe&rsquo;s drivetrain also matches the earlier concepts, with a rear-mounted 125kW/250Nm electric motor driving the rear wheels through a single-speed transmission, and powered by a lithium-ion battery cell.</p>  <p>  	BMW&rsquo;s press material describes the Concept Coupe as &quot;demonstrating the potential for conceivably extending the model range&quot;, suggesting that the three-door bodystyle may indeed reach production alongside the five-door.</p>  <p>  	The production i3 is expected to appear as a five-door first though, debuting some time in 2013.</p>  <ul class="list-style-more"><li><h4>Related News &amp; Reviews at TMR <span>&#9660;</span></h4></li>  	<li>  		<a href="http://www.themotorreport.com.au/tag/bmw i">BMW i cars</a> | <a href="http://www.themotorreport.com.au/tag/bmw">BMW </a>| <a href="http://www.themotorreport.com.au/tag/green">Green Cars</a> | <a href="http://www.themotorreport.com.au/tag/phev">Plug-in Hybrids</a></li>  	<li>  		<a href="http://www.themotorreport.com.au/tag/la-auto-show">LA Auto Show</a></li>  </ul>  <p>
