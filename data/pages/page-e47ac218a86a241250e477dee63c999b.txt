		<h1><a href="http://nuvein.org/2013/08/23/september-la-puente-artwalk-vendor-spots-available/" rel="bookmark">September La Puente Artwalk Vendor Spots available!</a></h1>
	
	<p class="date"><b>Posted on </b>| August 23, 2013 | <a href="http://nuvein.org/2013/08/23/september-la-puente-artwalk-vendor-spots-available/#respond" title="Comment on September La Puente Artwalk Vendor Spots available!">No Comments</a></p>
	
  
	<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="alignnone" alt="" src="https://fbcdn-sphotos-h-a.akamaihd.net/hphotos-ak-ash3/941264_406030159514160_167473021_n.jpg" width="244" height="244" /></p>
<p>The Nuvein Foundation will be organizing an outdoor Vendor and Artisan Bazaar at the La Puente Arcade Lot located at approximately 15903 Main Street, La Puente, CA 91744.</p>
<p><a href="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/LParcade.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-554" alt="LParcade" src="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/LParcade.jpg" width="428" height="179" /></a></p>
<p>Vendors will be placed and organized at the La Puente Arcade Lot by Nuvein Board members, Estella Neyer and Petra Nieto.</p>
<p>The Nuvein Foundation will be processing day of applications for booth spaces for the La Puente Artwalk <a href="https://www.facebook.com/events/590425840979612/">event</a> at the Arcade from 1pm-4pm. Applications for the 4th Annual Dia de Los Muertos &#8211; El Monte <a href="https://www.facebook.com/events/548616308484191/?ref=22">event</a> will also be processed on this dafe by Nuvein.</p>
<p>You can d0wnload the La Puente Artwalk vendor application <a href="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/UPMA-LPArtwalkVendorApplication.pdf">here</a>.</p>
<p>You can download the 2013 Dia de Los Muertos &#8211; El Monte vendor application <a href="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/DDLM2013VendorBoothApplication.pdf">here</a>.</p>
<p>For more details on the La Puente Artwalk, please follow the event page on <a href="https://www.facebook.com/LaPuenteArtwalk">Facebook</a>.</p>
<p>We hope that our artistic, creative, and inspiring artisans and craftworks can join us for the final 2013 La Puente Artwalk.</p>

		<h1><a href="http://nuvein.org/2013/01/21/the-sgv-arts-directory/" rel="bookmark">The SGV Arts Directory</a></h1>
	
	<p class="date"><b>Posted on </b>| January 21, 2013 | <a href="http://nuvein.org/2013/01/21/the-sgv-arts-directory/#respond" title="Comment on The SGV Arts Directory">No Comments</a></p>
	
  
	<p><em><strong>Nuvein dedicates itself to &#8216;voices rarely heard in a world increasingly devoid of diversity&#8217;.</strong></em><strong> &#8211; Enrique Diaz</strong></p>
<p>The San Gabriel Valley communities of Southern California are a shining example of diverse cultures and societies thriving side by side, where an ongoing process of cultural, ideological, and societal exchange is constant at every level from the evolving collegiate haunts of Claremont, Azusa, Pasadena, and Walnut to the changing demographics of assemblies and local governments such as those of Alhambra, Rosemead, El Monte, Baldwin Park, and evident in the flow and changes of businesses at the Valley Mall or Downtown El Monte, Old Town Monrovia, Uptown Whittier, and the many facets of the swapmeets, farmer&#8217;s markets, laundromats, and strip malls north and south of Valley Blvd.</p>
<p>But one thing is certain to connect these many communities and cultures, and that is art. Simply put, the need to create something of value to oneself, for another, or for others is a shared experience. This universal impulse has led societies to many great discoveries and has brought about change.</p>
<p>Nuvein&#8217;s founder, Enrique, believed in the unity of individuals that expressed itself in a variety of ways, so much so that Nuvein began its work as an online magazine publishing works of fiction, commentary, poetry, graphic and media art, as well as reviews that brought together individuals all over the globe in each volume of publication.Â  Following a successful nearly decade long run, the drive to affect change locally in the San Gabriel Valley emerged, which then gave rise to the Nuvein Foundation incorporating in El Monte.</p>
<p><strong>So, can you help us bring all artistic groups, organizations, and resources together in an Online Directory?</strong></p>
<p>We hope we can count on your help to make this happen.</p>
<p>This Directory will be for aspiring and emerging artists to get a hold of any chances for further education/training, securing encouragement and assistance, and finding opportunities to showcase their work.</p>

		<h1><a href="http://nuvein.org/2012/10/09/2012-dia-de-los-muertos-performance-schedule/" rel="bookmark">2012 Dia de los Muertos Performance Schedule</a></h1>
	
	<p class="date"><b>Posted on </b>| October 9, 2012 | <a href="http://nuvein.org/2012/10/09/2012-dia-de-los-muertos-performance-schedule/#respond" title="Comment on 2012 Dia de los Muertos Performance Schedule">No Comments</a></p>
	
  
	<p>Every year, the Nuvein Foundation works with the Downtown El Monte Business Association to create an event that highlights the traditions, culture, and artistic vision that defines the celebration of Dia de Los Muertos/Day of the Dead at the Valley Mall in Downtown El Monte, CA.</p>
<p>As part of the Centennial celebration of the incorporation of the city of El Monte, this event has secured acts that are deeply rooted in the Mexican, Chicano, and Latino traditions surrounding Dia de Los Muertos and are representative of the various regions of Mexico and Latin America that commemorate this event.</p>
<p>The 2012 Dia de Los Muertos organized by the Nuvein Foundation and sponsored by DEMBA is proud to present this years performers:</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/kimpalooza.jpg"><img class="alignleft  wp-image-459" title="kimpalooza" src="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/kimpalooza.jpg" alt="" width="337" height="261" /></a>Event Master of Ceremonies and Host:<br />
Kimberly CobianÂ  (11am-6pm)<br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong></strong>Kimberly Cobian is multi-cultural and multi-talented artist hailing from North Whittier, CA. She is a bilingual poet &amp; writer, belly dance instructor &amp; performer, consummate performance artist, and event organizer.</p>
<p>KimberlyÂ  is the president of the <a href="http://www.facebook.com/ZZyZxWriterZ">ZzyZx WriterZ</a> Group out of El Serreno, CA.Â  Her groupÂ  coordinated and produced the <a href="http://poetrypalooza-la.weebly.com/">Poetrypalooza Tour</a> in August of 2012 to rave reviews and record attendances in venues all over LA County.</p>
<p>She also serves on the Executive Board of the <a href="www.facebook.com/nuvein">Nuvein Foundation for Literature and the Arts</a>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/chuy.jpg"><img class="alignleft  wp-image-456" title="chuy" src="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/chuy.jpg" alt="" width="333" height="291" /></a><strong>Event DJ and Sound Systems Coordinator<br />
DJ Quinto SolÂ  &#8211; Jesus MontoyaÂ  (11am-6pm)<br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong></strong>Jesus Montoya (DJ Quinto Sol) is a premier entertainment and event producer hailing from Dana Point, CA. Jesus&#8217; company, <a href="http://www.djquintosol.com/index.html">DJ Quinto Sol Entertainment</a>, has been organizing and entertaining at events since 1995 all over CA and across the Continental United States.Â  DJ Quinto Sol is a member of the Orange County &#8211; Southern California chapter of the <a href="http://orangecoca.adja.org/">American Disc Jockey Association</a>.</p>
<p>A proud and devoted father, avid entertainer, dedicated philantro-activist, DJ Quinto Sol will have everyone in the crowd energized and entertained during any event and setting. He is bound to &#8216;echarle sabor&#8217; to any party/event and make even the most reluctant get up and dance.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/bpcuah.jpg"><img class="alignleft  wp-image-457" title="OLYMPUS DIGITAL CAMERA" src="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/bpcuah-1024x602.jpg" alt="" width="333" height="214" /></a><strong>Opening Performance (12pm-1245pm)<br />
Danza Cuauhtemoc Baldwin Park</strong></p>
<p><a href="http://www.danzacuauhtemocbp.org/Index.html">Danza Cuauhtemoc</a> is a Aztec Danza group hailing from Baldwin Park. CA under the guidance and instruction of Chris Silva. They offer community classes in Aztec Danza for children, youth, and adults of all ages.</p>
<p>Danza Cuauhtemoc is committed to preserving the traditions of Aztec/Mexica culture. Danza Cuauhtemoc has performed at Dia de Los Muertos events all over LA/SGV. They have performed at all <a href="http://nuvein.org/dia-de-los-muertos/">Nuvein Dia de Los Muertos</a>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/solecitos.jpg"><img class="alignleft  wp-image-458" title="solecitos" src="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/solecitos.jpg" alt="" width="334" height="248" /></a><strong>Second Performance (1:15pm-2:00pm)</strong><br />
<strong> Mariachi Solecitos de Jose Hernandez</strong></p>
<p>Mariachi Solecitos is a premier all adolescent and youth age mariachi group under the instruction of Jose Hernandez, director of the <a href="http://www.soldemexicoonline.com/">Mariachi Sol de Mexico</a>, proprietor of <a href="http://www.elcielitolindo.com/aboutus.html">Cielito Lindo Restaurant</a>, and head of the <a href="http://www.mariachiheritagesociety.com/">Mariachi Heritage Society</a>.</p>
<p>The Solecitos, like their acclaimed counterparts, have performed at venues, festivals, and concerts all across the country. The Solecitos mariachi group are instructed in the traditions and discipline of mariachi by the school run by director Hernandez and the Mariachi Heritage Society. Their performance is sure to amaze and delight one and all.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/eugeniopoesia.jpg"><img class="alignleft  wp-image-460" title="eugeniopoesia" src="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/eugeniopoesia.jpg" alt="" width="335" height="253" /></a><strong>Third Performance (2:30pm-3:15pm)</strong><br />
<strong> Eugenio Torres &amp; Grupo P.O.E.S.I.A.</strong></p>
<p>Eugenio &#8220;Genio&#8221; Torres is a poet, singer-s0ngwriter with Mexican and Latin American roots that influence his compositions. His works speak of social justice, faith, love, loss, and the conflicts that tradition and progress create.</p>
<p>Eugenio and fellow Latino and Mexican-American poet(a)s, singers, and performers have formed Poetas Organizados en un Espacio Social, Intelectual, y Artistico (<a href="http://www.firstpost.com/topic/place/hollywood-desdeaquitvcom-12-poetas-y-una-voz-poesia-video-Kcusr6T41IY-113-1.html">P.O.E.S.I.A.</a>) a group that has toured all over California, parts of Mexico, and across the US, organizing great events for the Hispanic communities and its poetic voices.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/bbgarcia.jpg"><img class="alignleft  wp-image-462" title="bbgarcia" src="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/bbgarcia.jpg" alt="" width="333" height="254" /></a>Fourth Performance (3:45pm-4:30pm)<br />
Bernard Garcia and the Cali-Caribe Cuartet</strong></p>
<p><strong></strong>Bernard Garcia, also known as B.b. Garcia and his group of musicians fuse afro-cuban, tropical, and California folk into Mexican-Carribean stylings that have delighted crowds all across Los Angeles, as well as the <a href="http://www.facebook.com/LaPuenteArtwalk?ref=ts&amp;fref=ts">La Puente Artwalk</a>.</p>
<p>Bernard Garcia and the Cali-Caribe Cuartet have performed at venues such as the Yost Theatre at their <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kiaff5Dhxlc&amp;feature=share">&#8220;Noche de Boleros&#8221;</a> event, and at various cultural events.Â  This year they will be touring <a href="http://nuvein.org/2012/09/09/dia-de-los-muertos-events-take-root-in-the-san-gabriel-valley/">Day of the Dead events across the San Gabriel Valley</a>, specifically, in Uptown Whittier, Rose Hills, El Monte, and Alhambra. Their music will delight you in a joyful nostalgia.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/bfcolores.jpg"><img class="alignleft  wp-image-463" title="bfcolores" src="http://nuvein.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/bfcolores.jpg" alt="" width="332" height="255" /></a>Closing Performance (5:00pm-5:45pm)<br />
Ballet Folklorico de Colores de Studio Danza</strong></p>
<p>Located in Whittier under the direction of Luisa Macias since 2007, the various performance teams have participated and been recognized at competitions all across the United States.</p>
<p>Having expanded to include the Ballet Folklorico de Colores, <a href="http://studiodanzausa.com/">Studio Danza</a> has only served to offer youth, children, and adolescent dancers with opportunities to grow in this traditional Mexican dance form. The Ballet Folklorico de Colores has performed at events all across California at cultural and community events in Whittier, El Monte, Pico Rivera, Long Beach, and other communities.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>We look forward to delighting our event crowd with these and other offerings of art, culture, music, poesia, and entertainment. </strong></p>
