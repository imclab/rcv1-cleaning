<p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><font color="#000000">Dear Reader,</font></span></p>

<p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><font color="#000000">You can make good money buying and selling used
cars in small towns, big cities...during roaring economies...and deep recessions...and you can do this anywhere in
the free world.</font></span></p>

<p align="left"><font color="#000000"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">The really cool thing is that the demand for
used cars is ever-green, which makes the business of buying and selling used cars for profit one of the
few</span>&nbsp;<u><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">true</span></u><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">&nbsp;recession proof
businesses.</span></font></p>

<p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><font color="#000000">How is this possible?</font></span></p>

<p align="left"><font color="#000000"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">Well, when you buy a new car and factor in the
monthly payments, full-coverage insurance, gas, maintenance and repairs, its easy to see why even a "cheap" new car
costing</span>&nbsp;<em><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">only</span></em><span style=
"FONT-SIZE: 12pt">&nbsp;$15,000.00 out the door has become totally out of reach for the average
consumer.</span></font></p>

<p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><font color="#000000">So its no wonder that more and more buyers
either can not afford to buy a new car, or they refuse to buy something that will lose HALF its value in the first
3 years!</font></span></p>

<p align="left"><strong><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">And due to those factors (and common
sense),</span>&nbsp;<font style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: #ffffaa"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">the demand for used cars
will never go away</span></font><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">.</span></strong></p>

<p align="left">&nbsp;</p>

<p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">I&rsquo;ve&nbsp;received a few angry emails
from people telling me I&rsquo;m a scammer and full of BS because they say there is&nbsp;<em>&ldquo;no way anyone would sell a
car for less than its worth.&rdquo;</em></span></span></p>

<p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">Okay, so&nbsp;maybe&nbsp;<em>you</em> don't sell&nbsp;your stuff for
less than its worth, but here are...</span></p>

<p align="left">&nbsp;</p>

<p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><strong><span style="FONT-SIZE: 14pt">9&nbsp;reasons why people sell
cars for less than they are worth...</span></strong></span></p>

<blockquote style="MARGIN-RIGHT: 0px"
            dir="ltr">
    <p align="left"><br>
    <span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><strong>1) Seller doesn't&nbsp;know car is worth more:</strong>&nbsp;If a seller
    doesn't know&nbsp;how to&nbsp;price a used car&nbsp;(and few do), then&nbsp;the tendency is to&nbsp;throw
    an&nbsp;asking&nbsp;price out there based on a guess&nbsp;without knowing if its over or
    under-priced.</span></p>

    <p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><strong>2) Seller knows car is worth more but they don't
    care:</strong>&nbsp;People lead busy lives and cherish what little free time they have. That means
    some&nbsp;sellers&nbsp;don't want to</span><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">&nbsp;spend&nbsp;their entire
    weekend&nbsp;fielding&nbsp;phone calls&nbsp;and showing their car just to make an extra $500.00 or whatever. So
    they sell it cheap and fast just&nbsp;to get it over with&nbsp;so they can enjoy the rest of their
    weekend.</span></p>

    <p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><strong>3) Seller is desperate:</strong>&nbsp;I don't know about
    you, but I've faced situations in my life where I&nbsp;was low on cash and had to pay bills...<em>now!</em>
    Well, sometimes the only way to get out of that bind is to sell something fast and cheap, and sometimes the
    thing being sold is a car.</span></p>

    <p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><strong>4) Seller thinks car needs major repairs, but it
    doesn't:</strong> I&rsquo;ve bought lots of used cars for less than what the sellers could have sold them for
    because&nbsp;the seller&nbsp;assumed&nbsp;their car&nbsp;needed major repairs...<em>but it didn't</em>. They
    panicked and unloaded the car cheap because they thought&nbsp;<em>&ldquo;Oh my gawd, I better dump
    this&nbsp;pile&nbsp;now before it breaks down and won&rsquo;t be worth anything!&rdquo;</em></span></p>

    <p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><strong>5)&nbsp;Seller knows&nbsp;car requires minor/major
    repairs and&nbsp;is open and honest about&nbsp;it:</strong> If its obvious a car needs repairs then most
    sellers know they will have to sell it for less.</span></p>

    <p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><strong>6)&nbsp;Sellers&nbsp;car&nbsp;needs repairs
    and&nbsp;tries to hide&nbsp;that&nbsp;from you:</strong>&nbsp;Thats not a problem when you learn how to know
    the difference between expensive and inexpensive repairs.</span></p>

    <p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><strong>7) Sellers car is stolen and seller will disappear the
    moment they get your money:</strong> Again,&nbsp;you can easily&nbsp;learn how to never&nbsp;fall prey to this
    kind of stuff.</span></p>

    <p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><strong>8) Seller doesn't care about making more when selling
    car:</strong> Believe it or not, there are&nbsp;some car sellers that just don&rsquo;t care about&nbsp;making as much
    money as they can when they sell their car.&nbsp;Its mind boggling to me&nbsp;because&nbsp;some of these
    sellers&nbsp;are struggling&nbsp;with low paying jobs and could really use the extra $500.00 or $1000.00 they
    could have gotten.</span></p>

    <p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt"><strong>9) The seller lacks intelligence:</strong> I guess you
    could argue that most of the reasons I&nbsp;just mentioned are due to a lack of intelligence. But hey,&nbsp;its
    not my place to pass&nbsp;out IQ tests to sellers before I buy their car.</span></p>
</blockquote>

<p align="left">&nbsp;</p>

<p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">Stuff gets sold all over the world for less than&nbsp;market value.
That stuff includes farm equipment, motorcycles, boats, houses, parcels of land, computers, businesses, toys,
surfboards, bikes, snowboards and a million other things.</span></p>

<p align="left"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt">The bottom line is that learning how to buy low and sell high is THE
rule for making serious money at this.</span></p>
