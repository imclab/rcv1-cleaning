            <p style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;"><span style="font-size:12px;">Prescott is filled with cultural spirit, as it is home to  premier arts and entertainment venues welcoming local, regional, national and  international performers, exhibits and special events all year. Community  murals are located downtown, at the library, Miller Valley School and at Acker  Park, with each mural documenting Prescott&rsquo;s history, the arts and more.</span></p>

              <p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0;"><a href="http://www.bronzesmith.com/"><strong>BRONZESMITH FINE ART FOUNDRY AND GALLERY</strong></a></p>
              <p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0;">7331&nbsp;East Second Street, Prescott Valley</p>
              <p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0;">928-772-2378</p>
              <p style="margin-top: 0;">Bronzesmith Fine Art Foundry and Gallery is a unique destination for sculptors, bronze sculpture collectors and art enthusiasts.&nbsp; Located in Prescott Valley, we offer a sculpture garden, gallery and foundry in one location.&nbsp; In our gallery you will discover exquisite traditional and contemporary limited edition bronze sculpture from today's leading artists.&nbsp; Hours are from 9:00-4:00 Monday-Friday with foundry tours on Thursday at 11:00 by appointment.&nbsp; The cost for the tour is $10.00 per person.&nbsp;</p>
              <p style="margin-bottom: 0;"><strong><a href="http://www.elksoperahouse.org" target="_blank">ELKS OPERA HOUSE</a></strong><br />
                117 E. Gurley   Street<br />
                928-443-8541 <br />
              <a href="http://www.elksoperahouse.org" target="blank"></a></p>
<p style="margin-top:0;"><span style="font-size:15px;"><span style="font-size:12px;">The  494-seat Elks Opera House is in a prominent structure on East Gurley Street,  just one-half-block from the Yavapai County Courthouse Plaza. The Elks Building  was listed in the National Register of Historic Places in 1978, and the Elks  Opera House was the setting for the First Lady Laura Bush's PRESERVE AMERICA  Award in 2004. The opera house is a significant venue for concerts, plays,  political events, films, conferences and an array of special events including  weddings, graduations and more. The theater has been restored to its 1905  glory, including opera boxes, new seats and complete paint and ceiling restoration  as well as the proscenium arch above the stage. <span style="color:#C0504D;">&nbsp;</span>With the completion of the restoration  and an investment of $1.75 million, the Elks Opera House Foundation resolved to  produce the best of the performing arts and has completed the first season with  a variety of presentations.&nbsp;</span></span></p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0;"><a href="http://www.mountainartistsguild.org"><strong>MOUNTAIN ARTISTS GUILD</strong></a></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom: 0;">228 N Alarcon Street</p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom: 0;">928-778-4009 / 928-445-2510</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0;"> <span style="font-size:12px;">Mountain Artists Guild is the oldest Visual Art Organization in the State of Arizona. George  Pippen was one of its founding members. &nbsp;It is a  nonprofit organization dedicated to the promotion of the visual arts. The Gallery consists of three rooms: the Main  Gallery presents a new juried show every three months; the Spotlight Room features  a solo or two person show each month; the third room, the Master's Room, is  focused on our local internationally known artists. We hold an Opening  Reception for this event each 4th Friday during Prescott's Art Walk.  We provide local artists with an opportunity to present and sell their art to  the public. Within the Main Gallery we have jewelry, ceramic pieces, and many  other original gift items. The Gallery is open weekdays from 10 am to 4  pm and on Saturdays and Sundays from 11 am to 3 pm. In addition, Mountain  Artists Guild sponsors two Fine Art Festivals on the historic Prescott  Courthouse Square, one in May and one in August. Please visit our website for more information about the Guild,  Gallery, workshops, and other events. </span></p>
<p align="justify" style="margin-bottom: 0;"><strong><a href="http://www.pfaa.net" target="_blank">PRESCOTT CENTER FOR THE ARTS</a></strong><br />
                208 N. Marina   Street<br />
                928-445-3286 <br />
              <a href="http://www.pfaa.net" target="blank"></a></p>
<p style="margin-top:0;"><span style="font-size:12px;">The Prescott Center for the Arts&rsquo; mission &lsquo;is to create  opportunities for the greater community to engage in and experience all facets  of the arts&rsquo; through theatre, concerts, special events, visual arts gallery,  scholarships and education.&nbsp; PCA is Prescott&rsquo;s longest running, largest  and most successful community theatre and gallery.&nbsp; New for 2012 &ndash; 2013  season is the anticipated expansion of Stage Too, a leased space for  rehearsals, classes and thought-provoking theatre.&nbsp; See the website for  more information about Stage Too and all PCA programs and to purchase tickets.</span></p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0;"><a href="http://www.prescottfilmfestival.com"><strong>PRESCOTT FILM FESTIVAL</strong></a></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom: 0;">PO Box 2731</p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom: 0;">928-458-7209</p>
<p style="margin-top:0;"><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 12px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; letter-spacing: normal; line-height: normal; orphans: auto; text-align: start; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; white-space: normal; widows: auto; word-spacing: 0px; -webkit-text-size-adjust: auto; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); display: inline !important; float: none;">The Prescott Film Festival hosts special film screenings year round and holds its annual festival the last week in July.&nbsp; The fest searches out the best in film from across the world and presents programs that &ldquo;Entertain, Inspire and Inform,&rdquo; &nbsp;celebrating both independent and studio films of all genres.&nbsp; Guest filmmakers from across the US and the globe attend many of the film screenings.&nbsp; &nbsp;Workshops for audience members and visiting filmmakers are held at special events and the annual fest.&nbsp; February is &ldquo;Oscar&reg; Month&rdquo; at PFF with Oscar&reg; nominated films screening all month and the &nbsp;annual &ldquo;An Evening at the Academy Awards&reg; &ldquo; fundraiser where the broadcast can be viewed up &ldquo;on the big screen!&rdquo;&nbsp; The festival screens at the Yavapai College Performing Arts Center and venues across the city of Prescott.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0;"><strong><a href="http://www.prescottsymphony.com">PRESCOTT POPS SYMPHONY </a></strong></p>
<p style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;">PO Box 10231</p>
<p style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;">928-776-2000</p>
<p style="margin-top:0;">Since its creation as a community summer orchestra in 1992,  the Prescott POPS Symphony, under the direction of Maestro Paul Manz, has  emerged as Prescott&rsquo;s premier performing orchestra with a unique contribution  to the culture of our community.&nbsp; Their  goal is to provide the highest quality, dynamic and accessible symphonic  entertainment to local audiences; to offer challenging and inspiring  performance opportunities to talented youth and seasoned musicians; and to  support the perpetual promotion of music as a vital part of the community  through donations and scholarships to schools and students. The Prescott POPS  Symphony is a nonprofit organization. Tickets are available through the Yavapai  College Community Events Office. </p>
<p align="justify" style="margin-bottom: 0;"><strong><a href="http://www.timstoyotacenter.com" target="_blank">TIM&rsquo;S TOYOTA CENTER</a><br />
</strong>3201 N. Main Street, Prescott   Valley<br />
928-772-1819 / <span style="font-size:12px;">866-772-1819 (toll free)</span><br /></p>
<p style="margin-top:0;"><span style="font-size:12px;">Built in 2006, this modern multi-purpose venue stands as a  premier site for concerts, sporting events, expos, stage shows and a variety of  other special events. The nearly 135,000-square-foot Tim&rsquo;s Toyota Center is  home to the Arizona Sundogs ice hockey team &ndash; 2007-08 champions of the Central  Hockey League.</span></p>
<p align="justify" style="margin-bottom: 0;"><strong><a href="http://www.ycpac.com/" target="_blank">YAVAPAI COLLEGE PERFORMING ARTS CENTER AND ART   GALLERY</a><br />
                </strong>1100 E. Sheldon<br />
              928-776-2000 / 877-928-4253 (Box   Office)<br /></p>
<p style="margin-top:0;"><span style="font-size:12px;">The Yavapai College Performing Arts Center hosts a  spectacular range of entertainment, from national touring performers like Kris  Kristofferson, Wynton Marsalis, and Mary Chapin Carpenter, to live satellite  broadcasts of the Metropolitan Opera, and weekly movies on a state-of-the-art  projection system.&nbsp; Following a full renovation of the interior, sound and  lighting in 2011, the Performing Arts Center is a thoroughly modern facility  that seats up to 1,100 guests and is available for rental for special  events.&nbsp; Situated within the hall is the Yavapai College Art Gallery,  which hosts exhibits by college and outside artists, as well as presentations  and cultural events throughout the year.</span></p>
<p align="justify" style="margin-bottom: 0;"><strong><a href="http://www.yc.edu" target="_blank">YAVAPAI COLLEGE SCULPTURE GARDEN</a><br />
                </strong>Friends of YC   Art, 1100 E. Sheldon, #6902<br />
                928-776-2025 <br />
</p>
<p style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;"><span style="font-size:12px;">The four-acre Sculpture Garden is a peaceful and innovative  site on the college campus. Located next to the Performance Hall and Art  Gallery, the park fuses gorgeous landscaping with contemporary sculpture. Its  meandering walks enhanced with a variety of beautiful trees and shrubs provide  a graceful setting for study, quiet contemplation and makes it a delightful  area for outdoor events. Currently, the garden is home to 12 sculptures.</span></p>

<p style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;">228 Alarcan Street</p>
<p style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;">928-778-6745</p>
<p style="margin-top:0;">Beginning its 47th season, the Yavapai Symphony Association  (YSA) presents the Phoenix Symphony Orchestra (PSO) in concert for Prescott and  surrounding communities. Providing music lovers a classical music experience,  YSA sponsors the concert season with the Phoenix Symphony Orchestra from  September to May. YSA also sponsors scholarships and cooperates with Phoenix  Symphony members in providing Masters classes for aspiring local music  students. These classes are taught by the Symphony musicians for local students  of all ages. As a follow up, YSA also provides to the students and parents free  tickets to PSO concerts. Each year YSA gives away thousands of dollars of these  tickets as a further means of encouragement to the youth of Yavapai County  thereby assuring the continuance of the appreciation of classical music in our  part of Arizona.&nbsp;YSA is also very proud of  its scholarship program for music-major college students. In 2012 six  scholarships were awarded in the amount of $16,000.</p>
