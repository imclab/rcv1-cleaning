                                    <h2><span>Best Answer</span> - Chosen by Asker</h2>
                              <div class="content">I have an alpha omega (eddie bauer, costco, etc its sold under differnet names) and an evenflo triumph advance.<br>
<br>
The Evenflo wins hands down.  It takes up less space ummm between the back of the back seat and the back of the front passenger seat so you don&#039;t have to pull your seat all the way up.  It is slightly wider than the alpha omega though.  For installation there&#039;s not a huge difference.  But the best part is the strap adjustment.  On the alpha omega the straps run under the baby&#039;s bum.  So when they are in the seat if there is more strap on one side than the other you have to pick the baby up and fix it.  On the Evenflo the straps are very easy to adjust (its a dial) and they seem to balance themselves.  The shoulder height is very adjustable.  You do have to loosen the straps each time you take the baby out but that&#039;s not such a big deal because its so easy to loosen and tighten them.<br>
<br>
The eveflo triumph advance consistently gets good reviews.  And its about 30% less than the alpha omega and way less than britax.  <br>
<br>
As far as I know it also has a higher front facing with full 5 point harness weight, but I&#039;m in Canada and some weight restrictions are different between Canada and the US (even though the seats are made in the same place).<br>
<br>
If space is an issue then the Sunshine Radian 65 is your best bet.  Its the smallest in how much car space it takes up, but its one of the widest as far as how much space baby has.  It manages that because the frame is metal not plastic.  It folds up and is &quot;portable&quot; but its pretty heavy from what I hear.<br>
<br>
The Learning Curve True Fit (I think its called) is also a really good seat.</div>
