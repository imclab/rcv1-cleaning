			<div class="entry_single">			
				<span id="intelliTxt"><p>Enter the BMW i3 Coupe &#8211; an eco-friendly little tech-friendly amalgamation of excellence inside and out, and one that&#8217;s essentially <a href="http://www.slashgear.com/bmw-i3-concept-coupe-ditches-steel-for-intelligent-ev-28258706/" target="_Blank">just been revealed this week.</a> As we spoke about just yesterday, this machine brings on an inter-car communications system, smartphone connectivity, and of course, a 170HP electric motor slice with lithium-ion batteries inside. It&#8217;s all topped off by a hardcore lighter yet stronger than steel CFRP (carbon reinforced plastic) passenger cell.</p>
<p><img src="http://cdn.slashgear.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/20121128_131322-580x325.jpg" alt="" title="20121128_131322" width="580" height="325" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-258999" /></p>
<p><span id="more-258990"></span></p>
<p>The CFRP brings on enough strength of its own that no B-pillar is required to keep its structure strong, this allowing two rear seats to be much more accessible than in the vast amount of such back-sitting environments in vehicles today. Also inside you&#8217;ll find wood, wool, leather, and an extended collection of renewable materials to remind you that yes, you are rolling forth in a green-minded machine.</p>
<p><p><center><iframe title="YouTube video player" width="584" height="362" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/itbH3kW1vA4" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></center></p>
</p>
<p>Entering and exiting the vehicle in the front from and to either side is also easier than ever as the area below the dash is empty. With no transmission tunnel-bound protrusions in your way, you can easily slide from side to side. Up top of the dash you&#8217;ll find two displays, the first being a 6.5-inch screen with information on your speed, power, and the like. The larger display is 8.8 inches and ready to bring on the BMW i ConnectDrive suite, with navigation, infotainment, messages, schedules, and one whole heck of a lot more.</p>
<p><img src="http://cdn.slashgear.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/20121128_131338-580x325.jpg" alt="" title="20121128_131338" width="580" height="325" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-258996" /></p>
<p>This vehicle does not currently have a release schedule, but as the i8 Spyder was recently revealed to be coming up as soon as 2013, we must assume that this EV isn&#8217;t that far behind. Have a peek at our <a href="http://slashgear.com/tags/bmw/" target="_blank">BMW tag portal</a> as well as our <a href="http://www.slashgear.com/cars/" target="_blank">Car portal</a> to keep track of everything BMW and the larger vehicle world into the future &#8211; big things are going down!</p>
