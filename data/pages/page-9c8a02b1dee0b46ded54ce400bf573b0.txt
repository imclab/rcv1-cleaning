                  <h2 class="title">2012 Notables Resources</h2>
                  <p><br />
                    <a href="http://mrschureads.blogspot.com/2012/03/resources-for-2012-notable-childrens.html" target="_blank">A Butterfly is Patient - Dead End in Norvelt</a><br />
				    <a href="http://mrschureads.blogspot.com/2012/03/resources-for-2012-notable-childrens_14.html" target="_blank">Heart and Soul - Passing the Music Down</a><br />
			      <a href="http://mrschureads.blogspot.com/2012/03/resources-for-2012-notable-childrens_7326.html?spref=tw" target="_blank">Requiem - Wonton</a></div>
				<div id="box3">
				  <ul class="style3">
						<li class="first"> The charge of the seven-member national committee is to select thirty titles each year that best exemplify the criteria established for the Notables Award. Books considered for this annual list are works of fiction, non-fiction, and poetry written for children, grades K-8. The books must meet one or more of the following criteria:</li>
					</ul>
					<ol>
					  <li>deal explicitly with language, such as plays on words, word origins, or the history of language;					    </li>
					  <li>demonstrate uniqueness in the use of language or style;</li>
					  <li>invite child response or participation.
					    
					    In addition, books are to:</li>
					  <li> have an appealing format;</li>
					  <li> be of enduring quality;</li>
					  <li>meet generally accepted criteria of quality for the genre in which they are written.
</li>
					</ol>
					
			
					<ul class="style3">
					 <li></li> 
					 <p><strong>Call for Notable Children's Books for Language Arts committee members</strong>: Interested and qualified persons should send a query email to incoming NCBLA chair, <a href="mailto:pbandre@bakeru.edu">Patricia Bandre</a>. </p>
					 <p> Please download and complete the <a href="docs/committee-member-application.pdf">application form</a> and review the committee expectations, obligations, and guidelines. New NCLBA committee members are chosen based on the following: Current CLA member, representative of the geographic regions of the United States (Northwest, Southwest, Midwest, South, and North), at least one member must be a current K-8 classroom teacher, and no candidate or member may currently be serving on any other national book committees (ALA, IRA, NCTE, or other). </p>
					 <p>All applicants will need to complete a one-page application, send a current curriculum vitae or resume, sign an obligation of membership form, and write 2-5 original book annotations. This application packet needs to be sent to <a href="mailto:pbandre@bakeru.edu">Patricia</a> on or before March 1, 2013.</p>
