<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/photos/volkswagen-e-bugster-concept-roof-down/" target="_blank"><img height="405" src="http://www.blogcdn.com/www.autoblog.com/media/2012/07/db2012au00365medium1-copy.jpg" vspace="4" width="628"/></a><br/>
<br/>
<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/volkswagen/">Volkswagen</a> has confirmed rumors that its 2013 <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/volkswagen/beetle/">Beetle</a> Convertible will drop its top at the 2012 <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/la-auto-show/">Los Angeles Auto Show</a> in late November of this year. While we haven't seen images of the new convertible yet, the automaker's all-electric <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2012/04/23/volkswagen-e-bugster-concept-lowers-its-lid-in-beijing/">E-Bugster</a> should give us a good indication of how the new model will look &ndash; minus the flared fenders, speedster windshield and two-seat passenger configuration, of course.<br/>
<br/>
It has also been reported, though not from VW, that new period-correct trim, with retro paint colors, upholstery and other details, will help capture the nostalgia of the "Fifties," "Sixties" or "Seventies."<br/>
<br/>
The automaker has stated that there will be three engine choices for North American customers (it appears EV fans will have to keep waiting). Under the front hood of standard models will be a naturally-aspirated 2.5-liter five-cylinder (earlier this week, we announced that the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2012/07/17/2014-vw-jetta-to-drop-five-cylinder-in-favor-of-turbo-four/">inline-five is being discontinued in the Jetta</a>). The Beetle Turbo will be fitted with a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder running on unleaded and the Beetle TDI receives a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder burning diesel.
