				<h1 id="title">Honda Urban SUV Concept </h1>
				<h2 id="dek">Perhaps they could call this Fit-based crossover the FitAll or FitbyFour. </h2>
			</hgroup>
		</header>
		<div class="byline-container">

    <p>
Honda is busy making good on its promise <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/next-honda-fit-will-spin-off-mini-crossover-sedan-for-u-s/" target="_self">to produce sedan and small-crossover variants of its next-generation Fit</a> subcompact; this Urban SUV concept debuting at the <a href="/2013-detroit-auto-show" target="_self">2013 Detroit auto show</a> previews the latter. The new, Mexican-built Fit arrives next year, but the production Urban SUV (which will be built at the same facility) won&rsquo;t hit the U.S. for another year after that. Given how cool the crossover concept looks, we hope it comes with a less snore-geric name. 
</p>

<p>
<b>I&rsquo;ll Take a Juke, Please, But Hold the Weird</b>
</p>

<p>
In terms of size and price, the Urban SUV will slot beneath the cash-cow CR-V in Honda&rsquo;s crossover lineup, putting it in the same league as the <a href="/reviews/2011-nissan-juke-sl-road-test-review" target="_self">Nissan Juke</a> and <a href="/reviews/2012-kia-soul-20-plus-manual-test-review" target="_self">Kia Soul</a>. Among this competition, the Honda definitely hews closer to the Juke&rsquo;s sporty brand than the Kia&rsquo;s urban funkiness. The concept gets aggressive yet sophisticated styling and a semi-circular, coupe-like roofline. Honda exaggerates the faux two-door appearance with door handles hidden in the C-pillar trim. Even though the roofline likely won&rsquo;t be as sexy on the production car, it probably won&rsquo;t be drastically less so&mdash;look close and you can see how the designers cleverly broke the side-window shape from that of the roof&rsquo;s (presumably) to eke out more rear headroom. 
</p>

<p>
Up front, the Urban SUV wears an adaptation of the same grille and fascia treatment found on the <a href="/reviews/2013-honda-accord-four-cylinder-manual-test-review" target="_self">newest Accord</a>, while the sides of the vehicle are minimally adorned with two sharp character lines that sweep from the front and rear fender bulges. Taken overall, the Urban SUV concept looks a lot like an Acura-ified Juke, minus the Nissan&rsquo;s, er, <i>character</i>, and it&rsquo;s darn attractive. 
</p>

<p>
Honda purists concerned that the automaker has skewed too far toward the form side of the form/function scale with the new crossover shouldn&rsquo;t be worried. The littlest Honda SUV will ride on the Fit&rsquo;s Global Compact Series architecture, complete with the Fit&rsquo;s signature centrally located fuel tank, which lives beneath the driver&rsquo;s seat to free up floor space in back. As a result, the Urban SUV will get the Fit&rsquo;s clever folding/flipping/flattening Magic Seat rear bench, which should afford more cargo-carrying configurations than you&rsquo;ll ever need. 
</p>

<p>
Details regarding the Urban SUV&rsquo;s interior and powertrain will be forthcoming, but Honda&rsquo;s fuel-efficient (and still-hilariously named) Earth Dreams technology will be part of the mix. Expect a small-displacement four-cylinder engine and the choice of front- or all-wheel drive. Honda promises &ldquo;next-generation telematics and a modern interior.&rdquo; Whew&mdash;for a second there we were worried that Honda was planning to fit its newest small car with a cassette player, a brick-sized car phone, and the dashboard from a first-generation CR-V. 
</p>

<p>
The tiny Fit is the best-driving car in its segment&mdash;one of the many reasons <a href="/features/2013-10best-cars-feature" target="_self">we&rsquo;ve elected it to our 10Best cars list</a> every year it&rsquo;s been on sale in the U.S. If Honda can deliver the Fit&rsquo;s basic dynamic and packaging goodness into the Urban SUV&rsquo;s taller package, we see no reason why it couldn&rsquo;t expand on the small hatchback&rsquo;s success. This is especially so if the production model is even three-quarters as attractive as the Urban SUV concept. 
</p>

<p>
The good news is that Honda&rsquo;s concepts usually don&rsquo;t change much before reaching consumers. But may we suggest a better name? We&rsquo;re thinking a cheeky spinoff of the Fit&rsquo;s pun-inviting moniker (<i>this car can</i> Fit<i> a ton of stuff!</i>)<i> </i>like &ldquo;FitAll&rdquo; (tall Fit) or &ldquo;Fitbyfour&rdquo; (do you really need this one spelled out?) might be appropriate.
