				  <p align="left"><strong>Jane                 Austen Information Page</strong></p>
				  <p><strong>Charlotte Bronte</strong><br>
				    Author of "Jane Eyre", "Villette", and other Victorian novels.</p>
				  <p><strong>Willa                   Cather</strong><br>
				    Author of pioneer novels featuring                   strong women like "My Antonia". Includes Willa Cather                   events, and publications by and about Cather. </p>
				  <p><strong>Emily                   Dickinson</strong></p>
				  <p> <strong>Charlotte Perkins Gilman</strong><br>
				    Early feminist author of <em>The Yellow Wallpaper</em> and <em>Herland.</em> </p>
				  <p><a href="http://i.am/zora"><strong>Zora Neale Hurston</strong></a><br>
				    This page dedicated to the novelist/anthropologist includes an                   anecdote of how one reader discovered Hurston, story excerpts,                   photos, information, essays, and links. </p>
				  <p><strong>Edith Wharton</strong><br>
				    American author of "Age of Innocence," "House of                   Mirth," and other novels. </p>
				  <p><a href="http://digital.library.upenn.edu/women/bronte/bronte-anne.html"><strong>Anne Bronte</strong></a><br>
				    Poet Anne Bronte's life and poetry are highlighted in this web                   page. Also included in this page is a bibliography relating to                   the Bronte sisters. </p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.geocities.com/SoHo/Lofts/4396/f_index.html"><strong>Emily                   Bronte</strong></a><br>
				    This web page devoted to the 19th century poet include a biography,                   samples of Emily's poetry, and poetry links.</p>
				  <p><a href="http://lang.nagoya-u.ac.jp/%7Ematsuoka/Eliot.html"><strong>George                   Eliot</strong></a><br>
				    Information on the life and writings of George Eliot, aka Mary                   Ann Evans -- author of <em>Middlemarch</em> and other novels.</p>
				  <p><strong>Virginia                   Woolf</strong></p>
				  <h3><a name="20">Contemporary Authors</a></h3>
				  <p><strong>Isabel Allende</strong><br>
				    Latina American writer. </p>
				  <p><a href="http://rhombus.net/artvt/artvt/p_alther.htm"><strong>Lisa                   Alther</strong></a><br>
				    This web page provides a guide to Alther's background, with pictures,                   interviews, critiques and descriptions of books. </p>
				  <p><strong>Maya                   Angelou</strong></p>
				  <p><strong>A.S. Byatt</strong><br>
				    Antonia Susan Byatt has written six novels, including Booker-prize                   winning <em>Possession.</em> </p>
				  <p><strong>Edwidge                   Danticat</strong></p>
				  <p><strong>Annie Dillard</strong><br>
				    Author of "Pilgrim at Tinker Creek" and "An American Childhood." </p>
				  <p><strong><a href="http://www.salon1999.com/weekly/interview960506.html">Louise                   Erdrich</a></strong><br>
				    Salon interview with Native American poet and author. </p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.kingsolver.com/"><strong>Barbara                   Kingsolver</strong></a><br>
				    Official website of popular author of "The Bean Trees," "Animal                   Dreams," "Pigs in Heaven," and "The Poisonwood Bible." </p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.bookwire.com/qbr/features/95-nov/mcmillan.html"><strong>Terry                   McMillan</strong></a><br>
				    This African-American best-selling author of <em>Waiting to Exhale</em> gives a short interview about the making of the movie based on                   the novel. </p>
				  <p><strong>Toni Morrison</strong><br>
				    Noble Prize-winning author of "Beloved" and other African-American-                   and woman-focused novels. </p>
				  <p><strong>Gloria Naylor</strong><br>
				    Novelist, works include "The Women of Brewster Place." </p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.burrows.com/poolbio.html"><strong>Maria                   Louise Pool</strong></a><br>
				    This Victorian lesbian author wrote novels about women set in                   New England and the South. This site contains a detailed biography                   and novel excerpts. </p>
				  <p><strong>Jane Smiley</strong><br>
				    Author of "A Thousand Acres" which won the National Book Critics                   Circle Award in 1991. </p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.luminarium.org/contemporary/alicew/"><strong>Alice                   Walker</strong></a><br>
				    This site of this activist/womanist author contains a biography,                   annotated excerpts of her works, a list of works, quotes, and                   interviews. </p>
				  <p><strong>Rebecca Wells</strong><br>
				    Author of "The Divine Secrets of the Ya-Ya Sisterhood." </p>
				  <p><a href="http://members.xoom.com/theproof/banana/index.htm"><strong>Banana                   Yoshimoto</strong></a><br>
				    A fan's devoted site to this Japanize author of "Kitchen." </p>
				  <p><strong>Dorothy                   Allison</strong><br>
				    Southern feminist, lesbian author of "Bastard Out of Carolina",                   "Trash", "Skin" and "Two or Three Things I Know for Sure." </p>
				  <p><strong>Julia Alvarez</strong><br>
				    Best-selling Dominican-American author of "How the Garcia Girls                   Lost Their Accents," "In the Time of the Butterflies," and "Yo!" </p>
				  <p><strong>Margaret Atwood</strong><br>
				    Canadian author of "Cat's Eye," "The Handmaid's                   Tale," and other novels, as well as an extensive collection                   of short stories, all with strong women protagonists. </p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.lasmujeres.com/sandracisneros/"><strong>Sandra                   Cisneros</strong></a><br>
				    List of books and biographical information about this chicana                   author of "House on Mango Street" and "Women Hollering Creek and                   Other Stories". This web site provides links to other hispanic                   authors. </p>
				  <p><a href="http://spider.invsn.com/fotpl/valley3d.htm"><strong>Joan                   Didion</strong></a><br>
				    Author of "After Henry" web page features a biography, information                   on works,and links to other sites. </p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.igc.apc.org/womensnet/dworkin"><strong>Andrea                   Dworkin</strong></a><br>
				    The site dedicated to the controversial feminist writer dispels                   some myths about her beliefs and samples her fiction works, interviews                   and speeches. </p>
				  <p><strong>Leslie Feinberg</strong><br>
				    Activist, author and speaker of the transgender movement. Hir                   "Stone Butch Blues" is a must-read. </p>
				  <p><strong>Maxine Hong Kingston</strong><br>
				    Chinese-American author of "The Woman Warrior" and other                   works. </p>
				  <p><strong>Anchee Minn</strong><br>
				    Chinese author of "Red Azalea" writes about political and social                   issues during Maoist China. </p>
				  <p><strong>Bharati Mukherjee</strong><br>
				    This Indian-American author seeks to record the experiences of                   the "new immigrant." </p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.margepiercy.com"><strong>Marge                   Piercy</strong></a><br>
				    Everything you've ever wanted to know about Marge Piercy, poet                   and author of "Woman on the Edge of Time," including her resume,                   list of books and reviews, reading schedule, and more. </p>
				  <p><a href="http://home.earthlink.net/%7Emlomonico/quindlen"><strong>Anna                   Quindlen</strong></a><br>
				    Anna Quindlen is a former columnist for the <em>New York Times</em>.                   Amazon.com describes her writing as, "personal, humorous, and                   thought-provoking." Author of "One True Thing" and "Living Out                   Loud." </p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.luminarium.org/contemporary/amytan/"><strong>Amy                   Tan</strong></a><br>
				    Interviews with and articles about the author of "The Joy Luck                   Club," "The Kitchen God's Wife," and "The Hundred Secret Senses." </p>
				  <h3><a name="mystery">Feminist Mystery Writers</a></h3>
				  <p><strong>Eleanor Taylor Bland</strong><br>
				    Eleanor Taylor Bland's sleuth is Marti MacAlister, an African-American                   woman homicide detective in Illinois.</p>
				  <p><strong>Rita Mae Brown</strong><br>
				    Southern feminist writer, her works include the "Sneaky Pie Brown"                   mystery series and the "lesbian classic" "Rubyfruit                   Jungle." </p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.nicolafurlong.com"><strong>Nicola                   Furlong</strong></a><br>
				    Mystery writer whose works feature female athletic detective characters</p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.suegrafton.com"><strong>Sue Grafton</strong></a><br>
				    This is the official site of the alphabetical mystery writer.                   Take a survey, read her biography, or search her books from "A"                   is for Alibi to "O" is for Outlaw. This site won the People's                   Choice Web Site award. </p>
				  <p><strong>Sparkle Hayter</strong><br>
				    Mystery author. </p>
				  <p><strong>Joan Hess</strong><br>
				    Feminist mystery novelist, her works feature Claire Malloy, a                   bookseller, and Arly Hanks, Chief of Police of Maggody, Arkansas. </p>
				  <p><strong>Susan Holtzer: The Mysterious Wolverine</strong><br>
				    Mystery novelist, including the "Ann Arbor" mystery series. </p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.ozemail.com.au/%7Eblinda/knott.htm"><strong>Margaret                   Maron</strong></a><br>
				    The site of this Edgar Award winning mystery novelist includes                   a biography and book information.</p>
				  <p><a href="http://www.scruz.net/%7Elmatera/LiaMatera.html"><strong>Lia                   Matera</strong></a><br>
				    This author of the Willa Jansson series has a site that includes                   information about booksignings, articles, and recommended links                   for entertainment and research. </p>
				  <p><strong>Sharyn McCrumb</strong><br>
				    This suspense novelist is the author of the Elizabeth McPhearson                   mysteries and the Ballad novel series. </p>
				  <p><a href="http://vme.net/dvm/sister-sleuth/"><strong>Terris                   McMahan Grimes Presents Sister Sleuth</strong></a><br>
				    This mystery novelist1s site explores mysteries written for and                   about people of color. Includes an interview with Grimes and information                   about her new novel. </p>
				  <p><strong>Annette Meyers</strong><br>
				    Author of the Smith and Wetzon series about two Wall Street headhunters,                   and co-author, with her husband Martin, of a historical series                   written under the pen name Maan Meyers. </p>
				  <p align="left"><a href="http://www.saraparetsky.com"><strong>Sarah                   Paretsky</strong></a><br>
				    Sara Paretsky is one of the best known women mystery authors,                   whose private detective V.I. Warshawski and gritty Chicago-based                   mysteries broke ground for women in the previously all male hard-boiled                   mystery game. </p>
				  <p align="left"><a href="http://www.imt.net/%7Egedison/scoppett.html"><strong>Sandra                   Scoppettone</strong></a><br>
				    This mystery/suspense author is best known for her Lauren Laurano                   series, which features a lesbian private investigator. Her site                   includes a biography, interview, summary of current projects,                   calendar of appearances, and reviews of other writer's books. </p>
				  <p align="left"><a href="http://www.webcom.com/cauldron/starhawk"><strong>Starhawk</strong></a><br>
				    Starhawk is the author of some suspenseful new age/science fiction                   feminist novels: "The Fifth Sacred Thing" and "Walking to Mercury." </p>
				  <p align="left"><strong>Marilyn Wallace: A Wallace Sampler</strong><br>
				    This suspense writer is also the editor of the <a href="http://www.sinc-ic.org">Sisters                   in Crime Anthologies</a>. </p>
				  <p align="left"><strong>Polly Whitney</strong><br>
				    Author of adventurous roller-blading mysteries.</p>
				  <h3 align="left"><a name="20"></a>Feminist Science Fiction                   Authors </h3>
				  <p align="left"><strong><a href="http://www.mzbfm.com">Marion Zimmer                   Bradley</a></strong><br>
				    Site of fantasy magazine and information about author.</p>
				  <p align="left"><a href="http://hubcap.clemson.edu/%7Esparks/Octav2.html"><strong>Octavia                   Butler</strong></a><br>
				    Visit the home page devoted to African-American science fiction                   writer Octavia Butler for a bibliography, links to a biography,                   and commentary.</p>
				  <p align="left"><a href="http://www.geocities.com/Area51/2593/index.htm"><strong>Ursula                   K. LeGuin</strong></a><br>
				    Site of science fiction writers<em> including</em> LeGuin. The site                   features interviews, links and a consise works list of fantasy                   for children and adults. </p>
				  <p align="left"><a href="http://ofb.net/%7Edamien/mckinley/"><strong>Robin                   McKinley</strong></a><br>
				    This site includes letters by the author, a biography, summaries                   of her books and surveys by readers. </p>
				  <p align="left"><strong>Constance Penley</strong><br>
				    Penley writes about sex and sexism within the world of science                   and technology. </p>
				  <p align="left"><strong>Sherri S. Tepper</strong><br>
				    Fantasy author of "The Awakeners" series. </p>
				  <h3 align="left"><a name="poetry"> </a>Feminist Poets </h3>
				  <p><a href="http://www.cwrl.utexas.edu/%7Emmaynard/Maya/maya5.html"><strong>Maya                   Angelou</strong></a><br>
				    James Baldwin writes,"You will hear the regal woman, the mischievous                   street girl; you will hear the price of black woman's survival                   and you will hear of her generosity. Black, bitter, and beautiful,                   she speaks of our survival" of Maya Angelou's poetry. </p>
				  <p><strong>Ellen Bass</strong><br>
				    Writes about sexual abuse in both Spanish and English. </p>
