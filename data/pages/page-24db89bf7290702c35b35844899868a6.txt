											<p id='intelliTxt' class="intro" data-module="article-intro">MDI, or Medium Dependent interface, is a type of Ethernet port featured on Network Interface cards. The device acts as both receiver and transmitter, and is usually configured so that the inputs and outputs are reversed. Such a configuration is known as MDIX. In other words, an MDI is a type of Ethernet port, and a MDIX is a type of Ethernet configuration. Such devices are new to Ethernet standards and can distinguish between straight-through cable (used to connect a computer to a network device, in most cases a network hub, switch or router) and crossover cable (used for connecting computing devices directly). A MDI/MDIX-equipped device will auto-detect the types used, so you don't have to worry which type of cable you plug in.
