					<h2>Eric Bana&#8217;s Ode to Muscle Car Culture</h2>
					<p class="title-meta"><span class="comments-head"><a href="http://www.mrautomotive.net/33/eric-banas-ode-to-muscle-car-culture/#respond" title="Comment on Eric Bana&#8217;s Ode to Muscle Car Culture">0 Comments</a></span> <span class="date">28 August 2010</span></p>
					                    
                    					<div class="entry">
						<a title="Eric Bana&#8217;s Ode to Muscle Car Culture" href="http://www.mrautomotive.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/FordXB.jpg" rel="lightbox"><img src="http://www.mrautomotive.net/wp-content/themes/dailyedition/thumb.php?src=wp-content/uploads/2010/08/FordXB.jpg&amp;w=250&amp;h=180&amp;zc=1&amp;q=90" alt="Eric Bana&#8217;s Ode to Muscle Car Culture" class="woo-image thumbnail alignright"  width="250"  height="180"  /></a>						<p>Aussie mega-actor Eric Bana has a new documentary out, <em>Love The Beast,</em> which chronicles his love of the automobile.  Specifically, a 1973 Ford XB coupe which he has owned since he was a teen.  Sadly, the XB was not available in the United States.  It&#8217;s the model upon which Mad Max&#8217;s blacked-out, supercharged, post-apocalyptic highway runner was built.</p>
<p>In any case, the film is great, and features some really great footage of Bana putting his rebuilt  600 hp monster through real-roads racing.  It&#8217;s just too bad that you can&#8217;t find these cars at Buy Here Pay Here car dealerships  in your area.  Sadly, they were never imported or sold in the United States.</p>
