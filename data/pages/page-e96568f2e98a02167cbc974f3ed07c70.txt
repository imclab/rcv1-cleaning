<h3>September 19, 2013, 7:00 PM - 8:30 PM</h3>
<div style="position:static;left:-40px;"><img src="/images/icons/w_lecture.gif" style="float:left;"></div>
<ul><P><strong>The Life, Work & Legacy of Ian Hornak</strong><br>"The Life, Work & Legacy of Ian Hornak" will be presented by Eric Ian Hornak Spoutz on Thursday, September 19, 2013 at 7:00 p.m. Ian Hornak was one of the founding artists of the Photorealist and Hyperrealist movements, whose lyrical and compelling paintings are on view in the Bowman and Kerstein Galleries. His nephew, Eric Ian Hornak Spoutz is an art historian, exhibition curator and fine art publisher who at the age of 16, worked as a salesman for his uncle. Upon Ian's death in 2002, Eric was appointed executor of the estate. He founded the Ian Hornak Foundation in 2003 and through 2006, he oversaw the Eric I. Spoutz Gallery in the Fisher Building in Detroit, Michigan, representing many of the founding Photorealist and Hyperrealist artists. He has been responsible for placing works of art into the permanent collections of numerous museums and institutions by many of America's preeminent artists including Franz Kline and Joan Mitchell. Spoutz also curated numerous museum exhibitions of his uncle's work, including the retrospective exhibition on view at the museum through October 13, 2013. Spoutz will give a first-hand illustrated lecture on Hornak's work, world and milieu. Ian Hornak was connected to many of the leading mid-century artists including, Robert Motherwell, Lee Krasner, Andy Warhol, Robert Indiana, Willem de Kooning and Lowell Nesbitt. Dinner for those who are interested will be served at 6:00 p.m. and is $25 for non-members and $20 for members. His lecture will begin at 7:00 p.m. and is $5 for non-members and free for museum members.</P></ul>

<h3>October 3, 2013, 7:00 PM - 8:30 PM</h3>
<div style="position:static;left:-40px;"><img src="/images/icons/w_lecture.gif" style="float:left;"></div>
<ul><P><strong>Forgotten Name, Forgotten Deeds: Ringgold Legacy</strong><br>"Forgotten Name, Forgotten Deeds: The Ringgold Legacy" is presented by George Anikis on Thursday, October 3, 2013. Held in conjunction with the release of his book by the same title, the lecture focuses on the contributions of one of Washington County's founding families, the Ringgolds - namely the contributions made by the Ringgold brothers, Major Samuel Ringgold, Rear Admiral Cadwalader Ringgold and Lieutenant Colonel George Hay Ringgold. The museum owns a beautiful miniature portrait of Major Samuel Ringgold (1796-1846) which will be on view during the lecture. Anikis graduated from the Johns Hopkins University School of Engineering and subsequently served two years at sea as a commissioned naval officer. He has held engineering positions in both private industry and government, spending the majority of his career working for NASA. He and his wife, Anne, moved to Washington County from Howard County in 1995 and from 2001 to 2011, he served on the Washington County Planning Commission, the last five years as chairman. Anikis is currently a member of the Washington County Historical Advisory Committee and the Antietam National Battlefield Advisory Committee. Dinner for those who are interested will be served at 6:00 p.m. and is $25 for non-members and $20 for members. His lecture will begin at 7:00 p.m. and is $5 for non-members and free for museum members.</P></ul>

<h3>September 29, 2013, 2:30 PM - 4:00 PM</h3>
<div style="position:static;left:-40px;"><img src="/images/icons/w_music.gif" style="float:left;"></div>
<ul><P><strong>D'Amore Duo</strong><br>The Perry/Brandenburg Recital presents the D'Amore Duo on Sunday, September 29, 2013 at 2:30 p.m. Founded in 1991, the D'Amore Duo features guitarist William Feasley and oboist Yeon Jee Sohn. Feasley was the first guitarist to receive Peabody Conservatory's Artist Diploma. Since making his debut in 1980 with the San Francisco Ballet, Feasley has maintained an active international touring schedule. Sohn received her degrees in oboe performance from Seoul National University, the Peabody Conservatory and the University of Maryland. Selections include "Serenade for Oboe and Strings" by Napoleon Coste (1805-1883), "El Chupete Azul" by Marcelo Ferraris (b. 1967) and "The Pateroller Sonata" by Peter Madlem (b. 1946). The concert is sponsored each year by Cinda and Spence Perry in memory of Mary Gaylor Brandenburg and Inez Hottsford Perry, two strong, talented and generous women who gave their children the opportunity to hear, to learn and to love music. This is a ticketed event - $5.00 for non-members, free for members and children 12 and under. A reception for the performers will follow.</P></ul>

<h3>October 13, 2013, 2:30 PM - 4:00 PM</h3>
<div style="position:static;left:-40px;"><img src="/images/icons/w_music.gif" style="float:left;"></div>
<ul><P><strong>Gallery Quartet</strong><br>Members of the Gallery Quartet will present a chamber music program featuring classical music. Gallery Quartet members include Cleveland Chandler, Jr. and Carl Tretter, violins, Jere Stern, viola and Robert Wright, cello.</P></ul>

<h3>October 27, 2013, 2:30 PM - 4:00 PM</h3>
<div style="position:static;left:-40px;"><img src="/images/icons/w_music.gif" style="float:left;"></div>
<ul><P><strong>Appalachian Wind Quintet</strong><br>For the annual Bowman Memorial Recital, the museum will present the 
Appalachian Wind Quintet. The group presents woodwind quintets from the classical period to the present. Each  performer has a rich knowledge of musical styles and vast experience with orchestral and chamber music repertoire. Formed in 1987, the group is known for its superb musicianship and innovative programming and has presented a variety of concerts and educational programs in the Mid-Atlantic region. Members of the quintet are flutist Barbara Spicher, oboist Ed Stanley, clarinetist Rick Mogensen, bassoonist Scott Cassada and French horn player Ed Schupp. This is a ticketed event - $5.00 for non-members, free for members and children 12 and under. A reception for the performers will be held following the concert. The recital is held annually in memory of Mary Bester Kneisley Bowman, a benefactor of the Museum and promoter of the Arts and History of Washington County.</P></ul>
