<p>A <b>celebrity</b> is a person, who has a prominent profile and commands some degree of public fascination and influence in day-to-day media. The term is often synonymous with wealth (commonly denoted as a person with <i>fame and fortune</i>), implied with great popular appeal, prominence in a particular field, and is easily recognized by the general public.</p>
<p>Various careers within the fields of sports and entertainment are commonly associated with celebrity status. These fields have produced prominent figures within these two industries.<sup id="cite_ref-I_want_to_be_famous_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-I_want_to_be_famous-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Western_world_kids_want_to_grow_up_to_be_famous_Children_in_developing_countries_want_to_be_doctors_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Western_world_kids_want_to_grow_up_to_be_famous_Children_in_developing_countries_want_to_be_doctors-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>While people may gain celebrity status as a result of a successful career in a particular field (primarily in the areas pertaining towards sports and entertainment), in other cases, people become celebrities due to media attention for their extravagant lifestyle or wealth (as in the case of a socialite); for their connection to a famous person (as in the case of a relative of a famous person); or even for their misdeeds (as in the case of a well-known criminal). Celebrities may be known around the world (e.g., pop stars and film actors), within a specific country (e.g., a top Australian rugby player); or within a region (e.g., a local television news anchor).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_and_cultural_implications">Regional and cultural implications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Regional and cultural implications">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Regional and cultural implications" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Cultures and regions with a significant population may have their own independent celebrity systems, with distinct hierarchies. For example, the Canadian province of Quebec, which is French-speaking, has its own system of French-speaking television, movie and music celebrities. A person who garners a degree of fame in one culture may be considered less famous or obscure in another. Some nationwide celebrities might command some attention outside their own nation; for example, the singer <a href="/wiki/Lara_Fabian" title="Lara Fabian">Lara Fabian</a> is widely known in the <a href="/wiki/French-speaking" title="French-speaking" class="mw-redirect">French-speaking</a> world, but only had a couple of <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> hits in the U.S., whereas the francophone Canadian singer <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a> is well known in both the French-speaking world and in the U.S.</p>
<p>Regions within a country, or cultural communities (linguistic, ethnic, religious) can also have their own celebrity systems, especially in linguistically or culturally distinct regions such as <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>. Regional radio personalities, <a href="/wiki/Newscasters" title="Newscasters" class="mw-redirect">newscasters</a>, politicians or community <a href="/wiki/Leader" title="Leader" class="mw-redirect">leaders</a> may be local or regional celebrities.</p>
<p>English-speaking media commentators and journalists will sometimes refer to celebrities as belonging to the <i><a href="/wiki/A-List" title="A-List" class="mw-redirect">A-List</a></i> or state that a certain actor belongs to the <i>B-List</i>, the latter being a disparaging context. These informal rankings indicate a placing within a hierarchy. However, due to differing levels of celebrity in different regions, it is difficult to place people within one bracket. A Brazilian actor might be a B-list action film actor in the US, but an A-list star in Portugal. An objective method of placing celebrities from any country into categories from <i>A-List</i> to <i>H-List</i> based on their number of <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> hits has been proposed.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Some elements are associated with fame, such as appearing on the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i>, being spoofed in <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_(magazine)" title="Mad (magazine)">Mad</a></i>, having a wax statue in <i><a href="/wiki/Madame_Tussauds" title="Madame Tussauds">Madame Tussauds</a></i>, or receiving a star on the <i><a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a>.</i></p>
<p>Certain people are known even to people unfamiliar with the area in which they excelled. If one has to name a famous boxer, they are more likely to name <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Ali" title="Muhammad Ali">Muhammad Ali</a> or <a href="/wiki/Mike_Tyson" title="Mike Tyson">Mike Tyson</a>, since their fame expanded beyond the sport itself.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Picasso" title="Pablo Picasso">Pablo Picasso's</a> style and name are known even to people who are not interested in art; likewise many know that <a href="/wiki/Harry_Houdini" title="Harry Houdini">Harry Houdini</a> was an illusionist, <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Woods" title="Tiger Woods">Tiger Woods</a> a golfer, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Gates" title="Bill Gates">Bill Gates</a> an entrepreneur, <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">Albert Einstein</a> a scientist; <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Mozart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_van_Beethoven" title="Ludwig van Beethoven">Beethoven</a> classical composers; <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Pavarotti" title="Luciano Pavarotti">Luciano Pavarotti</a> an opera singer.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fictional_implications">Fictional implications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fictional implications">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fictional implications" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The same phenomenon is true for fictional characters. When most people think of a superhero or a comic book celebrity, <a href="/wiki/Superman" title="Superman">Superman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Batman" title="Batman">Batman</a> will usually be the first that comes to mind. If one has to name a famous <a href="/wiki/Wizard_(fantasy)" title="Wizard (fantasy)" class="mw-redirect">wizard</a>, the names would typically be <a href="/wiki/Merlin" title="Merlin">Merlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gandalf" title="Gandalf">Gandalf</a> or <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Mouse" title="Mickey Mouse">Mickey Mouse</a> is perhaps the most famous <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_character" title="Cartoon character" class="mw-redirect">cartoon character</a> and fictional mouse in the world. The most famous movie <a href="/wiki/Monster" title="Monster">monsters</a> are <a href="/wiki/King_Kong" title="King Kong">King Kong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Godzilla" title="Godzilla">Godzilla</a>, the archetypical detective is <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes" title="Sherlock Holmes">Sherlock Holmes</a> and most people's idea of a <a href="/wiki/Spy" title="Spy" class="mw-redirect">spy</a> is <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Becoming_a_celebrity">Becoming a celebrity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Becoming a celebrity">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Becoming a celebrity" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>People may become celebrities in a wide range of ways: from their professions, following appearances in the media, committing a <a href="/wiki/Mass_murder" title="Mass murder">mass murder</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> or even by complete accident. The term "instant celebrity" describes someone who becomes a celebrity in a very short period of time. Someone who achieves a small amount of <a href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/transience" class="extiw" title="wikt:transience">transient</a> fame (through, say, <a href="/wiki/Media_circus" title="Media circus">hype</a> or mass media) may become labeled a "B-grade celebrity". Often, the generalization extends to someone who falls short of mainstream or persistent fame but who seeks to extend or exploit it.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Success">Success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Success">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Success" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are no guarantees of success for an individual to become a celebrity. Though celebrities come from many different working fields, most celebrities are typically associated with individuals that come from the fields of sports and entertainment or a person who is a public figure in that is commonly recognizable in mass media. With innate talent, passion, diligence, discipline, self-motivation and tenacity, being the core factors of achieving success to becoming a celebrity, fame and fortune sometimes occurs spontaneously with relatively little effort due to sheer luck, being fortunate with connections, or simply being at the right place during the right time. Celebrities are incessantly stereotyped and fantasized as individuals who possess exorbitant amounts of wealth and <a href="/wiki/Glamour_(presentation)" title="Glamour (presentation)">glamour</a>. They are also sometimes denigrated by the general public as being overpaid and publicly overrated compared to a normal worker such as a doctor, police officer or a teacher. Though glamor and wealth certainly plays a role for only famous celebrities, most people in the sports and entertainments sphere, be it music, film, television, radio, modelling, comedy, literature etc. live in obscurity and only an infinitesimal percentage (usually less than 1%) achieve fame and fortune. Due to the <a href="/wiki/Machiavellian" title="Machiavellian" class="mw-redirect">Machiavellian</a> and competitive nature within both industries, a vast amount of aspiring entertainers and athletes in the world, even some of the most talented may never be recognized and won't ever receive the opportunity to carve a name for themselves.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Difficulty">Difficulty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Difficulty">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Difficulty" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A large number of <a href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">athletes</a> who are unable to turn professional take a second job or even sometimes abandon their athletic aspirations in order to make ends meet. A small percentage of entertainers and athletes are able to make a decent living but a vast majority will spend their careers toiling from hard work, determination, rejection and frequent unemployment. For minor league to amateur athletes, earnings are usually on the lower end of the pay-scale. Many of them take second jobs on the side or even venture into other occupations within the field of sports such as coaching, <a href="/wiki/General_management" title="General management" class="mw-redirect">general management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Referee" title="Referee">refereeing</a> or recruiting and scouting up and coming athletes.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild" title="Screen Actors Guild">Screen Actors Guild</a>, a union well known for representing actors and actresses throughout Hollywood reports that the average television and film actor earns less than $5000 USD annually.<sup id="cite_ref-Actors.2C_Producers.2C_and_Directors_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Actors.2C_Producers.2C_and_Directors-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> Actors sometimes alternate between theater, television and film or even branch into other occupations within the <a href="/wiki/List_of_entertainment_industry_topics" title="List of entertainment industry topics" class="mw-redirect">entertainment industry</a> such as becoming a singer, comedian, producer, or a television host in order to be monetarily diversified, as doing one gig pays comparatively very little. For instance, <a href="/wiki/David_Letterman" title="David Letterman">David Letterman</a> is well known for branching into <a href="/wiki/Late_night_television" title="Late night television">late night television</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Talk_show_host" title="Talk show host" class="mw-redirect">talk show host</a> while honing his skills as a stand-up comedian, <a href="/wiki/Barbra_Streisand" title="Barbra Streisand">Barbra Streisand</a> ventured into acting while operating as a singer, or <a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood" title="Clint Eastwood">Clint Eastwood</a>, who achieved even greater fame in Hollywood for being a film director and a producer than for his acting credentials.</p>
<p>According to American entertainment magnate <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a>, entertainers and professional athletes make up less than 1% of all millionaires in the entire world.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> Less than 1% of all runway models are known to make more than $1000 USD for every fashion showcase. According to the US <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Labor_Statistics" title="Bureau of Labor Statistics">Bureau of Labor Statistics</a> the median wage for commercial and print models was only $11.22 per hour in 2006 and was also listed one of the top ten worst jobs in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-worst_jobs_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-worst_jobs-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Most models only draw in around $500 USD every showcase and only famous models that are high in demand such as <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Kerr" title="Miranda Kerr">Miranda Kerr</a> or <a href="/wiki/Gisele_B%C3%BCndchen" title="Gisele Bündchen">Gisele Bündchen</a> earn multi-million dollar salaries. Freelance writers and authors who aspire to be the next <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dan_Brown" title="Dan Brown">Dan Brown</a> are known to submit manuscripts of their latest literary creations hoping for their big break are only to be bombarded with numerous rejection letters from major publishing houses. Many aspiring comedians who dream of becoming the next <a href="/wiki/Russell_Peters" title="Russell Peters">Russell Peters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Seinfeld" title="Jerry Seinfeld">Jerry Seinfeld</a> never see the inside of a movie or television studio, but rather spend most of their careers doing stand-up in <a href="/wiki/Comedy_club" title="Comedy club">comedy clubs</a> and other small venues, hoping to be discovered. Because gigs can be infrequent, it can be very difficult to make a living as a freelance entertainer. As a result, many supplement their income by holding down other jobs on the side.</p>

<p>Some professional activities in fields such as ones that are commonly associated with celebrity prestige are careers within the sports and <a href="/wiki/List_of_entertainment_industry_topics" title="List of entertainment industry topics" class="mw-redirect">entertainment</a> sphere. Having a successful career such as being a <a href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">professional athlete</a> or an <a href="/wiki/Outline_of_entertainment" title="Outline of entertainment">entertainment industry based professionals</a> are careers that many average people can identify with but can only dream about pursuing. Only a very small percentage or a tiny fraction of people can ever make a name for themselves as celebrities in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_entertainment_industry_topics" title="List of entertainment industry topics" class="mw-redirect">cultural and entertainment spheres</a> (including music, film, television, radio, theater, modelling, literature etc.) or within the realm of sports.</p>
<p>Quite evidently, careers within the sports and entertainment sphere such as being an elite <a href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">professional athlete</a> on a sports team, or an <a href="/wiki/Entertainer" title="Entertainer" class="mw-redirect">entertainment figure</a> such as a musician that dominates the music charts frequently, or a television actor with lead roles on prime-time shows have a strong likelihood to become celebrities. Informal references by the general public and media have used to refer to celebrities as: <b>The stars</b>, <b>sports stars</b>, <b>rock stars</b>, <b>rap stars</b>, <b>supermodels</b>, <b>movie stars</b>, <b>TV stars</b>, <b>radio stars</b>, <b>music stars</b>, <b>superstars</b>, <b>stardom</b>, and <b>media personalities</b>.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Businessperson" title="Businessperson">Business leaders</a> which include successful entrepreneurs, financially successful investors, and top level CEO's of major corporations that regularly dominate the global business scene, top the daily business headlines and coverage of <a href="/wiki/Financial_market" title="Financial market">financial markets</a> have a strong likelihood to become celebrities. High-ranking politicians and top level <a href="/wiki/Government_official" title="Government official" class="mw-redirect">government officials</a> that dominate global political scene and foreign affairs, headline major <a href="/wiki/Current_event" title="Current event" class="mw-redirect">current events</a>, play a pivotal role in domestic and international politics have a tremendous impact in day-to-day media have a strong likelihood to become celebrities.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Prominent socialites, elite <a href="/wiki/Aristocracy_(class)" title="Aristocracy (class)">aristocrats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royal_families" title="Royal families" class="mw-redirect">royal families</a>, top level <a href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">professional athletes</a>, chart-topping musicians and pop singers, television and film actors with lead roles on prominently scheduled television shows and hit box office movies, internationally recognized supermodels and models are almost invariably celebrities.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Prominent media journalists, <a href="/wiki/Pundit_(expert)" title="Pundit (expert)" class="mw-redirect">pundits</a> on major nationally syndicated television shows, commentators on prominently scheduled television shows, nationally acclaimed media columnists and <a href="/wiki/Syndicated_columnist" title="Syndicated columnist" class="mw-redirect">syndicated columnists</a>, critically acclaimed and best-selling authors and writers, major national <a href="/wiki/Newscaster" title="Newscaster" class="mw-redirect">newscasters</a> and <a href="/wiki/News_analyst" title="News analyst">news analysts</a>, national <a href="/wiki/Reporter" title="Reporter" class="mw-redirect">television reporters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television_anchor" title="Television anchor" class="mw-redirect">television anchors</a>, national television <a href="/wiki/Game_show_host" title="Game show host" class="mw-redirect">game show hosts</a> on prominently scheduled game shows, radio personalities on prominently scheduled radio shows, comedians on major headlining comedy shows, <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">reality television personalities</a> on most prominently scheduled reality television shows, <a href="/wiki/Daytime_television" title="Daytime television">daytime television show</a> <a href="/wiki/Presenter" title="Presenter">hosts</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Late_night_television" title="Late night television">late night television show</a> <a href="/wiki/Presenter" title="Presenter">hosts</a> have a strong likelihood to become celebrities.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Individuals that host their own television show (as well as various components of television programs) have a strong likelihood to become a celebrity: Examples include shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Oprah_Winfrey_Show" title="The Oprah Winfrey Show">The Oprah Winfrey Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Doctors_(2008_TV_series)" title="The Doctors (2008 TV series)">The Doctors</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dr._Oz_Show" title="The Dr. Oz Show">The Dr. Oz Show</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Dr._Phil" title="Dr. Phil" class="mw-redirect">Dr. Phil</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Cooking_show" title="Cooking show">Cooking shows</a> such as <i><a href="/wiki/Emeril_Live" title="Emeril Live">Emeril Live</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/30_Minute_Meals" title="30 Minute Meals">30 Minute Meals</a></i> have spawned <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_chef" title="Celebrity chef">celebrity chefs</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Ramsay" title="Gordon Ramsay">Gordon Ramsay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rachael_Ray" title="Rachael Ray">Rachael Ray</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Flay" title="Bobby Flay">Bobby Flay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emeril_Lagasse" title="Emeril Lagasse">Emeril Lagasse</a> who are famed for their culinary aptitude. However fame based on one program may often prove short-lived after a program is discontinued.</li>

Gurus in all areas of life, from <a href="/wiki/Physical_fitness" title="Physical fitness">fitness</a> to real estate and <a href="/wiki/Personal_finance" title="Personal finance">personal finance</a> hand out advice and offer predictions and advice of authorities have a strong likelihood to become a celebrity. <li>A few humanitarian and religious leaders such as <a href="/wiki/Mother_Teresa" title="Mother Teresa">Mother Teresa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Desmond_Tutu" title="Desmond Tutu">Desmond Tutu</a> have achieved fame because of their charitable work around the world. Various pastors and other <a href="/wiki/Clergy" title="Clergy">religious figures</a> and activists such as <a href="/wiki/Rick_Warren" title="Rick Warren">Rick Warren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Al_Sharpton" title="Al Sharpton">Al Sharpton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Jackson" title="Jesse Jackson">Jesse Jackson</a> campaigned for various social and political causes have achieved celebrity status in mainstream media as well.</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Actors, musicians, models, directors, producers, comedians, television hosts and other entertainment-industry professionals are some of the highest-earning in America given that the entertainment industry based professional achieves a certain degree of success as a prerequisite. <a href="/wiki/Talent_agent" title="Talent agent">Talent agents</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sports_agent" title="Sports agent">sports agents</a>, <a href="/wiki/Press_agent" title="Press agent">publicists</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Talent_manager" title="Talent manager">talent managers</a> of such people get 10 percent of the gross income their successful clients make and these jobs are among the highest paying jobs in the sports and entertainment industries.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> Though it is a potentially a very lucrative field, depending on whom the agent or talent manager signs on as a client, they are less likely to become celebrities.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>While it’s true that the top television and film actors have become movie and television stars, musicians and pop singers that become pop stars, or <a href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">athletes</a> that become sports stars often become celebrities, the other professionals that play a more peripheral role in the entertainment sphere, such as television, music, and film directors and producers, screenwriters, playwrights, and <a href="/wiki/Animators" title="Animators" class="mw-redirect">animators</a> are less likely to attain celebrity status (albeit there are some exceptions, such as directors <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">Steven Spielberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino</a> and animator <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney" title="Walt Disney">Walt Disney</a>). Entertainment based occupations that are not as well known in the general public or are simply antiquated (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Illusionist" title="Illusionist" class="mw-redirect">magicians</a>, photographers, and some visual artists such as painters and <a href="/wiki/Pottery" title="Pottery">potters</a>) relative to the present day mass media industry are less likely to become celebrities (though there are exceptions such as illusionists <a href="/wiki/David_Copperfield_(illusionist)" title="David Copperfield (illusionist)">David Copperfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Criss_Angel" title="Criss Angel">Criss Angel</a>, or photographer <a href="/wiki/David_LaChapelle" title="David LaChapelle">David LaChapelle</a>).</p>
<p>In other sub-fields within the <a href="/wiki/List_of_entertainment_industry_topics" title="List of entertainment industry topics" class="mw-redirect">entertainment industry</a>, there is a similar situation. For instance, in the <a href="/wiki/Music_industry" title="Music industry">music industry</a>, the top-selling pop singers have a strong potential to become celebrities, but the behind-the-scenes professionals tend to remain little known to the general public (e.g. dancers, disc jockeys, <a href="/wiki/Record_producers" title="Record producers" class="mw-redirect">record producers</a>, and composers); though in recent years, Club DJs such as <a href="/wiki/David_Guetta" title="David Guetta">David Guetta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skrillex" title="Skrillex">Skrillex</a> and <a href="/wiki/Deadmau5" title="Deadmau5">Deadmau5</a> or composer <a href="/wiki/David_Foster" title="David Foster">David Foster</a> have achieved worldwide fame for their respective work in the music industry. High-end fashion and <a href="/wiki/Jewelry_designer" title="Jewelry designer">jewelry designers</a> are likely to become celebrities but will not garner the same and equal importance in the entertainment sphere compared to a successful film or television actor or a widely known musician.</p>
<p>A similar phenomenon is also true within the field of professional sports as <a href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">professional athletes</a> who are directly involved directly with the competition, the physical aspects of scoring and defending, as well as being widely spectated by fans are more than likely to become celebrities. However, other professionals that play a more peripheral role in the field of professional sports (e.g. coaches, <a href="/wiki/General_manager_(sports)" title="General manager (sports)" class="mw-redirect">general managers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Referee" title="Referee">referees</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sports_commentator" title="Sports commentator">commentators and announcers</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_professional_sports_team_owners" title="List of professional sports team owners">sports team owners and executives</a>, sports <a href="/wiki/NFL_commissioner" title="NFL commissioner" class="mw-redirect">commissioners</a>) who are more active in the internal aspects of field tend to remain in obscurity and are less likely to achieve celebrity status.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Outside of the sports and entertainment sphere, the top inventors, professionals such as doctors, lawyers, and scientists, etc. are unlikely to become celebrities even if they are enormously successful in their field due to society's disinterest in science, invention, medicine, and courtroom law which is not fictional.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Forbes_Celebrity_100">Forbes Celebrity 100</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Forbes Celebrity 100">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Forbes Celebrity 100" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a> Magazine releases an annual <a href="/wiki/Forbes_Celebrity_100" title="Forbes Celebrity 100">Forbes Celebrity 100</a> list of the highest paid celebrities in the world. The total earnings for all top celebrity 100 earners totaled $4.5 billion over the course of 2010 alone.</p>
<p>For instance, Forbes ranked <a href="/wiki/Media_mogul" title="Media mogul" class="mw-redirect">media mogul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Talk_show_host" title="Talk show host" class="mw-redirect">talk show host</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey" title="Oprah Winfrey">Oprah Winfrey</a> as the top earner "Forbes magazine’s annual ranking of the most powerful celebrities", with earnings of $290 million in the past year. Forbes cites that <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a> reportedly earned over $90 million in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-Lady_Gaga_Tops_Celebrity_100_List_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lady_Gaga_Tops_Celebrity_100_List-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2010, golfer <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Woods" title="Tiger Woods">Tiger Woods</a> was one of highest-earning celebrity athletes, with an income of $75 million and is consistently ranked one of the highest paid <a href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">athletes</a> in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-Lady_Gaga_Tops_Celebrity_100_List_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lady_Gaga_Tops_Celebrity_100_List-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Celebrity endorsements have proven very successful around the world where, due to increasing <a href="/wiki/Consumerism" title="Consumerism">consumerism</a>, an individual is considered a status symbol when they purchase a celebrity-endorsed product. On August 1, 2007 laws were passed banning health care professionals and public figures such as movie stars or pop singers from appearing in advertisements for <a href="/wiki/Medication" title="Medication" class="mw-redirect">drugs</a> or <a href="/wiki/Dietary_supplement" title="Dietary supplement">nutritional supplements</a>.</p>
<p>Numerous celebrities have become budding <a href="/wiki/Business_mogul" title="Business mogul" class="mw-redirect">business moguls</a> and established themselves as entrepreneurs, idolizing many well known business leaders such as <a href="/wiki/Bill_Gates" title="Bill Gates">Bill Gates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warren_Buffett" title="Warren Buffett">Warren Buffett</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>. For instance, basketball legend, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jordan" title="Michael Jordan">Michael Jordan</a> became an active entrepreneur involved with many sports related ventures including investing a minority stake in the <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Bobcats" title="Charlotte Bobcats">Charlotte Bobcats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Newman" title="Paul Newman">Paul Newman</a> started his own <a href="/wiki/Salad_dressing" title="Salad dressing" class="mw-redirect">salad dressing</a> business after leaving behind a distinguished acting career, or rap musician, <a href="/wiki/Birdman_(rapper)" title="Birdman (rapper)">Birdman</a> started his own <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>, clothing line, and an <a href="/wiki/Oil_well" title="Oil well">oil business</a>. Other celebrities such as <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Perry" title="Tyler Perry">Tyler Perry</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Lucas" title="George Lucas">George Lucas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">Steven Spielberg</a> have become successful entrepreneurs through starting their own film production companies and running their own <a href="/wiki/Movie_studio" title="Movie studio" class="mw-redirect">movie studios</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Paul_Newman_Donates_Salad_Dressing_Ownership_To_Charity_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paul_Newman_Donates_Salad_Dressing_Ownership_To_Charity-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Tabloid magazines and talk TV shows bestow a great deal of attention on celebrities. To stay in the public eye and build wealth in addition to their salaried labor, numerous celebrities have participating and branching into various business ventures and endorsements. Many celebrities have participated in many different endorsement opportunities that include: animation, publishing, fashion designing, cosmetics, <a href="/wiki/Consumer_electronics" title="Consumer electronics">consumer electronics</a>, household items and <a href="/wiki/Home_appliance" title="Home appliance">appliances</a>, cigarettes, <a href="/wiki/Soft_drink" title="Soft drink">soft drinks</a> and alcoholic beverages, <a href="/wiki/Jewelry_design" title="Jewelry design">jewelry design</a>, fast food, credit cards, video games, writing, and toys.<sup id="cite_ref-Best_And_Worst_Celebrity_Side_Businesses_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Best_And_Worst_Celebrity_Side_Businesses-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to various endorsements, a number of celebrities have been involved with some business and investment related ventures also include: and toddler related items, <a href="/wiki/List_of_professional_sports_team_owners" title="List of professional sports team owners">sports team ownership</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fashion_design" title="Fashion design">fashion retailing</a>, establishments such as restaurants, cafes, hotels, and casinos, management related ventures such as <a href="/wiki/Sports_management" title="Sports management" class="mw-redirect">sports management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Modeling_agency" title="Modeling agency">model management</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Talent_management" title="Talent management">talent management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record labels</a>, film production, publishing, <a href="/wiki/Beauty_salon" title="Beauty salon">salons</a>, health and fitness, and real estate.<sup id="cite_ref-Best_And_Worst_Celebrity_Side_Businesses_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Best_And_Worst_Celebrity_Side_Businesses-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Although some celebrities have achieved additional financial success from various business ventures, the vast majority of celebrities are not successful businesspeople and still rely on salaried labored wages in order earn a living. Most businesses and investments are well known to have a 90 to 95 percent failure rate within the first five years of operation. Not all celebrities eventually succeed with their own businesses and other related side ventures. Some celebrities either went <a href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/broke" class="extiw" title="wikt:broke">broke</a> or filed for bankruptcy as result of dabbling with such side businesses or endorsements. Though some might question such a validity since celebrities themselves are already well known, have mass appeal, and are well exposed to the general public. The average entrepreneur who is not well known and reputable to general public doesn't the same marketing flexibility and status-quo as most celebrities allow and have. Therefore compared to the average person who starts a business, celebrities already have all the cards and odds stacked in their favor. They can have an unfair advantage to expose their business ventures and endorsements and can easily capture a significant amount market share than the average entrepreneur.<sup id="cite_ref-7_Most_Embarrassing_Celebrity_Business_Failures_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7_Most_Embarrassing_Celebrity_Business_Failures-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="As_a_mass_media_phenomenon">As a mass media phenomenon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: As a mass media phenomenon">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: As a mass media phenomenon" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kim_Kardashian_2,_2012.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Kim_Kardashian_2%2C_2012.jpg/160px-Kim_Kardashian_2%2C_2012.jpg" width="160" height="234" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Kim_Kardashian_2%2C_2012.jpg/240px-Kim_Kardashian_2%2C_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Kim_Kardashian_2%2C_2012.jpg/320px-Kim_Kardashian_2%2C_2012.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kim_Kardashian_2,_2012.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf12/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>

<p>Celebrities often have fame comparable to <a href="/wiki/Royal_family" title="Royal family">royalty</a>. As a result, there is a strong public curiosity about their private affairs. The release of <a href="/wiki/Kim_Kardashian" title="Kim Kardashian">Kim Kardashian</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_sex_tape" title="Celebrity sex tape">sex tape</a> with rapper <a href="/wiki/Ray_J" title="Ray J">Ray J</a> in 2007 brought her to a new level of fame, leading to magazine covers, book deals, and <a href="/wiki/Keeping_Up_with_the_Kardashians" title="Keeping Up with the Kardashians">reality TV</a> series.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vivid_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vivid-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Celebrities may be resented for their accolades, and the public may have a love/hate relationship with celebrities. Due to the high visibility of celebrities' private lives, their successes and shortcomings are often made very public. Celebrities are alternately portrayed as glowing examples of perfection, when they garner awards, or as decadent or immoral if they become associated with a scandal. When seen in a positive light, celebrities are frequently portrayed as possessing skills and abilities beyond average people; for example, celebrity actors are routinely celebrated for acquiring new skills necessary for filming a role within a very brief time, and to a level that amazes the professionals who train them. Similarly, some celebrities with very little formal education can sometimes be portrayed as experts on complicated issues. Some celebrities have been very vocal with their political views. For example, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Damon" title="Matt Damon">Matt Damon</a> expressed his displeasure with 2008 U.S. Vice Presidential Nominee <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Palin" title="Sarah Palin">Sarah Palin</a>, as well as with the 2011 <a href="/wiki/United_States_debt-ceiling_crisis_of_2011" title="United States debt-ceiling crisis of 2011">United States debt-ceiling crisis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Famous_for_being_famous">Famous for being famous</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Famous for being famous">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Famous for being famous" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Famous_for_being_famous" title="Famous for being famous">Famous for being famous</a></div>
<p>Famous for being famous, in <a href="/wiki/Popular_culture" title="Popular culture">popular culture</a> terminology, refers to someone who attains celebrity status for no particular identifiable reason, or who achieves fame through association with a celebrity.<sup id="cite_ref-Jenkins_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jenkins-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> The term is a <a href="/wiki/Pejorative" title="Pejorative">pejorative</a>, suggesting that the individual has no particular talents or abilities.<sup id="cite_ref-JenJones_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JenJones-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup> Even when their fame arises from a particular talent or action on their part, the term will sometimes still apply if their fame is perceived as disproportionate to what they earned through their own talent or work.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Amy_Argetsinger" title="Amy Argetsinger">Amy Argetsinger</a> coined the term "famesque" to define entertainers such as actors and singers, or <a href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">professional athletes</a> who have become celebrities despite having achieved very little, if any, success in their careers.<sup id="cite_ref-Argetsinger_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Argetsinger-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup> Such is the case that includes athletes <a href="/wiki/Ronda_Rousey" title="Ronda Rousey">Ronda Rousey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Kournikova" title="Anna Kournikova">Anna Kournikova</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Leinart" title="Matt Leinart">Matt Leinart</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Danica_Patrick" title="Danica Patrick">Danica Patrick</a>, singer <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Simpson" title="Jessica Simpson">Jessica Simpson</a>, and actress <a href="/wiki/Sienna_Miller" title="Sienna Miller">Sienna Miller</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Argetsinger_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Argetsinger-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Families">Families</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Families">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Families" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Another example of celebrity is a family that has notable ancestors or is known for its wealth. In some cases, a well-known family is associated with a particular field. For example, the <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_family" title="Kennedy family">Kennedy</a> family is associated with US politics; The <a href="/wiki/House_of_Windsor" title="House of Windsor">House of Windsor</a> with royalty; <a href="/wiki/The_Osbournes" title="The Osbournes">The Osbournes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jackson_family" title="Jackson family">The Jacksons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chaplin_family" title="Chaplin family">Chaplin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Barrymore_family" title="Barrymore family">Barrymore</a> families with entertainment. Other well-known families include the <a href="/wiki/Hilton_family" title="Hilton family">Hilton</a> family, the <a href="/wiki/Bush_family" title="Bush family">Bush</a> family, and the <a href="/wiki/Kardashians" title="Kardashians" class="mw-redirect">Kardashian</a> family.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Restricted_access">Restricted access</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Restricted access">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Restricted access" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Access to celebrities is strictly controlled by their entourage of staff which includes <a href="/wiki/Publicist" title="Publicist">publicists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Talent_agent" title="Talent agent">agents</a>, <a href="/wiki/Personal_assistant" title="Personal assistant">personal assistants</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bodyguard" title="Bodyguard">bodyguards</a>. Even journalists find it difficult to access celebrities for interviews. An interview with writer and actor <a href="/wiki/Michael_Musto" title="Michael Musto">Michael Musto</a> cites:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<div class="Bug6200"><i>You have to go through many hoops just to talk to a major celebrity. You have to get past three different sets of publicists: the publicist for the event, the publicist for the movie, and then the celebrity's personal publicist. They all have to approve you.</i></div>
<div class="templatequotecite">—<a href="/wiki/Michael_Musto" title="Michael Musto">Michael Musto</a>,&#160;<cite><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>Celebrities often hire one or more bodyguards (or close protection officer) to protect themselves and their families from threats ranging from the mundane (intrusive <a href="/wiki/Paparazzi" title="Paparazzi">paparazzi photographers</a> or autograph-seeking <a href="/wiki/Fan_(person)" title="Fan (person)">fans</a>) to serious (assault, <a href="/wiki/Kidnapping" title="Kidnapping">kidnapping</a>, assassination, or <a href="/wiki/Stalking" title="Stalking">stalking</a>). The bodyguard travels with the celebrity during professional activities (movie shoots or concerts) and personal activities such as recreation and errands.</p>
<p>Celebrities also typically have security staff at their home, to protect them from similar threats.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Celebrity-stalking_has_common_threads_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Celebrity-stalking_has_common_threads-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="15_minutes_of_fame">15 minutes of fame</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 15 minutes of fame">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 15 minutes of fame" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>See also: <a href="/wiki/15_minutes_of_fame" title="15 minutes of fame">15 minutes of fame</a>, <a href="/wiki/One-hit_wonder" title="One-hit wonder">One-hit wonder</a></i></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:192px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Andy_Warhol_by_Jack_Mitchell.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Andy_Warhol_by_Jack_Mitchell.jpg/190px-Andy_Warhol_by_Jack_Mitchell.jpg" width="190" height="152" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Andy_Warhol_by_Jack_Mitchell.jpg/285px-Andy_Warhol_by_Jack_Mitchell.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Andy_Warhol_by_Jack_Mitchell.jpg/380px-Andy_Warhol_by_Jack_Mitchell.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Andy_Warhol_by_Jack_Mitchell.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf12/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Andy_Warhol" title="Andy Warhol">Andy Warhol</a> coined the phrase "<a href="/wiki/15_minutes_of_fame" title="15 minutes of fame">15 minutes of fame</a>".</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Andy_Warhol" title="Andy Warhol">Andy Warhol</a> famously coined the phrase "<a href="/wiki/15_minutes_of_fame" title="15 minutes of fame">15 minutes of fame</a>". "Celebrities" in the 21st century can now be famous simply by being in the right place at the right time. Certain "15 minutes of fame" celebrities can be average people seen with an A-list celebrity, who are sometimes noticed on entertainment news channels such as <a href="/wiki/E!_News" title="E! News">E! News</a>. These "celebs" are regular people who originally are not celebrities, becoming celebrities, and are often turned into celebrities based on the ridiculous things they do. "In fact, many reality show contestants fall into this category: the only thing that qualifies them to be on TV is that they're real."<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>One example: in 2004 British Television Personality Ahmed Aghil became so famous by appearing in Big Brother, that <b>Now</b> magazine stated "Ahmed our BB God", and a cult was created "Ahmania" short for Ahmed mania. Further, the quality newspaper The Guardian wrote a whole page about Ahmed Aghil, titled "AHMANIA RULES". But after 9 years Ahmed Aghil disappeared and only few fans do remember him. <sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Certain people are only remembered today because of a movie portrayal, certain story or <a href="/wiki/Urban_legend" title="Urban legend">urban legend</a> surrounding their life and less for their accomplishments. <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Salieri" title="Antonio Salieri">Antonio Salieri</a> was a famous and well-known 18th-century composer, but his fictional portrayal as an <a href="/wiki/Antagonist" title="Antagonist">antagonist</a> (for example, in the musical and film <i><a href="/wiki/Amadeus_(film)" title="Amadeus (film)">Amadeus</a></i>) has been more famous than his music since the end of the 20th century. <a href="/wiki/Roscoe_%22Fatty%22_Arbuckle" title="Roscoe &quot;Fatty&quot; Arbuckle" class="mw-redirect">Roscoe "Fatty" Arbuckle</a> and <a href="/wiki/O._J._Simpson" title="O. J. Simpson">O. J. Simpson</a> are more notorious for their association with murder trials than for their respective movie and sports careers. <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a> is more famous as a politician today than as a movie actor. Centuries after his death, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Mantegna" title="Andrea Mantegna">Andrea Mantegna</a> is now better known as the mentor of <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci" title="Leonardo da Vinci">Leonardo da Vinci</a> than for his own paintings.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_networking">Social networking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Social networking">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Social networking" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Celebrities have been flocking to <a href="/wiki/Social_networking" title="Social networking" class="mw-redirect">social networking</a> and video hosting sites such as YouTube, Twitter, Facebook, Google+, and MySpace.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup> Social networking sites allow celebrities to communicate directly with their fans, removing the middle-man known as traditional media. Social media humanizes celebrities in a way that arouses public fascination as evident by the success of magazines such as <i><a href="/wiki/Us_Weekly" title="Us Weekly">Us Weekly</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/People_(magazine)" title="People (magazine)">People Weekly</a></i>. Celebrity blogging have also spawned stars such as <a href="/wiki/Perez_Hilton" title="Perez Hilton">Perez Hilton</a> who is well known for not only blogging, but also <a href="/wiki/Outing" title="Outing">outing</a> celebrities.<sup id="cite_ref-Perez_Hilton.E2.80.99s_gay_witch_hunt_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Perez_Hilton.E2.80.99s_gay_witch_hunt-60"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup> Celebrities are now much more accessible to the public, through social media and also via <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.thehandbook.com/celebrities">celebrity databases</a>, which provide their agent and manager details.</p>
<p>Social media sites have even catapulted some to fame. <a href="/wiki/Tila_Tequila" title="Tila Tequila">Tila Tequila</a> for example, shot to stardom on MySpace.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_implications">Health implications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Health implications">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Health implications" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Common threats such as <a href="/wiki/Stalking" title="Stalking">stalking</a> have spawned a term called <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Worship_Syndrome" title="Celebrity Worship Syndrome" class="mw-redirect">Celebrity Worship Syndrome</a>, which a person becomes overly involved with the details of a celebrity's personal life.<sup id="cite_ref-Schumaker_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Schumaker-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup> Psychologists have indicated that though many people obsess over glamorous film, television, sport and <a href="/wiki/Pop_star" title="Pop star" class="mw-redirect">pop stars</a> per the disparity in salaries in society seems to value <a href="/wiki/Professional_athlete" title="Professional athlete" class="mw-redirect">professional athletes</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_entertainment_industry_topics" title="List of entertainment industry topics" class="mw-redirect">entertainment industry based professionals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64"><span>[</span>64<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65"><span>[</span>65<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
