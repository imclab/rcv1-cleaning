		<h1>10 Great LGBT Military Movies </h1>

    <h3>
      September 23 2011 5:00 AM ET 
          </h3>

        	    <p>In honor of the “don’t ask, don’t tell” repeal, we wanted to<br />
mention military flicks that are actually relevant to a queer or trans audience.<br />
But leaving out documentaries (like the brilliant <em>Paragraph 175</em>) and some great films like <em>Aimee &amp;<br />
Jaguar</em> and <em>November Moon</em> — all of which tackle the Nazi war era but from a<br />
primarily civilian perspective — it’s clear that movies about LGBT soldiers are<br />
still in their infancy. </p>
<p>Perhaps in the next decade we’ll see our community serving<br />
openly in a flourish of diverse military films (like <em>A Few Good Men</em>, <em>Universal Soldier</em>, <em>Avatar</em>,<br />
all of which we could argue for inclusion on this list but none that have<br />
openly LGBT characters). </p>
<p><PageImage/><strong><em>Serving in Silence: The Margarethe Cammermeyer Story</em> (1995) Lesbian</strong></pageimage/></p>
<p>
</p><p>A perfect one to watch to remind viewers of why DADT was a<br />
flawed policy. Glenn Close and Judy Davis both won Emmy awards for their roles<br />
in this iconic made-for-TV film about a decorated Army officer who decides to<br />
upgrade her security rating and in doing so tells an investigator she’s gay. As<br />
the Army proceeds to discharge her for the omission, Close as Cammermeyer<br />
fights back — alongside her family, partner and Lambda Legal. Cammermeyer wrote<br />
the intro to a <a href="http://www.advocate.com/Print_Issue/Advance/Five_Women_Who_Made_WAVES/">new<br />
book</a> on the time as well, called Making WAVES, which she surely did. </p>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: "Times New Roman";">In the videos, below, watch<br />
Close with Ryan Reynolds as her son, and then Close and commander: </span> </p>
<p><iframe width="560" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/BGUKmk--V-Q" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>
<p><iframe width="420" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/RNYFriU-ZpQ" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><img border="0" src="http://www.advocate.com/uploadedImages/ADVOCATE/ARTS_AND_ENTERTAINMENT/FILM/2011/1serving_silence.jpg" alt="SERVING IN SILENCE (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" title="SERVING IN SILENCE (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" style="border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" /><strong><em>Walk on Water</em></strong><strong><br />
(2004), Gay</strong> <br/><br />
Director Eytan Fox’s 2002 gay<br />
film, <em>Yossi<br />
and Jagger,</em> offered up a love story<br />
of two Israeli soldiers, but this later film is equally remarkable. It follows<br />
an Israeli Mossad agent assigned to assassinate a Nazi war criminal and<br />
questions the nature of what it means to hate and the nature of revenge. It’s<br />
not what some would call a “gay” film but it invariably is in the widest sense<br />
of the word. Watch a clip below (but it includes a spoiler).<iframe width="560" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/8hrI7brn5OM" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><img border="0" src="http://www.advocate.com/uploadedImages/ADVOCATE/ARTS_AND_ENTERTAINMENT/FILM/2011/1walkonwater.jpg" alt="WALK ON WATER (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" title="WALK ON WATER (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" style="border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" /><strong><em>Soldier’s Girl</em></strong><strong><br />
(2004), Transgender </strong> <br/><br />
One of the most provocative movies about military life is<br />
the story of Barry Winchell (played by Troy Garity) who falls in love with a<br />
transgender performer  while<br />
stationed on an infantry base in Tennessee. As his romantic relationship<br />
develops, Winchell’s friendship with his fellow soldiers — especially the<br />
repressed guy who dragged him to a drag club to begin with — and his safety in<br />
the military wither. Based on a true story of Calpernia Addams (now an actress<br />
who consulted on the film) and the real Barry Winchell, <em>Soldier’s Story</em> is an intelligent and heartbreaking must-see. After<br />
you’re done crying, look up what Calpernia’s up to now for inspiration of life<br />
after tragedy.<iframe width="420" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/1sRGi4UFqj8" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><img border="0" src="http://www.advocate.com/uploadedImages/ADVOCATE/ARTS_AND_ENTERTAINMENT/FILM/2011/1soldiersgirl.jpg" alt="SOLDIER'S GIRL (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" title="SOLDIER'S GIRL (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" style="border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" /><strong><em>Private Benjamin</em></strong><strong><br />
(1980) Bisexual</strong> <br/><br />
As the sheltered, spoiled Paris Hilton of her day, Goldie<br />
Hawn is brilliant as the Jewish American Judy Goodman-Benjamin, a woman who<br />
accidentally enlists in the Army after her hubby drops dead during sex on their<br />
wedding night. Boot camp kicks her ass, but Private Benjamin discovers she’s<br />
got a fight in her as well. Oft her nemesis, is the excellent Eileen Brennan as<br />
bisexual Captain<br />
Doreen Lewis, an uptight military rule-seeker who finds Benjamin’s<br />
frivolousness contemptuous. Lewis — who’s played for laughs a lot — starts the<br />
film in love with a man but in later scenes finds her constant companion in<br />
Sally Kirkland’s Helga and while a big bisexual (or lesbian) coming out would<br />
have been nice, this is the Army pre-2011we’re talking about.<iframe width="420" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/Dnpw4RBsKyo" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><img border="0" src="http://www.advocate.com/uploadedImages/ADVOCATE/ARTS_AND_ENTERTAINMENT/FILM/2011/1PrivateBenjamin.jpg" alt="PRIVATE BENJAMIN (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" title="PRIVATE BENJAMIN (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" style="border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" /><strong><em>Top<br />
Gun</em></strong><strong> (1986), Gay<br />
Adjacent</strong><br/><br />
OK so there’s no LGBT film festival playing this classic at a midnight showing,<br />
but <em>Top Gun</em> —<br />
the story of a handful of guys training to become elite fighter pilots for the<br />
U.S. — has some of the best homoeroticism (just say “Iceman” to any gay man and<br />
dreams of Val Kilmer’s half naked body still comes to mind), a bromance between<br />
Maverick and Goose (Tom Cruise and Anthony Edwards), and a closeted lesbian<br />
actress playing a hot semi-cougar flying instructor (Kelly McGillis). It don’t<br />
get much gayer than that. One enterprising YouTuber even recut the film as a 2<br />
½ minute love story between Kilmer and Cruise.<iframe width="420" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/ekXxi9IKZSA" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><img border="0" src="http://www.advocate.com/uploadedImages/ADVOCATE/ARTS_AND_ENTERTAINMENT/FILM/2011/1topgun_valkilmer.jpg" alt="TOP GUN (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" title="TOP GUN (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" style="border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" /><strong><em>A Marine Story</em></strong><strong><br />
(2010) Lesbian</strong> <br/><br />
It’s no wonder lesbians (and awards boards) love this film:<br />
star Dreya Weber, a hard bodied actress and aerial performer who starred in the<br />
<em>Gymnast</em>, and whose abs rival Jillian<br />
Michaels. Weber is Alex, a Marine who is discharged, goes home, plays Mr.<br />
Miyagi to a local teen who needs direction (Paris Pickard as Saffron).<br />
Throughout, both must face their proverbial demons. Did I mention the hard<br />
body?<iframe width="560" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/LF_7tdZAzP8" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><img border="0" src="http://www.advocate.com/uploadedImages/ADVOCATE/ARTS_AND_ENTERTAINMENT/FILM/2011/1marine_story2.jpg" alt="A MARINE STORY (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" title="A MARINE STORY (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" style="border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" /><strong><em>Stripes</em></strong><strong><br />
(1981) Gay Adjacent</strong> <br/><br />
This Bill Murray classic is an early one for the comedy<br />
king, who has played transgender (<em>Ed Wood</em>),<br />
gay adjacent (<em>What About Bob</em>, <em>Little<br />
Shop of Horrors</em>), and a lover of lesbians (<em>Wild<br />
Things</em>, <em>Broken Flowers</em>). Murray and Ivan Reitman (himself a comedy legend,<br />
behind the screen mostly) are new Army inducts at the start of the film, where<br />
a hilarious exchange between a recruiter in the pre-DADT era shows how much the<br />
funny men’s characters were OK with their sexualities. As the recruiter asks if<br />
either is gay, Murray says, “You mean like flaming?” and Reitman says “No but<br />
we’re willing to learn.” Hilarious.<iframe width="560" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/_L82jlR-37k" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><img border="0" src="http://www.advocate.com/uploadedImages/ADVOCATE/ARTS_AND_ENTERTAINMENT/FILM/2011/1stripes.jpg" alt="STRIPES (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" title="STRIPES (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" style="border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" /><strong><em>G.I. Jane</em></strong><strong><br />
(1997) Lesbian Adjacent</strong> <br/><br />
Sure, she’s supposedly straight to begin with but after a<br />
few designating scenes, Demi Moore as the Navy Seal Lt. Jordan O’Neal in <em>G.I.<br />
Jane</em> morphs into a butch hard-body. We<br />
never see her having sex with women but she turns down male advances, hangs<br />
with women coded lesbian on the beach, endures test after test to prove she can<br />
take it like a man, and at one point tells another soldier to suck her cock.<iframe width="560" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/lQg28Qwmaro" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><img border="0" src="http://www.advocate.com/uploadedImages/ADVOCATE/ARTS_AND_ENTERTAINMENT/FILM/2011/1GI-Jane.jpg" alt="G.I. JANE (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" title="G.I. JANE (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" style="border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" /><strong><em>Aliens</em></strong><strong><br />
(1986) Lesbian</strong> <br/><br />
Greatest. Sequel. Ever. (Don’t ever start with me <em>Godfather<br />
II</em> fans.) Sigourney Weaver is Lieutenant Ellen L.<br />
Ripley and Jenette Goldstein plays the lovely butchy Marine Pvt. Vasquez. They are space<br />
Marines on a quasi civilian-meets-military operation (the parameters of<br />
“company” is never clear) to a colony Ripley believes is inhabited by aliens.<br />
Ripley — no slouch in the body department — must accompany a team of rugged<br />
tough asses to the planet and survive. Thankfully, Vasquez is there to help.<iframe width="420" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/eycDq4NW88o" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><img border="0" src="http://www.advocate.com/uploadedImages/ADVOCATE/ARTS_AND_ENTERTAINMENT/FILM/2011/1aliens.jpg" alt="ALIENS (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" title="ALIENS (COURTESY) ADVOCATE.COM" style="border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" /><strong><em>Platoon</em></strong><strong><br />
(1986) Gay</strong> <br/><br />
Whole dissertations have been written about<br />
why <em>Platoon</em> is a gay film, or at the<br />
very least, so rife with homoerotic subtext that you’d have to be Fred Phelps<br />
to miss it. And it has little to do with the fact that there are barely any<br />
female roles in the movie. Christina Judith Hein’s <em><a href="http://www-copas.uni-regensburg.de/articles/issue_8/Christina_Judith_Hein.php">Battleground<br />
Masculinity: Gendertroublers and Gatekeepers in Oliver Stone's Platoon</a></em> is among the best to explain its<br />
gayness; read her if you doubt me. In the film, Charlie Sheen is Army private<br />
who volunteers for combat during the height of Vietnam in part because he’s an<br />
idealist. He soldiers through bullying and atrocities and along the way is torn<br />
between two camps of men — one hyper masculinized, the other more modern, and<br />
thus, more feminized men. The latter camp, led by Willem Defoe as Sgt. Elias,<br />
full of smoking and dancing and half-naked men, feels queer and uninhibited. As<br />
Hein points out, “ Rather untypical of a courageous and<br />
responsible authority figure in a war movie, [Elias] acknowledges Chris's<br />
presence by waving at him, languidly reclined in a hammock, only half-clad, and<br />
sensually eating a banana. The ceremony of initiation that he performs later on<br />
with newly recruited Chris involves not only the passing of marihuana smoke<br />
through the phallic barrel of a gun but is also accompanied by a conspiratorial<br />
look and smile on the side of Elias and a somewhat curious yet disconcerted<br />
gaze by Chris – as though he were checking if anyone might watch and disapprove<br />
of this erotically charged moment. With its homoerotic overtones, the scene is<br />
strikingly reminiscent of the sexually coded passing of smoke through a straw<br />
in Jean Genet's gay prison art film, <em>Un Chant d'Amour</em> (1950), and it dissolves into a scene<br />
of soldiers relaxedly dancing with each other.” The movie is riveting and<br />
challenging and imbued with a sense of what homo-awakening might have been like<br />
for some men during the ‘60s sexual liberation.<iframe width="560" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/pPi8EQzJ2Bg" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></br/></br/></br/></br/></br/></br/></br/></br/></br/></p>
