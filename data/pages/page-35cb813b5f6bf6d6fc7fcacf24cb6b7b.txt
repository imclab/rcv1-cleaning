<h1>Buying and Selling a Car in Spain</h1>
<h2>Information on how, as a foreigner in Spain, you can buy a new or used car. Includes information on what forms to fill out and how to find your local traffic department (<em>Jefatura de Trafico)</em>...</h2>

<p>A foreigner may buy a Spanish-registered car in Spain provided they have one of the following:</p>

<ul>
	<li>an official residency card (<em>Tarjeta de Residencia</em>) <em>or</em></li>
	<li>proof that they are a Spanish home owner (by showing title deeds) <em>or</em></li>
	<li> documentation that proves the holder is a registered member of the local community, the <strong><a class="external" href="/moving/certificado-de-empadronamiento/"><em>certificado de empadronamiento</em></a></strong> (available     from the Town Hall / <em>Ayuntamiento</em>) issued within the three months prior to     the purchase) <em>or</em></li>
	<li>a rental contract for a duration of minimum one year <em>or</em></li>
	<li>an<strong> <a class="external" href="/moving/nie-number/">NIE/NIF</a></strong> number (Foreigner's Identification     Number) in Spain</li>
</ul>
<h5>Buying a New or Used Car in Spain</h5>

<p>A new or used vehicle bought (or leased) from a dealership will almost certainly have the registration and ownership transfer handled by the dealer. The dealer may also offer insurance contracts. Many dealerships will take the old vehicle as part exchange.</p>

<p>Used cars (<em><strong>coches </strong></em><strong><em><strong>d</strong>e segunda mano</em></strong> / <strong><em>coches de occasion</em></strong>) are available for purchase through dealerships or private sale. They may be advertised in automobile magazines and journals such as <em>Coche Actual</em>, <em> Mi Coche</em>, <em> Motor en Mano</em>, and<em> Anuncio Motor</em>, through classified advertisements in newspapers, or on websites such as <a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.ebay.es"><strong>eBay Spain</strong></a> and <strong><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.supermotor.com/">Supermotor</a></strong>.</p>

<h4>The sale and exchange</h4>

<p>When buying a second-hand used car it is important to see originals of all the paperwork. This is particularly important when buying privately, to ensure that the person selling the car has the right to sell it. Be sure to match the name on the paperwork to those on the sellers' personal identification document.</p>

<p>Once the sale is agreed, the seller provides the following:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Vehicle registration document (<em><strong>Permiso de Circulación</strong></em>) </li>
	<li>Proof of roadworthiness (a valid <strong>ITV test certificate</strong>)</li>
	<li>Receipt for <strong> municipal vehicle tax</strong> (<em>Impuesto Municipal sobre Vehículos     de Tracción Mecánica</em>)</li>
	<li>Personal identification such as an NIE/NIF card (plus photocopy for the     buyer)</li>
</ul>

<p>The buyer and seller should draw up a <strong>sale agreement </strong> (<em>Contrato de Compraventa</em>) or the seller may provide an <strong>invoice</strong>. Either should note the details of the     vehicle, names of buyer and seller, the sale price agreed and date and time that the transfer takes place (this final point is so that if there are traffic fines associated with the vehicle they can be assigned to the correct person).</p>

<p>The buyer provides a <strong> transfer of ownership</strong> form (<em>Solicitud de Transmisión de Vehículos</em>) which is signed by the buyer and the seller. It is a duplicate form with the buyer and seller each keeping a copy.<br />
 <strong></strong></p>

<p><strong>Note</strong>: Further documentation may be requested by some province or autonomous region authorities.</p>

<ul>
	<li>The transfer of ownership form (<em>Solicitud de Transmisión de Vehículos</em>) can be     downloaded from the DGT (<em><strong>Dirección General de Tráfico</strong></em>) website: <a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.dgt.es/portal/es/oficina_virtual/vehiculos/modelos_impresos/"><strong>Click     here</strong></a></li>
</ul>
<h4>Payment</h4>

<p>A bank cheque (<strong><em>Cheque Bancario</em></strong>) is generally regarded as the normal way to make large payments in a private transaction in Spain. Personal cheques are not guaranteed.</p>

<h4>Insurance</h4>

<p>The existing insurance may be transferred by the seller to the new owner. The seller may instead choose to cancel the policy or transfer it to another vehicle and the new owner may arrange their own insurance. The insurance must be arranged before the new owner drives the vehicle.</p>

<ul>
	<li>Information from the DGT: <a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.dgt.es/portal/es/oficina_virtual/vehiculos/cambio_titularidad"><strong>Click     here</strong></a> (in Spanish) </li>
</ul>
<h5>Registration of the Change of Owner (<em>Transferencia de Vehículos</em>)</h5>

<p>Transfer of ownership is finalised at the <em>Jefatura de Tráfico</em>. The buyer and seller may go together to submit paperwork, or do it individually. It is possible to visit the traffic office in person or to employ a <em>gestor</em> to do so.</p>

<h4>What the seller does</h4>

<p>The seller should notify the local Traffic Office (<em>Jefatura de Tráfico</em>) of sale or transfer. Take a copy of the registration document and completed transfer of ownership form to the traffic department.</p>

<p>Within 15 days of the sale, the buyer should be able to provide the seller with proof that the paperwork has been completed. If this is not possible the seller should contact the local Traffic Office to inform them that the car has been sold (<em>notificación de venta</em>).</p>

<p>Use the <em>Notificación Transmisión de Vehículos</em> form.</p>

<ul>
	<li>Notification of sale form (<em>Notificación Transmisión de Vehículos</em>): <a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.dgt.es/was6/portal/contenidos/documentos/oficina_virtual/vehiculos/cambio_titularidad/9.07A-Castellano.pdf"><strong>Click     here</strong></a><strong> </strong>(PDF in Spanish)</li>
</ul>

<p>It is important to make sure that the transfer is properly completed, otherwise the seller may be held responsible for the unpaid tax or fines incurred by the new owner. The seller may apply to the local Traffic Office for deregistration papers (<em>baja de vehículo</em>) to avoid later consequences.</p>

<ul>
	<li>Application form (<em>Solicitud de baja de vehículo</em>) can be downloaded     from the DGT website: <a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.dgt.es/portal/es/oficina_virtual/vehiculos/modelos_impresos/"><strong>Click     here</strong></a></li>
</ul>
<h4>What the buyer does</h4>

<p>The buyer must register the vehicle in their name at the vehicle registry (<em>Registro de Vehículos</em>) at the local traffic department (<em>Jefatura de Tráfico</em>).</p>

<p>This transfer of the vehicle's registration document must be made within 30 days of purchase. The cost of the transfer is currently 4 percent of the value of the vehicle, however if a buyer chooses to use the help of a <em>gestor</em>, the buyer will pay an additional fee for this service.</p>

<ul>
	<li>To find the local <strong><em>Jefatura de Tráfico</em></strong>: <strong><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.dgt.es/portal/es/oficina_virtual/dir_telefonos/jefaturas/?tit=%C2%BFD%C3%B3nde%20se%20puede%20solicitar?">Click     here</a></strong> (in Spanish)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>The documents to supply are</strong>:</p>

<ul>
	<li>The copy of the transfer of ownership form (<em>Solicitud de transmisión de vehículos</em>) </li>
	<li>The copy of the sale agreement and a copy of the ID of the seller</li>
	<li>The relevant paperwork provided by the seller: 
<ul>
	<li>Vehicle registration document (<em>Permiso de Circulación</em>) </li>
	<li>ITV certificate as proof of roadworthiness</li>
	<li>Receipt for municipal vehicle tax </li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<p>In addition, a foreign buyer will need to provide the following documents to complete the transfer:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Proof of habitation in Spain as outlined above (proof by means of <em>residencia</em>, NIE certificate,     local town registry document, <em>Certificado     de Empadronamiento</em>,     property title deeds or rental contract)</li>
</ul>
