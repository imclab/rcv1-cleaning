style="clear:both;"></div><p>We&#8217;ve always liked the clean lines of this Audi Quattro Coupe body style but in US models were known for weak engines and limited tuning potential. What they all need is delivered in this custom example; perfect stance, S2 front body panels, and upgraded 20V ur-S4 power. Find it available <a
target="_self" href="http://bringatrailer.com/thats-an-older-listing-its-no-longer-available/#r=true&year=&car=%20"  target="_blank">here on VWVortex</a> Forums in Renton, Washington for $12,500 where it was for sale a while back and is now offered at this lower price.</p><p><a
target="_self" href="http://bringatrailer.com/thats-an-older-listing-its-no-longer-available/#r=true&year=&car=%20" target="_blank"><img
title="1990 Audi Turbo Quattro Coupe 20V Transplant Front" src="http://bringatrailer.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/10/1990_Audi_Turbo_Quattro_Coupe_20V_Transplant_Front_1.jpg" border="0" alt="1990 Audi Turbo Quattro Coupe 20V Transplant Front" width="480" height="340" /></p><p><span
id="more-2365"></span></a></p><p>The S2 bodywork which integrates the grille into the hood and offers wrap-around projector headlamps was never offered on US-spec coupes but was used on convertibles the fantatic RS2 wagons. The bumper is also an RS2 unit and the whole car is painted in Glasurit Aviator Gray Pearl from an Audi TT. The wheels are the highly polished factory units from an A6 4.2 V8 sedan. Special thanks to Andrew Holliday of <a
target="_self" href="http://bringatrailer.com/thats-an-older-listing-its-no-longer-available/#r=true&year=&car=%20"  target="_blank">SnapRiot</a> for the action shot above.</p><p><img
title="1990 Audi Turbo Quattro Coupe 20V Transplant Rear" src="http://bringatrailer.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/10/1990_Audi_Turbo_Quattro_Coupe_20V_Transplant_Rear_1.jpg" border="0" alt="1990 Audi Turbo Quattro Coupe 20V Transplant Rear" width="480" height="319" /></p><p>The car utilizes parts engineered by <a
target="_self" href="http://bringatrailer.com/thats-an-older-listing-its-no-longer-available/#r=true&year=&car=%20"  target="_blank">2Bennett</a> and <a
target="_self" href="http://bringatrailer.com/thats-an-older-listing-its-no-longer-available/#r=true&year=&car=%20"  target="_blank">034 Motorsport</a>, two of the best Audi shops around. California buyers might even be able to get the car through SMOG testing if it is taken to one of those shops for preparation. The turbo engine really wakes up the car. class="commentbody"><p>Nice conversion and appears to be very cleanly done. Too bad he insists on posting that photoshop-enhanced-cartoon-pic as the lead as the car doesn&#8217;t seem to need the fakery.</p><p>A AAN engine is very reliable, once you learn about a few preventative things like ignition and cam sensors.  This one appears to be &#8220;RS-2 plus&#8221; level which means lots more power but should still be highly street-driveable, and if not, can easily be down-tuned in software. Having the factory RS2 Porsche intake manifold gets extra bonus points and I wonder if he actually somehow scored a real RS2 Euro motor for this? Most of these conversions are in-place upgrades of stock AAN&#8217;s from UrS4-S6&#8242;s.</p><p>Re: the emissions &#8211; should NOT be a big deal even in CA. OBD-I and a tailpipe sniff should be the MOST that is required for this car, and something that is entirely passable for an AAN motor unless there are drastic valve or ring or turbo blow-by issues.</p><p>Other mods are all exactly right for this kind of car including the Porsche monoblocks. A few slightly dumb poser items like the shaved rear wiper wouldn&#8217;t put me off this but the interior pics hint that it might be a bit rougher inside than outside.  Get the impression that lots of money went into mechanicals and paintjob but almost none into interior restoration, which is now due.</p><p>These cars are much more fun to drive than the period reviews would have you believe, even with the underpowered 7A normally aspirated motor.  Yes heavy and not a Porsche competitor in handling or quickness but a fun daily driver nonetheless and looks really good with the Euro nose bits as this one has. Stick this 300++bhp RS2 AAN in it and you&#8217;ve really got something&#8230;</p><div
