        <td bgcolor="#DDDDDD"><font face="Arial">In terms of the lives of average people, there is
        little doubt that the automobile is the most revolutionary invention in the history of
        transportation since the wheel.&nbsp;The basic premise of the automobile is simple; choose
        a wheeled vehicle from the many types typically pulled by horses or oxen, add a motor and
        create a self-propelled, personal transportation vehicle. </font></td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td bgcolor="#DDDDDD" valign="top" align="left" colspan="2"><font face="Arial">The
        earliest ancestor of the modern automobile is probably the Fardier, a three-wheeled,
        steam-powered, 2.3-mph vehicle built in 1771 by Nicolas Joseph Cugnot for the French
        minister of war.&nbsp; This cumbersome machine was never put into production because it
        was much slower and harder to operate than a horse-drawn vehicle. <p>Amedee Bollee, also a
        Frenchman, built an improved 12-passenger steam car in 1873, but the steam engine proved
        impractical for a machine that was intended to challenge the speed of a
        horse-and-buggy.&nbsp; The invention of the practical automobile had to await the
        invention of a workable internal combustion engine.</font></td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td bgcolor="#DDDDDD" valign="middle" align="center">
        <img src="../../images/inventions/auto-c.jpg" alt="First Automoblie Patent - 1886" border="5" width="100" height="150"></td>
        <td bgcolor="#DDDDDD" valign="top" align="left"><font face="Arial">The milestone vehicle
        was built in Germany in 1889 by Gottlieb Daimler and Wilhelm Maybach.&nbsp; Powered by a
        1.5 hp, two-cylinder gasoline engine, it had a four-speed transmission and traveled at 10
        mph.&nbsp; Another German, Karl Benz, also built a gasoline-powered car the same year. The
        gasoline-powered automobile, or motor car, remained largely a curiosity for the rest of
        the nineteenth century, with only a handful being manufactured in Europe and the United
        States.&nbsp; </font></td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td bgcolor="#DDDDDD" colspan="2"><font face="Arial">The first automobile to be produced
        in quantity was the 1901 Curved Dash Oldsmobile, which was built in the United States by
        Ransom E. Olds. Modern automobile mass production, and its use of the modern industrial
        assembly line, is credited to Henry Ford of Detroit, Michigan, who had built his first
        gasoline-powered car in 1896.&nbsp; Ford began producing his Model T in 1908, and by 1927,
        when it was discontinued, over 18 million had rolled off the assembly line.</font></td>
      </tr>
