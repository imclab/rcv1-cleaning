<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://xaivierchia.blogspot.com/2011/01/procedure-of-selling-buying-second-hand.html'>Procedure of Selling / Buying a Second-Hand Car in Malaysia</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'>
<span class='post-labels'>
Categorised as:
<a href='http://xaivierchia.blogspot.com/search/label/Business' rel='tag'>Business</a>,
<a href='http://xaivierchia.blogspot.com/search/label/Experince%20Sharing' rel='tag'>Experince Sharing</a>,
<a href='http://xaivierchia.blogspot.com/search/label/Motor' rel='tag'>Motor</a>
</span>
<span class='post-comment-link'>
</span>
</div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<b>1. Document preparation </b><br />
i. Get a set of <b>transfer forms</b> needed in the nearest J.P.J. branch.<br />
a) Borang Semak (TM3).<br />
b) Transfer of ownership form (JPJK3). (JPJK3A is for deceased owner)<br />
c) Form to certify new ownership (TM-AB). (It is needed only if the new owner does not able present to JPJ)<br />
<br />
ii. <b>Fill up</b> the form and <b>sign it </b>wherever necessary.<br />
<br />
iii. The new owner&#8217;s identification card or MyKad have to be <b>certified by</b>:<br />
a) An officer of the Transport Department.<br />
b) A Commissioner of Oaths.<br />
c) A Justice of Peace of J.P.<br />
d) A Judge from the Session Court.<br />
<a name='more'></a><br />
(If you wish to save some money, you could go to J.P.J. office. An officer there will help you certify the above documents.)<br />
<br />
<b>2. Puspakom -</b><b> Car checking </b><br />
Find the nearest Puspakom center to do this checking.<br />
(please check it at http://www.puspakom.com.my/html/inspection_branches.aspx). Although your car should be in a good condition to pass the test, it is good idea to send it for servicing before sending it for inspection purposes. After that, Puspakom authorities will supply a K5 certificate which is required in the car transferring by the J.P.J. authorities.In my case (private car), it took me about one hour and the cost is just RM30.<br />
<br />
<b>3. New insurance purchasing</b><br />
The new owner is required to buy a new insurance (under his/her name).<br />
<br />
<b>4. Submitting all the documents and the transfer form to the J.P.J. for transfer.</b><br />
Another cost is a transfer fee of RM100. That's all  for today. More fascinating articles and sharing will be  updated from time to time in Xaivier Blog. Therefore, you are welcome to  <a href="http://xaivierchia.blogspot.com/p/free-subscribe.html">subscribe our feed</a>, look at our <a href="http://xaivierchia.blogspot.com/p/site-map-of-xaivier-blog.html">sitemap</a> or simply visit our <a href="http://xaivierchia.blogspot.com/">Homepage</a>.<br />
<br />
Written by: Xaivier Chia<br />

P/S: I paid RM250 for so-called "middle man" (Motor Insurance Agent) for processing step 1, 3 and 4 after I settle <b>Puspakom</b> myself. In fact, it was a not bad experience for me in Puspakom. Just drive my car to Puspakom and then wait for checking. That's all. Simple and save money.<br />
<br />
P/S: For latest information about "Procedure of Selling / Buying a Second-Hand Car in Malaysia", please consult J.P.J. officer in your nearest J.P.J. branch or log on www.jpj.gov.my.

<p>
For latest information about &quot;Procedure of Selling / Buying a Second-Hand Car in Malaysia&quot;, please consult J.P.J. officer in your nearest J.P.J. branch or log on www.jpj.gov.my.<br /><br />-----------------------------------------------<br />By the way, I found any other similar post that might be useful as stated below:<br /><br />Items to be presented at JPJ:<br />1. RM100 postal order. <br />2. A4 sized photocopy of IC (seller &amp; buyer), front &amp; back on the same page. <br />3. Car Registration Card of course. <br />4. 2 copies of Form JPJK3 (filled &amp; signed by both parties. Forms can be downloaded from internet. Don&#39;t mistaken JPJK3 for JPJK3A which is for deceased owner.) <br />5. Presence of seller &amp; buyer at JPJ. If the seller cannot be present, he/she has to sign another JPJ form titled &#39;Surat Akuan&#39; (which is available from JPJ, not online, or from any Commissioner of Oath) in front of a Commissioner of Oath as well as have the commissioner verify and stamp the photocopy of IC. <br />Any other letter from the C of O is not valid. After that, the buyer&#39;s presence alone at JPJ is adequate to complete the transfer. <br /><br />Asked an agent at JPJ how much they charge if they were to do it for me, RM130. These agents have got all the forms ready, even blank &#39;Surat Akuan&#39; signed and stamped by commissioner of oath! <br /><br />Source:<br />http://www.motortrader.com.my/cars/forums/forumsearch.aspx?forumid=148&amp;threadid=935305
</p>

<p>
Hi Mr. Fox,<br /><br />For insurance matter:<br />Buyer, new owner, must have an insurance policy with his name; <br />Seller can transfer existing NCD to his new car;<br />If the insurance still not reach its maturity date (one year, normally), seller can refund it, or transfer/sell it to the buyer. But more fee is required if buyer has less NCD than the seller (for paying the discount which was given the seller, not the buyer). NCD of the seller will not be affected and cannot be transfered.<br /><br />For others, I am not very sure. <br /><br />In my case, I brought my current car from my relative by cash. In fact, both things, ownship transfer and payment, are done separately. <br /><br />For secure, normally, you can try to ask the banker to transfer the money to seller <b>only if the ownership transfer is successful</b>. The first step apparently is to apply a loan. One the loan is done, then ownership transfer. And Finally, allow banker to process the payment to the seller.<br /><br />Without proper agreement: <br />1. the seller will be trouble after transfering ownership if the buyer does not able to get loan for payment.  <br />2. the buyer will not get his purchase after paying.<br /><br />I hope these will help you. Good luck.
</p>

<p>
Hi Mark,<br /><br />Personally, I will:<br /><br />1. make sure the dealer has a good reputation in the market (e.g. already doing this business for years, have a legal licence, shop).<br /><br />2. (if possible)ask a full payment before sign the ownership transfer doc. supposedly, the dealer will ask you to sign an agreement to safeguard his interest as well, i.e. the car owner must be transfered successfully, otherwise, you should return back all money within few days. <br /><br />3. require an duplicated copy of all signed documents (both seller and buyer) for your personal record.<br /><br />4. make sure you have received the full payment before confirm with JPJ about the ownership transform. Supposedly, JPJ will call you to ensure you have agreed to the application before they proceed the application. <br /><br />Lastly, the most safest way is to make an agreement in black and white about this application, with a present of one witness, who will sign the document as well. <br /><br />Example of agreement:<br />http://www.donnyspi.com/images/vsa.pdf<br /><br />From other useful website:<br />i.e. &quot;In my opinion, many motorists do not know the correct procedures to adopt when selling a vehicle. What they normally do is:<br /> i) Sign the MV3 form.<br /> ii) Provide 2 copies of their MyKad to the new owner.<br /> iii) Hand the Registration card (blue book) to the purchaser or finance company and that&#8217;s about it.<br /> <br />They should in fact:<br /> i) Inform in a letter to the Registrar of Motor Inland Vehicles (R.M.I.V) of the sale<br /> ii) Inform the Police authorities of the sale too.<br /> iii) Inform the insurance company of the sale as well.<br /> <br />Ideally, a simple agreement should then be concluded between you and the purchaser of your car.<br /> <br />In it, state that as from henceforth, you are no longer liable for:<br /> i) Any traffic offences that might be caused by the said vehicle.<br /> ii) For any accident which might involve the vehicle which is registered under your name.<br />&quot; (source: http://driving-school.com.my/driving-in-malaysia/how-to-transfer-vehicle-ownership-in-malaysia/)<br /><br /><br />All the best.
</p>

<p>
Hi Wk Yong,<br /><br />As a practical rule of thumb, it is better for you to do the ownership transfer together with the buyer in JPJ personally.<br /><br />Two things you should concern about:<br />1. Eventhough you have receive the payment from buyer, you are still the owner of the car before the ownership transfer is done. In other words, what you should do if any bad thing happens related to the car before the ownership transfer is done? <br /><br />2. How does the payment make to your account? By cash deposit or by cheque or by direct account transfer? Can the buyer hold on and cancel the cash transfer within one working day? To avoid such worry, an agreement signed by both parties (buyer and seller) is quite useful.<br /><br />I believe if buyer/seller really want to buy or sell their car, they are very willing to sign those agreement to protect both parties&#39; interests.<br /><br />All the best.

Hi Jen Yap Yong,<br /><br />Based on my friend&#39;s experience, an extra legal fee is needed for bank to transfer ownership if there is an outstanding loan by previous owner. Moreover, earlier settle up the loan will also have extra penalty charges. Supposedly, the seller need to bear these charges. <br /><br />Supposedly, you need to do ownership transfer first (i.e. go to puspakom first then JPJ) before buying a new insurance under new owner&#39;s name (a.k.a. your name).<br /><br />The thing is whether the ownership of the car can be successfully transfered to you. Because Puspakom will verify the safety of car, then JPJ will verify the background of the car. <br /><br />To safeguard both interests, it is better to have an agreement as a black and white. <br /><br />Additionally, you should also make sure the price of RM20,000 inclusive and exclusive of what items, e.g. insurance, road tax, and other costs.<br /><br />So, what you can do first is to check some background of the car via JPJ website. Then, make sure the car condition can pass Puspakom inspection. <br /><br />10K is quite huge portion (50%). I hope my humble opinion can help you.<br /><br />All the best.

<p>
General JPJ transfer car ownership procedure: <br /><br />1. Get the forms ready for transfer. You can download the forms from JPJ website. Forms you need are as follow: JPJK3, TM-1, TM-AB (photocopy both buyer/seller IC).<br /><br />2. After completing, go to JPJ and submit all the forms together with the original Car Registration Card, new buyer insurance cover note and PUSPAKOM inspection report.  Some JPJ branch required buyer and seller to be present but some only buyer will do. Seller unable to present must get their photocopy of IC certified by Comissioner of Oath.<br /><br />3. Pay RM100 (you can buy postal order from JPJ itself).<br /><br />4. Wait for the transfer name to complete and collect back the transferred ownership of car registration card on the same day. <br /> <br />Source: http://www.jpj.my/Ownership_Transfer_and_Payment.htm
</p>

Hi andalus,<br /><br />Generally, you need an agreement that agreed and signed by both parties (seller and buyer) plus a third party witness to secure both interests.<br /><br />If the buyer want to buy your car via cash, normally, the buyer should pay some deposit according to your agreement.<br /><br />And then, the seller need to complete the ownership transfer after received the deposit within the period stated in the agreement.<br /><br />After that, the buyer must pay all the remainder within the period stated in the agreement (e.g. 7 days after the ownership have completed the owership transfer)<br /><br />About your loan balance, if you settle it earlier, Banker will charge you some extra fee; if you ask Banker to help you to transfer the owership, you still need to bear other extra cost. <p>
Hi there,<br /><br />For conventional method (e.g. hires runner and hardcopy document), signatures of both seller and buyer are indispensable.<br /><br />Ownership transfer via internet can save hidden cost (from middle man). Based on my memory and previous homework (that I forgot to record it properly):<br /><br />1. Both seller and buyer must have their registered 1malaysia ID first. 1Malaysia ID can only be register in certain Kiosk machine. Please try to check it from RHB bank.<br /><br />2. Of course, the car is still needed to be inspected by Puspakom to ensure the car is legal and safe. To do this, either seller or buyer need to drive the car to nearest puspakom center to that inspection. <br /><br />3. Login the website (supposedly is myeg), the fill in the relevant information and upload required document.<br /><br />4. New owner must have an insurance for that car under his / her name.<br /><br />I hope this &quot;incomplete&quot; sharing can save you some time to the right direction and complete your ownship transfer via internet.<br /><br />Best wishes
</p>

Normally, as a seller, we sell our cars to dealer and get FULL payment directly. Whether they manage to sell the car is a business risk that they should take. <br /><br />I think the practice you mentioned is to save two ownership transfer fees (from you to seller, then to new owner) to one transfer fee (you and new buyer). So, it is fine. However, do remember to ask dealer to sign an acknowledgement or agreement about he/she are FULLY responsible for the car after the date you handle the car to dealer.
