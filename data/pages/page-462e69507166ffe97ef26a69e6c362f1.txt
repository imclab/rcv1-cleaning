				<h2>The New Go-To Book on Christianity and the Arts?<div class="post-avatar" style="float: right;" ><img alt='The New Go-To Book on Christianity and the Arts? avatar' src='http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/751ca69ad1bc11cc345ac1f5abfab781?s=64&amp;d=http%3A%2F%2F1.gravatar.com%2Favatar%2Fad516503a11cd5ca435acc9bb6523536%3Fs%3D64&amp;r=G' class='avatar avatar-64 photo' height='64' width='64' /></div></h2>
				<div class="entry">
					<p><a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1433535971/thegospcoal-20"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-24586" title="EE" src="http://thegospelcoalition.org/blogs/justintaylor/files/2013/04/EE-300x462.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="462" /></a>Tim Keller on Jerram Barrs&#8217;s <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1433535971/thegospcoal-20"><em>Echoes of Eden: Reflections on Christianity, Literature, and the Arts</em></a>, forthcoming next month from Crossway:</p>
<blockquote><p><em>Echoes of Eden</em> is the most accessible, readable, and yet theologically robust work on Christianity and the arts that you will be able to find. It is biblical, theologically sound, filled with examples, and edifying. It anticipates and answers well all the most common questions that evangelical people ask about the arts. I highly recommend it.</p></blockquote>
<p>Here is the table of contents:</p>
<blockquote><p>1 God and Humans as Creative Artists<br />
2 Imitation, the Heart of the Christian&#8217;s Approach to Creativity<br />
3 Building a Christian Understanding of the Artist&#8217;s Calling<br />
4 How Do We Judge the Arts?<br />
5 Echoes of Eden: God&#8217;s Testimony to the Truth<br />
6 The Conversion of C. S. Lewis and Echoes of Eden in His Life<br />
7 Echoes of Eden in Tolkien&#8217;s <em>Lord of the Rings</em><br />
8 Harry Potter and the Triumph of Self-Sacrificing Love<br />
9 Shakespeare and a Christian Worldview<br />
10 Jane Austen, Novelist of the Human Heart<br />
Appendix: The &#8220;Outing&#8221; of Dumbledore</p></blockquote>
