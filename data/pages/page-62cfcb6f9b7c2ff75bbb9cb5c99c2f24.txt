		<p>“Being childless, this has been a real eye-opener.”</p>
<p>Melanie Sanders says this with a slight air of understandable exasperation, herding the well-behaved but oh-so-numerous children in the cast of Amarillo Little Theatre’s latest Mainstage production.</p>
<p>“Cheaper by the Dozen,” which chronicles the real-life adventures of the Gilbreth family, opens Thursday for a two-week run.</p>
<p>The patriarch, Frank (played by Nolan Huckabay), was an esteemed efficiency expert who put his theories to the test by raising 12 children with wife Mary (played by Huckabay’s actual wife Carrie).</p>
<p>Yes, they were like a Roaring Twenties version of the Duggar family, only without the television cameras.</p>
<p>Allen Shankles, ALT’s managing/artistic director, said the play was chosen to serve as a collaboration between the theater and its academy program, now nearly 700 students strong.</p>
<p>It was not chosen as any kind of reflection of Shankles’ desire — nor anyone else involved with the show, including Sanders, the assistant director — to have a dozen children of their own.</p>
<p>“No, I can’t (imagine that),” Shankles said, chuckling. “And that goes back to my personality. I cannot imagine it, and frankly, I probably would not have imagined working with all of them.</p>
<p>“But it’s been a real pleasure for me, because they’ve been so well prepared and so well schooled,” Shankles said. “They’re just like little journeymen (actors). They know what’s supposed to happen.”</p>
<p>Carrie Huckabay agreed.</p>
<p>“These are good kids. A lot of these, I’ve had in (academy) class, so I’ve had the chance to mold them,” she laughed. “And I’ve been grooming Nolan for 14 and a half years.”</p>
