<p>If, like us, you love building your favorite cars on online car configurators and spend hours chatting on car forums (but not during work hours, we swear), you might be interested in checking out <em>Auto Club Revolution</em>.<span id="more-510537"></span> An online multi-player game played through your browser, <em>ACR</em> gives players a chance to build and race their ideal cars, test out other rides, and buy and sell their virtual vehicles.</p>
<p>More than 45 car makers passed along the specs of their rides so the game&#8217;s developer, Eutechnyx, could accurately depict the vehicles; as you can tell from the images below, it looks like they did a pretty nice job. The game should go live early next year, but you can score beta access by voting on what cars and tracks should be included in the game <a href="http://vote.autoclubrevolution.com/">via <em>Auto Club Revolution</em>&#8216;s website</a>. Best of all: basic access is free, so you can save your money for your real car if that&#8217;s how you roll.<br />

<em><strong>Press Release:</strong></p>
<p>Eutechnyx Announces Details of Auto Club Revolution, Free to Play Community-Driven Game Created With the Auto Industry</p>
<p>Tuesday 14 September, 2010 – Gateshead, UK &#8211; Eutechnyx, the world’s leading independent racing game developer, has released the first details about the upcoming online experience, Auto Club Revolution, or ACR.  Crafted in close collaboration with the world’s greatest automotive manufacturers, ACR combines visually stunning fully-licensed vehicles and parts with extensive community features to deliver a completely new way for auto enthusiasts to interact with their obsessions. All within a browser, players can customize their dream rides (or what they drove to work this morning) and show them off, test drive and examine vehicles, buy and sell their creations, and even enter thrilling multiplayer races designed for fun at every skill level. </p>
<p>Jaguar, Lotus, Bugatti, McLaren, Pagani, BMW, Mini, Bentley and Chevrolet are just some of the 45 plus manufacturers who have provided Eutechnyx with detailed information in order to faithfully recreate their model ranges. Not only will current and historical cars be featured in Auto Club Revolution, but several of these companies will be premiering concept and pre-production cars in the game, giving players their first ever taste of the latest cars prior to commercial release.</p>
<p>“Auto Club Revolution lets car enthusiasts take their dream garage and make it a virtual reality – then gather with other like-minded people and have a blast interacting and playing,” says Darren Jobling, COO, of Eutechnyx. “It’s completely free-to-play with premium items, accessible to all players of all ages and abilities, and offers a level of realism never before seen in a racing game or automotive online world.”</p>
<p>In ACR the community helps shape the experience, a process that has already begun with the ACR Voting site.  On that site, players are voting for the cars they’d like to see in the game, as well as the tracks or cities they’d like to drive.  This is not a short-term plan: players will always be able to shape the content they’d like to see in the game. Whether it’s specific types of cars, brands, locations, or anything else players are interested in, if the players want it, it’ll be in ACR. To register your vote for the cars you want to see in ACR, please visit http://vote.autoclubrevolution.com/.<br />
Voting players are rewarded with advanced Beta access to ACR.</p>
<p>“ACR is the result of listening to the fans and looking towards the future,” says Eutechnyx CEO, Brian Jobling.  “We’ve been making racing games for over a decade, which has given us an excellent understanding of what people want from these experiences.  The idea here is to make a game that fits how people want to play now, and give it the flexibility let it evolve.”</p>
<p>Auto Club Revolution will launch in early 2011. </em></p>
