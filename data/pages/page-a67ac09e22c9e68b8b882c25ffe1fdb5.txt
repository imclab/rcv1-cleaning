				<h1 class="entry-title">Car Nut Dentist Office is Tribute to the 50&#8242;s and 60&#8242;s Car Culture</h1>
		
		<div class="post-info"><span class="date published time" title="2011-08-05T07:16:15+00:00">August 5, 2011</span>  By <span class="author vcard"><span class="fn"><a href="http://tailfinsandchrome.com/author/rickcastillo/" class="fn n" title="rickcastillo" rel="author">rickcastillo</a></span></span> <span class="post-comments"><a href="http://tailfinsandchrome.com/2011/08/car-nut-dentist-office-is-tribute-to-the-50s-and-60s-car-culture/#comments">Leave a Comment</a></span> </div>		<div class="entry-content">
			<p><a href="http://tailfinsandchrome.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/Dentist-Office-Tail-Fins-and-Chrome-2.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2061" title="Dentist-Office-Tail-Fins-and-Chrome (2)" src="http://tailfinsandchrome.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/Dentist-Office-Tail-Fins-and-Chrome-2.jpg" alt="" width="600" height="450" /></a></p>
<p>Orthodontist and car nut Dr. David Myers designed his Arkansas practice to resemble a 1950s diner/drive-in/service station. Myers built all of the furniture by hand with his father&#8217;s help. A 1947 Mercury coupe fitted with a 1946 Ford grille and a surfboard roof resembles a woodie wagon and acts as the reception desk, while a 1956 Olds 98 and a pink 1959 Cadillac have been made into couches. Myers&#8217; fondness for the work of legendary custom painter and pinstriper Larry Watson was the inspiration behind recreating Watson&#8217;s shop as his private office. Even his office desk is a tribute to Watson&#8217;s candy-and-white pearl 1959 Caddy.</p>
<p>Special Thanks to Marty Giles (a Tail Fins and Chrome viewer) for sending us the photos.</p>
