        	<span class="paragraph-0">
				<p>Artwork on display in the dining room of Clay County Medical Center show that hospital staff aren’t just talented in their profession, they also bring artistic ability to the table.</p>
			</span>
        	<span class="paragraph-1">
				<p>Submitted works include a set of six photographs by assistant director of radiology Justin Begnoche, who started his own photography business; photography by hospital CEO Ron Bender; oil painting, watercolor and a pen and pencil drawing by Tyce Young, clinic representative in the hospital’s IT department; paintings by Lori Beikman, who’s employed in planning and development department of the hospital; and three cross-stick pieces by Linda Yarrow, dietitian at Clay County Medical Center.</p>
			</span>
                    <p>Young, who oil painting is titled “Jesus Chris Super Car” said he enjoys art “that displays an obscure sense of humor.”</p>
                
                    <p>His pen and pencil drawing portrayed the famous “Big Boy” restaurant icon in a new job working for the circus.</p>
                
                    <p>“I really enjoy illustrations of children’s books,” Young said in the artist statement accompanying the artwork.</p>
                
                    <p>Beikman, who took art classes at Kansas State University, said in her artist’s statement said her favorite art styles are expressionism and impressionism and names sever post-impressionist artists as her favorite artists, including Picasso and Van Gogh.</p>
                
                    <p>Her paintings emulated styles of those artists. The four on display are large paintings titled  Waiting, Coffee Break, Two Women and Last Call.</p>
                
                    <p>One of Linda Yarrow’s cross-stitch pieces is a wedding portrait of her in-laws that Yarrow designed herself and took 240 hours to stitch. Yarrow has cross-stitched for 30 years. The Lord’s Prayer on display is one of her favorite pieces and the “Susan Singleton” piece is a reproduction of a piece stitched by 12-year-old girl in 1760.</p>
                
                    <p>“The types of designs I like change through the years,” Yarrow said her artist statement. “My current focus is on historical reproductions and primitive and Adam and Eve samplers.”</p>
                
                    <p>The employee art show runs until Aug. 12 and will be followed by the employee family art show, which starts Aug. 13, Lorene Peterson’s watercolors on display beginning Sept. 3; Keath Meyers’ photography starting on Oct. 15; and Fleta Hanlon’s artwork starting Feb. 10.</p>
