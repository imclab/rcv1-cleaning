<p>            <span style="font-size: small;">The state of Maryland designated downtown Bethesda as an Arts &amp; Entertainment District effective July 1, 2002. The Bethesda Urban Partnership is the manager of the Bethesda's Arts &amp; Entertainment District.</span></p>
<p>            In 2004, the Bethesda Arts &amp; Entertainment District Board was granted 501 (c) (3) status by the IRS.&nbsp; This allows the Board to fundraise, as a not-for-profit organization, and build awareness for new arts &amp; Entertainment projects in downtown Bethesda.&nbsp; Our Mission Statement is: &quot;The Bethesda Arts &amp; Entertainment District Board shall create and implement arts and entertainment projects that contribute to the artistic, cultural and economic growth of downtown Bethesda&quot;.<br />
            &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <br />
            From award-winning theatre to independent films, Bethesda's Arts &amp; Entertainment District is filled with inspiring artists and arts venues. Unique galleries and public art fill the streets throughout the downtown. The Art &amp; Soul of Bethesda arts publication details arts organizations, public art, cinemas and live theatre present within downtown Bethesda's Arts &amp; Entertainment District.</p>
<hr />
            <br />
            <em>Watch for announcements for exciting, new art projects in Bethesda coming soon!</em><br />
            &nbsp;<br />
            Special tax incentives are in place that will benefit artists, arts enterprises and developers that are located within Bethesda Arts &amp; Entertainment District.
<p>            <strong>Benefits:</strong><br />
            *Artists who live in Montgomery County and work in the Bethesda Arts &amp; Entertainment District can receive certain income tax breaks.</p>
<p>            *Developers who renovate or construct spaces for arts' use can be exempt from paying certain property taxes on the value of the renovations for up to 10 years.</p>
<p>            *Arts enterprises that charge the Admissions and Amusement tax are exempt from this tax.</p></td>
