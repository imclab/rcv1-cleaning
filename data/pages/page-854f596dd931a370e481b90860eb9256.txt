<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6842054730503959430' itemprop='description articleBody'>
The Mindanao Creative Writers Group, Inc., and the Mindanao State University-Iligan Institute of Technology&#8217;s Office of the Vice Chancellor for Research and Extension (OVCRE) are accepting applications from writers to the 18th Iligan National Writers Workshop (INWW) to be held on May 23-27, 2011 in Iligan City.<br /><br />Panelists this year are Erlinda Kintanar Alburo, Leoncio P. Deriada, Merlie M. Alunan, German V. Gervacio, Steven Patrick C. Fernandez, Antonio R. Enriquez, Ralph Semino Galan, Christine Godinez-Ortega and this year&#8217;s keynote speaker, Pearlsha B. Abubakar, 9th INWW Fellow (2002).<br /><br />Fifteen (15) slots, five each from Luzon, Visayas and Mindanao and one (1) slot each for the Manuel T. Buenafe Writing Fellowship (MTBWF) and the Ricardo Jorge S. Caluen Bursary for Creative Writing or a total of 17 slots are available for writing fellowships to the 18th INWW.<br /><br />Applicants are required to submit five poems, or, one short story, excerpt of a novel, or, a one-act play in Filipino, English or in Sebuano, Hiligaynon, Kinaray-a, Waray, Chabacano (with English or Filipino translations) along with the applicant&#8217;s biodata, two 2X2 photos and a certification that his/her work is original. For short stories, excerpts of novels or plays, please submit a hard copy and a CD with the manuscripts encoded in MS Word. Those submitting excerpts of novels or works on progress &#8212; please provide a one page summary of the novel. Unpublished works are preferred and applicants who have attended regional workshops are given priority.<br /><br />Writing fellows will be given free board and lodging and a travel allowance. Applications must be postmarked on or before March 25, 2011. No applications or manuscripts will be accepted if sent by fax or e-mail. Applicants are also advised to keep copies of their manuscripts since these will not be returned. Announcements of this year&#8217;s writing fellows are released during the third week of April 2011.<br /><br />Applicants to this year&#8217;s workshop may download application and other forms at http://www.msuiit.edu.ph/research-extension/downloads.php.<br /><br />Send all applications to the 18th INWW Director c/o OVCRE, MSU-IIT, Iligan City. For more information call Pat Cruz, Leda Gonzales or Ofelia Taneo at telefax (063) 2232343; or e-mail: inww_ovcremsuiit@yahoo.com

</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-3017209156444841973' itemprop='description articleBody'>
UST Holds 3rd National Criticism Workshop<br /><br />The University of Santo Tomas is now accepting applications to the 2011 UST J. Elizalde Navarro National Workshop in Criticism on the Arts and Humanities to be held in Baguio City on the last week of May 29 to June 4, 2011.<br /><br />The workshop is endowed and organized by the Varsitarian, the 83-year-old official student publication of UST. It is held in honor of the late Nationalist Artist for the Visual Arts and art critic J. Elizalde Navarro, who was art editor and critic-poet of the Varsitarian during his student days, and in connection with the Quadricentennial Celebration of UST which was established in 1611.<br /><br />Fellowships will be given to 12 promising young critics who would like to enhance their analytical, research and writing skills. <br /><br />To be considered for the fellowship, applicants should submit a scholarly, properly documented critical essay (10-15 pages, double-spaced, 12-point font) on the following art forms (painting, sculpture, architecture, dance, drama, music, film or photography) on or before April 30, 2011.<br /><br />Manuscripts should be submitted in hard copy and on CD, preferably in MS Word, together with an updated resumé, a recommendation letter from an academic mentor or an art critic, a certification that the works are original, and two 2X2 ID pictures.<br /><br />Address all applications to Assoc. Prof. Ferdinand M. Lopez and Asst. Prof. Ralph Semino Galán, Workshop Conveners, Faculty of Arts and Letters, University of Santo Tomas, España Street, Sampaloc , Manila. E-mail ralphseminogalan@gmail.com for further inquiries.

<span style="font-weight:bold;">An Invitation to Literature Teachers and Professors in the Visayas</span><br /><br />Dear Sir/Madam:<br /><br />Warm greetings from the Philippine PEN Board of Directors!<br /><br />The Philippine Center of International PEN (Poets, Playwrights, Essayists, Novelists) is conducting a series of teaching literature workshops in selected regions of the Philippines this year. Called &#8220;For Love of the Word: Workshops on Teaching Philippine Literature in High School and College,&#8221; this project is geared toward retooling and upgrading the skills of literature teachers in both secondary and tertiary levels. This project is supported by PEN International in UK, and the Swedish International Development Cooperation Agency (SIDA). The first workshop was successfully held last March 25 in Far Eastern University-Manila.<br /><br />The second of the series, a lecture-workshop on teaching poetry and fiction in English, Filipino and regional languages, will be held on May 6, from 8:30am to 4:00pm, at the University of San Agustin&#8217;s Conference Room, Blanco Hall, General Luna St., Iloilo City. PEN authors and Metrobank Outstanding Teacher Awardees Dr. Marjorie Evasco and Dr. Leoncio Deriada will serve as the facilitators.<br /><br />In this regard, may we invite your faculty members to participate in this workshop?  There is no registration fee.  A workshop kit and a certificate of attendance will be given to each participant.<br /><br />At this point, PEN is making arrangements with possible patrons to sponsor the lunch for the participants.  We have not finalized anything though.  However, may we assure you that there are decent and affordable eateries located within and outside the campus.<br /><br />For pre-registration, please submit the name/s of your participant/s and your school to PEN.  You may email PEN at philippinepen@yahoo.com, or please text +639175287491. <br /> <br />Thank you for your kind attention. We look forward to your participation.<br /><br /> <br />Very truly yours,<br /><br /> <br />Joselito B. <div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://ralphseminogalan.blogspot.com/2011/01/to-pamiyabe-fellows-2011.html'>To the Pamiyabe Fellows 2011</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-2422458628107471852' itemprop='description articleBody'>
Hi guys and gals! Here is a funny poem by the American poet laureate Billy Collins. Hope you have a laugh or two, as well as some insights why some metaphors work and others don't, why some lines sing without trying too hard to do so. Hope you have learned the basics in spinning tales (for the fictionists) and conjuring images(for the poets). Remember always the Three Rs necessary to become a wordsmith: Reading, Writing (the initial consonant is silent, right?), and Rewriting... Feel free to post comments or ask questions regarding specific aspects of the writer's craft. <br /><br /><br /><br /><span style="font-weight:bold;">Workshop</span><br />by Billy Collins<br /><br />I might as well begin by saying how much I like the title.   <br />It gets me right away because I&#8217;m in a workshop now   <br />so immediately the poem has my attention,<br />like the Ancient Mariner grabbing me by the sleeve.<br /><br />And I like the first couple of stanzas,<br />the way they establish this mode of self-pointing<br />that runs through the whole poem<br />and tells us that words are food thrown down   <br />on the ground for other words to eat.   <br />I can almost taste the tail of the snake   <br />in its own mouth,<br />if you know what I mean.<br /><br />But what I&#8217;m not sure about is the voice,<br />which sounds in places very casual, very blue jeans,   <br />but other times seems standoffish,<br />professorial in the worst sense of the word<br />like the poem is blowing pipe smoke in my face.   <br />But maybe that&#8217;s just what it wants to do.<br /><br />What I did find engaging were the middle stanzas,   <br />especially the fourth one.<br />I like the image of clouds flying like lozenges   <br />which gives me a very clear picture.<br />And I really like how this drawbridge operator   <br />just appears out of the blue<br />with his feet up on the iron railing<br />and his fishing pole jigging&#8212;I like jigging&#8212;<br />a hook in the slow industrial canal below.<br />I love slow industrial canal below. All those l&#8217;s.<br /><br />Maybe it&#8217;s just me,<br />but the next stanza is where I start to have a problem.   <br />I mean how can the evening bump into the stars?   <br />And what&#8217;s an obbligato of snow?<br />Also, I roam the decaffeinated streets.<br />At that point I&#8217;m lost. I need help.<br /><br />The other thing that throws me off,<br />and maybe this is just me,<br />is the way the scene keeps shifting around.   <br />First, we&#8217;re in this big aerodrome<br />and the speaker is inspecting a row of dirigibles,   <br />which makes me think this could be a dream.   <br />Then he takes us into his garden,<br />the part with the dahlias and the coiling hose,   <br />though that&#8217;s nice, the coiling hose,<br />but then I&#8217;m not sure where we&#8217;re supposed to be.   <br />The rain and the mint green light,<br />that makes it feel outdoors, but what about this wallpaper?   <br />Or is it a kind of indoor cemetery?<br />There&#8217;s something about death going on here.<br /><br />In fact, I start to wonder if what we have here   <br />is really two poems, or three, or four,   <br />or possibly none.<br /><br />But then there&#8217;s that last stanza, my favorite.<br />This is where the poem wins me back,<br />especially the lines spoken in the voice of the mouse.<br />I mean we&#8217;ve all seen these images in cartoons before,<br />but I still love the details he uses<br />when he&#8217;s describing where he lives.<br />The perfect little arch of an entrance in the baseboard,   <br />the bed made out of a curled-back sardine can,   <br />the spool of thread for a table.<br />I start thinking about how hard the mouse had to work   <br />night after night collecting all these things<br />while the people in the house were fast asleep,   <br />and that gives me a very strong feeling,<br />a very powerful sense of something.<br />But I don&#8217;t know if anyone else was feeling that.   <br />Maybe that was just me.<br />Maybe that&#8217;s just the way I read it.

<h2>About Me</h2>
<div class='widget-content'>
<a href='http://www.blogger.com/profile/07418348816129379862'><img alt='My Photo' class='profile-img' height='80' src='http://bp0.blogger.com/_4yvx4jAjdyA/SBKwltSuFOI/AAAAAAAAAAc/o-uXPzCFf2E/S220/crop+pic+with+marby.jpg' width='60'/></a>
<dl class='profile-datablock'>
<dt class='profile-data'>
<a class='profile-name-link g-profile' href='http://www.blogger.com/profile/07418348816129379862' rel='author' style='background-image: url(//www.blogger.com/img/logo-16.png);'>
Ralph Semino Galan
</a>
</dt>
<dd class='profile-textblock'>Teaches literature, humanities and writing subjects at the Faculty of Arts and Letters in UST. Graduated magna cum laude with a Literature degree from  MSU-IIT. Fellow to the national writers' workshops (Silliman [1995], U.P.[1996] and Iligan[1997]), and to the 2nd ASEAN Writers' Conference on Poetry. Poems in English and Filipino have won prizes from the Philippine Panorama (1993) and Home Life Magazine (1998). Has an M.A. in Creative Writing from UP-Diliman. Writes book reviews for the Philippine Daily Inquirer. First chapbook "The Southern Cross and Other Poems" was launched December 2005 by the NCCA as part of its UBOD New Authors Series.Juror of the recently established PHILSTAGE Awards for the Performing Arts. Board member of the Council of Department Chairs of English (CDCE)and Philippine Association for Language Teaching (PALT), Inc.; active member of College English Teachers Association (CETA), PEN International Philippine Chapter and Unyon ng mga Manunulat sa Pilipinas (UMPIL). Currently co-adviser (with Nerisa del Carmen Guevara) of Teatro Tomasino.</dd>
</dl>
