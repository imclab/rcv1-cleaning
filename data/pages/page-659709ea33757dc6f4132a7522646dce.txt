Costa Rica is really getting into the swing of Art and Entertainment. Art Galleries are now opening in San Jose, Jaco, Escaz&uacute; and many tourist traps spots are displaying <a target="_blank" href="http://www.ticotimes.com/costa-rica-arts-entertainment/artists/">regional artists.</a> Websites are now springing up with information on movies, art and critical narratives.&nbsp; <a href="http://ticotimes.com/costa-rica/santa-ana-industry-residence" target="_blank">Santa Ana </a>and neighboring Escaz&uacute; have long been magnets for artists. Escaz&uacute; is home to some contemporary artists: Christina Fournier; the brothers Jorge, Manuel, Javier, and Carlos Mena; and Dinorah Bolandi, who was awarded the nation's top cultural prize. Classical music has now become a part of the culture with the National Symphony Orchestra and Sura Chamber Choir. Costa Rica holds an <a target="_blank" href="http://www.ticotimes.com/detail/link-72.html">International Festival of Music</a> during the last two weeks of August and recently a new <a target="_blank" href="http://ticotimes.com/costa-rica/first-imax-theater-opens-in-costa-rica-3-d-viewing">3-D IMAX threater</a> opened up in Escaz&uacute;.<br />
