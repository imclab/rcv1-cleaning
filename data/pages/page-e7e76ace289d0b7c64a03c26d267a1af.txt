<h1 class="paddingL10">            
Britax Convertible Car Seat Recall: Everything You Need to Know
</h1>
<span class="date">by <a href="http://thestir.cafemom.com/blogger/89/nicole_fabian_weber"><strong>Nicole Fabian-Weber</strong></a>
November  6, 2012 at  3:12 PM
</span>
<div class="clearfix">                
<div class="shareTools clearfix largeShare spaceTop5" id="share-post-146167">
</div>               
<div class="floatR aPostInfo spaceTop10">
<a href="#comments" class="commentTag black">3</a>
</div>
</div>
<div class="articleBody clearfix paddingL10">
<p><img class="userImageLeft" src="http://cdn-ugc.cafemom.com/gen/constrain/500/500/80/2012/11/06/15/4a/we/popnuqg3k0.jpg" alt="britax car seat" width="230" height="258" />If you have a<strong> Britax Convertible Car Seat</strong>, as I know many (including myself) do, you might want to listen up. Some models are<strong> about to be recalled</strong>. I know -- not something a mom likes hearing, but no need to worry, we've got <strong>everything you need to know</strong>.</p> <p><strong>Models that have been recalled:</strong> Boulevard 70 G3, Advocate 70 G3, and Pavilion 70 G3. (Model numbers: E9LJ91A, E9LJ91M, E9LJ91S, E9LJ92E, E9LJ93P, E9LJ93S, E9LK91A, E9LK31A, E9LK31Q, E9LK32D, E9LK32Z, E9LK33Q, E9LL11A, E9LL11Q, E9LL12D, E9LL12Z, E9LG81A, E9LG83N, E9LG83P, E9LG83X, E9LG83Y, E9LL21A, E9LL23P, E9LL23Y.)</p>
<p><strong>The reason they've been recalled:</strong> The seats, which were manufactured between June 2012 and August 2012, have a softer material in the chest pad that has the potential to be bitten or chewed into little pieces by a child who's using the seat (we all know how much babies and kids like biting things!). If the child bites off a piece of the material, it may pose a choking hazard that results in injury or death.</p>
<p><strong>What will Britax do:</strong> The company will provide car seat owners with replacement HUGS pads that are made from a stronger material, along with instructions on how to replace the pads -- at no cost. Current owners can remove the HUGS pads and continue using the seat until the replacement pads are received.</p>
<p><strong>Who to call:</strong> Contact <a href="http://www.consumeraffairs.com/recalls04/2012/britax-recalls-convertible-child-restraints.html" target="_blank">Britax Customer Service Department</a> at 1-888-427-4829 if you have any questions, or to request replacement chest pads for the seat.</p>
<p><strong>Do you have a Britax car seat?</strong></p>
