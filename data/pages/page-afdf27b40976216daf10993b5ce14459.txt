<p><span style="font-family: arial,helvetica,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">John joined BBC Radio in 1986 and moved to television as a Script Editor on EastEnders in 1994. He later moved from being Deputy Head of BBC Drama to become Channel 4&rsquo;s Head of Drama, commissioning Shameless, Sex Traffic, Not Only But Always and Omagh. In 2004 he returned to the BBC as Controller of Continuing Drama and Head of Independent Drama, commissioning Life on Mars, The Street, Waterloo Road, Bodies and many more, as well as overseeing the four longrunning series: EastEnders, Holby City, Casualty and Doctors. He also created and runs the BBC Writers&rsquo; Academy &ndash; now in its seventh year.</span></span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: arial,helvetica,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Where</span>: Curtis Auditorium, Herschel Building (off Percy Street), Newcastle University, Newcastle-Upon-Tyne, NE1 7RU.</span></span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: arial,helvetica,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">When</span>: Friday 23 November, 5.30pm onwards.</span></span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: arial,helvetica,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">This event is free, all are welcome, and there is no need to book.</span></span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: arial,helvetica,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For more information about the Newcastle Centre for the Literary Arts (NCLA) click <a href="http://www.ncl.ac.uk/ncla/">here</a>.</span></span></p></div><div class="clear"></div></div>	</div>
