    <h3>An Emily Carr Degree with Benefits</h3>
    
    <p>At NIC, Fine Arts students can complete a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree from Emily Carr University at the Comox Valley campus. For graduate Heather Koning, the program's access to professional artist instructors and amazing studios make her career shift worthwhile. <a href="../../PDF_docs/Pages%20from%2012_11_College_Connection_Special_Edition_web_p3.pdf">More <img alt="pdf" src="../../images/icons/acrobat.gif" /></a>  
</p>

    <h3>
        Great Resources</h3>
    <p>
        In the Shadbolt Studio, a modern, 7520 sq ft building which contains separate ceramics,
        painting and sculpture studios, NIC Fine Arts students enjoy access to a comprehensive
        variety of tools and materials. For example, NIC is one of the only colleges in
        the country to provide a commercial-quality silk screen press for student learning.
        In addition to hinge top table presses, the screen press allows to screen-printing
        students to gain hands-on experience that employers value. Take a <a href="/galleries/tours/?campus=cv">
            virtual tour </a>online. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    </p>

    <h3>Digital Visual Arts Students 
        Help Elevate the Arts</h3>
    <p>In June 2013, Fine Arts students from NIC's <a href="../../programs/courses.aspx?subj=FIN&code=200#result_anchor">Digital Visual Arts I</a> class participated in <a href="http://cvagstarthere.wordpress.com/" target="_blank">Media = Art = Social Change <img alt="Leave site" src="../../images/icons/leave_site.gif" /></a>, a collaborative art project at the <a href="http://www.comoxvalleyartgallery.com/exhibitions2013.html" target="_blank">Comox Valley Art Gallery <img alt="Leave site" src="../../images/icons/leave_site.gif" /></a> and part of the 2013 Elevate the Arts Festival in Courtenay. 
    </p>


        </div>
        <div class='page_details_right'>
            

    <img style="float:right; vertical-align:bottom;margin-top:15px;" vspace="5" hspace="5" src="../../images/spotlight_photos/propotter_web.jpg" alt="Professional Potter" />

    <h3>Professional Potter Advanced Diploma</h3>

    <p>
    Take your pottery design and production skills to the next level with NIC’s new <a href="program/professional_potter_advanced_diploma">Professional Potter program</a>. Study full time over 10 months in NIC’s 4,000 sq. ft studios, access a wide range of kilns, and gain valuable business skills needed to take your work to market.
    </p>

    <h3 style="margin-top: 0px">
        Unique Experiences</h3>
    <p>
        Each year, ceramics students from NIC Fine Arts program have the exciting opportunity
        to fire a Tozan Anagama Kiln, one of only four that exist in the world. Unlike modern
        commercial kilns, the Tozan Anagama’s intense wood fire-driven heat produces one-of-a-kind
        creations. The intensive process requires the help of many people over several days,
        and students gain a complete, hands-on understanding of ceramic firing, from concept
        to creation. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    </p>
    <h3>
        See and Be Seen</h3>
    <p>
        Join a supportive community of artists who show their work at professional venues in the Comox Valley. NIC’s fine arts students regularly join exhibits at Courtenay’s Muir Gallery, the Potters Place Gallery, as well as the Comox Valley Art Gallery and Courtenay and District Museum, which host annual exhibits for students in the <a href="../../program/emily_carr_institute_bachelor_of_fine_arts_external">Bachelor of Fine Arts</a> and <a href="../../program/professional_photography">Professional Photography</a> programs. 


    </p>

        Fine Arts &amp; Design Shows<br />
        Draw Crowds</h3>

        community. Often hosted with great food, a bar, and live DJ talent, these student-run
