						<h1>Buying or selling a used vehicle</h1><div class="page-options" id="pre-page-options">
	<ul>

	</ul>
</div>
		<h2>Used car safety ratings</h2>
    <p>If you are buying a used car, it's worth considering whether your car's safety and design features are likely to protect you or harm other road users in a crash. The <a href="~/media/Safety/Driver guide/Buying a safer vehicle/Used_car_safety_ratings_2012.ashx">Used Car Safety Ratings Buyer's Guide 2012 for Passenger vehicles built 1996–2008</a> can help you decide whether that car you've set your sights on is right for you. <a href="http://www.howsafeisyourcar.com.au/brochures.php#ucsr" target="_blank">Previous editions of the buyer's guide*</a> are available, with safety ratings of passenger vehicles built as far back as 1982.</p>
    <h2>How safe is your car?</h2>
    <p>View the <a href="/Safety/Driver-guide/Buying-a-safer-vehicle.aspx">buying a safer vehicle</a> section before your next purchase, or visit the <a href="http://www.howsafeisyourcar.com.au/" target="_blank">How Safe Is Your Car*</a> website for detailed information about used car safety ratings and safety features that people should look for when buying used cars.</p>
    <h2>Buying a used vehicle?</h2>
    <img style="WIDTH: 150px; HEIGHT: 110px" class="image-right" alt="An image of a vehicle for sale" src="~/media/Registration/Buying or selling a used vehicle/4SALE.ashx?w=150&amp;h=110&amp;as=1" /> <p>If you are buying a used vehicle you need to ensure that there is a current <a href="/Registration/Buying-or-selling-a-used-vehicle/Safety-certificates.aspx">Queensland safety certificate</a> displayed. A safety certificate identifies the authorised inspection station that issued the certificate, and it must be displayed on the vehicle from the time it is offered for sale.<br /><br />If a Queensland safety certificate is not displayed, it is likely the vehicle has not been checked and should not be considered for purchase. <a href="/Registration/Buying-or-selling-a-used-vehicle/Buyers-and-sellers-checklist.aspx">A buyer's checklist is available</a>.</p><h2>Selling a vehicle?</h2>If you are selling a registered vehicle, you will need to obtain and display a <a href="/Registration/Buying-or-selling-a-used-vehicle/Safety-certificates.aspx">Queensland safety certificate</a> from the moment you offer the vehicle for sale. The safety certificate must be displayed on a conspicuous part of the vehicle. Contact an approved inspection station to arrange an inspection. Fees for inspections are paid directly to the station. <a href="/Registration/Buying-or-selling-a-used-vehicle/Buyers-and-sellers-checklist.aspx">A seller's checklist is available</a>. <p>If you fail to display a Queensland safety certificate on the vehicle from the moment you offer it for sale you may receive an on-the-spot fine of $550.</p><p>Note: You are required to obtain and display a safety certificate on a registered vehicle when it is being offered for sale, even if you plan on cancelling the registration when the vehicle is sold. </p><p class="dislaimer">*The content found by using this link is not created, controlled or approved by this department. No responsibility is taken for the consequences of viewing content on this site.</p>
