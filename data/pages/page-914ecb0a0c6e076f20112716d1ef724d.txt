<h1 class="entry-title">Beijing Auto Show Preview: Mercedes Concept Style Coupe</h1>
	<address class="byline author vcard">By <a href="/author/jonathan-schultz/" class="url fn" title="See all posts by JONATHAN SCHULTZ">JONATHAN SCHULTZ</a></address></header>
<!-- The Content -->
<div class="entry-content"><div class="w480"><img id="100000001496994" src="http://graphics8.nytimes.com/images/2012/04/18/automobiles/wheels-conceptbeijing/wheels-conceptbeijing-blog480.jpg" alt="Mercedes-Benz Concept Style Coupe." width="480" height="320" /><span class="credit">Mercedes-Benz USA</span><span class="caption">Mercedes-Benz Concept Style Coupe.</span></div>
<p>Two years ago at the Beijing auto show, Mercedes-Benz stretched the form language, as well as the spelling, of the <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2006/11/26/automobiles/26BRAKE.html">shooting brake</a> body style with a design study it called the <a href="http://wheels.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/04/21/beijing-auto-show-preview-mercedes-benz-shooting-brake/">Concept Shooting Break</a>. Now the German automaker is poised to rekindle the semantics wars with the Concept Style Coupe, a compact four-door vehicle scheduled to make its official debut in Beijing on April 23.</p>
<p>The concept, described by Mercedes as the &#8220;heartbeat of a new generation&#8221; (not to be confused with the Concept A-Class, the &#8220;<a href="http://wheels.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/04/20/mercedes-benz-concept-a-class-wants-the-world-and-wants-it-soon/">pulse of a new generation</a>”), further demonstrates the company&#8217;s desire to bolster the bottom of its product portfolio. Shown within the past year were redesigns of the <a href="http://wheels.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/03/06/mercedes-benz-a-class-trading-upright-for-swept-back/">A-Class</a> and <a href="http://wheels.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/09/13/mercedes-benz-b-class-is-coming-to-america/">B-Class</a>, two volume-oriented compact models currently sold outside the United States.</p>
<p>Mercedes will offer a preview of the concept this week in Los Angeles, at a <a href="http://www.moca.org/audio/blog/?p=3141">multimedia art fair</a> curated by Mike D of the <a class="tickerized" title="More articles about the Beastie Boys." href="http://topics.nytimes.com/top/reference/timestopics/organizations/b/beastie_boys/index.html?inline=nyt-org">Beastie Boys</a> and organized by <a class="tickerized" title="More articles about Jeffrey Deitch." href="http://topics.nytimes.com/top/reference/timestopics/people/d/jeffrey_deitch/index.html?inline=nyt-per">Jeffrey Deitch</a>, the director of the city&#8217;s <a href="http://www.moca.org/">Museum of Contemporary Art</a>.</p>
<p><span id="more-159841"></span></p>
<p>The concept wears design cues put forward by the redesigned A- and B-Class, as well as by other recent Mercedes show cars. The diamond-like honeycomb pattern and crossbar on the grille were initially seen on the Concept A-Class, shown at the 2011 <a class="tickerized" title="More articles about the New York International Auto Show." href="http://topics.nytimes.com/top/reference/timestopics/subjects/n/new_york_auto_show/index.html?inline=nyt-classifier">New York auto show</a>. Opposing creases on the car&#8217;s flanks have been employed for concepts like the <a href="http://wheels.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/02/24/mercedes-benz-f-800-style-concept-to-showcase-green-technologies/">F800</a> and <a href="http://wheels.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/09/15/mercedes-benz-f-125-concept-bridges-hybrid-and-hydrogen/">F125</a>.</p>
<p>According to Mercedes, the concept is equipped with a 208-horsepower turbocharged 4-cylinder gasoline engine and a dual-clutch transmission. Comand, the company&#8217;s telematics system, provides access to applications stored in the cloud, not on the car&#8217;s harddrive.</p>
<p>The concept&#8217;s name perpetuates a tendency that began with the Mercedes-Benz CLS, <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2011/08/21/automobiles/autoreviews/beauty-contestants-with-curves-talent-and-german-accents.html">billed as a four-door coupe</a> when it arrived in 2004. Other manufacturers, notably Volkswagen with its CC, née Passat Comfort Coupe, have since used the term to differentiate these four-door models, with elegantly sloping greenhouses and high belt lines, from traditional <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2011/12/04/automobiles/autoreviews/2012-volkswagen-passat-achtung-kmart-shoppers.html?">three-box sedans</a>.</p>
<p><em>Wheels will bring you news of notable debuts in Beijing next week, when press previews for the show begin.</em></p>
