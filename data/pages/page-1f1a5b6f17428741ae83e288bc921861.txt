<p>Luxury brands have been expanding into smaller vehicles for a while now, and the <a title="Lincoln MKC Concept: Luxury Compact Crossover At Detroit Auto Show" href="http://www.greencarreports.com/news/1081646_lincoln-mkc-concept-luxury-compact-crossover-at-detroit-auto-show">Lincoln MKC Concept</a> unveiled at the Detroit Auto Show continues that trend.</p>
<p>The smallest member so far of Ford's struggling lxuury brand has been the MKZ mid-size sedan, but the compact crossover concept shows the brand will go smaller.</p>	    <div class="ad floating-ad" id="adsense1" style="width:300px;height:250px;">

<p>While it's built on the underpinnings of the 2013 Ford Escape, the MKC shares no body panels with that popular crossover.</p>
<p>Instead, it layers on the luxury and shows off the usual Lincoln styling cues and the brand's signature all-glass roof.</p>
<p>Inside, it replaces Ford's practical dashboard with high design and elegant materials.</p>
<p>The MKC Concept has the predictable touchscreen for minor controls--though we seriously doubt it's a match for the excellent graphics and instantaneous response of the 17-inch screen in the Tesla Model S--and pushbuttons for gear selection, to replace a plebian lever on the console.</p>
<p>Interior finishes include light cream leather seats and subtle aluminum accents.</p>
<p>As for fuel efficiency, Ford gave essentially no detail on the powertrains that might be used in the production version of the Lincoln MKC.</p>
<p>It may be limited to the larger of two EcoBoost engines fitted to the Ford Escape--the 240-horsepower 2.0-liter four, which returns 25 mpg combined--and there's some speculation that a hybrid version will also be offered.</p>
<p>If there were an MKC Hybrid, it would use the same powertrain already on sale in the 2013 Ford C-Max Hybrid and 2013 Ford Fusion Hybrid.</p>
<p>Here's hoping that the fuel-efficiency ratings of any future Lincoln MKC Hybrid <a title="Ford Hybrids' Fuel Economy Failing To Live Up To EPA Ratings?" href="http://www.greencarreports.com/news/1080580_ford-hybrids-fuel-economy-failing-to-live-up-to-epa-ratings">reflect real-world gas mileage</a> better than the 47-mpg ratings of those two Fords.</p>

<p>We're sure to have more news about the production version of the Lincoln MKC crossover later this year.</p>
