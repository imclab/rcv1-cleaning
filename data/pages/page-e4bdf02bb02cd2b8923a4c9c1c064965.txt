		If you're in the market for a good, used vehicle, then you basically have two options: <ol><li>Visit local used car dealers in your area.</li><li>Find private owners whose vehicles are for sale.</li></ol><p>
If you're interested in buying from a dealer, they're not hard to find -- either online or in your local community. You can find them in the phone book. You probably pass by many of them when you're going about your daily business. And, most have their own websites listing their current inventory.<p>Private sellers, on the other hand, are harder to find. Other than coincidentally passing by someone's property and noticing a &quot;For Sale&quot; sign placed in the window of their vehicle, or tediously scouring through the local classifieds in the newspaper (which are not organized in any meaningful way), vehicles that are for-sale-by-owner are much harder to come by.<p>Which is why doing your research online can be quite helpful when you're in the market for a used vehicle. The Internet has enabled us to fine-tune our search criteria in order to view only the EXACT results we're interested in.<p><h3>Review of Popular Online Classifieds</h3>
This article contains the results of a thorough review of six (6) popular online classifieds websites. Each of these sites provides services for both selling AND buying vehicles, but this review focuses specifically on the pros and cons of each individual site as viewed by a potential BUYER interested in purchasing a good, used vehicle. While each of these sites yielded better than average results, the &quot;best&quot; ones appear near the top of the list.<p><sub>(&quot;Vehicles Found&quot; indicates the number of vehicles for sale that each site found, as a result of my search for a Ford Explorer within 50 miles of a given zip code.)</sub><p><a href="http://autotrader.com/" onclick="zT(this, '1/XJ')"><b>autotrader.com</b></a><br />
<b>Pros:</b><ul><li>Ability to search for &quot;certified pre-owned&quot; vehicles, increasing buyer confidence</li><li>Also have auction-style sales, allowing you to place incremental bids on a vehicle</li>
<li>Ability to sort results by various categories</li>
<li>Can compare used vehicles side by side</li>
<li>Vehicle reviews and other resources available at the site</li>
<li>Most comprehensive list of search fields to fine-tune the search process</li><li>Most vehicles found</li></ul><b>Cons:</b><ul><li>Fair number of dealerships represented, but many private sellers as well</li>
<li>Must do a separate click to see the photo for a vehicle</li></ul>
<b>Advanced Search Fields:</b><ul><li>type of vehicle (truck, SUV, minivan, etc.)</li><li>make</li><li>model</li><li>price range</li>
<li>distance from your home</li><li>range of years</li><li>color</li>
<li>mileage</li><li>doors</li><li>engine (cylinders)</li><li>fuel type</li>
<li>drive type (2wd/awd/4wd)</li><li>transmission (auto vs manual)</li>
</ul><b>Vehicles Found: </b>274<p><a href="http://www.cars.com/carsapp/national/?szc=37211&srv=adlocator&amp;act=populate&ft=1&amp;tf=quick_usedforsale-default.tmpl&page=used&amp;rn=7&zc=&amp;rd=30" onclick="zT(this, '1/XJ')"><b>Cars.com</b></a><br />
<b>Pros:</b><ul><li>Ability to quickly find classics (1982 or earlier)</li>
<li>Ability to compare vehicles located in your search - side by side</li>
<li>Other helpful resources on the same page: auto reviews, prices and research stats, buying guide</li><li>Ability to sort results by various categories</li><li>Lots of private sellers</li><li>Ability to email dealerships directly to haggle on price</li></ul>
<b>Cons:</b><ul>
<li>Have to do a separate click to see the photo for a vehicle</li>
