		<!-- com.omniupdate.div label="maincontent" group="Business" button="817" position="top" break="break" --><!-- com.omniupdate.editor csspath="/_resources/ou/editor/main-two-column.css" cssmenu="/_resources/ou/editor/main.txt" width="1200" --><h1>Automotive</h1>
                  <p>Oklahoma City Community College offers an Associates of Applied Science Degree for the "Automotive Technology Internship Program"(ATIP), the "General Motors Automotive Service Educational Program" (ASEP), the Paint/Refinishing &amp; Non-Structural Repair 	Program, and a new Diesel Technology Program. A student also has the option of obtaining a "Automotive Certificate of Mastery" in the "Maintenance and Light Vehicle Repair Program 	(MLRP). All programs are certified by the National Automotive Technicians Education 	Foundation (NATEF) and prepare the student for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) certification. </p>

                  <p>Oklahoma City Community College has been training automotive technicians since 1978 and began one of the first corporate GM ASEP Programs in 1986. Students completing automotiv programs are placed and well prepared to enter and succeed in the automotive industry. </p>

                  <p>All automotive programs combine work in the classroom, lab activities, and practical on -theÂ· job experience in industry approved work sites.
