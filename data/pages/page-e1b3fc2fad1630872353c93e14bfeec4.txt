	<h1>Buying a new car in Ireland</h1>
	
<ul class="anchorlinks"><li><a href="#leb1f9">Information </a></li><li><a href="#l49eec">Importing a car</a></li><li><a href="#l6010a">VRT Rates</a></li><li><a href="#l23f7d">Further information</a></li></ul>
<div>

<div class="topic">

<div class="topic base_introduction">
<h2 class="topic base_introduction_title" id="leb1f9">Information </h2>

<p>Generally, all new cars and cars that are imported into Ireland are subject
to Vehicle Registration Tax (VRT) and must be registered with the Revenue
Commissioners. If you are buying a new car or are <a shape="rect" href="/en/moving_country/moving_to_ireland/coming_to_live_in_ireland/importing_car_into_ireland.html">importing
a car into Ireland from abroad</a>, you will need to do three things before you
can drive your car: </p>
<ul><li>Pay Vehicle Registration Tax (VRT) </li>
  <li>Have motor insurance </li>
  <li>Pay motor tax </li>
</ul><p>All motorists are required to carry a valid driving licence with them at all
times when driving. Further information is available in our documents on <a shape="rect" href="../driver_licensing/learner_driving_permits_for_cars_and_work_vehicles.html">learner
driving permits</a> and <a shape="rect" href="../driver_licensing/full_driving_licence.html">full driving
licences</a>.</p>

<h3>Vehicle labelling</h3>

<p>Since July 2008 a labelling system has been introduced which allows
consumers to compare the carbon (CO2) emissions of new cars. The first section
of the label shows the seven emission bands and identifies the band for the
particular vehicle to which a label is attached. The second section of the
label provides three important points of information for consumers: </p>
<ul><li>the rate of VRT payable on the vehicle, </li>
  <li>the annual motor tax payable </li>
  <li>the amount of fuel required to run the vehicle over 18,000 kilometres
  </li>
</ul><p>The third section of the label supplements the information provided in the
first and second sections, and introduces the importance of driving behaviour.
The remaining sections of the label provide essential information on the actual
vehicle to which it is attached, including make, model, engine capacity,
transmission, fuel type and consumption. </p>

<h3>Vehicle Registration Tax</h3>

<p>Every new car in Ireland is liable for Vehicle Registration Tax, which is
payable when the car is first registered. This is a legal requirement and any
delay in registering your car or paying the tax may lead to substantial
penalties. </p>

<p>Imported cars must also be registered. To register an imported car, you must
book an appointment at an NCTS centre to have the car examined and to pay the
VRT. See our document on <a shape="rect" href="/en/moving_country/moving_to_ireland/coming_to_live_in_ireland/importing_car_into_ireland.html">importing
a car</a>.</p>

<h3>Buying from a car dealer</h3>

<p>If you buy your new car from a dealer (car showrooms, etc.), then it is
<span>the dealer's responsibility</span> to register the vehicle and pay the
tax before giving it to you. The price of the vehicle should include the cost
of the VRT. Your vehicle will be supplied to you with its <a shape="rect" href="../../vehicle_standards/vehicle_registration_numbers.html">registration
plates</a> already fitted.</p>

<p>Once the vehicle has been registered and the VRT paid, you (or your motor
dealer) will receive:</p>
<ul><li>A receipt for the VRT paid showing the registration number assigned to
    your car. </li>
  <li>A Form RF 100 for use when you are applying for road tax. </li>
</ul><h3>Vehicle registration plates</h3>

<p><a shape="rect" href="../../vehicle_standards/vehicle_registration_numbers.html">Vehicle
registration plates</a> showing the assigned registration number must be
displayed on your car within 3 days of the date of registration. If you buy
your car from a dealer, it will have its registration plates fitted by the time
you take possession of it. If you buy the car privately, you will need to get
registration plates - most motor dealers make these to order. </p>

<h3>Vehicle registration certificate</h3>

<p>The vehicle registration certificate for your car is issued by the
Department of Transport, Tourism and Sport. This will be posted out to you
after you have applied to your local motor tax office to pay motor tax on your
vehicle. </p>

<h3>Motor Insurance</h3>

<p>It is a legal requirement in Ireland to have motor insurance if you want to
drive your car in a public place. Read more about the <a shape="rect" href="../motor_tax_and_insurance/motor_insurance.html">requirement for motor
insurance here</a>.</p>

<h3>Motor Tax</h3>

<p>Motor tax in Ireland is a charge imposed by the Irish Government on motor
vehicles. Revenue from motor tax is used to maintain and upgrade the road
network. The charge for motor tax for a new car is based on the car's CO2
emissions and on engine size for other vehicles. Some vehicles are exempt. Read
more about the <a shape="rect" href="../motor_tax_and_insurance/motor_tax_rates.html">requirement for motor
tax here</a>.</p>

<h3>Tax reliefs and exemptions</h3>

<p>There are different reliefs and exemptions from VRT and further information
is available from your <a shape="rect" href="http://www.revenue.ie/en/contact/index.html">local Revenue tax
office</a>. Relief is also available for;</p>
<ul><li><a shape="rect" href="../../transport_and_disability/tax_relief_for_disabled_drivers_and_disabled_passengers.html">certain
    disabled drivers</a> </li>
  <li>Visitors to Ireland who have owned their vehicles abroad for more than 6
    months and who will be resident here temporarily </li>
  <li>People who have owned their vehicles abroad for more than 6 months and
    who are moving permanently to Ireland </li>
  <li>People posted to Ireland as part of the diplomatic corps </li>
</ul><p><span>NOTE:</span> If you are moving to Ireland and are among those exempt
from paying VRT you cannot sell your vehicle for more than 12 months after the
vehicle is registered. If you are required to pay VRT, then you can sell your
vehicle here in Ireland when you wish, once it has been registered. Further
information is available from your local Revenue tax office.</p>

<h3>Electric cars</h3>

<p>There is an exemption from VRT for series production electric vehicles until
31 December 2013. </p>

<p>From January 2011 until December 2012, a grant scheme is available for
battery-powered electric vehicles (BEV) and plug-in hybrids meeting specific
standards. The grants are as follows:</p>
<ul><li>€5,000 for BEVs costing €20,000 or more </li>
  <li>€2,000 to €4,500 for BEVs costing less than €20,000 </li>
  <li>€2,500 for hybrids costing €18,000 or more </li>
</ul><p>Further information on the grant scheme is available on the <a href="http://www.seai.ie/Grants/Electric_Vehicle_Grant_Scheme/">website of the
Sustainable Energy Authority of Ireland (SEAI)</a>.</p>

<h3>Car scrappage scheme</h3>

<p>A car scrappage scheme was introduced from January to December 2010. Under
the scheme VRT relief of up to €1,500 was provided where a car of 10 years or
older was <em>scrapped</em> and a new car of CO2 emissions bands A or B
purchased. The scheme was extended for the period January 2011 to June 2011
with reduced VRT relief of up to €1,250. </p>

<p>Being scrapped meant that the old car has been <a shape="rect" href="how_to_dispose_of_an_end_of_life_vehicle.html">taken to an official End
of Life Vehicles (ELV) authorised treatment facility</a> and a Certificate of
Destruction issued by the facility in respect of the car. </p>
</div>
<div class="topic base_rules">
<h2 class="topic base_rules_title" id="l49eec">Importing a car</h2>

<p>If you are bringing your car to Ireland temporarily (i.e., while on holiday,
a business trip, etc.) <span>you do not have to pay VRT</span> on the
vehicle.</p>

<p>If you are importing a new car from another EU country you have to pay VAT,
usually when registering the car. A new car means a car that has been in
service for 6 months or less, or has been driven for 6,000 kilometres or less.
The VAT is payable even where you have paid VAT in the other country.</p>

<p>If you are importing a new or second hand car from outside the EU, VAT (and
customs duty) is payable.</p>

<p>More information is available in our document on <a shape="rect" href="/en/moving_country/moving_to_ireland/coming_to_live_in_ireland/importing_car_into_ireland.html">importing
a vehicle into Ireland</a>.</p>
</div>
<div class="topic base_rates">
<h2 class="topic base_rates_title" id="l6010a">VRT Rates</h2>

<p>Vehicle Registration Tax (VRT) is based on the Open Market Selling Price
(OMSP) of the vehicle. You may be able to obtain an estimate of the VRT for a
vehicle from <a shape="rect" href="https://www.ros.ie/VRTEnquiryServlet/ShowVRT">Revenue's Vehicle
Registration on-line Enquiry System</a>. </p>

<p>Since July 2008 VRT for cars (Category A) is no longer based on engine size
but on the level of CO2 emissions from the car.</p>
