<h3 class="entry-title"><a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/08/27/soho-rep-announces-new-season/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Soho Rep Announces New Season">Soho Rep Announces New Season</a></h3>
	<address class="byline author vcard">By <a href="/author/erik-piepenburg/" class="url fn" title="See all posts by ERIK PIEPENBURG">ERIK PIEPENBURG</a></address></header>
<!-- The Content -->
<div class="entry-content"><div class="w190 right"><a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/08/27/soho-rep-announces-new-season/"><img src="http://graphics8.nytimes.com/images/2013/08/27/theater/27ireland/27ireland-articleInline.jpg" id="100000002407744" width="190" height="184" alt="Marin Ireland"/></a><span class="credit">Michael Tammaro</span> <span class="caption">Marin Ireland</span></div>
<p>Soho Rep has big ambitions for its small new season. On Tuesday the downtown theater company announced it will mount two full productions in 2013-14: the New York premiere of David Adjmi’s &#8220;Marie Antoinette,&#8221; and &#8220;An Octoroon&#8221; by Branden Jacobs-Jenkins.</p>
<p>&#8220;Marie Antoinette&#8221; (Oct. 9-Nov. 3) is Mr. Adjmi&#8217;s (&#8220;3C&#8221;) modern take on the doomed French queen. The show had its premiere last year at Yale Rep in a coproduction with American Repertory Theater. Reprising their roles from that production will be the actors Marin Ireland as Marie, Steven Rattazzi as Louis XVI and David Greenspan as a sheep. Rebecca Taichman will direct. The play is Ms. Ireland&#8217;s first role at Soho Rep since her heralded turn in Sarah Kane&#8217;s <a href="http://theater2.nytimes.com/2008/10/10/theater/reviews/10blas.html">&#8220;Blasted&#8221;</a> in 2008. Other cast members include Marsha Stephanie Blake, Karl Miller and Jen Ikeda; additional casting is to be announced. In his <a href="http://theater.nytimes.com/2012/11/13/theater/reviews/david-adjmis-marie-antoinette-at-yale-repertory-theater.html">review of the show at Yale Rep</a>, Charles Isherwood called it a &#8220;cursory comic strip of a play&#8221; that echoes the Marie depicted in Sofia Coppola&#8217;s <a href="http://movies.nytimes.com/2006/10/13/movies/13mari.html">anachronistic, punk-infused movie</a> starring Kirsten Dunst.  <a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/08/27/soho-rep-announces-new-season/#more-316543" class="more-link">Read more&#8230;</a></p>

<h3 class="entry-title"><a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/08/27/restored-film-of-an-ill-fated-mt-everest-expedition-to-show-at-london-festival/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Restored Film of an Ill-Fated Mt. Everest Expedition to Show at London Festival">Restored Film of an Ill-Fated Mt. Everest Expedition to Show at London Festival</a></h3>
	<address class="byline author vcard">By <a href="/author/daniel-mcdermon/" class="url fn" title="See all posts by DANIEL MCDERMON">DANIEL MCDERMON</a></address></header>
<!-- The Content -->
<div class="entry-content"><div class="w480"><a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/08/27/restored-film-of-an-ill-fated-mt-everest-expedition-to-show-at-london-festival/"><img src="http://graphics8.nytimes.com/images/2013/08/27/arts/27artsbeat-everest/27artsbeat-everest-blog480.jpg" id="100000002408027" width="480" height="270" alt="Mount Everest, center."/></a><span class="credit">Prakash Mathema/Agence France-Presse — Getty Images</span> <span class="caption">Mount Everest, center.</span></div>
<p>A newly restored version of a film documenting an early attempt to climb Mount Everest will be shown as part of this year&#8217;s BFI London Film Festival, the British Film Institute <a href="http://www.bfi.org.uk/news-opinion/news-bfi/announcements/epic-everest-announced-lff-archive-gala">announced on Tuesday</a>. &#8220;The Epic of Everest,&#8221; directed by the British explorer Capt. John Noel, followed the 1924 expedition led by George Mallory, in which <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/1999/05/05/world/a-body-on-mt-everest-a-mystery-half-solved.html">both Mallory and another climber, Andrew Irvine, died</a>.</p>
<p>It remains unknown whether Mallory and Irvine  made it to Everest&#8217;s summit before they disappeared &#8212; if they did, they would have beaten Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay by nearly 30 years. The 1924 attempt was the third British-led expedition to Everest. Noel also filmed and photographed the second expedition, in 1922. For the 1924 effort, according to a <a href="http://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=F10612F93E5C147A93C5AB1788D85F438285F9">report in The Times</a>, Noel used four cameras, aiming to capture &#8220;the fascination of those secluded, lofty, divinely beautiful mountains of Tibet and the implacable majesty of the supreme mountain herself.&#8221;</p>
<p>In a statement, Robin Baker, the film institute&#8217;s head curator, said &#8220;The Epic of Everest&#8221; portrayed &#8220;a key moment in the history of mountaineering and remains an enduring monument to Mallory and Irvine.&#8221; After the Oct. 18 premiere of the restoration at the festival, &#8220;The Epic of Everest&#8221; will also be released in British theaters. The restoration includes a new score composed by Simon Fisher Turner, who will perform it live at the premiere. The <a href="http://www.bfi.org.uk/lff">BFI London Film Festival</a> runs  Oct. 9 to Oct. 20.</p>

<h3 class="entry-title"><a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/08/26/weekly-ticket-sales-drop-under-1-million-for-broadways-spider-man/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Weekly Ticket Sales Drop Under $1 Million for Broadway&#8217;s &#8216;Spider-Man&#8217;">Weekly Ticket Sales Drop Under $1 Million for Broadway&#8217;s &#8216;Spider-Man&#8217;</a></h3>
	<address class="byline author vcard">By <a href="/author/patrick-healy/" class="url fn" title="See all posts by PATRICK HEALY">PATRICK HEALY</a></address></header>
<!-- The Content -->
<div class="entry-content"><div class="w480"><a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/08/26/weekly-ticket-sales-drop-under-1-million-for-broadways-spider-man/"><img src="http://graphics8.nytimes.com/images/2013/08/26/theater/26spider/26spider-blog480.jpg" id="100000002406735" width="480" height="341" alt="A scene with the original cast of "Spider-Man: Turn Off the Dark" at the Foxwoods Theater."/></a><span class="credit">Sara Krulwich/The New York Times</span> <span class="caption">A scene with the original cast of &#8220;Spider-Man: Turn Off the Dark&#8221; at the Foxwoods Theater.</span></div>
<p>For the first time since performances began in November 2010, the Broadway musical “Spider-Man: Turn Off the Dark” took in less than $1 million for a standard eight-performance playing week, with last week’s gross totaling $966,952. While ticket sales have been softening since last fall for the $75 million musical, the most expensive in Broadway history, a spokesman on Monday linked last week’s total to “fallout” from a well-publicized foot injury suffered by the cast member Daniel Curry during the Aug. 15 performance.</p>
<p>The record-low $966,952 gross is notable because, over the last two years, the &#8220;Spider-Man&#8221; producers have explicitly pointed to their seven-figure grosses as a sign of audience validation for a musical that critics <a title="Times review." href="http://theater.nytimes.com/2011/06/15/theater/reviews/spider-man-turn-off-the-dark-opens-after-changes-review.html?pagewanted=all">widely panned.</a> The producers have also been optimistic about earning back the show’s $75 million capitalization, but that feat would require weekly box office grosses in the $1.5 million range for several years. Last week’s gross was below the show’s weekly running costs, which are in the low seven figures, and far below the $2.94 million that the show grossed for nine performances during the New Year’s holiday week in the 2011-12 season. That week <a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/01/03/broadway-box-office-soars-with-spider-man-flying-highest-ever/">set a record for the highest gross ever</a> on Broadway.  <a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/08/26/weekly-ticket-sales-drop-under-1-million-for-broadways-spider-man/#more-316490" class="more-link">Read more&#8230;</a></p>

<h3 class="entry-title"><a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/08/26/new-york-film-festival-to-showcase-documentaries-on-science-and-democracy/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to New York Film Festival to Showcase Documentaries on Science and Democracy">New York Film Festival to Showcase Documentaries on Science and Democracy</a></h3>
	<address class="byline author vcard">By <a href="/author/daniel-mcdermon/" class="url fn" title="See all posts by DANIEL MCDERMON">DANIEL MCDERMON</a></address></header>
<!-- The Content -->
<div class="entry-content"><p>Movies  about science and democracy and a series devoted to cinematic portraiture will anchor the documentary portion of the New York Film Festival, the Film Society of Lincoln Center <a href="http://www.filmlinc.com/nyff2013/blog/nyff-documentaries-revivals-scorsese-carax-ray-weerasetakhul-resnais">said on Monday</a>.</p>
<p>The Applied Science series will present three films about technology: Mark Levinson&#8217;s &#8220;Particle Fever&#8221; tells the story of the physicists at CERN and the search for the Higgs boson; &#8220;Google and the World Brain,&#8221; directed by Ben Lewis, recounts the search giant&#8217;s efforts to digitize the collections of the world&#8217;s libraries; and &#8220;Tim&#8217;s Vermeer,&#8221; directed by Teller, <a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/07/29/a-documentary-by-teller-explores-the-magic-of-vermeer/">investigates the techniques</a> used by the 17th-century painter.</p>
<p>Another series, How Democracy Works Now, will focus on films by Michael Camerini and Shari Robertson. They set out in 2001 to track efforts to overhaul immigration in the United States, following advocates and legislators in Washington in efforts to pass a comprehensive immigration bill. The filmmakers plan to complete 12 films in total; 10 are to be presented in the festival.</p>
<p>Motion Portraits, the last series, will include eight features that take decidedly different approaches toward the portrait. Among them are Nancy Buirski&#8217;s &#8220;Afternoon of a Faun: Tanaquil Le Clercq,&#8221; which illuminates Le Clercq&#8217;s role as <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2012/02/19/t-magazine/inside-the-mind-of-tanaquil-le-clercq.html?pagewanted=all">the wife and muse</a> of George Balanchine, and &#8220;The Dog,&#8221; directed by Allison Berg and Frank Keraudren, which recounts the tale of John Wojtowicz, whose attempt to rob a Chase Manhattan Bank in Gravesend, Brooklyn, <a href="http://cityroom.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/08/22/a-botched-robbery-went-hollywood/">inspired the film &#8220;Dog Day Afternoon.&#8221;</a></p>
<p>The festival opens on Sept. 27. A full list is at <a href="http://www.filmlinc.com/nyff2013/blog/nyff-documentaries-revivals-scorsese-carax-ray-weerasetakhul-resnais">the film festival&#8217;s Web site</a>.</p>

<h3 class="entry-title"><a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/08/26/mtv-awards-show-prompts-a-familiar-complaint/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to MTV Awards Show Prompts Familiar Complaint">MTV Awards Show Prompts Familiar Complaint</a></h3>
	<address class="byline author vcard">By <a href="/author/james-c-mckinley-jr/" class="url fn" title="See all posts by JAMES C. MCKINLEY JR.">JAMES C. MCKINLEY JR.</a></address></header>
<!-- The Content -->
<div class="entry-content"><div class="w480"><a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/08/26/mtv-awards-show-prompts-a-familiar-complaint/"><img src="http://graphics8.nytimes.com/images/2013/08/26/arts/26artsbeat-miley2/26artsbeat-miley2-blog480.jpg" id="100000002406235" width="480" height="354" alt="Robin Thicke and Miley Cyrus at the Video Music Awards show on Sunday."/></a><span class="credit">Eric Thayer/Reuters</span> <span class="caption">Robin Thicke and Miley Cyrus at the Video Music Awards show on Sunday.</span></div>
<p>MTV has once again outraged an advocacy group that wants to reduce the level of adult content and profanity on television. The <a href="http://w2.parentstv.org/Main/">Parents Television Council</a>, a nonprofit group known for criticizing shows, including &#8220;Family Guy,&#8221; for explicit content and adult jokes, took the network to task for airing condom ads and staging performances with skimpy costumes and sexually suggestive dancing during <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/27/arts/music/mtv-video-music-awards-review.html?ref=arts">the Video Music Awards</a> on Sunday.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/08/26/mika-brzezinski-miley-cyrus-vma_n_3816462.html">Some</a> <a href="http://www.today.com/entertainment/brooke-shields-hannah-montana-co-star-miley-cyrus-its-bit-8C10995696">viewers</a> have said that <a href="http://www.mtv.com/videos/misc/942064/we-cant-stop-blurred-lines-give-it-2-u-medley.jhtml">Miley Cyrus&#8217;s performance</a>, which included miming coitus with Robin Thicke and rump-shaking among dancing bears, was in particularly poor taste.</p>
<p>The Parents Television Council&#8217;s complaint about the show, which echoes <a href="http://query.nytimes.com/search/sitesearch/#/%22Parents+Television+Council%22+MTV/since1851/allresults/1/allauthors/newest/">similar statements</a> it has made in years past, took on greater weight this year because Ms. Cyrus&#8217;s father, Billy Ray Cyrus, <a href="http://w2.parentstv.org/Main/About/AdvisoryBoard.aspx">sits on the group&#8217;s advisory board</a>. Representatives for Mr. Cyrus did not respond to requests for comment, but he may have made his opinion known on Twitter last night, <a href="https://twitter.com/billyraycyrus/status/371898452415762432">writing</a>: &#8220;Thanking God for so many blessings tonight. Continue to pray for world peace. More love &#8230; less hate.&#8221;</p>
<p>MTV rated the program TV-14, <a href="http://www.tvguidelines.org/ratings.htm">which means</a> the program &#8220;contains some material that many parents would find unsuitable for children under 14 years of age.&#8221; But the Parents Television Council said the show crossed a line and that the network should have warned parents against letting 14-year-olds &#8212; eighth graders, typically &#8212; view it without supervision. They also complained that the ads for birth control and R-rated movies during the show were aimed at an adult audience.  <a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/08/26/mtv-awards-show-prompts-a-familiar-complaint/#more-316434" class="more-link">Read more&#8230;</a></p>
