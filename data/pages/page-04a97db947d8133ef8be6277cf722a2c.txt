		<h2>2012 Hyundai Sonata&#8230;Convertible?</h2>
		<em class="date">May 26th, 2010
<a href="http://www.carsdirect.com/author/armaan-almeida/" rel="author">by Armaan Almeida <span class="meta-nav"></span></a></em>
							
		<div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 477px"><img title="2011 Hyundai Sonata" src="http://cdc-img.ibsrv.net/cms/2010/5/26/2011_hyundai_sonata_convertible49761023719174639.jpg" alt="Image Courtesy of Auto Spies" width="467" height="249" /><p class="wp-caption-text">Image Courtesy of Auto Spies</p></div>
<p><a href="http://pricinginsider.carsdirect.com/2010/01/19/top-ten-cars-to-look-forward-to-in-2010-2011-hyundai-sonata/comment-page-1/" target="_blank">We called it.</a> And in today&#8217;s edition of the L.A. Times, the notion that <a href="http://www.latimes.com/business/la-fi-hyundai-20100526,0,3396157.story" target="_blank">Hyundai is catching Toyota</a> emanates, yet again.  Though this time in pulp.  Hyundai has really stepped up their game. Not only have they decided to offer their flagship Sonata in a <a href="http://www.hybridcars.com/vehicle/hyundai-sonata-hybrid.html" target="_blank">hybrid</a> form, it seems that they are also poised to bring the same vehicle to the market as a convertible. The Sonata Convertible will aim to fill the shoes currently occupied (but not for long) by the <a href="http://www.carsdirect.com/2010/chrysler/sebring" target="_blank">Chrysler Sebring Convertible</a>.</p>
<p>The Long-running Sebring has been alone in the 4-seater convertible market for several years. Although a popular market share a few decades ago, these vehicles have by and large gone the way of the dodo. The Sebring (although the target of a little bit of criticism in recent years) has been the virtually the only choice in this category.</p>
<p>The new Sonata convertible will be seeking to change that. With more dramatic styling than the Sebring, (and hopefully a more affordable price tag) the Sonata may have a shot at grabbing a chunk of a more or less neglected market share.</p>
<p>The new convertible will likely be available for 2012 or 2013 in all the trim lines that are currently offered for the sedan line, although the hybrid will probably not be available for at least a year. Although it is still in the planning phases, if the Sonata convertible will be offered it may just represent a significant change in a market sector that hasn’t seen activity in a long time.</p>
