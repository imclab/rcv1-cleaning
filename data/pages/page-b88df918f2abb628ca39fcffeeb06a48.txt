<h1>Buying or Selling a Car in Portugal</h1>
<h2>Information on the paperwork and process involved  whether buying a car from a dealership or a secondhand car through a  classified advertisement...</h2>

<p>The Government Traffic Department, <em>Instituto da Mobilidade e dos Transportes Terrestres</em>, <a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.imtt.pt"><strong>IMTT</strong></a> (previously the DGV) deals with all aspects of car ownership, registration and licensing.</p>

<p>The Institute of Registration and Notaries (<em>Insitituto dos Registos e do Notariado</em>, <a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.irn.mj.pt/IRN/sections/inicio"><strong>IRN</strong></a>) provides civil registry offices where citizens take care of many administrative matters, including vehicle registration.</p>

<ul>
	<li>Vehicle registration forms and information may be downloaded from the <em>Registo     de Veículos</em> section of the IRN website: <a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.irn.mj.pt/IRN/sections/irn/a_registral/registo-de-veiculos"><strong>Click     here</strong></a> (in Portuguese)</li>
	<li>Registration may be completed online: <a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.automovelonline.mj.pt/AutoOnline/FrontOfficeController?action=changeLanguage&amp;language=en_EN."><strong>Click     here</strong></a><strong> </strong>(in English and Portuguese)</li>
	<li>To find a local IRN office: <a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.irn.mj.pt/IRN/sections/irn/contactos"><strong>Click     here</strong></a></li>
</ul>

<p>Note that most of the websites and documentation needed are in  Portuguese. For those who don't speak Portuguese, staff at the Citizens  Shops (<em>Lojas do Cidadão</em>) can help with all matters concerning vehicles and registration.</p>

<ul>
	<li>For a list of the local Citizens Shops: <strong><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.portaldocidadao.pt/PORTAL/pt/LojaCidadao">Click here</a></strong></li>
</ul>
<h5>Buying a New Car</h5>

<p>Dealerships selling new cars take care of the registration of the vehicle on behalf of the buyer. Dealerships also often offer insurance deals (it is a legal requirement for all vehicles to have at least third party car insurance).</p>

<h4>Documents</h4>

<p>When the vehicle is turned in for destruction to a VFV-compliant centre, the owner provides the following documents:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Form 9 (<em>Modelo 9 IMTT</em>) which cancels registration 
<ul>
	<li>To access the form online: <a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.imtt.pt/sites/IMTT/Portugues/Formularios/Documents/Modelo9.pdf"><strong>Click         here</strong></a></li>
</ul>
</li>
	<li>Vehicle registration documents </li>
</ul>
<h5>Buying and Selling a Second Hand Car</h5>

<p>Buying a second hand or used car (<em>de segunda mão/usado</em>) in  Portugal is generally more expensive than in other European Union  countries. There are several reasons for this, including the fact that  depreciation is slower and higher taxes on purchases means that new cars  tend to hold their value better than they do in other countries.</p>

<p>A foreigner may buy a Portuguese-registered car in Portugal provided they have at least one of the following documents:</p>

<ul>
	<li>An official Residence Permit (<em>Cartão de Residência</em>) <em> or</em></li>
	<li>Proof that they are a Portuguese home owner (by showing title deeds) <em> or</em></li>
	<li>A rental contract for a minimum duration of one year</li>
</ul>
<h4>Where to find a second hand car</h4>

<p>Used cars for sale are available through dealerships, or may be advertised in automobile magazines, such as:</p>

<ul>
	<li><strong><em><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.guiadoautomovel.pt/">Guia Automóvel</a></em></strong> (monthly) </li>
	<li><strong><em><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.autohoje.com/">Auto-Hoje</a></em></strong> (weekly)</li>
	<li><strong><em><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.autocomercio.net/">Auto-Comércio</a></em></strong> (monthly)</li>
</ul>

<p>Newspapers also carry motor classified advertisements:</p>

<ul>
	<li><strong><em><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.ocasiao.pt/">Auto Ocasião</a></em></strong> </li>
	<li><strong><em><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.correiomanha.pt/">Correio da Manha</a></em></strong></li>
	<li><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://jn.sapo.pt/paginainicial/"><strong><em>Jornal de Noticias</em></strong></a></li>
</ul>

<p>Online publications:</p>

<ul>
	<li><strong><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.automoveis.pt/">Automóveis.pt</a></strong> </li>
	<li><strong><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.autogeiza.pt/">Auto Geiza</a></strong></li>
</ul>

<p>When scrolling down online publications look for words such as <strong><em>classificados</em></strong> (classifieds) and then <strong><em>automoveis</em></strong> or <strong><em>carros</em></strong> (cars). The site may specify new cars (<em>novos</em>) or second hand cars (<em>usados</em>).</p>

<p>There are also two Internet auction sites (<em>Leilões</em>) in the Lisbon area which deal in cars:</p>

<ul>
	<li><strong><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.megaleiloes.com/">Mega Leilões</a></strong> </li>
	<li><strong><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.miau.pt/">Miau</a></strong> (Portuguese ebay-type site)</li>
</ul>
<h4>Transfer of ownership</h4>

<p>It is the <strong> seller's responsibility</strong> to provide the form for the registration of the change of ownership (<em>Transferência de Propriedade </em>or <em>Contrato verbal de compra e venda</em>). This Form 2 (<em>Modelo 2</em>), available from the IRN, contains information about the vehicle, the previous owner and the new owner. At the point of sale it should be completed and signed by both the buyer and the seller.</p>

<p>It is the <strong>buyer's responsibility</strong> to register the change of ownership at the local IRN vehicle registration department (<em>Instituto dos Registos e do Notariado</em>) within 30 days of purchase.</p>

<ul>
	<li>To download the form<strong> </strong>for <strong> registration of change of ownership </strong>(<em>Requerimento de Registo Automóvel </em>): <a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.irn.mj.pt/IRN/sections/irn/a_registral/servicos-externos-docs/impressos/automovel/impressos-de-registo-de"><strong>Click     here</strong></a></li>
	<li>To find the local civil registry IRN office: <strong><a class="external" target="_blank" href="/r.asp?http://www.irn.mj.pt/IRN/sections/irn/contactos">Click here</a></strong> </li>
</ul>
<h4>The seller must provide</h4>

<p><strong>At the point of sale the seller should provide the following:</strong></p>

<ul>
	<li>The transfer of ownership Form 2 for completion and signing</li>
	<li>The vehicle's registration papers:<br />
 This will either be a <strong>DUA</strong> All-In-One Vehicle Document (<em>Documento Único Automóvel</em>) also   called license plate certificate (<em>Certificado de Matrícula</em>) or two documents:

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li><strong>vehicle registration document </strong>(<em>Título de Registo de Propriedade</em>)</li>
	<li><strong>log book</strong> (<em>Livrete</em>)</li>
</ul>
</li>
	<li><span style="background-color: #ffffff">Proof of roadworthiness: a valid <strong> IPO</strong> (<em>Inspecção Periódica Obrigatória</em>) test   certificate</span></li>
	<li>Green Technical Inspection card/certificate (<em>Calendário das Inspecções</em> <em>Técnicas de Veículo</em>,   also called: <em>Carta Inspecção Técnica Periódica</em>) which details the vehicle's inspection history and when the next inspection is due</li>
	<li>Owner's Municipal Vehicle Tax Contribution (<em>Imposto Municipal de</em> <em>Veiculo</em>)   documents or card as proof of paid up vehicle tax</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Note</strong>: The DUA document of more recently registered vehicles incorporates the information previously kept in three separate documents.</p>

<h4><strong>Registering the new owner</strong></h4>

<p>A foreign buyer will need to provide the following documents when registering the vehicle at the IRN office:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Proof of residence in Portugal as outlined above (proof by means of residence card, property title deeds or rental contract)</li>
	<li>Proof of vehicle roadworthiness (the IPO test certificate) provided by the   seller </li>
	<li>The vehicle registration papers provided by the seller </li>
	<li>Proof of up-to-date payments of vehicle tax (<em>Imposto Municipal de   Veículos</em>)   provided by the seller </li>
	<li>A registration fee</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Note</strong>: The registration of transfer of ownership of a used vehicle bought from a dealership will be handled by the dealer who should also provide a warranty and service history.</p>

<p>If, following the process above, the buyer does not submit the change of ownership documents to the IRN vehicle registration department, responsibility for all fines or accidents involving the vehicle remain the responsibility of the seller. It is strongly advised to confirm that the buyer has completed the transfer process.</p><div id="copyrightInformation"><div style="text-align:center;"></div></div>
