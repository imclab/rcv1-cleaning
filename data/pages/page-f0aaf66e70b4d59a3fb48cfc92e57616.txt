			<h1 class="xxlarge">2013 Hyundai Genesis Coupe 2.0T Auto</h1>
			<p class="postmetadata">
								<span class="author">By <a href="http://www.roadtestreviews.com/author/NigelTheLimey/" title="Posts by Nigel Atkinson" rel="author">Nigel Atkinson</a></span>				 / <time datetime="2012-05-11" pubdate>11 May 2012</time>				 / <a href="http://www.roadtestreviews.com/2013-hyundai-genesis-coupe-2-0t-auto/#respond" title="Comment on 2013 Hyundai Genesis Coupe 2.0T Auto">0 comments</a>			</p>
			<div class="post-content">
				<p><strong><br />
<span style="color: #800000;"><a href="http://www.roadtestreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/genesis.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-663" title="Hyundai Genesis Coupe 2.0T" src="http://www.roadtestreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/genesis.jpg" alt="Hyundai Genesis Coupe 2.0T" width="300" height="187" /></a>What&#8217;s Good</span></strong><br />
Great Styling<br />
Rorty engine<br />
Handling</p>
<p><strong><span style="color: #800000;">What&#8217;s Not Good</span><br />
</strong>Doesn&#8217;t feel fast<br />
Clumsy automatic transmission<br />
Plasticky interior</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Road Test Reviews score: <span style="color: #800000;">6</span> <span><span style="color: #800000;">out of 10</span><br />
<strong>Dealer Experience &#8211; Frank Hyundai: <span style="color: #800000;">5 out of 10<br />
</span></strong><br />
</span></strong></p>
<blockquote><p>Thanks to <strong><span style="color: #800000;">Frank Hyundai</span></strong> for letting us test the <strong>2013 Hyundai Genesis Coupe 2.0T</strong> <strong>Auto.</strong> I really wanted to test a manual transmission but we couldn&#8217;t get one so &#8230;..oh well. I had high hopes for this car after all it looks terrific and if you are in the market for a small 2 door sports car with a 4 cylinder engine then this is one to look into. The outgoing model was not particularly good so let&#8217;s hope this one will wow us.</p>
<p>So the engine is all new this year up from 210 hp to <strong>275hp and 274lbs torque</strong>.</p></blockquote>
<p>The problem is the torque is there but it seems to <a href="http://www.roadtestreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/genesis-engine.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-664" title="genesis-engine" src="http://www.roadtestreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/genesis-engine.jpg" alt="genesis-engine" width="280" height="184" /></a>kick in too high in the rev range. When you&#8217;re out of boost the car labors, then the turbo comes on suddenly, making it really tough to be smooth mid-corner&#8230;<em>.I&#8217;ll come back to that later.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><em>Ok I am back</em>&#8230;.The new twin scroll turbo and continuously variable valve timing means that max torque arrives at 2000rpm&#8230;&#8230;really? It didn&#8217;t feel like it, but I believe this may have something to do with the transmission. You see in <strong><em>flappy paddle shift</em></strong> mode it takes an age to choose a gear, especially under hard acceleration and in auto mode it can&#8217;t find the right gear&#8230; it hiccups and burps a bit before engaging and this makes the car feel not particularly fast. It&#8217;s like the guy at the Bingo place picking a number, he takes a while.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Hyundai claims 0-60 in 5.7 seconds</strong> but it just seems much less sharp than it should be.Sure the car corners well, feels well glued to the road (<em>new suspension to thank</em>) but it doesn&#8217;t have that sports car feel. You know just adding more power is not always the best way Hyundai. On the plus side it sounds great on the move with a rorty honking noise coming from the exhaust. Outside the car it sounds raucous and we like that a lot.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="http://www.roadtestreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/interior1.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-668" title="Hyundai Genesis Interior" src="http://www.roadtestreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/interior1.jpg" alt="Hyundai Genesis Interior" width="313" height="170" /></a>The inside doesn&#8217;t match what the outside promises, but it is all new and has a great<br />
look but suffers from a cheap feel and a lack of attention to detail. The soft feel<br />
plastics work but they are mixed with gaudy looking hard plastic in other areas. This is something that really needs some fine tuning.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The seats are not very supportive and for taller drivers would be uncomfortable (<em>lack of</em> <em>headroom</em>) after a while. The steering wheel blocks the speedometer when it is in the lower position.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>This car is squarely aimed at under 35&#8242;s  because no one in this age group wants to be caught driving a Mustang!  The upcoming  Scion FRS and Subaru BRZ are going give this car a hard time as they are more purist sports cars outright.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>So not what I expected overall; it checks certain boxes but it feels a little lazy and I would expect more from Hyundai at this point.  As I stepped out of the car I felt like I had a drive in a competent car that completely lacked zing, and how would life be without a little bit of Zing? <strong>Boring&#8230;&#8230;.</strong></p>
<p><strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>What would we do?</strong><br />
Bin the lazy transmission and fit a DSG transmission <em>a la</em> VW Gti or buy a manual transmission (<em>which we haven&#8217;t tested yet</em>), upgrade the interior (its cheap) and you might have a winner here. Otherwise were heading to the Subaru dealer and getting a <strong>Subaru BRZ</strong> which we should be testing soon, stay tuned&#8230;&#8230;.</p>
