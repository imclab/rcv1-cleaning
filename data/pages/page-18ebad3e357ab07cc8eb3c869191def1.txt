		<h2><a href="http://fineart.about.com/b/2013/08/25/more-art-museums-in-new-mexico.htm" zT="1/1jS">More Art Museums in New Mexico</a></h2>
		
		<div class="date">Sunday August 25, 2013</div>	
	<div class="entry"><p>Here are more profiles of art museums in New Mexico, including employment info and how to apply for a job.</p>

<p><strong>Taos</strong></p>

<ul>
	<li><a style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;" href="http://fineart.about.com/od/New-Mexico-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-Taos-Art-Museum-NM.htm" zT="1/1UH">Taos Art Museum</a><span style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;"> in Taos is housed in an adobe-style building which was once home to Russian artist Nicolai Fechin.</span></li>
	<li><a style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;" href="http://fineart.about.com/od/New-Mexico-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-Harwood-Museum-of-Art-University-of-New-Mexico-Taos-NM.htm" zT="1/1UH">Harwood Museum of Art at University of New Mexico</a><span style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;"> in Taos has a collection of over 3,000 works.</span></li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Santa Fe</strong></p>

<ul>
	<li><a style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;" href="http://fineart.about.com/od/New-Mexico-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-Museum-of-Indian-Arts-and-Culture-Laboratory-of-Anthropology-NM.htm" zT="1/1UH">Museum of Indian Arts and Culture</a><span style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;"> in Santa Fe has over 75,000 objects in its collection.</span></li>
	<li><a style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;" href="http://fineart.about.com/od/New-Mexico-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-The-New-Mexico-Museum-of-Art-Santa-Fe-New-Mexico.htm" zT="1/1UH">The New Mexico Museum of Art</a><span style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;"> in Santa Fe has over 20,000 objects, which reflect the state's diverse cultures.</span></li>
</ul></div>

		<h2><a href="http://fineart.about.com/b/2013/08/20/step-by-step-art-appraisal-of-a-van-gogh-painting.htm" zT="1/1jS">Step by Step Art Appraisal of a van Gogh Painting</a></h2>
		
		<div class="date">Tuesday August 20, 2013</div>	
	<div class="entry"><p>In light of the tragic news that the Detroit Institute of Art (DIA)'s valuable art collection may be auctioned off to pay the city's creditors, <a href="http://www.artspace.com/magazine/art_101/anatomy_of_an_art_appraisal?utm_source=Sailthru&utm_medium=email&utm_term=Master&utm_campaign=August_18_2013_Editorial_Weekly" zT="1/XL/X[" target="_new">Artspace</a> ran an interesting article about appraising one of its key artworks.</p>

<p>Art appraiser Elizabeth von Habsburg, who co-authored <i>Appraising Art: The Definitive Guide to Appraising the Fine and Decorative Art</i> took a professional eye to DIA's van Gogh's <i>Bank of the Oise at Auvers</i>. The Artspace article goes step by step explaining how von Habsburg assesses a work of art. She said, "This has everything you want in a van Gogh: lovely brushstrokes, strong colors, strong figures. The brushstrokes, especially, are what you want and here they are everywhere." </p>

<p>Learn more about <a href="http://fineart.about.com/od/Art-Appraisers/Art-Appraisers.htm" zT="1/1UH">art appraising</a>.</div>

		<h2><a href="http://fineart.about.com/b/2013/08/20/art-museums-in-new-mexico.htm" zT="1/1jS">Art Museums in New Mexico</a></h2>
		
		<div class="date">Tuesday August 20, 2013</div>	
	<div class="entry"><p>New Mexico is the home to many great art museums ranging from folk art to Modernism. If you live in New Mexico, you may want to check out the job listings of the following museums:</p>

<ul>
	<li><a href="http://fineart.about.com/od/New-Mexico-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-Museum-of-International-Folk-Art-Santa-Fe-New-Mexico.htm" zT="1/1UH">The Museum of International Folk Art</a> in Santa Fe, New Mexico opened to the public in September 1953 and has over 135,000 artifacts from more than 100 countries in its collection.</li>
	<li><a href="http://fineart.about.com/od/New-Mexico-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-The-Museum-of-Contemporary-Native-Arts-MoCNA-New-Mexico.htm" zT="1/1UH">Museum of Contemporary Native Arts (MoCNA)</a> in Santa Fe, New Mexico opened to the public in 1972 and has a permanent collection of over 7,500 works.</li>
	<li><a href="http://fineart.about.com/od/New-Mexico-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-The-Albuquerque-Museum-of-Art-and-History-New-Mexico.htm" zT="1/1UH">The Albuquerque Museum of Art and History</a> in Albuquerque, New Mexico opened to the public in 1967; its permanent collection includes approximately 7,000 works which focus on art and culture of the American Southwest.</li>
	<li><a href="http://fineart.about.com/od/New-Mexico-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-The-Roswell-Museum-and-Art-Center-New-Mexico.htm" zT="1/1UH">The Roswell Museum and Art Center</a> in Roswell, New Mexico opened to the public in 1937. Its permanent collection includes both fine art, decorative art and historical objects which reflect the changes in the Southwest.</li>
	<li><a href="http://fineart.about.com/od/New-Mexico-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-SITE-Santa-Fe-New-Mexico.htm" zT="1/1UH">SITE Santa Fe</a> in Santa Fe, New Mexico opened to the public in 1995. Its purpose was to host the first international biennial of contemporary art in the USA. SITE does not maintain a permanent collection, but hosts year-round exhibitions of contemporary art.</li>
	<li><a href="http://fineart.about.com/od/New-Mexico-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-Georgia-O-Keeffe-Museum-Santa-Fe-New-Mexico.htm" zT="1/1UH">The Georgia O'Keeffe Museum</a> in Santa Fe, New Mexico opened to the public in July 1997 and is devoted to exhibiting the artwork of its namesake. Its permanent collection includes over 3,000 works.</li>
</ul></div>

		<h2><a href="http://fineart.about.com/b/2013/08/15/more-art-museums-in-vermont.htm" zT="1/1jS">More Art Museums in Vermont</a></h2>
		
		<div class="date">Thursday August 15, 2013</div>	
	<div class="entry"><p>Here are more profiles of art museums in Vermont. And if you're in Shelburne, be sure to go the Grand Opening of the Shelburne Museum's Pizzagalli Center for Art and Education on Sunday, August 18, 2013.</p>

<ul><li><a href="http://fineart.about.com/od/Vermont-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-St-Johnsbury-Athenaeum-Vermont.htm" zT="1/1UH">The St. Johnsbury Athenaeum</a> in St. Johnsbury, Vermont was established in 1871. Its library has more than 45,000 books, and the gallery is known for its fine collection of paintings by the Hudson River School artists.</li>

<li><a href="http://fineart.about.com/od/Vermont-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-Shelburne-Museum-Vermont.htm" zT="1/1UH">The Shelburne Museum</a> was established in 1947, and its permanent collection includes over 150,000 works of art, design and Americana. </li>

<li><a href="http://fineart.about.com/od/Vermont-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-The-Middlebury-College-Museum-of-Art-Vermont.htm" zT="1/1UH">The Middlebury College Museum of Art</a> in Middlebury was established in 1968. It has more than 2,500 objects from the ancient world to today, and includes antiquities, Asian art, and western painting and sculpture.</li>

<li><a href="http://fineart.about.com/od/Vermont-Art-Museums/p/Long-Profile-Of-The-Southern-Vermont-Arts-Center-Manchester-Vermont.htm" zT="1/1UH">Southern Vermont Arts Center (SVAC)</a> in Manchester was established in 1922 by a small group of painters. Its collection includes approximately 800 pieces of 19th and 20th century works.</li></ul></div>
