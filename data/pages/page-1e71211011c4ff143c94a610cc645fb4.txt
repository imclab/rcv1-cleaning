        	<span class="paragraph-0">
				<p>ST. MICHAELS -- Award-winning author Laura Oliver will address "Adding Humor to the Personal Essay: Ten Easy Tips" for the Eastern Shore Writers Association at 11 a.m. Saturday at the Town Dock Restaurant. The program is free and open to the public.</p>
			</span>
        	<span class="paragraph-1">
				<p>"Even poignant personal essays become more memorable when you have mastered this useful and versatile skill," Oliver said. "This is a rare opportunity to learn the how-to of literary humor writing and to have fun while doing it. As W. H. Auden said: 'Among those whom I like, I can find no common denominator, but among those whom I love, I can; all of them make me laugh.' This talk will demonstrate the transformative power of humor and how to create it."</p>
			</span>
                    <p>Oliver is the author of "The Story Within: New Insights and Inspiration for Writers," which was chosen as one of the best writing books of the year by <em>Poets &amp; Writers</em> and <em>The Writer Magazine</em>.</p>
                
                    <p>Her essays and short stories appear in numerous regional and national periodicals such as <em>The Washington Post</em>, <em>Country Living</em>, <em>The SUN Magazine</em> and <em>Glimmer Train</em>. She has taught creative writing at the University of Maryland and currently teaches writing at St. John's College and The Writer's Center.</p>
                
                    <p>Nominated for a Pushcart Prize, her work has won numerous distinctions, including a Maryland State Arts Council Individual Artist Award in Fiction and Arts Council of Anne Arundel County Annie Award. Her Master of Fine Arts is from Bennington College. For more information, visit <a href="http://www.thestorywithin.com">www.thestorywithin.com</a>.</p>
                
                    <p>All writers and guests are welcome to attend this free event. An RSVP is not required but can be sent to <a href="http://jrevealverizon.net">jrevealverizon.net</a>.</p>
                
                    <p>For more information on the Eastern Shore Writers Association, visit <a href="http://www.easternshorewriters.org">www.easternshorewriters.org</a> or write to Hal Wilson, President, ESWA, P.O. Box 1773, Easton, MD 21601.</p>
