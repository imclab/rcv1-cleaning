					<h1 class="entry-title">Drop Top Customs Chevy Camaro Convertible</h1>

</script></div><p style="text-align: center;"><a rel="attachment wp-att-5499" href="http://fancytuning.com/2009/10/drop-top-customs-chevy-camaro-convertible/drop-top-customs-chevy-camaro1/"><img class="size-full wp-image-5499 aligncenter" title="drop-top-customs-chevy-camaro-convertible" src="http://fancytuning.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/10/drop-top-customs-chevy-camaro1.jpg" alt="drop-top-customs-chevy-camaro-convertible" width="500" height="332" /></a></p>
<p><strong>Drop Top Customs</strong> has taken the <strong>Chevy Camaro</strong> and done something not many would even consider doing to their car, chop the roof off! The <strong>tuning</strong> company is based out of Florida, under Convertible Builders LLC, and they do some of the most interesting modifications I&#8217;ve ever seen. Taking several different models, they turn each into a convertible soft-top with extensive custom work and structural reinforcements. Some other models they have converted to convertibles include the Dodge Challenger, Cadillac CTS, and the Chrysler 300C. This 2010 <strong>Chevrolet Camaro</strong> will be revealed at the SEMA Auto Show, and should be the center of attention with this customization.</p>
<p><span id="more-5495"></span>The owner, Jeff Moran established the company as Coachbuilders Limited in 1976 and the low-volume soft-top business has been a success ever since. <strong>Chevy</strong> was planning to make a convertible <strong>Camaro</strong> from the factory, however it is at least 18 months away since GM has put the build officially on hold. Jeff Moran doesn&#8217;t believe that is would be a &#8220;sound business move for GM to take on a project that has greater potential to lose money than to be profitable.&#8221; To have your Camaro converted by <strong>Drop Top Customs</strong> it will cost you 18,000 USD, and they have already started taking orders. Visit their Facebook page or website directly to follow all of their progress and custom builds.</p>
