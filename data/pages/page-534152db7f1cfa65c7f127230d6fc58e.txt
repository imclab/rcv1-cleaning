										<h2>Custom Car Culture loses an icon: Dean Jeffries</h2>
										
										<div class="author">
											 posted by <a href="http://www.round2corp.com" title="Visit RebaR&#8217;s website" rel="author external">RebaR</a> 6:53 PM										</div>
										<div class="date">
											Friday, May 10, 2013										</div>
									</div></div></div></div>
								</div></div></div></div>
							</div>
							
							<div class="text-box">
								<p>If Dean Jeffries thought you were crazy, he&#8217;d say you were &#8220;bananas.&#8221; If he liked what you were doin&#8217;, he&#8217;d say &#8220;that&#8217;s bitchin&#8217;!&#8221; The car modeling world, as well as 1:1 custom car culture has lost a great man. His disposition was humble, yet his self taught talents were immense. Sure, he started with pin striping and flame painting, but what about those cars! Who can argue the design and execution of the Mantaray? If it&#8217;s not the most exciting custom car ever built, we don&#8217;t know what is. In fact, back in the 1960s, plans to produce the Mantaray as a 1/25 model kit were drawn up by the original Kats at AMT. Later, the plans landed in the hands of MPC, but a slot car instead of a static kit was the single item to be produced.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Other 1:1 custom cars designed and built by Dean that became incredibly successful model kits include the Monkeemobile and Coyote dune buggy, both released by MPC in 1:25 scale. The infamous Green Hornet Black Beauty was released as a 1:32 scale kit by Aurora and later by Polar Lights.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>We feel privileged to have known Dean and are very sad about his passing. We hope that wherever he is, he&#8217;s striping and building bitchin&#8217; customs. <a href="http://www.autoworldblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/AMM957_MonkeeMobile.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-374" src="http://www.autoworldblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/AMM957_MonkeeMobile-300x144.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="144" /></a></p>
