            <span class="breadcrumb"><a href="/arts/index.htm">IAC</a>&nbsp;&gt;&nbsp;<a href="/arts/2654.htm">Indiana Cultural Districts</a>&nbsp;&gt;&nbsp;Bloomington Entertainment and Arts District (BEAD)</span>
            <span>Bloomington Entertainment and Arts District (BEAD)</span>
          </h2>
          <div id="col2content">
            <p>
<P><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: ">&nbsp;<SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: "><IMG height=133 src="/arts/files/BEADLogo.jpg" width=105>&nbsp; </SPAN>&nbsp;<IMG height=135 src="/arts/images/BEADimage_gallery.jpg" width=195>&nbsp;&nbsp; <IMG height=135 src="/arts/images/BEADimage_recordshop.jpg" width=195><BR><BR></SPAN></P>
<P><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: "></SPAN><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: ">Experience what makes Bloomington Bloomington.</SPAN></P>
<P><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: ">Beats. Eats. Arts. Digs. Shops.<BR></SPAN><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: ">60 blocks of fabulosity.<BR></SPAN><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: ">Found only in downtown Bloomington.</SPAN></P>
<P><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: ">The Bloomington Entertainment and Arts District (BEAD) is galleries, live music, performing arts, over 90 restaurants and 100 specialty shops, parks, hotels, cozy neighborhoods in ten distinct character districts â each with a personality all its own.&nbsp; Walkable, rideable, don't miss-able.&nbsp; A cultural district like no other.</SPAN></P>
<P><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: ">To make BEAD your next arts and entertainment destination go to <A href="http://www.visitbead.com/">http://www.visitbead.com/</A>.<BR></SPAN><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: ">Itâs got the rundown on everything there is to see and do in downtown.<BR><BR></SPAN><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: ">Start your visit at the official BEAD Info Stop at the BCT Box Office &amp; Downtown Visitor Center at 114 E. Kirkwood Avenue.&nbsp; There youâll find more information on BEAD and other attractions throughout Bloomington. </SPAN></P>
<P><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: ">BEAD is an economic development project of the City of Bloomington.&nbsp; For more information contact <A href="mailto:bead@bloomington.in.gov">bead@bloomington.in.gov</A> or call 812.349.3534.</SPAN><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: "></P>
<H3>Official Proclamation</H3>
<P>BEAD celebrated the official public launch of the community's designation as a state-certified cultural district on Saturday, September 4, 2010 during their annual Fourth Street Festival of Arts &amp; Crafts.</P>
<P>Local leaders read a proclamation from Governor Mitch Daniels.&nbsp; Elected officials that took part in the celebration included State Representative Eric Koch, State Senator Vi Simpson, State Representative Matt Pierce, State Representative Peggy Welch, and City of Bloomington Mayor Mark Kruzan.&nbsp; For a taste of the celebration, play the video below.</P></SPAN><SPAN style="FONT-FAMILY: ">
