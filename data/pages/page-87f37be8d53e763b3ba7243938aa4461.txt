        <h3>About The Museum of Fine Arts, Houston</h3>
        <p><p>The Museum of Fine Arts, Houston, is one of the largest art museums in America, with more than 300,000 square feet of space devoted to the display of art. The collection contains over 63,000 works of art.   There are two major museum buildings, the Caroline Wiess Law Building and the Audrey Jones Beck Building; two facilities for the <a href=" http://www.mfah.org/visit/glassell-junior-school/" target="_blank">Glassell School of Art</a>, the <a href="http://www.mfah.org/visit/glassell-studio-school/" target="_blank">Studio School of Art</a> for adults and the Glassell Junior School; two house museums that exhibit decorative arts, <a href="http://www.mfah.org/visit/bayou-bend-collection-and-gardens/" target="_blank">Bayou Bend Collection and Gardens</a> and <a href=" http://www.mfah.org/visit/rienzi/" target="_blank">Rienzi</a>; the Lillie and Hugh Roy Cullen Sculpture Garden; and 18 acres of public gardens.  For more information about the MFAH go to <a href="http://www.mfah.org/" target="_blank">mfah.org</a>.&nbsp; To learn more about exhibitions now on view at the MFAH go to <a href="http://www.mfah.org/exhibitions/" target="_blank">mfah.org/exhibitions</a>. &nbsp;For a complete calendar of events and activities at the MFAH go to <a href="http://www.mfah.org/calendar/" target="_blank">mfah.org/calendar</a>. &nbsp;</p></p>
