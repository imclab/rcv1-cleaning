			<dl id="collateral-tabs" class="collateral-tabs">
				<dt class="tab"><span>Overview</span></dt>								<dd class="tab-container"><div class="tab-content"><div class="std"><p>
The <em>EMC Masterpiece Series, Literature and the Language Arts</em> program is carefully structured to teach all students, even those who are struggling, to read great literature&mdash;and even be transformed by it.
</p>

<h4>
See the difference!
</h4>
<ul>
 <li><strong>Classic and contemporary literature selections</strong> from authors representing a variety of cultural and ethnic viewpoints</li>
 <li><strong>Intensive reading strategies</strong> (before, during, and after reading) to ensure understanding</li>
 <li><strong>Integrated language arts and writing skill development</strong> integrated with the literature</li>
</ul>

<h3><a id="Pupil's Edition"></a>Pupil's Edition</h3>
<ul>
 <li>Mix of easy, moderate, and challenging selections offers flexibility to meet diverse student needs</li>
 <li>Process approach to reading grants each student access to every selection</li>
 <li>Prereading pages tap prior experiences, build background knowledge, and engage interest</li>
 <li>Embedded, fact-based Guided Reading questions check comprehension and call out key points</li>
 <li>Graduated post-reading questions develop higher-level thinking ability</li>
 <li>Skill Builders in grades 6-8 and Integrating the Language Arts in grades 9-12 provide full integration of language arts skills following the literature selections</li>
 <li>Writer's Journal following each selection provides easy, moderate, and challenging critical and creative writing prompts </li>
 <li>Guided Writing lesson after each of the twelve literature units provides: 
<ul>
 <li>Direct, step-by-step guidance for each stage of the writing process</li>
 <li>Professional and student models to help students explore a variety of modes and forms</li>
 <li>Language, grammar, and style lesson integrated within the writing lesson</li>
 <li>Guided Writing software support (see <a href="#gwcd">Guided Writing on CD-ROM</a>)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<h3><a id="Annotated Teacher's Edition"></a>Annotated Teacher's Edition</h3>
<ul>
 <li>Teacher pages cover best practice for using <em>Literature and the Language Arts</em> to meet goals in reading, literature study, writing, other language arts skills, and collaborative learning</li>
 <li>Cross-Curricular Connections and Teaching the Multiple Intelligences provide varied, engaging activities</li>
 <li>Individual Learning Strategies offer individualized instruction for:
<ul>
 <li>Motivation</li>
 <li>Reading proficiency</li>
 <li>English language learning</li>
 <li>Special needs</li>
 <li>Enrichment</li>
</ul>
</li>
 <li>Answer key and list of ancillary materials supplied for each selection</li>
 <li>Historical, literary, biographical, and other background information appears throughout the margins</li>
</ul>

<h3><a id="Literacy Resource Binder"></a>Literacy Resource Binder</h3>
<p>
The Literacy Resource binder provides comprehensive support for making the quality literature in <em>Literature and the Language Arts</em>
accessible to all students.
</p>

<h4>Lesson Plans with Alternative Teaching Options and Readability Guides</h4>
<p>
Lesson Plans for each selection include:
</p>
<ul>
 <li>Readability Guides that rate each selection as easy, moderate, or challenging, based on readability scales, author's style, subject matter, vocabulary, syntax, and selection length</li>
 <li>Lesson Plans that serve as a road map to the entire program, providing overview of all components needed to teach each literature selection</li>
 <li>Ideas for whole-group and flexible-group instruction</li>
 <li>Strategies to address readability considerations</li>
 <li>Meaningful before-reading, during-reading, and after-reading activities</li>
 <li>Alternative teaching options and strategies for teaching developing readers, advanced readers, and English language learners</li>
 <li>Homework suggestions</li>
</ul>

<h4>Reading Strategies Resource</h4>
<p>
This resource provides step-by-step instructions on how to teach reading strategies in general and for each selection in the program. Each integrated Reading Strategies Mini-Lesson includes:
</p>
<ul>
 <li>A Student Guidesheet with reading strategy identification (based on readability level), instruction, and practice</li>
 <li>Standardized Test Practice specific to the reading strategy in the Mini-Lesson</li>
