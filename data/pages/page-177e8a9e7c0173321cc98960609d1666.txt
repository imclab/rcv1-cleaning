      <h1>The 32nd Northern California Book Awards</h1>
      
			<div class="sharethis-container">
				<span class="st_twitter" displayText="Tweet" st_via='KQED'></span>
<span class="st_facebook" displayText="Share"></span>
<span class="st_email" displayText="Email"></span>

      <p class="bylineShareThis">
      
      By <a href="/arts/profile/index.jsp?essid=116193">Ingrid Rojas Contreras</a> <span>|</span> May 22, 2013
      
      </p>
      
      
      <div class="article-pic-left"><!-- article-pic-left -->
        <img src="http://u.s.kqed.net/2013/05/21/nbcabooks200.jpg" alt="Large Image" width="200" height="200" />

<!-- media clips related to essence -->
	   
	   
	   
	   
	   		   
	   
	   
	   	   
	   
	   
	   			   


       
      <p>While the very hip of the Bay went out to enjoy the sun this past Sunday, May 19, 2013, frolicking in vinyl and silver fabrics at Bay to Breakers, a group of book lovers holed up at the Koret Auditorium at the San Francisco Main Library to celebrate yesteryear's darlings for the 2013 Northern California Book Awards.</p>
 
<p><b>About the winners:</b></p>
 
<p><b>The Fred Cody Lifetime Achievement Award</b> went to Kay Ryan, the 2008 U.S. Poet Laureate. She has coined the most amazing word: "blander." You can read the poem <a href=http://www.poetryfoundation.org/poem/172276>online</a>. Her latest book is <i>The Best of It: New and Selected Poems</i>.</p>
 
<p><b>Fiction:</b> <i>A Partial History of Lost Causes</i> by Jennifer DuBois, a debut novel about the meeting of unlikely characters.</p>
 
<p><b>Poetry:</b> D.A. Powell's <i>Useless Landscape, or a Guide for Boys</i>, described as a "book of erotic, even cosmic regret" by NPR's Kevin Young.</p>
 
<p><b>General Non-fiction:</b> <i>Green Illusions: The Dirty Secrets of Clean Energy and the Future of Environmentalism</i> by Ozzie Zehner details all the ways in which we do not have an energy crisis, but a consumption crisis.</p>
 
<p><b>Creative Non-fiction:</b> <i>God's Hotel: A Doctor, a Hospital, and a Pilgrimage to the Heart of Medicine</i> by Victoria Sweet, a memoir of working at the country's last almshouse and the nature of caring for others.</p>
 
<p><b>Children's Literature:</b> <i>Baby Bear Sees Blue</i> by Ashley Wolff and <i>Who Could That Be at This Hour</i> by Lemony Snicket will both be a child's delight.</p>
 
<p><b>Translation in Fiction:</b> Claudio Magris' <i>Blindly</i>, translated by Anne Milano Appel, is narrated by a lucid madman whose telling extends throughout two centuries.</p>
 
<p><b>Translation in Poetry:</b> <i>Opera Omnia</i> translated by Art Beck is the work by the 6th-century Roman poet, Luxorius.</p>
 
<p>Last but not least, <i>Al-Mutanabbi Street Starts Here: Poets and Writers Respond to the March 5, 2007, Bombing of Baghdad's "Street of the Booksellers"</i> received special recognition.</p>
 
<p><center><iframe align="center" src="http://www.flickr.com/slideShow/index.gne?group_id=&user_id=63729367@N02&set_id=72157633549352699&text=" frameBorder="0" width="500" height="500" scrolling="no"></iframe></center></p>
</p>
 
<p>The 32nd Northern California Book Awards took place May 19, 2013 at the San Francisco Library. For <a href="www.poetryflash.org">more information</a> on this and future award ceremonies visit poetryflash.org.</p>

<p>All photos by Ingrid Rojas Contreras.</p>
