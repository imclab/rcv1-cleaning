					<p align="justify">Leasing has become popular because it offers people a way to drive the vehicles they want — often better vehicles than they could buy — for less money compared to purchase. Like BMW Lease. </p>
					<h3 align="justify">Leasing has become increasingly popular over the years because of two main factors. </h3>
					<ul>
						<li align="justify">
							First, the cost of new cars has spiraled upwards recently, often escalating prices out of reach of average buyers. </li>
						<li align="justify">
							Second, tax law changes in the late 1980&#39;s eliminated interest deductions on automobile loans, further increasing the cost of ownership.</li>
					</ul>
					<p align="justify">The net effect is that people have become increasingly eager to find ways to make their personal vehicles more affordable. </p>
					<h3 align="justify">Leasing is simply a method of paying for the use of a car, truck, SUV, or van over a specified period of time</h3>
					<p align="justify">Once the consumer and dealer agree on a price, and the lease contract has been signed, the dealer then sells the car to the leasing company at the agreed upon price. The leasing company then leases the car to the consumer, based on that price. For this reason, price becomes the most important factor in what a person pays in monthly payments. </p>
					<h3 align="justify">The car dealer acts as an agent for the leasing company. </h3>
					<p align="justify">This way, <b>the consumer is relieved of having to interact directly with the leasing company until they start to make monthly payments.</b> The dealer works out the terms of the leasing agreement on behalf of the leasing company. For this service, the leasing company usually pays the dealer a commission, which adds to the dealer's profit on the deal. <b>Once the contract is signed, the consumer's relationship is with the leasing company, not the dealer, unless it is an issue with the vehicle itself.</b> 
					</p>

Furthermore, the consumer agrees to keep the car for a specified number of months — standard leases are for 18, 24, 36, or 48 months — and is expected to hold onto the vehicle through the end of the lease contract. <br>
					<br>At the end of the lease the consumer is expected to return the vehicle to the leasing company with no more than normal wear and tear. And so the consumer will have to pay for any damage or extra miles over and above the contract-specified limits. </p>
