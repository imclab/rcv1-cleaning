<a href="http://ca.autoblog.com/photos/volkswagen-cross-coup-concept-0/#photo-5641888/"><img src="http://www.blogcdn.com/ca.autoblog.com/media/2013/02/img3792-1360865455.jpg" style="margin: 4px; width: 630px; height: 420px;"/></a><br/>
<br/>
The <a href="http://ca.autoblog.com/tag/Cross+Coupe/">VW Cross Coupe</a> is based on <a href="http://ca.autoblog.com/volkswagen/">Volkswagen's</a> new Modular Transverse Matrix and roundly showcases styling elements that will make their way onto future Volkswagen crossovers. Being one of the more understated <a href="http://ca.autoblog.com/2013/02/12/concept-cars-coming-to-toronto-auto-show-2013/">concept cars</a> at the show, it looks more 'believable' and closer to production the many of the wildly designed shells gracing the <a href="http://ca.autoblog.com/toronto-auto-show/">Toronto auto show</a> floor.<br/>
<br/>
Being a concept car, the designers were set wild, and the result is a unique electric/diesel hybrid power unit that allows the Cross Coupe to achieve a fuel consumption figure of just 1.8L/100km while developing 306 hp total output - twin-electric motors and TDI diesel combined.<br/>
