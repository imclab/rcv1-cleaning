<h2><a id="general" name="general">General Literature and Lesson Plan Sites</a></h2>
<img src="librarygraphics/la_readingcomprehension_s.gif" alt="Girl and Book" width="195" height="113" align="right" />
<ul>

<li><a href="http://www.thinkfinity.org/welcome">Thinkfinity</a> - Searchable site with literature lesson plans.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.carolhurst.com/">Carol Hurst's Children's Literature Web Site</a> - This site is 
filled with activities for teaching literature especially in the content areas. </li>

<li><a href="http://www.readwritethink.org/">Read, Write, Think</a> - Lesson plans for language arts from 
the International Reading Association and the National Council of Teachers of English. </li>

<li><a href="http://www.mrdonn.org/literature.html">Lesson Plans for Childrens' Books</a> - by Mrs. Donn.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.scholastic.com/teachers/lesson-plans/free-lesson-plans">Lesson Plans from Scholastic</a> - There are many lesson plans from Scholastic Publishers. There are also <a href="http://www.scholastic.com/teachers/lesson-plan/graphic-organizers-reading-comprehension">Graphic Organizers</a> for reading comprehension and records of books read.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.learnnc.org/">Learn North Carolina</a> - Search for literature lesson plans or take a look at their Best of the Web for links to many literature sites.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.educationworld.com/a_special/reading.shtml">Reading Fun</a> - Many ideas for reading projects from Education World.</li>

<li><a href="../booksf/kidsbooks.html#top">Books for Children and Young Adults</a> - Links to literature and 
reading promotion web sites. A part of the Resources for School Librarians web site. </li>

<li><a href="http://www.webenglishteacher.com/">The Web English Teacher</a> - Lots and lots of ideas for using 
literature. </li>

<li><a href="http://www.readingrockets.org/books/interviews/">Video Interviews with Top Children's Book Authors and Illustrators</a> - Online video interviews. This site is by Reading Rockets.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.crayola.com/for-educators/lesson-plans.aspx">Crayola Lesson Plans</a> - If you wish to incorporate a craft activity into your reading or library research, this is a useful site.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.educationworld.com/a_bestbooks/archives/students.shtml">Best Books for Students and Classroom Teaching Themes</a> - Books on an elementary level which will go with classroom themes such as holidays, science, sports, and many others. This may also help you to find books for reading aloud. This is from Education World.</li>

<li><a href="computers.html">E-Books and E-Readers, Tablets and Apps</a> - If you wish to add e-books and apps to your literature program. This page is part of the Resources for School Librarians web site.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.ehow.com/how_4883707_write-childrens-book-lesson-plan.html">How to Write a Children's Book Lesson Plan</a> - For early elementary grades. This is by Peggy Epstein on eHow.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.renlearn.com/whatkidsarereading/">What Kids are Reading 2013</a> - This is a report from Renaissance Learning on the most read books for grades k - 12.</li>

</ul>
<h2><a id="reports" name="reports">Book Reports</a></h2>
<img src="librarygraphics/la_bookreports_s.gif" alt="Book Reports" width="222" height="113" align="right" />
<ul>

<li><a href="http://www.readwritethink.org/classroom-resources/lesson-plans/choosing-right-book-strategies-916.html">Choosing the Right Book</a> - Strategies for beginning readers (grades K - 2). This lesson plan is from ReadWriteThink.</li>

<li><a href="http://teachers.net/lessons/posts/685.html">Collection of Book Report Ideas</a> - 109 ideas from Barbara D. Martin.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.educationworld.com/a_curr/archives/bookreport.shtml">Book Report Makeover Archive</a> - Are you tired of the same old book reports? Here are some new ideas from Education World.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.webenglishteacher.com/bookreports.html">Book Reports</a> - Lots of ideas from the Web English Teacher web site.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.scholastic.com/teachers/article/classroom-activities-25-book-report-alternatives">25 Book Report Alternatives</a> - From Scholastic.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.educationworld.com/a_lesson/lesson/book_report_ideas.shtml">Fresh Ideas for Book Reports</a> - From Education World.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.brighthubeducation.com/elementary-school-activities/3068-classroom-book-sharing-activity-make-a-sundae-poster/">Sundae of the Week</a> - Students create a picture of an ice cream sundae to describe their book. This plan is by Kellie Hayden.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.readwritethink.org/classroom-resources/student-interactives/book-cover-creator-30058.html">Book Cover Creator</a> - A site where students may create book covers for the books which they have read. There are also lesson plans and instructions for use.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.booktrailersforreaders.com/">Book Trailers for Readers</a> - A new way to present book reports. You may also need to watch <a href="http://www.booktrailersforreaders.com/How+to+make+a+book+trailer">How to Make a Book Trailer</a></li>

<li><a href="http://www.abcteach.com/directory/subjects-language-arts-writing-book-reports-466-2-1">Book Report Forms</a> - From ABC Teach.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.busyteacherscafe.com/printables/reading.html">Reading Printables</a> - Book report forms, reading logs, and other reading related materials from the Busy Teacher's Corner.</li>

<li><a href="http://highland.hitcho.com.au/readingforms.htm">Reading Forms</a> - Book report forms including forms specifically for genre books.</li>
</ul>

 From Education World.</li>

<li><a href="http://asiasociety.org/education/resources-schools/elementary-lesson-plans/twice-upon-time-multi-cultural-cinderella">Twice Upon A Time: A Multicultural Cinderella Unit</a> - Cindrella stories from many nations. From Ask Asia.</li>

<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.readwritethink.org/classroom-resources/lesson-plans/genre-study-collaborative-approach-270.html">Genre Studies: A Collaborative Approach</a> - For gds. 3 - 5. From ReadWriteThink.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.learnnc.org/lp/pages/3222">Guess the Genre</a> - An elementary lesson plan from Learn NC.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.brighthubeducation.com/lesson-plans-grades-3-5/47691-teaching-fiction-genres/">Learning About Fiction Genres in the Elementary School Library</a>. - This is an article from Bright Hub.</li>

<li><a href="http://school.discoveryeducation.com/lessonplans/programs/DLA_intermediate_fiction_nonfiction/">Science Fiction</a> - For grades 6 - 8.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.lessonplanet.com/article/language-arts/science-fiction-lesson-plans">Science Fiction Lesson Plans</a> - From Lesson Planet.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.42explore.com/fantscifi.htm">Fantasy and Science Fiction</a> - Teaching ideas and related web sites from 42 eXplore</li>

<li><a href="http://www.kimskorner4teachertalk.com/readingliterature/genres/fantasy/raising_dragons.htm">Raising Dragons</a> - This is an introductory lesson in the elements of fantasy. It is based on the book <i>Raising Dragons</i> by Jerdine Nolan.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.MysteryNet.com/learn/">Teaching Mysteries</a> - Lesson plans from MysteryNet.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.42explore.com/mystery.htm">Mystery</a> - Teaching ideas and related web sites from 42 eXplore.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.scholastic.com/teachers/collection/historical-fiction-genre-study">Historical Fiction Genre Study</a> - from Scholastic.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.educationworld.com/a_lesson/03/lp301-03.shtml">Historical Fiction Timeline</a> - Link reading to social studies. This is a lesson plan for all grades from Education World.</li>

<li><a href="http://jlbenet.com/education.html">Teaching the Horror Genre</a> - This site has links to lesson plans. a horror handout, and horror discusssion questions. This site is by J. B. Benet.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.proteacher.org/c/296_Genre_Studies.html">Genre Studies</a> - Teachers report ideas they have used for teaching genres.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.cde.ca.gov/ci/cr/rl/litrlgenres.asp">Genre Definitions</a> - From the California Department of Education.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.breitlinks.com/my_libmedia/genres.htm">YA Genres</a> - Good for definitions.</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="http://www.scholastic.com/librarians/ab/booktalks.htm">Book Talks</a> - for Scholastic books.</li>
<li><a href="http://digitalbooktalk.com/">Digital Booktalks</a> - Maybe your students would like to do a digital booktalk.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.freetech4teachers.com/2011/03/11-good-digital-storytelling-resources.html?utm_source=feedburner&amp;utm_medium=feed&amp;utm_campaign=Feed%3A+freetech4teachers%2FcGEY+%28Free+Technology+for+Teachers%29">Eleven Good Digital Storytelling Resources</a> - by Free Technology for Teachers.</li>

<h2><a id="poetry" name="poetry">Poetry</a></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.educationworld.com/a_special/poetrymonth.shtml">Poetry Lesson Plans</a> - Many plans for using poetry for all grades from Education World.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.teach-nology.com/teachers/lesson_plans/language_arts/poetry/">Poetry Lesson Plans</a> - From Teachnology.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.poetryteachers.com/">Poetry Teachers.Com</a> - Many ideas for teaching poetry.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.theteachersguide.com/poetrymonth.htm">Poetry Lesson Plans</a> - from The Teacher's Guide.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.poets.org/page.php/prmID/41">National Poetry Month</a> - Celebrate this event in April. This site is by the Academy of American Poets.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.poetryfoundation.org/browse/">The Poetry Foundation</a> -This site will help you to find poems by subject, title, author, and a number of other criteria.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.abcteach.com/directory/subjects-language-arts-writing-poetry-465-2-1">Poetry Worksheets</a> - These are mainly for acrostic forms. From ABC Teach.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.readwritethink.org/classroom-resources/student-interactives/acrostic-poems-30045.html">Acrostic Creator</a> - Online tool and lesson plans to help students create acrostic poems.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.readwritethink.org/classroom-resources/student-interactives/diamante-poems-30053.html">Diamante Poems</a> - An online tool for creating diamante poems, and lesson plans which may be used even if you do not want to go online.</li>
</ul>

<h2><a id="elem" name="elem">Elementary Level</a></h2>
<ul>

<li><a href="http://www.texaslibrarian.com/">Texas Librarian</a> - "Connecting Books, Reading Strategies and Lesson Plans for PreK-5 Librarians". This site also has nice lists of titles for the months of the year.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.bookloons.com/HandHTML/weeloons4.html">Book Related Printables</a> - Links to sites for printable activities for mainly early elementary level books. From the BookLoons web site.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.dltk-teach.com/books/index.htm">Children's Book Breaks</a> - Craft ideas, bookmarks, 
and writing paper templates for early elementary children's books such as Arthur, Clifford, and others. </li>

<li><a href="http://www.readingrockets.org/">Reading Rockets</a> - a project to promote reading by early elementary age students. It is a collaboration between PBS and just about every other group concerned with early literacy. There is a section for librarians which may be found on the menu choice For Other Professionals. There are also a large number of articles - some with teaching activities.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.grandviewlibrary.info/read_alouds.htm">Recent Read Alouds from the Grand View Elementary School Library</a> - A year's worth of read alouds for grades 1 - 5.</li>

<li><a href="http://picturebookmonth.com">Picture Book Month</a> - Celebrated in November. This site has a nice calendar with a suggested book for each day. There are also suggested activities.</li>

<li><a href="http://themes.atozteacherstuff.com/">Themes</a> - From A to Z Teacher Stuff. There are activities for holidays, weather,  back to school, authors, and more.</li>

<li><a href="http://eduscapes.com/caldecott/index.htm">Caldecott Connections</a> - List of winners and teaching ideas from Eduscapes.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.scholastic.com/teachers/article/teaching-caldecott-winners">Teaching Caldecott Winners</a> - Some ideas from Scholastic.</li>
<li><a href="http://teachers.net/lessons/posts/2097.html">Newbery Award Reading Project</a> - For grades 5 and up.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.learnnc.org/lp/pages/3867?ref=search">The Wolf in Children's Books</a> - For grade 3. Students learn the definitions of protagonist and antagonist, and also research wolves in nonfiction books. From Learn NC.</li>
<li><a href="http://puppetsforlibraries.wordpress.com/basic-guide-to-using-puppets/">Basic Guide to Using Puppets</a> - From the Puppets in Libraries blog.</li>

</ul>

<li><a href="http://www.adlit.org/">AdLit</a> - All about adolescent literacy. This site is for teachers and parents of students in grades 4 - 12. There are sections on classroom strategies, and videos so that you can see these strategies in action.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.edb.utexas.edu/resources/booksR4teens/">Books R4 Teens</a> - Book summaries, teaching ideas, related media and texts, and 
links to related web sites. This site is by the University of Texas at Austin.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.ala.org/yalsa/teenreading/trw/trw2010/home">Teen Read Week</a> - in October.</li>

</ul>

<h2><a id="tech" name="tech">Children's Books and Technology</a></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://eduscapes.com/ladders/index.html">Literature Learning Ladders</a> - Literature lesson plans 
with Internet links. There are activities for Newbery and Caldecott winners in addition to popular children's books. </li>

<li><a href="http://www.slj.com/category/books-media/reviews/apps/">Apps Reviews</a> - From School Library Journal</li>

<li><a href="http://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/ipad/">iPad Book Apps Available Now</a> - Reviews from Kirkus Reviews</li>

<li><a href="http://readingtech.wikispaces.com/">Reading 2.0</a> - a wiki on reading and book promotion.</li>

<li><a href="http://www.mediabistro.com/appnewser/ten-ipad-apps-based-on-classic-childrens-book_b12733?red=en">Ten iPad Apps Based on Classic Children's Books</a> - An article By Dianna Dilworth on June 22, 2011.</li>

<li><a href="https://www.biblionasium.com">Biblionasium</a> - An online reading community for children, parents, and teachers. This is a social reading community for young readers. Teachers may submit booklists.</li>

<li><a href="computers.html">E-Books and E-Readers, Tablets and Apps</a> - If you wish to add e-books and apps to your literature program. This page is part of the Resources for School Librarians web site.</li>

</ul>

<h2> <a id="misc" name="misc">Miscellaneous</a></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://epe.lac-bac.gc.ca/100/200/301/lac-bac/page_by_page-ef/www.lac-bac.gc.ca/3/2/index-e.html">Page by Page: Creating a Children's Book</a> - This

manuscript for publication. By the National Library of Canada.</li>
