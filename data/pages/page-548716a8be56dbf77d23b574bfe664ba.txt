<p>One of the best ways to experience Los Angeles is by visiting its incredible cultural attractions. LA has more museums and theaters than any U.S. city, and a thriving music and gallery scene. LA’s world class museums include the stunning Getty Center in Brentwood and the Los Angeles County Museum of Art on Museum Row. Monthly artwalks take place throughout the city, including popular events in Downtown LA and Venice Beach. <h1>
Literary LA </h1>
</hgroup>
</header>
 
 
<div class="lainc-landing-content-image">
<img typeof="foaf:Image" src="http://dtfjihky7xwic.cloudfront.net/sites/default/files/styles/landing_content_image/public/Culture/Literary%20LA/last-bookstore.jpg" width="386" height="290"/> </div>
 
 
<div class="lainc-landing-content-text">
 
<div class="lainc-landing-content-info">
<div class="lainc-landing-content-info-summary">
<p>From literary icons to unique bookstores, Los Angeles is one of the best cities in the country for book fans. Despite LA’s sunny weather, some of its most famous characters were at home in the night, such as Raymond Chandler’s Philip Marlowe and James M. Cain’s characters. The heirs to Chandler and Cain include Michael Connelly and Walter Mosley. John Fante’s Ask the Dust takes place in Bunker Hill, and influenced Charles Bukowski. The LA Times Festival of Books welcomes thousands for a celebration of the written word. Read on for LA Tourism’s guide to the best of literary Los Angeles.</p> </div>
