One of the biggest challenges automakers face when designing a high-performance car is making sure that it is both fast and reliable. For General Motors, any car that might be taken to the track by its owner &ndash; like the Corvette, Camaro Z/28 (shown above) and the Cadillac CTS-V, for example &ndash; undergoes a rigorous and strenuous 24-hour test by engineers at the Milford Proving Grounds, as pointed out by <em>Car and Driver</em>.<br/><br />

Though the test isn&#8217;t for 24 hours straight, the cars are pushed as hard as possible by some of GM&#8217;s best drivers with only the brakes and tires replaced frequently.<br/><br />
<br/><br />
We don&#8217;t want to ruin the fun for you, but it is an interesting article that tells just some of what GM does to develop its sports cars. Check out the full article over at <em>Car and Driver</em> for the rest of the story.</div>

<img alt="2014 Toyota Corolla" src="http://www.autocarpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/b82a1__001-2014-toyota-corolla.jpg"/><br/><br />
<br/><br />
We&#8217;ll be publishing our First Drive of the 2014 Toyota Corolla later today, but right now, we can reveal that the eleventh-generation compact will be priced from $  16,800 when it hits dealerships this fall (*not including a $  810 destination charge).<br/><br />
<br/><br />
Four trim levels will be offered, and that sub-$  17,000 price point reflects the base L grade with a six-speed manual transmission. Adding the four-speed automatic (yep) brings that price up to $  17,400, which gets you the usual features standard on this class of car, including Bluetooth connectivity, eight airbags and &ndash; a first for this price point &ndash; LED headlamps.<br/><br />
<br/><br />
From there, the LE trim ($  18,300) adds a backup camera, cruise control, keyless entry, Entune audio (including a 6.1-inch touchscreen) and a brand-new continuously variable transmission replacing the four-speed auto. There&#8217;s even an Eco version of the LE trim that uses a different engine tune to provide fuel economy of up to 42 miles per gallon on the highway.<br/><br />
<br/><br />
The top-trim S, pictured above, comes with a modified front fascia design, rear lip spoiler, leather-wrapped steering wheel, sport seats and more for $  19,000. There are paddle shifters mated to the CVT with seven &#8220;gears,&#8221; and optional 17-inch wheels are available. Toyota will offer the S trim with a six-speed manual as part of the Plus package, and the Premium pack adds Softex synthetic leather seats. All in, a fully loaded Corolla comes in just under $  24,000. Not bad, at all.<br/><br />
<br/><br />
Stay tuned for our full impressions of the new Corolla in just a few hours. In the meantime, scroll down to read Toyota&#8217;s official pricing release.</div>

<img alt="Infiniti Q30 Concept" src="http://www.autocarpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/959ae__Q30-Hero-AutoBlog.jpg"/><br/><br />
<br/><br />
Behold, the Q30 Concept. Slated for a worldwide debut in Germany next month at the Frankfurt Motor Show, this concept previews a new entry-level premium compact that Infiniti hopes will attract younger buyers to the brand, as well as give it a competitor in a burgeoning new class that&#8217;s quickly filling up with options.<br/><br />
<br/><br />
What will set the Q30 apart? Aside from a bold and aggressive design that picks up where the new Q50 and many concepts have left off, Infiniti says the shape combines elements from three bodystyles, all of which appeal to the sort of buyer it&#8217;s hoping snare. Can you guess which three? No peeking at the press release down below. Give yourself ten arbitrary points if your answer was coupe, crossover and hatchback.<br/><br />
<br/><br />
Infiniti promises a ride height that will be high enough to earn crossover credentials, and we&#8217;re guessing it&#8217;s got an actual hatch back there, which leaves the coupe. Where&#8217;s the coupe in the Q30&#8242;s design? The influence of a two-door is a little more difficult to see with the naked eye, but what the Q30 shares with some four-door coupes on the market is a roofline that peaks and begins to slope back down before the B-pillar and rear set of doors.<br/><br />
<br/><br />
There&#8217;s no information available yet on what powers the Q30 Concept, and hence even less about what will power the production version, but we expect to learn more more in the coming weeks leading up to this triple threat&#8217;s reveal in Frankfurt.</div>
