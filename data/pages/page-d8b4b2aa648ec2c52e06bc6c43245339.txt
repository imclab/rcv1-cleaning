If you want to save on costly services you will need to bring your thinking back to cheap alternatives. Learn how to get free labor on car repair, auto body repair, and maintenance.<p>  <i><b>Your car,</b></i> You know, that life line between you and the world. A major repair job could cost you thousands. I had a new motor put in my car for $25. No, this is not a mistake, twenty five dollars (there, I spelled it out so you won't think I forgot some zeros). I had this done by licensed mechanics, who were not my friends or relatives, and who did this professionally at their full time business. I used the local vocational high school's automotive repair department. The motor cost me the $25 dollars at a local used car parts yard, or junk yard.</p><p>  The mechanic's these schools employee are licensed master mechanics. The students do all the work, under their watchful eyes. This car has been running for 4 years now, with only needing small minor repairs not pertaining to the motor. They do all kinds of jobs for no money, or very minimal money. You have to purchase the parts if it is not something they have access to from the many donations they receive. Brakes, transmission, starters, and the list goes on. Anything you can have done at a garage, they can do.</p><p>  They also do a bang up (no pun intended) job on auto body work. A small dent could cost you a thousand dollars or more.at your local auto body business. Here the cost is nothing. Even if the insurance on your car will cover most of the damage, there are still big deductibles you need to come up with. If you take it to the nearest vocational school you will have to fork out nothing. Your insurance isn't tapped either, so you stay in good standing with them.</p><p>  Car maintenance can also be very expensive, they do oil changes, tire rotations, tune ups and anything else that falls under the category of maintaining your car. The cost is minimal, just for parts if they are needed.</p><p>  To make an appointment it is as easy as picking up the phone. Most bigger towns and cities have at least one technical school near by. You can also call your local high school automotive department . They usually are not as involved as a technical school, but under the same conditions of licensed mechanics, they can do brakes, oil changes, and other smaller to moderate jobs. The bigger jobs such as transmissions and motors, are probably only done at the technical schools. There are a few public high schools that do the bigger sophisticated repair work. You just need to do the research on where the best place is to go for what you need at the time.</p><p>  There are two small draw backs from this cost free car repair idea. The work is done during the school hours. If the repair is expected to take many hours, you will sometimes have to leave your car there for a day or two. This could be a big inconvenience for you, but most of the time when you weigh the options, the massive amount of money you can save will win out. If you are like most families today, you are probably a two car family and can get by for a few days without too much added stress.</p><p>  The other draw back comes from the word "school", have you guessed it yet? The school year is only nine months, so if your car breaks down during the summer you are out of luck. I actually stay mindful of the school year winding down and if I anticipate needing brakes, oil change, or any other maintenance , I will make an appointment about a month before school gets out. Don't wait last minute though, they are squeezing appointments in up until about 2 weeks before the end of the school year.</p><p>  I like to tip the students upon the finish of the repair work. They can't take money, so depending on the size of the job I find other ways to tip them. For smaller jobs I might show up with a couple of dozen of donuts for them when I come to retrieve my car. The time they saved me thousands by putting in a new motor, I treated them to pizza from a local restaurant along with a case of cola.</p><p>  Try calling your local technical high school for an appointment in their automotive department next time you need something done to your car. Whether it be fixing a transmission, a crushed bumper, or an oil change, you will be surprised at the good work done in these student shops all across the country. Through the years, I have known many people that take this alternate route when needing car repairs. The only difference between the school repair shop and the local repair shop is the price. Give it a try and save some money.</p></div></div><div class="ad_unit"><!-- [zone48] 630x220 --><script src="http://scripts.chitika.net/yahoo/ac-article-south.js"></script></div><div class="shirt_tail" id="shirt_tail"><p class="title">Published by <a href="http://contributor.yahoo.com/user/557660/roz_zurko.html">Roz Zurko</a></p><p class="bio">Roz is a published freelance writer originally from Milford CT, a bedroom community for New York City.
