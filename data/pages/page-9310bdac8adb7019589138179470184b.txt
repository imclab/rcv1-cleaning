                <td colspan="3" rowspan="3" valign="top"><p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif">The Asheville art scene is booming. Each year, more artist studio tours, events and art shows appear. Here are our favorite annual Asheville art events. Plan your next trip around one of these! <a href="american_style.htm">AmericanStyle Magazine names Asheville as the Number One Small City for Arts in USA </a></font></p>
                  <h3><b>Asheville Art Events &amp; Festivals Calendar </b></h3>
                  <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><i>For art any day of the year (along with special openings and shows), see our <a href="galleries.html">Asheville Gallery Guide</a> or  visit the <a href="folk_art.htm">Folk Art Center</a> or <a href="folk_art_center_exhibition.htm">Folk Art Center</a> with rotating exhibitions.</i></font></p>
                  <p><a href="mountain_art_mangum.htm"><em>NC Art Book  features Mountain Paintings</em></a></p>                  
                  <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><b>February 22-24, 2013: Arts &amp; Crafts Antique Show and Conference</SPAN><br>
                      </SPAN></b>For the past nineteen years Arts and Crafts enthusiasts - including new collectors, veterans, curators, craftsperson, authors, builders, architects, designers, editors, and bungalow owners - have traveled to the Blue Ridge Mountains and The Grove Park Inn for this show. Admission. <a href="http://www.arts-craftsconference.com" target="_blank"><br>
        </a></SPAN></SPAN><a href="arts_crafts_conference_grove_park.htm">Read more about the Arts and Crafts Conference</a></font></p>                  
                  <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif">                    <b>April 5, 2013: Downtown Asheville Art Walk<br>
                          </b></font>(First Friday Every Month April-December) Visit 25 galleries, museums, and jewelry studios within a walkable half mile radius of downtown with fun-filled evening full of exceptional art and activities, 5-8 PM. <font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><a href="http://www.ashevilledowntowngalleries.org/" target="_blank"><br>
          </a><a href="galleries.html">See our Asheville Gallery Guide for places to visit everyday </a></font></p>
                  <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><strong>April 20, 2013: Potters of Madison County</strong><br>
                    26 ceramic artists from the Southeast showcase diverse contemporary selection at the Marshall High Studios in <a href="marshall.htm">downtown Marshall</a>, 9 AM-5 PM. No admission fee. </font></p>
                  <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><strong>April 27, 2013: Fire on the Mountain Blacksmith Festival</strong><br> 
                  Held in Downtown <a href="spruce_pine.htm">Spruce Pine</a> from 10 AM-4 PM featuring vendors, live demonstrations, hands-on tent with rotating teachers and visiting blacksmiths from around the region and country. </font></p>                  
  <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><strong>April 27, 2013: Greening Up the Mountain</strong><br>
                  Annual street festival in Sylva that features music, art and craft. <a href="sylva_greening_up_mountain.htm">Read more about Greening Up the Mountain. </a></font></p>
  <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><b>April 27-28, 2013: Weaverville Art Safari<br>
  </b>Weaverville, 10 miles north of Asheville, is the home and workplace of many of the area artists. The core activities of the Weaverville Art Safari are self-guided driving tours of the various artists' studios, tucked away in the countryside communities of Weaverville, Barnardsville, and Alexander. Free. <a href="http://www.weavervilleartsafari.com/" target="_blank"><br>
  </a><a href="weaverville_art_safari.htm" target="_blank">Read more about the Weaverville Art Safari.</a></font></p>
  <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><strong>May 3, 2013:</strong> <b>Downtown Asheville Art Walk<br>
  </b></font>(First Friday Every Month April-December) Visit 25 galleries, museums, and jewelry studios within a walkable half mile radius of downtown with fun-filled evening full of exceptional art and activities, 5-8 PM. <font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><a href="http://www.ashevilledowntowngalleries.org/" target="_blank"><br>
  </a><a href="galleries.html">See our Asheville Gallery Guide for places to visit everyday </a></font></p>
  <p><strong>May 4-5, 2013: Blue to Black Art Weekend<br>
    </strong>From the BLUE Ridge Parkway to BLACK Mountain,  a driving, free self-guided driving tour in East Asheville  and a walking tour in downtown Black Mountain. Visit any or all of the 40 studios during the tour, whether you area serious art patron or just a browser of beautiful and interesting works. Saturday &amp; Sunday, 10 AM-6 PM. Special events on Thursday and Friday prior. <br>
    <a href="east_studio_tour.htm">Read more about the Blue to Black Art Weekend </a></p>
  <p><strong>May 4-5, 2013: Art Trek Tryon</strong><br>
  Approximately 40 artists in <a href="tryon.htm">Tryon</a>, <a href="saluda.htm">Saluda</a>, Columbus, NC and Landrum, SC open their studios to the public free of charge. &nbsp;</p>                  
  <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><b>May 9-12, 2013: Lake Eden Arts Festival</b><br>
          Celebrate Music, Discover Community, and Experience Culture at LEAF in a beautiful lakefront setting near Black Mountain (about 12 miles from downtown Asheville). Five Stages of Folk &amp; World, Bluegrass, Blues, African, Celtic, and jams with headline performers. Handcrafts, folk art, dancing, Poetry SLAM, healing arts workshops, water recreation, and camping. Admission. <a href="leaf.htm" onClick="MM_openBrWindow('http://www.theleaf.com','leaf','toolbar=yes,location=yes,status=yes,menubar=yes,scrollbars=yes,resizable=yes,width=800,height=600')"><br>
          See our LEAF Guide</a></font></p>
  <p><strong>May 11, 2013: Second Saturdays in River Arts District</strong><br>
    Join the artists of the River Arts District the second Saturday, May-December, for a day of artist demonstrations, classes, open studios and fun. Spend the day hopping from studio to studio to view a range of mediums and an abundance of creativity.<br>
  <a href="river_arts_district_events.htm">See our River Arts District Events</a></p>
  <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><strong>May 11-12, 2013: Fiber Weekend </strong><br>
                    <span class="style21">Fiber Weekend is Mother&rsquo;s Day weekend with craftspeople at the Folk Art Center sharing their inspiration and expertise in a variety of textile arts. Demonstrations on Saturday include: quilting and appliqu&eacute;, doll making, weaving, mixed media fiber arts, clothing design, printing and surface design, tapestry weaving, and natural dyeing. On Sunday, see the </span></font>Handmade Wearables Fashion Show. Free. <br>
                  <a href="folk_art.htm">Read more about the Folk Art Center. <font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif">                  </font></a></p>                  
  <P><strong>May 18, 2013: Montford Arts and Music Festival</strong><br>
                    This street festival, including musical acts, food, and a juried art show, highlight the artistic strength of one of Asheville&rsquo;s oldest neighborhoods. This festival is free to the public, and sponsored by the Montford Neighborhood Association.  Free. <a href="http://www.montfordfestival.org" target="_blank"><br>
                    Go to their Web site</a>.</P>                  
  <P><strong>May 18, 2013: Saluda Arts Festival</strong><br>
                    The quaint mountain village of Saluda celebrates its heritage and arts culture  from 10 AM to 5 PM on Historic Main Street in downtown Saluda. See a dazzling diversity of 2D and 3D art media including oil, watercolor, and acrylic paintings, pottery, wood turning, blacksmithing, sculpture, jewelry, painted furniture, stained glass, and more.&nbsp; Music representing Americana, blues, folk, and contemporary will be ongoing.<br>
                    <a href="http://www.saluda.com" target="_blank">Go to their Web site</a>.</P>                  
  <P><strong><b><font size="4" face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><img src="images2012/art-guide2.jpg" alt="Art Festival" width="300" height="413" hspace="5" align="right"></font></b>May 25-26, 2013: Kenilworth Artists Open Studio Tour</strong><br>
                    Kenilworth neighborhood, just five minutes from downtown Asheville, annual Open Studio Tour features 20+ jewelers, painters, glass-workers, fabric artists, potters, woodworkers, photographers, and more! They open their studio doors to welcome visitors for sales and demonstrations. 10AM-5PM. <br>
                    <a href="kenilworth_studio_tour.htm">See our Kenilworth Art Studio Tour Guide.</a></P>                  
                  <P><strong>May 25, 2013: Burnsville Spring Arts Festival</strong><br> 
                    Food, live music and handmade crafts from 10 AM-4 PM on the Burnsville Town Square. Free. </P>                  
                    <P> <strong>June 1, 2013: Clay Day at Folk Art Center</strong><br>
                    This free event (10AM-4PM) features craft demonstrations and hands-on activities for children and adults. Members of the Southern Highland Craft Guild demonstrate throwing on the potter's wheel, hand building and surface design on clay, among other techniques.A highlight of the day is the Make and Take Raku Firing. Buy a $10 pot, glaze it and watch as expert potters raku fire it for you. Raku is a ceramic firing process which uses fire and smoke to create unique patterns and designs.<br>
                    <a href="folk_art.htm">Read more about the Folk Art Center</a>. </P>
                    <P><strong>June 7, 2013: <font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif">Downtown Asheville Art Walk</font></strong><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><br>
                  </font>(First Friday Every Month April-December) Visit 25 galleries, museums, and jewelry studios within a walkable half mile radius of downtown with fun-filled evening full of exceptional art and activities, 5-8 PM. <br>
                  <a href="galleries.html">See our Asheville Gallery Guide for places to visit everyday </a></P>
                  <P><strong>June-December 2013: Sculpture for the Garden Exhibition</strong><br>
                    Grovewood Gallery in Asheville presents its annual outdoor sculpture invitational with contemporary sculptures by nationally-recognized artists. Sculptures range from playful pieces suitable for the home or garden, to works for public spaces and corporate settings. This event is open to the public and free of charge.<br>
                    <a href="garden_sculpture.htm">Read more about Sculpture for the Garden.</a></P>                  
                    <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><b>June 7-9, 2013: Toe River Studio Tour</b> <br>
          About an hour north of Asheville, tucked in the lush green mountains is one of the finest collections of artists and craftsmen in the world and the acclaimed <a href="penland.htm">Penland School of Crafts</a>. A unique blend of contemporary and traditional cultures. More than 100 fine artists and crafts people in every medium open their studios for a free, self-guided cultural adventure. Free. <a href="toe_river_studio_tour.htm"><br>
          Read more about Toe River Studio Tour.</a></font></p>                  
                    <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><b>June 8-9, 2013: River District Artists Studio Stroll</b><br>
  The Studio Stroll is your chance to tour the studios, meet with the artists and purchase artwork. Within just a few blocks are dozens of eclectic mix of creative artists - from traditional to cutting-edge. Near downtown Asheville. Free. <br>
  </a><a href="river_arts.htm">Read more about the River Arts District</a>.</font></p>                  
                    <p><strong>June 15, 2013: Bluff Mountain Festival</strong><br>
                    This free, family-friendly festival in <a href="hot_springs.htm">Hot Springs</a> features great old-time and bluegrass music, ballads, clogging and square dance, and a silent auction and raffle of regional art work, local crafts, vacation packages, and more. The festival produced by Madison&nbsp;County Arts Council, begins at 10 AM, offers an array of delicious foods and ends with a square dance until dark. <br>
                  Go to <A 
href="http://www.madisoncountyarts.com" target="_blank">Madison County Arts Web site.</A></p>                  
                    <p><strong>June 6, 2013: Dillsboro Rafters &amp; Crafters </strong><br>
                    Traditional &amp; contemporary mountain crafts from  juried artists, fine art, live music, dance and storytelling. Front Street in the center of Dillsboro is lined with tents featuring all kinds of art and fine crafts. 10 AM-5 PM. Free.</p>
                    <p><strong>June 15, 22 &amp; 29, 2013: Asheville Art in the Park</strong><br>
See fine arts and crafts Saturdays in  Pack Square Park in downtown.<br>
<a href="art_in_park.htm">Read more about Art in the Park</a> </p>
                    <p><strong>June 22-23, 2013: Blue Ridge Mountains Arts &amp; Crafts Show</strong><br>
Held indoors at the Haywood County Fairgrounds located on Highway 209 (758 Crabtree Road) near <a href="waynesville.htm">Waynesville</a> and Lake Junaluska, 8 AM-5 PM with 50 crafters offering all handmade items. Free parking. <a href="http://www.bracaorg.com" target="_blank">Go to their Web site</a></p>
                    <p><strong>June 29-30, 2013:  Summertime Art &amp; Crafts Show</strong><br>
                    Held in the Ramsey Center arena on the campus of Western Carolina University in Cullowhee, the show is home to some of the most respected and unique artisans in the Appalachian Mountains with more than 100 exhibitors showcasing their contemporary and heritage art &amp; crafts. An array of items will be displayed for sale, including: fine art, woodworking, pottery, weaving and gourd art. Heritage crafts such as shaker brooms, pine needle baskets and folk dolls will also be available, along with heirloom fabric items. <a href="http://www.mountainartisans.net" target="_blank">Go to the Summertime Art &amp; Crafts Web site</a>. </p>
                    <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><b>July 5, 2013: Downtown Asheville Art Walk<br>
                    </b></font>(First Friday Every Month April-December) Visit 25 galleries, museums, and jewelry studios within a walkable half mile radius of downtown with fun-filled evening full of exceptional art and activities, 5-8 PM. <font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><a href="http://www.ashevilledowntowngalleries.org/" target="_blank"><br>
                    </a><a href="galleries.html">See our Asheville Gallery Guide for places to visit everyday </a></font></p>
                  <p><strong>July 6-7, 2013: Freedom&nbsp;Arts &amp; Crafts Show</strong><br>
  The Blue Ridge Artists and Crafters Association, a local nonprofit group has an indoor show with approximately 50 crafters, free parking, free admission, and food at Haywood County Fairgrounds in <a href="waynesville.htm">Waynesville</a>. 
  <a href="http://www.bracaorg.com" target="_blank">Go to their Web site</a>. </p>
                  <p><strong>July 13, 2013: Second Saturdays in River Arts District</strong><br>
Join the artists of the <a href="river_arts.htm">River Arts District</a> the second Saturday, May-December, for a day of artist demonstrations, classes, open studios and fun. Spend the day hopping from studio to studio to view a range of mediums and an abundance of creativity.<br>
<a href="river_arts_district_events.htm">See our River Arts District Events</a></p>
                  <p><font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif"><b>July 18-21, 2013: Craft Fair of the Southern Highlands</b><br>
          The Craft Fair, held in the <a href="civic_center.htm">Civic Center</a> in downtown Asheville, has been an eagerly anticipated event in the mountains since 1948. The fair showcases work by members of the Southern Highland Craft Guild, with over 900 members throughout the mountain regions of nine states. Members' work must be juried and accepted by a committee of peers, based on quality, design and craftsmanship. Admission.<br>
          See our <a href="craft_fair_southern_highlands.htm" target="_blank">Craft Fair of the Southern Highlands Guide. </a></font></p>
