<p>The <b>1930s</b> (years from <a href="/1930" title="1930">1930</a>–<a href="/1939" title="1939">1939</a>) were described as an abrupt shift to more <a href="/Radical" title="Radical">radical</a> and <a href="/Conservative" title="Conservative">conservative</a> lifestyles, as countries were struggling to find a solution to the <a href="/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>, also known in <a href="/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> as the <i><a href="/World_Depression" title="World Depression">World Depression</a></i>.
</p><p>The <a href="/Gloomy" title="Gloomy">gloomy</a> conditions that arose led to a religious revival and the rise of <a href="/Conservatism" title="Conservatism">conservatism</a> that rejected the <a href="/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberalism</a> of the <a href="/1920s" title="1920s">1920s</a>, which began to be viewed as a decade of "sin." After 1933, the economy began a gradual recovery which wouldn't reach the level of prosperity of 1930 until <a href="/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.  In both <a href="/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europe</a> and <a href="/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a>, <a href="/Fascism" title="Fascism">Fascism</a>, <a href="/Nazism" title="Nazism">Nazism</a>, and <a href="/Stalinism" title="Stalinism">Stalinism</a> dominated as the solution, all of them <a href="/Totalitarian" title="Totalitarian">totalitarian</a> regimes. In <a href="/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a>, the rise of <a href="/Militarism" title="Militarism">militarism</a> occurred. 
</p><p>The beginning of <a href="/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> in <a href="/1939" title="1939">1939</a> ended the 1930s.
</p>

<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="?title=1930s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Visual arts">edit</a>]</div><a name="Visual_arts"></a><h2>Visual arts</h2>
<p><a href="/Social_Realism" title="Social Realism">Social Realism</a> became an important <a href="/Art_movement" title="Art movement">art movement</a> during the <a href="/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a> in the United States in the 1930s. Social realism generally portrayed imagery with socio-political meaning. Other related American artistic movements of the 1930s were  <a href="/American_scene_painting" title="American scene painting">American scene painting</a> and <a href="/Regionalism_%28art%29" title="Regionalism (art)">Regionalism</a> which were generally depictions of rural America, and historical images drawn from American history. <a href="/Precisionism" title="Precisionism">Precisionism</a> with its depictions of industrial America was also a popular art movement during the 1930s in the USA. During the <a href="/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a> the art of <a href="/Photography" title="Photography">Photography</a> played an important role in the Social Realist movement. The work of <a href="/Dorothea_Lange" title="Dorothea Lange">Dorothea Lange</a>, <a href="/Walker_Evans" title="Walker Evans">Walker Evans</a>, <a href="/Margaret_Bourke-White" title="Margaret Bourke-White">Margaret Bourke-White</a>, <a href="/Lewis_Hine" title="Lewis Hine">Lewis Hine</a>, <a href="/Edward_Steichen" title="Edward Steichen">Edward Steichen</a>, <a href="?title=Gordon_Parks&amp;action=edit" class="new" title="Gordon Parks">Gordon Parks</a>, <a href="?title=Arthur_Rothstein&amp;action=edit" class="new" title="Arthur Rothstein">Arthur Rothstein</a>, <a href="?title=Marion_Post_Wolcott&amp;action=edit" class="new" title="Marion Post Wolcott">Marion Post Wolcott</a>, <a href="?title=Doris_Ulmann&amp;action=edit" class="new" title="Doris Ulmann">Doris Ulmann</a>, <a href="/Berenice_Abbott" title="Berenice Abbott">Berenice Abbott</a>, <a href="/Aaron_Siskind" title="Aaron Siskind">Aaron Siskind</a>, <a href="?title=Russell_Lee_%28photographer%29&amp;action=edit" class="new" title="Russell Lee (photographer)">Russell Lee</a>, <a href="/Ben_Shahn" title="Ben Shahn">Ben Shahn</a> (as a photographer) among several others were particularly influential.
</p><p>The <a href="?title=Works_Progress_Administration&amp;action=edit" class="new" title="Works Progress Administration">Works Progress Administration</a> part of the <a href="?title=Franklin_D._Roosevelt_Administration&amp;action=edit" class="new" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt Administration">Roosevelt Administration</a>'s <a href="/New_Deal" title="New Deal">New Deal</a> sponsored the <a href="?title=Federal_Art_Project&amp;action=edit" class="new" title="Federal Art Project">Federal Art Project</a>, the <a href="?title=Public_Works_of_Art_Project&amp;action=edit" class="new" title="Public Works of Art Project">Public Works of Art Project</a>, and the <a href="?title=Section_of_Painting_and_Sculpture&amp;action=edit" class="new" title="Section of Painting and Sculpture">Section of Painting and Sculpture</a> which employed many American artists and helped them to make a living during the <a href="/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>.
</p><p><a href="/Mexican_muralism" title="Mexican muralism">Mexican muralism</a> was a <a href="/Mexican_art" title="Mexican art">Mexican art</a> movement that took place primarily in the 1930s. The movement stands out historically because of its political undertones, the majority of which of a <a href="/Marxist" title="Marxist">Marxist</a> nature, or related to a social and political situation of post-revolutionary Mexico. Also in Latin America <a href="/Symbolism_%28arts%29" title="Symbolism (arts)">Symbolism</a> and <a href="/Magic_Realism" title="Magic Realism">Magic Realism</a> were important movements.
</p><p>In Europe during the 1930s and the <a href="/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>, <a href="/Surrealism" title="Surrealism">Surrealism</a>, late <a href="/Cubism" title="Cubism">Cubism</a>, the <a href="/Bauhaus" title="Bauhaus">Bauhaus</a>, <a href="/De_Stijl" title="De Stijl">De Stijl</a>, <a href="/Dada" title="Dada">Dada</a>, <a href="/German_Expressionism" title="German Expressionism">German Expressionism</a>, <a href="/Expressionism" title="Expressionism">Expressionism</a>, <a href="/Symbolism_%28arts%29" title="Symbolism (arts)">Symbolist</a> and <a href="/Modernist" title="Modernist">modernist</a> painting in various guises characterized the art scene in <a href="/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> and elsewhere.
</p>
