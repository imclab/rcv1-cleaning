        <h1>Chicco NextFit Convertible Car Seat</h1>
        <small><em>by</em> <span>Kate</span> <em>on</em> <span>March 25th, 2013 <span style="margin:0px 5px;">|</span>  <a href="http://theshoppingmama.com/2013/03/chicco-nextfit-convertible-car-seat/#comments" title="Comment on Chicco NextFit Convertible Car Seat">20 Comments &#187;</a></span> <span style="margin:0px 5px;">|</span> <span>In</span> <em><a href="http://theshoppingmama.com/category/baby-gear-reviews/" title="View all posts in Gear" rel="category tag">Gear</a>, <a href="http://theshoppingmama.com/category/on-the-go/" title="View all posts in On the Go" rel="category tag">On the Go</a></em></small>
        <div class="entry">
            <p>In 2007 when my first son was 53 weeks old, my son moved to a convertible car seat. He was forward facing.</p>
<p>In 2009, we turned my daughter&#8217;s car seat forward facing when she was 14 months old.</p>
<p><em>When you know better, you do better. &#8211; Maya Angelou</em></p>
<p>I know better now. I know it is far <a href="http://theshoppingmama.com/2011/03/new-aap-rear-facing-car-seat-recommendations/">safer for my children to ride rear facing longer</a> so I will do better. Max will remain rear facing until he outgrows the height or weight limit of his car seat. That right there is one reason I am totally excited to be using the new <a href="http://www.chiccousa.com/nextfit/" target="_blank">Chicco NextFit Convertible Car Seat</a>. It has a rear facing weight limit of 40 pounds and boasts an innovative installation system that makes it easy to get the correct fit, angle and install every time.</p>
<p><a href="http://theshoppingmama.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/chicco-nextfit-convertible-car-seat.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-74599" alt="chicco nextfit convertible car seat" src="http://theshoppingmama.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/chicco-nextfit-convertible-car-seat.jpg" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>The NextFit is Chicco&#8217;s first convertible car seat offered in the US, and it joins the highly rated <a href="http://theshoppingmama.com/2012/02/top-5-essentials-for-new-mamas-from-sheri-gurock-of-magic-beans/" target="_blank">Chicco KeyFit Infant Car Seat</a>. The mission of the car seat is to provide the ultimate in safety through a simple, accurate and secure install and use program. Because ultimately, your child&#8217;s car seat is only safe as possible if it&#8217;s installed correctly.</p>
<p><a href="http://theshoppingmama.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/chicco-nextfit-easy-install-simple-accurate-secure.png"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-74600" alt="chicco nextfit easy install simple accurate secure" src="http://theshoppingmama.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/chicco-nextfit-easy-install-simple-accurate-secure.png" width="650" /></a>You know how some car seats require a pool noodle or rolled towel to achieve the correct recline position? Not the NextFit. It has a <strong>9-position leveling system</strong> that enables it to sit correctly in a wide range of cars. The NextFit has the same <strong>RideRight bubble level indicator</strong> as the KeyFit which takes the guesswork out of the seat angle. And, hallelujah, no more wrestling with the car seat to get a tight belt attachment! The <strong>SuperCinch LATCH tightener helps reach a tight and secure fit</strong>.</p>
<p>So, once the car seat is installed correctly it has to be belted correctly, and the NextFit has innovations in that area, too. The NextFit can be used with children from 5 to 65 pounds so it has a <strong>chest clip with two positions to fit the torso of smaller and larger children</strong>. As your child grows, there&#8217;s no need to rethread (phew!), as the <strong>harness straps adjust in height AND width</strong> as you move the headrest.</p>
<h3>Ages &amp; Stages for Safe Car Seat Use</h3>
