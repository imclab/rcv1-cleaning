    <p><b>What It Is: </b>The rear-wheel-drive <a href="/features/2013-subaru-brz-and-2013-scion-fr-s-a-study-in-comparison-and-contrast-feature" target="_self">Scion FR-S</a> will be offered in softtop convertible form, we've learned, confirming recent rumors. (The images you see here are renderings.) Unlike the <i>Fast and Furious</i>&ndash;ized FR-S roadster that an aftermarket tuner recently showed in California, the production FR-S convertible should retain its rear seats.</p><p><b>Why It Matters:</b><b> </b>The FR-S&mdash;as well as the Toyota GT 86 that&rsquo;s sold elsewhere and the nearly identical <a href="/reviews/2013-subaru-brz-limited-vs-route-napoleon-road-test" target="_self">Subaru BRZ</a>&mdash;is a reasonably priced, relatively light, rear-wheel-drive sports car. In other words, the vehicle enthusiasts have been begging automakers to actually build. A convertible version will add volume, which means more income for Toyota and happy accountants. Happy accountants are more likely to approve projects for fun cars in the future.</p><p><b>Platform: </b>The convertible will, of course, be built on the same platform as the joint Toyota/Subaru coupe. This architecture also stands a good chance of being used for other Toyota models. It's versatile enough to be enlarged, and could be the basis of a small four-door sedan or larger two-door coupe. Product planners are hard at work on the possibilities, a source tells us, although we're not sure if any of the spinoffs have been approved for production.</p><p>We don't yet know whether Subaru has any plans for this platform beyond the BRZ, but it seems less likely. Subaru already has a lineup of sporty four- and five-door vehicles, with a <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/subaru-wrx-and-sti-to-get-dedicated-platform-for-2013-drop-%E2%80%9Cimpreza%E2%80%9D-from-their-names/" target="_self">new WRX and STI expected soon</a>.</p><p><b>Powertrain: </b>The same 200-hp, 2.0-liter boxer-four from the FR-S and BRZ coupes will be used for the convertible. Manual and automatic transmissions, both of the six-speed variety, will be offered. Roof or not, we&rsquo;ve learned that <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/confirmed-no-scion-fr-s-turbo-subaru-brz-maybe-eventually/" target="_self">no Scion FR-S will get a turbocharger</a>. </p><p>A bigger coupe or other eventual models based on the FR-S&rsquo;s architecture likely would get larger engines to deliver the power and refinement customers in those segments expect.</p>

<p><b>Competition: </b>The most direct competitor will be the Mazda MX-5 Miata, a car to which the FR-S and BRZ coupes are already compared. But many sun-seekers care about style rather than driving dynamics, and some want a back seat that the Miata doesn&rsquo;t provide. For them, there are two convertibles that hit below the $30K mark: the six-cylinder Ford Mustang and the <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/2014-volkswagen-beetle-convertible-on-sale-early-next-year-will-offer-manualdiesel-combo-new-york-auto-show/" target="_self">Volkswagen Beetle</a>. </p><p>A bigger rear-wheel-drive Toyota coupe would probably target the <a href="/reviews/2013-hyundai-genesis-coupe-first-drive-review" target="_self">Hyundai Genesis coupe</a>, the Mustang, the Chevy Camaro, and perhaps even the BMW 1-series. A Toyota-badged sedan could compete with the Volkswagen Jetta GLI and <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/confirmed-next-generation-subaru-wrx-will-have-turbocharged-2-0-liter-flat-four/" target="_self">Subaru WRX</a>. It&rsquo;s also possible Lexus would get a version, which could sit below the IS and face the upcoming <a href="/news/2013-audi-a3-quattro-first-ride-review" target="_self">Audi A3 sedan</a>, the future <a href="/news/mercedes-benz-concept-style-coupe-news" target="_self">Mercedes-Benz CLA-class</a>, and the underwhelming <a href="/reviews/2013-acura-ilx-first-drive-review" target="_self">Acura ILX</a>. </p><p><b>Estimated Arrival Time and Price: </b> Toyota and Subaru need to satisfy demand for their rear-wheel-drive coupes, launch ad campaigns, and get the production facilities up to full speed first and foremost. The earliest we could see a convertible debut is a year from now, but a debut in late 2013 or early 2014 is more likely. Any products spun off the platform are at least two years out. </p><p>The convertible version of the Scion FR-S will top the coupe&rsquo;s $24,930 base price by a few grand. Toyota has been cautious about pricing the FR-S so far, offering no factory options besides an automatic transmission, and the company is worried that a $30,000 price tag on a Scion would be a nonstarter with dealers. We agree, and think the droptop FR-S will come in below that psychological barrier. </p>
