									<div class="entry-content">
										<p>“The 39 Steps,” a suspenseful romantic comedy based on the 1935
movie by Alfred Hitchcock, will play at the Marian Theatre in Santa
Maria from Feb. 10 through March 6, then in the Solvang Festival
Theater from June 16 through July 3.</p><p>Four actors play more than a hundred characters in this Tony-winner
that kept Broadway rollicking for the past three years.</p><p>PCPA calls the play part juicy spy novel and part Monty Python,
which still preserves the brilliance created by Hitchcock, the
master of suspense.</p><p>Andrew Philpot plays the suave hero, Richard Hannay, who learns
from a beautiful spy about a plot of international espionage. The
woman is killed in Hannay’s flat and he suddenly finds himself
caught up in a race from London to Scotland in hopes of stopping
military secrets from being smuggled out of the country.</p><p>As he searches for the secret of “The 39 Steps” he is doggedly
pursued by the police who believe he is a murderer. Three of the
female roles will be played by Stephanie Philo, while the remaining
multitude of characters (spies, policemen, inn keepers, traveling
salesmen and more) are played by just two actors, credited as Clown
No. 1 (Peter S. Hadres) and Clown No. 2 (Evans Eden
Jarnefeldt).</p><p>Director Mark Booher says the play is much like a roller coaster,
full of twists and turns and delightful fun. Scenic designer is
Kent Homchick; costume designer, Juliane Starks; lighting designer,
Tamar Geist; sound designer, Elisabeth Rebel; and stage manager,
Christine Collins.</p><p>The play remains very close to the film, particularly in terms of
its frenetic timing. But putting the movie on stage requires
ingenious theatrical devices to instantly go from a chase scene on
a train, to a plane crash, to a luxurious Scottish manor, then to a
London flat, or suddenly the London Palladium.</p><p>Also occurring at breakneck speed are nearly 100 costume changes,
which often happen right before the audience in the blink of an
eye.</p><p>“The 39 Steps” was one of a dozen motion pictures Alfred Hitchcock
made in England before coming to America. Though the novel has been
adapted for the screen nearly half a dozen times, Hitchcock’s is
considered to be the finest to date. And the remakes aren’t over
yet. It was announced last fall that Robert Towne (“Chinatown”)
would be writing the screenplay and directing a new remake of “The
39 Steps” with a release anticipated sometime this year.</p><p>For single tickets or more information, call the box office at
922-8313 between 12:30 and 7 p.m. Wednesdays through Sundays, or
visit www.pcpa.org.</p><p>For information about discounted group sales for 12 or more
patrons, send an e-mail to groups@pcpa.org or call(800)
PCPA-123.</p><p>Season tickets offer the best seating, lowest prices and exclusive
privileges, PCPA officials noted. For more information, go to the
website, call the box office to receive a subscription brochure, or
e-mail</p><p>boxoffice@pcpa.org.</p><p>Now in its 47th season, PCPA Theaterfest is the Central Coast’s
resident professional theater company and the drama department at
Hancock College. It has sent thousands of trained actors and
theatrical technicians into theater professions.</p>

Santa Maria; Solvang Festival Theater, 420 2nd St, Solvang</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Tickets: 922-8313, www.pcpa.org</li>
</ul>
