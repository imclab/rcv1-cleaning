			<div class="entry-meta">
									<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://molly743.wordpress.com/2013/04/03/tips-on-how-to-choose-a-piano-teacher/" title="11:01 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">April 3, 2013</span></a>					<span class="by-author"><span class="sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://molly743.wordpress.com/author/molly743/" title="View all posts by molly743" rel="author">molly743</a></span> </span>							</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
				<p>There are a lot of people today who have problems in choosing the best instructor to teach them how to play the piano. Likewise, there are also experienced pianists who wanted to accumulate more knowledge in order for them to enhance their skills. Fortunately, there is one French concert pianists who managed to come up with the tips on how to choose the best piano teacher.</p>
<p>Celine Gaurier-Jouberts’ Tips</p>
<p><a href="http://celinegaurierjoubert.co.uk/">Celine Gaurier-Joubert</a> is a concert pianist and a master teacher of music specializing in Piano courses based in London. She teaches in the London Piano Institute, a famous school for Piano learners. According to Celine, it would be easy to look for the best piano teacher if you follow some guidelines. First, you should try research in the internet. You can also contact music teachers and ask them about piano instructors. Also, it would be best if you will not just focus within your area because the right instructor you are looking for might be miles away from you. Secondly, during the initial conversation with the instructor, you should introduce yourself and tell the instructor and what you wish to achieve in music. Thirdly, set appointment with the teacher you choose. During discussion, the instructor must talk to you about your musical interest and wishes. In this stage, the instructor will know how to adapt to your wishes and be ready to teach in accordance to your background and personality. Lastly, you should feel comfortable with the instructor and know about her/his kind of music so that you can build a common ground.</p>
<p><img src="http://www.londonmusicteachers.co.uk/articles/img/pianist-playing-music.jpg" alt="" /></p>
<p>Other considerations in choosing the teacher</p>
<p>Other consideration for choosing your piano teacher is the tuition fee. Remember that the higher the tuition fee, the better quality you can get. However, it is not always the case. Another consideration will be the background of the instructor. It is a good point that the instructor experienced <a href="http://urbanlegends.about.com/library/weekly/aa062501b.htm">several performances</a> as concert pianist. Lastly, develop the right habit in learning to play the piano.</p>
<p>To conclude, great concert pianists started from zero background in music. If you will have the right teacher then most probably you might become famous like any other musical geniuses. Look here for more details <a href="http://cgj.org.uk/">http://cgj.org.uk/</a> .</p>

							<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://molly743.wordpress.com/2013/04/03/how-your-child-can-become-a-concert-pianist/" rel="bookmark">How Your Child Can Become a Concert&nbsp;Pianist</a></h2>			
			<div class="entry-meta">
									<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://molly743.wordpress.com/2013/04/03/how-your-child-can-become-a-concert-pianist/" title="8:01 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">April 3, 2013</span></a>					<span class="by-author"><span class="sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://molly743.wordpress.com/author/molly743/" title="View all posts by molly743" rel="author">molly743</a></span> </span>							</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
				<p>What kind of parent does not want his or her child to become someone great or at least good at something? Parents want to send their children to the best schools to get the best education and give the best chance at a successful life. If you think your child has a propensity for music, especially the piano, then why not allow your child to dream of becoming a concert pianist? </p>
<p>Start Early</p>
<p>Studies have shown that the formative years of children, from four up to twelve years, <a href="http://www.londonmusicteachers.co.uk/choosing_a_music_teacher.php">Concert Pianist</a> are the most crucial part if we want them to truly become a master of something. During these years, they manage to learn more information and acquire more skills. Whatever they learn during these years is ingrained in them until they become what people will call a “natural”.</p>
<p><img src="http://www.londonmusicteachers.co.uk/articles/img/pianist-playing-music.jpg"></p>
<p>Get the Best Piano Teacher</p>
<p>Honestly, this may cost you. Obviously, these piano teachers did not become who they are overnight. But there are lots of piano teachers out there. In fact, you may not need to walk too far to find one. However, what you are really paying for is the experience and the knowledge they can teach your children. This is not something you should take easily. Remember that what your child learns will be very hard to unlearn. You want your child to learn the right techniques and ways to give them a better advantage at being a great concert pianist.</p>
<p>Get the Best Piano</p>
<p>Have you ever tried to learn to drive without a car? Was it easy? No? Well, have you ever tried to learn to drive using a…well let’s just say it was a confusing car? As soon as you get on a properly functioning car, you get confused because you had learned from such a poor car. The same happens when you try learning from a mediocre piano. Once you get a good one, you start thinking it sounds or feels wrong because all these years, you’ve been playing on a bad piano. It develops wrong habits especially on children. You do not want this to happen to your concert-pianist-to-be.</p>
<p>Give your child the best shot and provide nothing but the best.Reference taken from here <a href="http://celinegaurierjoubert.co.uk/">http://celinegaurierjoubert.co.uk/</a> .</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
									<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://molly743.wordpress.com/2013/03/04/the-famous-paintings-list-styles-of-painting-commonly-used/" title="12:02 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">March 4, 2013</span></a>					<span class="by-author"><span class="sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://molly743.wordpress.com/author/molly743/" title="View all posts by molly743" rel="author">molly743</a></span> </span>							</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
				<p>In the art of painting, there exist many styles expressed by artists throughout time. Usually an artist stays with one style and seldom deviates from it. A style is how the appearance of the painting relates to the artist’s other works of art, the time period the art was created, where it was created, and its viewpoint. The style of the painting becomes its representation. Paintings are often classified or categorized based on styles.<br />
In art circles, the style is also termed as the art movement. It is the already categorized form of arm. In all cultures, there are many styles that exist. Here are the most common styles from some of the <a href="http://totallyhistory.com/art-history/famous-paintings/">famous paintings list</a> throughout the centuries.<br />
19th century<br />
 Expressionism (1890s – 1930s)<br />
It usually depicts emotions and a distorted interpretation of reality.<br />
Most Famous Arts: The Scream by Edvard Munch, Woman I – VI by Willem de Kooning<br />
 Impressionism (1860s – 1920s)<br />
It is an art style commonly associated with French artists. This is art uses the effects of natural light in its subjects, especially the sun.<br />
Most Famous Arts: Impression, soleil levant by Claude Monet, Bridge at Villeneuve-la-Garenne by Alfred Sisley, Girl with a Hoop by Pierre-Auguste Renoir<br />
 Naturalism<br />
It is also referred to as Realism. This work of art illustrates the subjects in the painting in its most truthful way. Artistic alterations are usually avoided or not encouraged.<br />
Most Famous Arts: Bonjour, Monsieur Courbet by Gustave Courbet, A Norman Milkmaid at Greville by Jean-Francois Millet<br />
 Modernism (1860s – present)<br />
It is the vastest style in art that is still used until now. It is an art style that is the complete opposite of realism or naturalism. It encourages the creativity and the most artistic expressions of artists.<br />
Most Famous Arts: Les Demoiselles d’Avignon by Pablo Picasso, No. 5, 1948 by Jackson Pollock<br />
21st century<br />
<img alt="" src="http://molly743.files.wordpress.com/2013/03/famous-paintings-made-of-vegetables12.jpg?w=421&#038;h=574" width="421" height="574" /><br />
 Computer art and Digital art<br />
Newer artist are using technology as their medium.<br />
 Graffiti art<br />
Art that is usually found on walls of public locations and buildings, although can be interpreted as vandalism. It is still a work of art that has existed even back to the ancient civilizations of Greece, <a href="http://totallyhistory.com/us-history/us-presidents">totallyhistory.com</a> Egypt and the Roman Empire.</p>
<p>These are only a selected number of styles that ever existed in the world of art. If you are interested to learn more, it is suggested to go through all the classical and modern styles, not just in the Western category, but expand through the Far Eastern and to the African selections.</p>
