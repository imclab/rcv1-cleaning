					<div class="entry-meta">
						<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://www.autointhenews.com/chevrolet-celebrates-custom-car-culture/" title="7:27 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">July 18, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://www.autointhenews.com/author/admin/" title="View all posts by Auto in the News">Auto in the News</a></span>					</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
						<a id="dd_start"></a><p><a href="http://www.autointhenews.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/Chevrolet-badge-image.jpg" ><img class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-6969" style="margin: 5px;" title="2011 Chevrolet Volt image" src="http://www.autointhenews.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/Chevrolet-badge-image-175x175.jpg" alt="2011 Chevrolet Volt image" width="175" height="175" /></a>Chevrolet will be celebrating custom-car culture in Detroit next month when the automaker sponsors the 17<sup>th</sup> annual Woodward Dream Cruise. Although cars from enthusiasts will be the show’s focal point, according to <a title="Lubbock Chevrolet" href="http://www.hamiltongmcountry.com" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.hamiltongmcountry.com']);">Lubbock Chevrolet</a>, the automaker plans to highlight some of its newest models, including the Cruze, Camaro, Corvette, and Volt.<span id="more-6968"></span></p>
<blockquote><p>“Chevrolet and the Woodward Dream Cruise are a natural partnership,” said Rick Scheidt, vice president, Chevrolet marketing. “The Woodward Dream Cruise is the ultimate grassroots automotive event. It is open to everyone. It’s free to attend. You can drive, or just watch. You can bring the family. It is a true celebration of automotive passion and performance, and we are pleased that Chevrolet can help make it possible.”</p></blockquote>
