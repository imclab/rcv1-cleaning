<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
Lucy Lawless: 'Spartacus will surprise fans'
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5735852365281356219' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<img src="http://i25.tinypic.com/2ir5it1.jpg" width="200"/><br /><br />Spartacus star Lucy Lawless has revealed that forthcoming mini-series Gods of the Arena will surprise viewers.<br /><br />The actress told IGN that characters who debuted in original series Blood and Sand would reappear in the six-part prequel, but would be different to how fans remembered them.<br /><br />"The audience might feel smart because they know what's coming, but we're still going to surprise you," she claimed. "We're still going to twist it up."<br /><br />She added: "All the relationships are different. Solonius (Craig Walsh) and Batiatus (John Hannah) were actually great friends once."<br /><br />Lawless explained that she was looking forward to exploring a more positive side to her character Lucretia.<br /><br />"I, personally, am looking forward to seeing all of the characters as they were before they became a train wreck," she explained. "To see them hopeful, idealistic and with plans."<br /><br />She also admitted that she was pleased to be reunited with her co-star Hannah.<br /><br />"He's so much fun, for starters," she said "And he's really smart [and] professional. And he just helped anchor the whole production. Because we had a lot of actors for who it was their first gig ever and to have somebody like John come in and set a tone of complete professionalism was bloody wonderful."<br /><br />Spartacus: Gods of the Arena will air on Starz in January.<br /><br />source: digitalspy
