In fact, it’s 107 feet larger than the <strong>London Eye</strong> and 9 feet taller than the <strong>Singapore Flyer.</strong></p> <p>The target date for the first “all aboard” is in June, and each revolution of this monster will take 30 minutes high above the desert, mountain and Strip views from one end of Las Vegas and Clark County to the other. No word yet on the projected 2013 opening date of the rival wheel across from <strong>Mandalay Bay.</strong></p> <p>Linq, which recently had some of the <strong>2012 Miss Universe Pageant</strong> contestants visit for a ceremonial groundbreaking with exec <strong>Jon Gray,</strong> will be in the promenade between the <strong>Flamingo</strong> and the newly named <strong>Quad,</strong> formerly <strong>Imperial Palace.</strong> <strong>O’Sheas</strong> is already closed and will be demolished for this expansion.</p> <p><strong>BILL’S GAMBLIN’ HALL AND SALOON</strong></p> <p>The neighboring <strong>Bill’s Gamblin’ Hall and Saloon</strong> also gets a total new look as <strong>Vegas DeLuxe</strong> first reported, and a name yet to be determined. <strong>Caesars Entertainment</strong> will spend $200 million once Bill’s is closed in February for the 18-month makeover. <strong>Drai’s After Hours</strong> will relocate to <strong>Bally’s</strong> next door during construction.</p> <p>Veteran nightlife czar <strong>Victor Drai</strong> will run the new, 65,000- square-foot rooftop dayclub and night party pool zone of the boutique hotel similar to his <strong>W</strong> outpost in Hollywood. There will be beautiful views of the bikini beauties bronzing from the High Roller!</p> <p><strong>HARMON HOTEL</strong></p> <p>The mystery of what will happen with the world’s biggest and most expensive billboard also will be solved in 2013.
