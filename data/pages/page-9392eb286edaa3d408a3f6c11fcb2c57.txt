<h3>Books and Literature Chronology</h3>
<ol class="timelineList">
<li class="firstItem">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 23, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Claudia La Rocco reviews book A Marker to Measure Drift by Alexander Maksik.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/23/books/alexander-maksiks-new-novel-a-marker-to-measure-drift.html" title="On an Aegean Island, an Ugly Past Rolls in With the Tide" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 22, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Book The Anatomy of Fashion: Why We Dress the Way We Do by Colin McDowell examines clothing as its relates to specific boy parts.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/22/fashion/a-new-book-looks-at-the-body-and-its-armor.html" title="A New Book Looks at the Body and Its Armor" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 21, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Michael S Roth reviews book Why Teach? In Defense of a Real Education by Mark Edmundson.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/21/books/mark-edmundsons-essays-ask-why-teach.html" title="How Four Years Can (and Should) Transform You" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 21, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Elmore Leonard, prolific crime novelist whose louche characters, deadpan dialogue and immaculate prose style established him as a modern master of American genre writing, dies at age 87; to his admiring peers, Leonard reinvented the popular crime thriller form  for a new generation, lifting it to a higher literary shelf.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/21/books/elmore-leonard-master-of-crime-fiction-dies-at-87.html" title="A Novelist Who Made Crime an Art, and His Bad Guys &#8216;Fun&#8217;" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item lastItem">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 20, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Larry Rohter reviews book George Orwell: A Life in Letters, selected and annotated by Peter Davison.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/20/books/george-orwells-letters-fill-out-a-complex-personality.html" title="At Home at Animal Farm: An Orwell Collection" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 19, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Dwight Garner reviews books The Big Disconnect: Protecting Childhood and Family Relationships in the Digital Age by Catherine Steiner-Adair, The Distraction Addiction: Getting the Information You Need and the Communication You Want, Without Enraging Your Family, Annoying Your Colleagues and Destroying Your Soul by Alex Soojung-Kim Pang and The App Generation: How Today's Youth Navigate Identity, Intimacy and Imagination in a Digital World by Howard Gardiner and Katie Davis.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/19/books/3-books-offer-ways-to-cut-the-cord-if-only-briefly.html" title="Resisting the Siren Call of the Screen" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 14, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Adam Langer reviews book In Times of Fading Light by Eugen Ruge.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/14/books/in-times-of-fading-light-spans-generations-in-germany.html" title="Family Transcends a Wall Between East and West" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 14, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Profile of Zander Hollander, who for decades compiled sports statistics, team rosters, profiles and predictions in his Complete Handbook series, giving ardent fans yearbooks brimming with information and insight on baseball, basketball, football and hockey.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/14/sports/for-true-sports-fans-before-the-internet-there-were-the-complete-handbooks.html" title="For Sports Fans, Before the Internet, There Were the Complete Handbooks" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 13, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Michiko Kakutani reviews book JFK's Last Hundred Days: The Transformation of a Man and the Emergence of a Great President by Thurston Clarke.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/13/books/jfks-last-hundred-days-by-thurston-clarke.html" title="Kennedy, and What Might Have Been" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 12, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">David Haglund reviews book Necessary Errors by Caleb Crain.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/12/books/in-necessary-errors-a-young-gay-man-moves-to-1990s-prague.html" title="Reconstructing a Lost Time of Love and Literary Ambitions" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 11, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Fred Andrews Off the Shelf column highlights book Story of My People by Edoardo Nesi.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/11/business/in-story-of-my-people-recalling-an-industry-and-way-of-life.html" title="In &#8216;Story of My People,&#8217; Recalling an Industry (and Way of Life)" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 11, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Look column presents photos of graffiti by Brazilian street artists featured in Rafael Schacter book The World Atlas of Street Art and Graffiti.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/11/magazine/the-street-artists-of-brazil.html" title="The Street Artists of Brazil" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 9, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Jane Austen is now insatiable cottage industry in 200th birthday year for Pride and Prejudice; Janeiac board game instructions and board are shown.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/09/books/on-austen-new-books-banknotes-and-board-games.html" title="Jane, Plain No More: A Year of Austen Glamour" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 9, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Janet Maslin reviews book Hothouse: The Art of Survival of Art at America's Most Celebrated Publishing House, Farrar, Straus & Giroux.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/09/books/in-hothouse-boris-kachka-tells-the-farrar-straus-story.html" title="Gentlemen at Work" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 8, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Random House will publish expanded version of graduation speech given by writer George Saunders at Syracuse University in book called Congratulations, by the Way.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/08/business/media/graduation-speech-by-george-saunders-to-become-a-book.html" title="Graduation Speech by George Saunders to Become a Book" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 6, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Dale B Martin reviews book Zealot: The Life and Times of Jesus of Nazareth by Reza Aslan.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/06/books/reza-aslans-zealot-the-life-and-times-of-jesus-of-nazareth.html" title="Still a Firebrand, 2,000 Years Later" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 5, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Sleeper literary hit of summer 2013 is The Silent Wife, thriller about a Chicago couple's crumbling marriage; tragically, author A S A Harrison died of cancer at age 65 just weeks before book's publication.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/05/books/the-novel-silent-wife-benefits-from-success-of-gone-girl.html" title="A Sleeper Success Defines Novelist&#8217;s Debut and Legacy" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
</li>
<li class="item hide">
<h6 class="date">Aug. 5, 2013</h6>
<p class="summary">Maria Russo reviews book The Love Affairs of Nathaniel P by Adelle Waldman.<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/05/books/in-love-affairs-of-nathaniel-p-women-flummox-a-writer.html" title="Brooklyn, Where Exes May Lurk, Tests an Emotional Fugitive" class="inTextRefer">MORE »</a></p>
