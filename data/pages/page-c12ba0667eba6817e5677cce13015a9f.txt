			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/2012/02/02/2001-bmw-325xi-awd-german-daily-driver/" title="Permalink to 2001 BMW 325xi &#8211; AWD German Daily Driver" rel="bookmark">2001 BMW 325xi &#8211; AWD German Daily Driver </a></h2>
			<img src='http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcarimages/60px325xi.jpg' style='display:inline; float:left' class='projectblogimage'>
			<div style='clear:both'></div>
			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep-author meta-prep">	</span>  <a href="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/2012/02/02/2001-bmw-325xi-awd-german-daily-driver/" title="5:10 pm" rel="bookmark" class="entry-date">February 2, 2012</a> <span class="meta-sep"></span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n authorlink" href="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/author/2001-bmw-325xi/" title="View all posts by 2001 BMW 325xi">2001 BMW 325xi</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			<!-- <div class='blogline'>&nbsp</div> -->
				<div style='clear:both'></div>   
			<div class="entry-content">
				<p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-186" title="BMW325xi.Stock.Fuelculture" src="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/BMW325xi.Stock_.Fuelculture-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="600" height="400" /></p>
<p>Project BMW 325XI was picked up as a comfortable daily driver that could see weekend track duty safely and reliably. While the 2.5L inline 6 isn&#8217;t the most powerful powerplant, it&#8217;s definitely got enough torque to move out of it&#8217;s own way on a race track while still delivering an average of 28 MPG on the highway.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-185" title="Gingerman.2001BMW325xi" src="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Gingerman.2001BMW325xi.jpg" alt="" width="500" height="277" /></p>
<p>Since taking delivery of Project 325XI, it&#8217;s seen a few upgrades including an M3 front bumper, M3 CSL spoiler, a better flowing exhaust system, H&amp;R Sport springs, and an <a href="http://www.fuelculture.com/aem/cold-air-intake-22-670p-770552064">AEM intake</a>. It&#8217;s seen track action at Gingerman Raceway, Autobahn Country Club, and Road America where it has proven that the 325XI&#8217;s all wheel drive system provides loads of grip in all weather conditions.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-184" title="2001BMW325xi.FuelcultureGarage" src="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/2001BMW325xi.FuelcultureGarage-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="600" height="400" /></p>
<p>As a Fuel Culture project vehicle, Project 325XI will continue to evolve as a worthy track vehicle, while still maintaining the creature comforts that makes a BMW a BMW, further fortifying BMW&#8217;s former catch phrase “The Ultimate Driving Machine.”</p>

				<h2><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-177" title="2012HyundaiVeloster.FuelCulture.Drifting.Forza4" src="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/2012HyundaiVeloster.FuelCulture.Drifting.Forza4_-600x337.jpg" alt="" width="600" height="337" /></h2>
<p>Last week Turn 10 Studios made the Hyundai Veloster DLC available to all Forza Motorsport 4 users for free. Our Project Hyundai Veloster which we unveiled at the 2011 SEMA Show in the Hyundai booth. Someone quickly made a 100% replica of the build which you can download and start playing with today!</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-178" title="HyundaiVeloster.SEMA2011Build.fuelculture" src="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/HyundaiVeloster.SEMA2011Build.fuelculture-600x337.jpg" alt="" width="600" height="337" /></p>

			<img src='http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcarimages/60pxveloster.jpg' style='display:inline; float:left' class='projectblogimage'>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep-author meta-prep">	</span>  <a href="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/2012/01/12/cleaning-up-the-engine-with-a-wire-tuck-and-chasebays-products/" title="1:00 pm" rel="bookmark" class="entry-date">January 12, 2012</a> <span class="meta-sep"></span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n authorlink" href="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/author/2012-hyundai-veloster/" title="View all posts by 2012 Hyundai Veloster">2012 Hyundai Veloster</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

<p>The engine bay is one of the areas we focused on to complete our over the top 2012 Hyundai Veloster build. Here is what the engine bay looks like with the factory battery and trey removed along with the engine. As you can see both fuses boxes are located in the car along with tons of other clutter.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-166" title="HyundaiVeloster.WireRelocation" src="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/HyundaiVeloster.WireRelocation-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="600" height="400" /></p>
<p>This is where all the wires are being worked on once we pull the wiring into the interior cabin from the engine bay. Our permanent relocation of the two fuse boxes plans to be behind the glovebox pictured here. This was easily accessed once we removed some of the heat and a/c items that are placed back here.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-167" title="AEMintake.HondaCivicEP3." src="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/AEMintake.HondaCivicEP3.-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="600" height="400" /></p>
<p>Since we cleaned up the engine bay so much it provided a ton of space to place product. We didn&#8217;t have enough days in our build to loan the car to AEM for a complete intake R&amp;D so we eyeballed a fitment from a 2002-2005 Honda Civic Si. The <a href="http://www.fuelculture.com/aem/short-ram-intake-system-22-508">AEM product 22-508</a> which ended up working perfectly for our build.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-168" title="AEMintake.NitrousExpress.Installed" src="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/AEMintake.NitrousExpress.Installed-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="600" height="400" /></p>
<p>Here we are putting the final placement of the AEM intake in the car. As you can see the clearance is pretty minimal but with a similar setup to the K series Honda engine we were able to make the product work for this build. While we completed the installation of the AEM intake we also ran and connected our Nitrous Express lines.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-169" title="HyundaiVeloster.EngineBay.Finished" src="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/HyundaiVeloster.EngineBay.Finished-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="600" height="400" /></p>
<p>After some more tweaks we completed the engine bay build with the help of some sponsors. With Chase Bays we managed to run a <a href="http://www.fuelculture.com/chase-bays/brake-booster-eliminator-cb-u-bbe">Brake Booster Eliminator (CB-U-BBE)</a> which greatly cleaned up our back firewall area. We used Password:JDM Fender Washers to clean up the overall engine bay. You can see these in this photograph holding on the plastic radiator shroud.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-172" title="HyundaiVeloster.AEMintake.PasswordJDM.ChaseBays" src="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/HyundaiVeloster.AEMintake.PasswordJDM.ChaseBays-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="600" height="400" /></p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-171" title="HyundaiVeloster.EngineBay.Done" src="http://www.fuelculture.com/projectcars/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/HyundaiVeloster.EngineBay.Done_-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="600" height="400" /></p>
<p>A close up look at the Veloster shows how our EP3 Honda Civic AEM intake fit into the engine bay. It is a very tight fit and Veloster owners shouldn&#8217;t worry since AEM will be making a specific application for this car. This is the <a href="http://www.fuelculture.com/chase-bays/engine-ground-kit-cb-u-grnd">Chase Bays Engine Ground Kit (CB-U-GRND)</a> which can be located in both images and is a great way to make basic items in your engine bay look clean and unique. This completed our engine bay clean up and preparation for the cars debut at the 2011 SEMA Show.</p>
