      <h1 class="postTitle"><a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/bmw-4-series-coupe-concept-to-debut-at-detroit-auto-show/" rel="bookmark">BMW 4-series Coupe Concept to Debut at Detroit Auto Show</a></h1>

      
      
      <div id="creditsHolder" style="margin:10px 0 0 0;">

        <div id="fbLikeHolder1" style="display:inline-block;"></div>

        <small class="post-credits">
        November 12, 2012 at 6:18 pm by <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/author/jens-meiners/" title="Posts by Jens Meiners" rel="author">Jens Meiners</a>        </small>
      </div>

        <div id="divButtons"></div>

        <div id="attachment_97151" class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 636px"><img class="size-large wp-image-97151" title="2011 BMW 335i coupe" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" data-lazy-src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/P90054822_highRes-626x417.jpg" alt="" width="626" height="417" /><noscript><img class="size-large wp-image-97151" title="2011 BMW 335i coupe" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/P90054822_highRes-626x417.jpg" alt="" width="626" height="417" /></noscript><p class="wp-caption-text">2011 BMW 335i coupe</p></div>
<p>The <a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/reviews/2012-bmw-335i-sedan-test-review">current-generation BMW 3-series sedan</a> has been out for a year, and the <a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/reviews/2013-bmw-3-series-sports-wagon-first-drive-review">3-series Sports Wagon</a> already is on sale in Europe and will hit U.S. showrooms next spring. What&#8217;s missing? The coupe, of course. After all, the 3-series started out as a two-door in 1975—a four-door wasn&#8217;t offered until 1982, when the second-generation arrived. The wait for the F30-generation 3-series coupe is coming to an end, however. At January&#8217;s Detroit auto show, BMW will unveil a 3-series–based two-door concept, previewing the next-gen 3-series coupe. However, we&#8217;re hearing from European sources that the Bavarians finally will change their nomenclature and call this car the 4-series, although BMW hasn&#8217;t confirmed that.<span id="more-97107"></span></p>
<hr noshade="noshade" />
<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/comparisons/2012-bmw-328i-vs-2013-cadillac-ats-comparison-test">Comparison Test: 2012 BMW 328i vs. 2013 Cadillac ATS</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/comparisons/2012-bmw-m3-vs-2012-mercedes-benz-c63-amg-comparison-test">Comparison Test: 2012 BMW M3 Coupe vs. 2012 Mercedes-Benz C63 AMG Coupe</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/comparisons/2012-bmw-328i-vs-2012-audi-a4-20t-2012-infiniti-g25-2012-mercedes-benz-c250-sport-2012-volvo-s60-t6-awd-comparison-tests">Comparison Test: 2012 BMW 328i vs. 2012 Audi A4 2.0T, 2012 Infiniti G25, 2012 Mercedes-Benz C250 Sport, 2012 Volvo S60 T6 AWD</a></li>
</ul>
<hr noshade="noshade" />Not only will the 4-series coupe be previewed by the concept, but the next-gen 4-series convertible will take styling cues from the concept, as well. Unsurprisingly, the U.S. market is very important for both variants. Technical details are scarce at the moment, but don&#8217;t expect major changes from the F30 sedan&#8217;s turbocharged 2.0-liter inline-four and turbo 3.0-liter straight-six. And as far as the M4—are we the only ones struggling to come to terms with this moniker?—be prepared for a return to six cylinders, albeit boosted.</p>
<p>Expect further details on the 4-series concept closer to the start of the 2013 Detroit auto show, which kicks off January 14.</p>
