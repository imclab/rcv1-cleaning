						 <h1 class="articleh1">Used Car Prices Up Thanks to Success of Certified Pre-Owned Programs</h1>
														<span class="createdby">By Liz Opsitnik</span><br/>
																			<span class="createdate">Wednesday, Oct 20 2010 14:18</span>
							<br/><br/>
												<p>Due to the success of certified pre-owned vehicle sales, the entire used car industry is experiencing a price boost. The average price paid in the U.S. for a three-year-old used vehicle in September was $18,832, <a href="http://www.edmunds.com/help/about/press/165786/article.html" target="_blank"><span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 255);">Edmunds.com estimates</span></a>. This is an increase of $1,471, or 8.5 percent, from September 2009.</p>
<p>&quot;Used cars are in great demand and relatively short supply, so their prices are remarkably high,&quot; Edmunds.com Analyst Joe Spina said in a statement. &quot;Automakers understand that many traditional new car buyers are considering used cars, and in response are emphasizing their certified pre-owned car sales programs through which they can earn revenue on used car sales. This effort will keep used car prices high since CPO cars sell for a higher price than comparable non-certified used cars, raising the ceiling for the entire used car market.&quot;</p>
<p>Three-year-old used vehicles, like the <a href="http://www.autoloandaily.com/new-car-research?searchby=makes&amp;make=BMW&amp;model=X5" target="_blank"><span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 255);">BMW X5</span></a>, <a href="http://www.autoloandaily.com/new-car-research?searchby=makes&amp;make=Acura&amp;model=MDX" target="_blank"><span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 255);">Acura MDX</span></a> and <a href="http://www.autoloandaily.com/new-car-research?searchby=makes&amp;make=Chevrolet&amp;model=Tahoe" target="_blank"><span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 255);">Chevrolet Tahoe</span></a>, saw a price increase of more than 20 percent in September compared to the same month a year ago, Edmunds.com reports.</p>
<p><img alt="BMW X5" src="/images/stories/bmw x5.jpg" /> </p>
<p><span style="color: rgb(153, 153, 153);"><i>Used BMW X5 SUVs saw price increases of almost $8,500 in September.</i></span></p>
<p>When considering if you should buy a new, used or certified pre-owned car, one thing to look at is the financing. Generally, a used car loan rate will be higher than a new car loan rate, which means it will cost more to finance. Some certified pre-owned programs offer discounted auto loan rate incentives on select models to attract buyers. Even luxury automakers offer auto loan rate incentives on their certified pre-owned models.</p>
<p>In October, <a href="http://www.autoloandaily.com/car-deals/rebates-and-incentives/2470-audi-incentives-october-2010" target="_blank"><span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 255);">Audi</span></a>, <a href="http://www.autoloandaily.com/car-deals/rebates-and-incentives/2477-jaguar-incentives-october-2010" target="_blank"><span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 255);">Jaguar</span></a>, <a href="http://www.autoloandaily.com/car-deals/rebates-and-incentives/2478-land-rover-range-rover-incentives-october-2010" target="_blank"><span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 255);">Land Rover</span></a> and <a href="http://www.autoloandaily.com/car-deals/rebates-and-incentives/2479-bmw-mini-incentives-october-2010" target="_blank"><span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 255);">BMW</span></a> are offering certified pre-owned car loan rate discounts on select models. General Motors has offered <a href="http://www.autoloandaily.com/auto-loan-advice/used-car-loans/2196-auto-loan-rate-incentive-gm-certified-used" target="_blank"><span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 255);">certified pre-owned auto loan rate incentives in the past</span></a>.</p>
<p>&quot;The fact that there is high demand for used cars is good news for consumers selling or trading in their cars, but not for buyers,&quot; said Edmunds.com Senior Consumer Advice Editor Philip Reed. &quot;We recommend that anyone looking at a lightly used car should also research prices and incentives on new cars, because the deals on some new cars make them even less expensive than used cars.&quot;</p><BR>
