 <h2>The culture of Italy</h2><br />
                         From antiquity until at least to all the 16th century, the Italian peninsula was the central place of Western culture, fulcrum or origin of universal phenomena as the Roman Empire, Roman Catholic Church, Humanism and
                        Renaissance.<br />
                        During these times, Italy produced some of the greatest painters, sculptors, poets, musicians, mathematical and architects in history.<br />
                        Italian painters, sculptors, composers, and architects also dominated the Baroque art movement that began near the end of the Renaissance and ended in the 1700&#39;s.<br />
                         Both the internal and external facets of Western Civilization were born on the Italian peninsula, whether one looks at the history of the Christian faith, civil institutions (like the Senate), modern astrology, philosophy, law,
                        art, science, nuclear energy or social customs and culture.<br />
                        Italy did not exist as a political state until its unification in 1861. Due to this comparatively late unification, and the historical autonomy of the regions that comprise the Italian peninsula, many traditions and customs that
                        are now recognized as distinctly Italian can be identified by their regions of origin.<br />
                         Italy&#39;s contributions to the cultural and historical heritage of Europe remain immense. Italy&#39;s global and international impact in politics, history, <a href="art.php">art</a>, culture, philosophy, literature,
                        archaeology, science, opera, cuisine, architecture, fashion, education, religion, <a href="cinema.php">cinema</a>, entertainment and music remain vast up to this day.<br />
                         Elements which are famous of the Italian culture are its opera and <a href="music.php">music</a>, its iconic <a href="cuisine.php">gastronomy and food</a>, which is commonly regarded amongst the most popular in the world, its
                        cinema, its collections of priceless works of art, and its fashion.<br />
                         Italy is home to the greatest number of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_World_Heritage_Sites_in_Europe#Italy" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">UNESCO World Heritage Sites</a> (45) to date.<br />
                         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Italy" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Read more about Culture in Italy at wikipedia.org</a> <br />

                         <h2>Italy Education System</h2><br />
                         Education in Italy is free and compulsory from 6-16 years of age, and is divided into five stages: kindergarten (scuola materna), elementary school (scuola elementare), middle school (scuola media), high school (liceo) and
                        university (universit&agrave;).<br />
                         Italy has a high public education standard, beating that of the UK and Germany. Italy has both public and private education systems.<br />
                         Italy also boasts a large selection of universities, with the University of Bologna (founded in ca. 1088) being the oldest in the Western World, and La Sapienza University in Rome being the biggest in Italy.<br />
