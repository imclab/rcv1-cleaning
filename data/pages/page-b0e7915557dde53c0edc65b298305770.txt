<h3 class="storytitle" id="post-42">
			<a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/2008/08/13/installing-windshield-wipers/" title="Permalink to Installing Windshield&nbsp;Wipers" rel="bookmark">Installing Windshield&nbsp;Wipers</a>
		<span class="date">August 13, 2008</span></h3>
<div class="meta">Filed under: <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/category/uncategorized/" title="View all posts in Uncategorized" rel="category tag">Uncategorized</a> &#8212; SnotNosed @ 10:23 pm <br /> </div><!-- end META -->

<div class="storycontent">
<p>Frequently, windshield wipers are the most unnoticed when you are regularly examining your <a href="http://www.autopartsplace.com">auto parts</a>. Unless you realize it is broken, you won’t normally give it attention.<br />
<a href="http://autopartstumble.files.wordpress.com/2008/08/wiper-blade-with-bent-flexorjpgsized.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-43" src="http://autopartstumble.files.wordpress.com/2008/08/wiper-blade-with-bent-flexorjpgsized.jpg?w=300&#038;h=225" alt="" width="300" height="225" /></a><br />
Now, if your wipers don’t operate anymore, you may have to replace them. Here are simple procedures on how to install new windshield wipers:</p>
<p>• First, drop by your auto supply shop to look for the right size. It may vary on the make and model of your car.</p>
<p>• When you have purchased the appropriate wiper size, prepare your materials.</p>
<p>• Begin by taking up the wiper arm up in order for you to with it. You may switch on your car for a few minutes to switch on the wipers and put them in the appropriate position.</p>
<p>• Let go of the blade by pinching down on the wiper.</p>
<p>• Install your new wipers as stated in the manufacturer’s instruction. Lock all the hardware in place and be certain to test them prior to driving.</p>

<div class="meta">Filed under: <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/category/dangerous-vehicle/" title="View all posts in dangerous vehicle" rel="category tag">dangerous vehicle</a>,<a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/category/ford/" title="View all posts in Ford" rel="category tag">Ford</a>,<a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/category/mazda/" title="View all posts in Mazda" rel="category tag">Mazda</a> &#8212; SnotNosed @ 1:23 pm <br /> Tags: <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/ford/" rel="tag">Ford</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/ford-ranger/" rel="tag">Ford Ranger</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/four-wheel-drive/" rel="tag">Four-Wheel drive</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/mazda/" rel="tag">Mazda</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/mazda-b-series/" rel="tag">Mazda B-Series</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/pickup/" rel="tag">Pickup</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/unsafe-cars/" rel="tag">unsafe cars</a><br /></div><!-- end META -->

<div class="meta">Filed under: <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/category/dangerous-vehicle/" title="View all posts in dangerous vehicle" rel="category tag">dangerous vehicle</a>,<a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/category/ford/" title="View all posts in Ford" rel="category tag">Ford</a>,<a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/category/mercury/" title="View all posts in Mercury" rel="category tag">Mercury</a> &#8212; SnotNosed @ 5:01 am <br /> Tags: <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/ford/" rel="tag">Ford</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/ford-escape/" rel="tag">Ford Escape</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/four-door-suv/" rel="tag">Four-Door SUV</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/mercury/" rel="tag">Mercury</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/mercury-mariner/" rel="tag">Mercury Mariner</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/unsafe-cars/" rel="tag">unsafe cars</a><br /></div><!-- end META -->

<div class="storycontent">
<p align="justify">  <img src="http://autopartstumble.files.wordpress.com/2008/02/08fordescape_03.jpg?w=224&#038;h=227" alt="08fordescape_03.jpg" height="227" width="224" />         <img src="http://autopartstumble.files.wordpress.com/2008/02/0377303-lg.jpg?w=334&#038;h=227" alt="0377303-lg.jpg" height="227" width="334" /></p>
<p align="justify">Ford&#8217;s small SUV, the Escape, and its superficially diverse sibling, the Mariner, were given a general assessment of &#8220;Poor&#8221; (the lowest probable rating) from the IIHS for lacking driver torso- and head-safeguard in the Institutes’ side-impact collide test (exclusive of the optional side airbag package). Waiting for the &#8217;08 would be a great move, as front side airbags, side curtain bags and solidity control will all at last be standard.</p>

		<span class="date">February 27, 2008</span></h3>
<div class="meta">Filed under: <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/category/dangerous-vehicle/" title="View all posts in dangerous vehicle" rel="category tag">dangerous vehicle</a>,<a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/category/nissan/" title="View all posts in Nissan" rel="category tag">Nissan</a> &#8212; SnotNosed @ 4:36 am <br /> Tags: <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/extended-cab-pickup/" rel="tag">extended-cab pickup</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/nissan/" rel="tag">Nissan</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/nissan-frontier/" rel="tag">Nissan Frontier</a>, <a href="http://autopartstumble.wordpress.com/tag/unsafe-cars/" rel="tag">unsafe cars</a><br /></div><!-- end META -->

<div class="storycontent">
<p align="justify">Stability control is optional on the Frontier, and recommended considering its three-star NHTSA rollover rating. The King Cab Frontier has surprisingly low three-star NHTSA frontal crash-test ratings (most other pickups do quite well in the frontal tests), though a four-door Crew Cab Frontier was given a better four-star frontal rating. The Frontier was also rated &#8220;Poor&#8221; (out of good, acceptable, marginal or poor) by the IIHS in rear impact. Both Frontiers tested didn&#8217;t have side airbags or side curtain bags, which are also optional.</p>
