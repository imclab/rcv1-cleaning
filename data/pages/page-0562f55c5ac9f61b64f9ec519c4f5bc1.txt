dangerously close to awesome</a><br />Perhaps  you&rsquo;ve seen the conversions that allow you to replace the wheels and  tires on your car with tracks, so that you have a tank like set up on  each wheel. &nbsp;It works well in areas that are very muddy or rugged  terrain. &nbsp;Set ups like these are very popular in areas with large  surface mining operations or in Ski resort areas.<br /><br />The  issue with these setups is that while not permanant, it&rsquo;s not very easy  to switch out if you wanted to go back to a standard setup. &nbsp;With this  new innovation from AD Boivin, that may all end. &nbsp;Now rather than having  to take off the wheels and tires and bolt tracks to the hubs, you  simply drive onto the tracks, and the wheels drive the tracks.<br /><br />While  still in the prototype stage, AD Boivin is looking to move them into  production quickly in which time they will be made available for the  full size trucks from the big 2.5.<br /><br /><a href="http://delicious.com/redirect?url=http%3A//hooniverse.com/2012/04/25/allow-us-to-present-the-best-craigslist-ad-of-all-time/">Greatest Craigslist ad of all time</a><br /><br /><a href="http://delicious.com/redirect?url=http%3A//jalopnik.com/5904713/yes-thats-a-mig-fighter-jet-driving-down-a-busy-street">Yes, That's A MiG Fighter Jet Driving Down A Busy Street</a><br />Cars  are for roads, boats do the water thing and planes rule the sky, it&rsquo;s  just the way God intended. &nbsp;But this next article is something you DO  NOT see everyday, and we found it on Jalopnik. &nbsp;To promote last year&rsquo;s  Slovak International Airshow . . . in . . . Slovakia, event planners  parked a Russian MiG-29 fighter at a local mall. &nbsp;That would get my  attention. &nbsp;But you simply cannot land a high-performance jet-powered  fighter in front of a Macy&rsquo;s, especially one that&rsquo;s been decommissioned.  &nbsp;So how do you get an un-flyable aircraft into an urban area? &nbsp;Well,  you tow it. &nbsp;And that&rsquo;s just what they did. &nbsp;The photo accompanying the  article makes it look like the plane is taxiing through downtown  Bratislava. &nbsp;The tow vehicle is invisible from the angle the picture was  taken. &nbsp;Check it all out at RoundAboutShow.com.<br /><br /><a href="http://delicious.com/redirect?url=http%3A//www.gizmag.com/cricket-trailer/22006/">Cricket Trailer: "The covered wagon for the new frontier"</a><br />There  is a certain segment of the population that likes to get away from it  all, be it families, outdoor adventurer types, or people who just like  to explore, having an RV, or popup trailer is nice to have so that you  can rest where you want to. &nbsp;With the downsizing of America&rsquo;s car fleet,  RV makers are starting to look at smaller and lighter solutions that  can be towed behind smaller cars and crossovers.<br /><br />The  Cricket was designed by a former NASA engineer who designed living  modules for the International Space Station! &nbsp;What he came up with is a  &nbsp;simple, lightweight, flexible outdoor living space that only weighs  1,300 pounds (just under 600 kilos). &nbsp;It can sleep two adutls in comfort<br /><br /><a href="http://delicious.com/redirect?url=http%3A//carscoop.blogspot.com/2012/04/ford-explorer-backs-into-brand-new-hand.html%3Futm_source%3Dfeedburner%26utm_medium%3Dfeed%26utm_campaign%3DFeed%253A%2BCarscoop%2B%2528CARSCOOP%2529">Ford Explorer Backs Into Brand New, Hand-Built Sports Car</a><br /><br /><a href="http://delicious.com/redirect?url=http%3A//carscoop.blogspot.com/2012/04/ford-of-spain-comes-up-with-aroma-that.html%3Futm_source%3Dfeedburner%26utm_medium%3Dfeed%26utm_campaign%3DFeed%253A%2BCarscoop%2B%2528CARSCOOP%2529">Ford of Spain Comes up with an Aroma that Supposedly Makes Used Cars Smell Like New Cars</a><br />The  alluring aroma of a new car. &nbsp;It&rsquo;s an olfactory cocktail of volatile  organic compounds and other aromatic carcinogens. &nbsp;But as time marches  on these pleasant odors starts to fade; stanky fast-food wrappers,  mildew in the carpet padding and jockstrap from the gym bag out back  start to take control, and the overall aroma becomes decidedly second  hand. &nbsp;But Ford is taking this issue seriously, after all, used-car  buyers deserve the same fragrance experiences new-vehicle owners enjoy,  right? &nbsp;Right! &nbsp;The company&rsquo;s operation in Spain has concocted a unique  way of making pre-owned vehicles smell like their factory-fresh  brethren. &nbsp;They&rsquo;ve created a perfume of sorts and it&rsquo;s playing a central  part in a new marketing campaign for its certified-used program.  &nbsp;Clever! &nbsp;Check out the video, which, curiously, is in English.<br /><br />It  looks like it would smell great, but I just can&rsquo;t put my finger on why I  wouldn&rsquo;t want this. &nbsp;The idea leaves a bad taste in my mouth. &nbsp;Does  that sound unreasonable to you?</p>
