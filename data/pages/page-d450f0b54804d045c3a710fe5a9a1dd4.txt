          <h1 align="center">Sell Car Without Title</h1>
        <br />
<hr>
<table width="100%"  border="0">
  <tr>
    <td width="46"><a href="https://plus.google.com/113480827957978564105?rel=author" target="_blank"><img src="http://www.quickcashauto.com/images/anna_finger.jpg" alt="author" border="0" align="absmiddle" /></a></td>
    <td width="169"><em>By <a href="https://plus.google.com/113480827957978564105?rel=author" target="_blank">Anna Finger</a><br />
        </em><small class="dummy">Find me on Google+</small></td>
		<td width="421" class="dummy"><em>Find out how our service keeps you from  answering emails and taking  calls to sell your car. We <a href="http://www.quickcashauto.com/Contact-Quick-Cash-Auto.html">eliminate the aggravation</a> caused by selling privately.</em></td>
  </tr>
</table>
<hr>
<table width="40%"  border="0" align="right">
	  <tr>
	    <td><img src="images/Car-Without-Title.jpg" alt="Car Without Title" width="256" height="172" border="1" /></td>
      </tr>
</table>   <p><span class="style1">Depending on the laws in your neighborhood, you may or may not be able to <a href="http://www.quickcashauto.com/Selling-Your-Car-Articles/Sell-Your-Car-Today.html" title="Sell Your Car: Today!">sell your car</a> without a title. Before you try to sell your car without a title, you 
should talk to your local DMV to see if it is possible, or even legal. Even if it is legal to sell your car without a title, most buyers are usually very hard 
up to purchase them. </span></p>
<p><span class="style1">A car's title proves who owns the vehicle. Most car titles contain the same basic information: owner's name and address, make, model, 
  color and <a href="http://www.quickcashauto.com/Car-Business-Articles/Save-Gas-With-ScanGauge.html" title="Save Gas With ScanGauge">mileage</a> at time of purchase. If you try to register a car without a title, it may be a problem because the DMV then has no way of knowing if you 
  are the true owner.</span></p>
<table width="40%"  border="0" align="right">
	  <tr>
	    <td><a href="http://www.quickcashauto.com/Quick-Cash-Quote.html"><img src="../images/banners/ad-300x250.jpg" alt="Free Quote: Cash For Your Vehicle" width="300" height="250" border="0" align="right" /></a></td>
      </tr>
</table>   <p><span class="style1">Most cars that are sold without titles are because the title is in the possession of a third party, due to loans. In these cases, the seller should either pay 
off the balance of the loan before selling the vehicle, or arrange for the car to be sold while the lien holder transfers the <a href="http://www.quickcashauto.com/Selling-Your-Car-Articles/Sell-Car-Without-Title.html" title="Selling Your Car Without A Title">title</a> to the new owner. </span></p>
<p><span class="style1">Some just 
  lose the title to the vehicle they are attempting to sell. Obtaining a replacement isn't hard, but there may be some cases where that is not an option. If this 
  becomes the case, you must either fill out a "lost title" form at your local DMV office, or go through a title service. </span></p>
<p><span class="style1">These normally work by providing you 
  (for a fee) with a <a href="http://www.quickcashauto.com/documents/Bill_of_Sale_ Template_Automotive.pdf" target="_blank">bill of sale</a> for your vehicle, which you then take to the DMV and obtain a title. Before using a title service, check the laws in your state. </span></p>
