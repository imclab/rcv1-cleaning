					<h1 class="heading">Certified pre-owned vehicle buyers may get free trial of Sirius XM satellite radio</h1>

					<div class="entry-meta">
						<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://www.autoliquidator.com/news/60578/certified-pre-owned-vehicle-buyers-may-get-free-trial-of-sirius-xm-satellite-radio/" title="9:58 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">March 12, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://www.autoliquidator.com/news/author/admin/" title="View all posts by Auto Specialist">Auto Specialist</a></span>					</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
                    
                    <div class="divider"></div>


					<div class="entry-content">
						<p>Consumers buying a used car may soon be able to take advantage of a free trial allowing them to use Sirius XM satellite radio in their vehicles for a limited time, reports Seeking Alpha.</p>
<p>According to the source, approximately 32 million vehicles currently in operation are equipped with a satellite radio. Moreover, more than 60 percent of all new cars being created come standard with this technology, and that figure rises with each passing year.</p>
<p>&nbsp;Because close to half of all people who buy cars with a satellite radio become paying customers, Sirius is creating a new program that would allow used car buyers to try out XM radio before they purchase the service.</p>
<p>The company has signed agreements with several major car manufacturers &#8211; including <a href="http://www.autoliquidator.com/newcars/volvo" class="dnautolink">Volvo</a>, <a href="http://www.autoliquidator.com/newcars/porsche" class="dnautolink">Porsche</a>, <a href="http://www.autoliquidator.com/newcars/chrysler" class="dnautolink">Chrysler</a>, General Motors, <a href="http://www.autoliquidator.com/newcars/ford" class="dnautolink">Ford</a>, <a href="http://www.autoliquidator.com/newcars/nissan" class="dnautolink">Nissan</a> and <a href="http://www.autoliquidator.com/newcars/bmw" class="dnautolink">BMW</a> &#8211; that would allow people who buy a certified pre-owned vehicle to enjoy a free satellite radio trial.</p>
<p>The source states that it remains unclear how the free trial would operate. One suggested possibility is that the certified pre-owned car dealers may pay Sirius for a short period of service, while another is that the company simply provides satellite radio for free.</p>
