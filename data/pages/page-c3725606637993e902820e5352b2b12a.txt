            <div class="module_intro" itemprop="description">If you're a caring parent who wants your child to be safe in the car, you're probably searching for <b>the safest convertible car seat in 2013</b>. Since you're interested in the safety of your child, I would like to share with you a few pieces of information related to car seat safety.<br />
<br />
The most important thing I learned while doing research about safety of car seats is that there probably isn't a good way (at least for now) to measure safety of car seats. <b>If a car seat meets the Government's requirements, it can be sold on the market.</b> The thing is - the car seat can either pass the test or fail, there aren't any grades or ratings. Since car seats are fairly inexpensive (compared to e.g. cars), there aren't any independent agencies that <b>specialize</b> in testing safety of car seats.<br />
<br />
<b>if you'd like to immediately go to the list of best convertible car seats, <a rel="nofollow"  href="#module161842395">click here</a>.</b><br />
<br />
There are some organizations (like Consumer Reports) that do their own tests, but often their methods are questionable and it's really difficult to say whether their results are of value or they don't present any value at all. If you'd like to know why is that, check out Car-Seat.org forum for further explanations, <a rel="nofollow"  href="http://www.car-seat.org/showthread.php?t=22509" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">this</a> and <a rel="nofollow"  href="http://www.car-seat.org/showthread.php?t=14959" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">this</a> topics in specific.<br />
<br />
<b>How to find the safest convertible car seat?</b><br />
To answer this question I would like to quote one of the users of car-seat.org forum: &quot;<i>In the end, the safest car seat is one that fits your vehicle, fits your child and will be used correctly each and every trip.(..) Not every car seat is compatible with every vehicle, and some seats are outgrown by a child long before they're ready for the next step. A child will be safer in a 5pt harness at the age of 6 - even one without EPS/EPP foam and no side impact protection, than they would be in a booster that had both.</i>&quot;.<br />
<br />
So, to find the safest convertible car seat you should start by looking for car seats that meet your and your child's requirements. You can always look for some extra safety features as well. When searching for a certain model, I suggest <b>you should consider only the most known (and quality) brands</b> and choose a model that is already used by thousands of people . Why? If it had any safety issues, they'd be spotted by other parents and already fixed, so you don't have to worry that the model you've chosen isn't safe for your child.<br />
