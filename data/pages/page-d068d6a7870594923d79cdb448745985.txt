	<h4 id="IPDescriptionTitle">
		About the <span id="IPDescriptionTitleItemName">Clek Foonf Convertible Car Seat</span>
	</h4>
	<div id="IPDescription" itemprop="description">
		<p><em><strong></strong></em>We take car seats seriously, as should you: a good car seat, correctly installed, is essential for safe travel for kids. And Clek, the makers of the Olli and Oobr boosters, are absolutely obsessed with safety, as demonstrated by the engineering, research, and top-of-the-line materials used in their previous creations.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>But all car seats are designed for safety &ndash; so what really makes Clek stand out? Clek&rsquo;s answer to this dilemma: create seats with a look that really pops, and names that are impossible to forget. The <strong>Clek Foonf Convertible Car Seat</strong> has the same sleek, modern look as its sister seats, and that unusual name, which is derived from the German word for &ldquo;five.&rdquo; Safety and style: Foonf has it all.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Foonf is Clek&rsquo;s first convertible car seat, but it shares many features with the other Clek seats. The light but strong magnesium frame and the easy-to-install rigid LATCH system (forward-facing) are classic Clek. For rear-facing installation, the Foonf will use flexible LATCH connectors and a snap-in anti-rebound bar. Both rear- and forward-facing installation have built-in lockoffs, and a patented energy absorption system is designed to slow down the seat in a crash.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>And then there&rsquo;s the surprisingly narrow profile of the Foonf. We don&rsquo;t know how they did it, but Clek managed to create a seat that&rsquo;s one inch narrower on the outside than the Diono Radian, one of our narrowest seats, but one inch wider on the inside than the Britax Advocate. All we know is: a convertible car-seat that can fit three in a row in your backseat is a rare and beautiful thing.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Foonf is designed to be the next step after your infant car seat, with the recommendations for use starting at six months: install it rear-facing for kids weighing 15-50 pounds, 25-43 inches in height; forward-facing for kids weighing 20-65 pounds, 25-49 inches in height.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>width: 17" at widest point, 13" at base. Seat depth: 12.5". Seat height: 22.5" - 26.5".</strong></p>	</div>
