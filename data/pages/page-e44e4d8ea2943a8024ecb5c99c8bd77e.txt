				<h4 class="meta">June 30th, 2010 by <span class="author"><a class="url vcard fn" href="">Stu</a></span><br /></h4>

					<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://manonthemove.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/Designing-Women-The-Intersection-of-Art-Culture-and-Car-Design-The-Museum-of-the-City-of-New-York-103rd-Fifth-Avenue-NYC.jpg" rel="lightbox[8499]"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-8500" title="Designing Women: The Intersection of Art, Culture and Car Design - The Museum of the City of New York" src="http://manonthemove.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/Designing-Women-The-Intersection-of-Art-Culture-and-Car-Design-The-Museum-of-the-City-of-New-York-103rd-Fifth-Avenue-NYC.jpg" alt="" width="900" height="731" /></a></p>
<p>The <a href="http://manonthemove.com/2010/03/26/cars-culture-and-the-city-fifth-avenue-103rd-street-nyc/" target="_blank">Museum of the City of New York</a>, in conjunction with GM, hosted a tribute to the women of automotive design on Monday night. The event featured lots of documentary-style interview clips, politically incorrect GM promotional videos from the day and, the best part, live digital sketches done on the big screen by Cadillac&#8217;s Christine Park (see design progression after the jump). You might be familiar with <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mz3F86eRiSs" target="_blank">Christine&#8217;s exploits</a> on Cadillac XTS, the dashing concept that precedes the brand&#8217;s next generation flagship. Pictured here, GM&#8217;s famous &#8220;Damsels of Design&#8221; posed with none other than Harley Earl, GM&#8217;s first VP of Design. Earl created the need for an Art and Color Division at GM and brought us the industry&#8217;s first concept car, the <a href="http://www.chuckstoyland.com/potpourri/conceptcars/generalmotors/BUICK%20Concept/1%2038%20BUICK%20Y-Job.jpg" target="_blank" rel="lightbox[8499]">Buick Y-Job</a>. Wise beyond his years, Earl not only ushered in a sense of style and personalization to mass produced cars but hand picked a team of female designers to <a href="http://www.carofthecentury.com/damsels_of_design_with_harley_earl.htm" target="_blank">impress the papers</a> and present a unique perspective to the design process Vintage GM video and more glorious archive pics after the jump&#8230;</p>
