    <h2>
	Libraries &amp; Museums</h2>
<p>
	The University of Texas at Austin’s library system is the nation’s fifth-largest academic library and is one of the top 10 research libraries in the nation.</p>
<p>
	<img alt="Student browsing the book stacks at the Fine Arts Library" class="image-top-float-right" src="/sites/default/files/images/Fine-Arts-Library_Students-2006-by-CSM_0072.jpg" />From the <a href="http://www.lib.utexas.edu/lsl/">Life Science Library</a> to the <a href="http://www.lib.utexas.edu/pcl/">Perry-Castañeda Library</a>, the public can access more than nine million volumes within the 17 libraries on campus.</p>
<p>
	The <a href="http://www.cah.utexas.edu/">Dolph Briscoe Center for American History</a> houses thousands of books, manuscripts, newspapers, photos and maps relating to various aspects of America’s past, including historical collections on Congress and the media and entertainment industries.</p>
<p>
	Numerous special collections can be found within the campus libraries, including the <a href="http://www.lib.utexas.edu/benson/">Nettie Lee Benson Latin American Collection</a>, the <a href="http://www.lib.utexas.edu/apl/aaa/">Alexander Architectural Archive</a> and the <a href="http://www.lib.utexas.edu/admin/cird/collections/stephen.html">Ruth Stephan Poetry Collection</a>.</p>
<p>
	The <a href="http://www.hrc.utexas.edu/">Harry Ransom Center</a> holdings include the <a href="http://www.hrc.utexas.edu/exhibitions/permanent/wfp/">world’s first photograph</a>, the papers of Pulitzer Prize-winning author Norman Mailer and the Watergate papers of Washington Post reporters Bob Woodward and Carl Bernstein.</p>
<p>
	The <a href="http://www.lbjlibrary.org/">LBJ Library and Museum</a> houses more than 40 million documents relating to the career of President Lyndon B. Johnson.</p>
<p>
	The <a href="http://www.utexas.edu/tmm/">Texas Natural Science Center</a> conducts research, holds more than 5.7 million specimens in its collections and promotes public awareness of biological diversity through its exhibits, labs and outreach programs.</p>
<p>
	The <a href="http://blantonmuseum.org/">Blanton Museum of Art</a> contains more than 17,000 works, including Old Master paintings, modern and contemporary American and Latin American Art, and a broad collection of prints and drawings.</p>
<h2>
	Exhibition &amp; Performance Spaces</h2>
<p>
	The <a href="http://utvac.org/">Visual Arts Center</a> is a vibrant collaboration and exhibition space created by the College of Fine Arts' Department of Art and Art History. Highlighting internationally acclaimed student and faculty research, the center is a magnet of research and exhibition, drawing students, scholars and the public together to share and explore the visual arts.</p>
<p>
	The <a href="http://landmarks.utexas.edu/">Landmarks Public Art Program</a> gets you up close and personal with contemporary and modern art sculptures placed throughout campus. The program brings the finest works of public art to the main campus to support the university as a leading research institution, enhance its aesthetic character and provide a source of civic pride and welfare.</p>
<p>
	The <a href="http://www.finearts.utexas.edu/aah/galleries/att_eec_gallery.cfm">Courtyard Gallery</a> at the AT&amp;T Executive Education and Conference Center is curated by Jade Walker and presents a wide range of artwork produced by faculty and alumni from the Department of Art and Art History in the College of Fine Arts.</p>
<p>
	With seating for 2,900 people, <a href="http://www.texasperformingarts.org/venues/bass_concert_hall">Bass Concert Hall</a>, the flagship theatre of Texas Performing Arts, is the largest in Austin. The space boasts a vast stage, an orchestra pit capable of holding 100 musicians, dressing rooms to accommodate more than 100 performers, computerized lighting, advanced sound and rigging systems, and a mammoth backstage area.</p>
<p>
	The <a href="http://www.texasperformingarts.org/venues/brockett">Brockett Theatre</a> is an intimate setting of 244 seats. Used most frequently for student productions of the Department of Theatre and Dance, this theatre helps develop budding new talent into experienced performers as part of Texas Performing Arts' educational mission.</p>
<p>
	Specially designed for its excellent acoustics, <a href="http://www.texasperformingarts.org/venues/bates">Bates Recital Hall</a> is a 700-seat venue that is capable of hosting everything from symphony orchestras, major choral presentations and concert bands to ensembles and solo recitals. The hall also houses the Visser-Rowland organ, the country's largest tracker organ. The space serves performances by professional visiting musicians and recitals by UT students and faculty members.</p>
