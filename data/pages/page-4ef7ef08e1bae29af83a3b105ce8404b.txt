		    <table border="0" cellspacing="5">
			    <tr>
				    <td><strong>Date:</strong> <span id="ctl00_EngineContent_lblDate">26 May 2009</span></td>
				    <td style="padding: 0 0 0 20px;"><strong>Author Type:</strong> <span id="ctl00_EngineContent_lblAuthorType">Event PR</span></td>
			    </tr>
			    <tr>
				    <td colspan="2"><strong>Author:</strong> <span id="ctl00_EngineContent_lblAuthor">Castrol Extreme Auto Show</span></td>

	    <p align="justify"><font face="Tahoma" size="2">On Saturday, June 13 to Tuesday June 16, 2009, the Castrol Extreme Auto Show, in association with BP Ultimate and Dunlop, will be held at the Coca Cola Dome in North Riding, north of Johannesburg. The Show will open daily from 9am to 6pm, and admission prices are R60-00 for adults and R30&nbsp; for children under 12 and pensioners. Tickets are available at the door, or through New Metro outlets in the Gauteng Area.<br /></font><strong><div align="justify"><font face="Tahoma" size="2">&ldquo;It&rsquo;s an Art Form&rdquo;</font></div><p align="justify"><br /><br /><font face="Tahoma" size="2">For the 2009 Castrol Extreme Auto Show, the sixth show celebrating South Africa&rsquo;s burgeoning specialist car culture since it was first held in 2004, the theme of the show will be an impassioned celebration of personalised motorcars and motorcycles, and the people who worship them<br /><br />&ldquo;You need to view this phenomenon as an art form, &ldquo;says show organiser Graeme Ayerst, of AEC Exhibitions.<br /><br />&ldquo;The people that build these machines see the motorcar or motorbike as a canvass, a means through which they can express themselves. And believe me, these people ARE artists, as anyone who has visited one of our previous shows will verify. The amount of ingenuity and skill, not to mention the imagination and pre-planning that goes into these creations, is staggering.&rdquo;<br /><br />A secret of the Castrol Extreme Auto Show&rsquo;s success to date &ndash; it is far and away the biggest custom car show in the country &ndash;is that each ensuing year it is not only bigger, but different from the preceding year&rsquo;s show.<br /><br />&ldquo;This year the pivotal show-piece, if I can put it like that, will be the daily auction of specialist motor vehicles conducted by the world famous Bernie the Bolt and Michael Naicker, in my opinion the funniest guy in South Africa. The value of cars and bikes on auction will run into millions in total, and everything must be sold,&rdquo; states Ayerst.<br /><br />Bernie the Bolt, aka Bernie Fineman, is the star of London Shop Chop, the BBC television show broadcast world-wide to over 70-million people, and viewed locally on DSTV&rsquo;s Discovery Channel. <br /><br />Michael Naiker, the world&rsquo;s only white Indian,&nbsp; is a character that&nbsp; is in fact wholly a product of the imagination of South African stand-up comic Kevin Perkins. Bernie and Kevin will also be doing roving &ldquo;auction giveaways&rdquo; where show-goers can win all sorts of motoring-related prizes, from alloy wheels and sound systems to car-care kits.<br /><br />As for the cars themselves, this year there will 280 prime custom vehicles on show, including muscle cars, street rods,&nbsp; original-look classics, custom hot-hatches and custom motorcycles and choppers. <br /><br />The Castrol Extreme Auto Show has become well known for it all-action side shows, and the 2009 visitors will not be disappointed.<br />Star attractions throughout the weekend will include<br /></font><ul><li><div align="justify"><font face="Tahoma" size="2">A makeover of an awesome new VW Scirocco conducted at the show under the guidance of Bernie the Bolt and carried out by Big Boss Auto of Alberton </font></div></li><li><div align="justify"><font face="Tahoma" size="2">Drifting exhibitions involving two cars running at the same time </font></div></li><li><div align="justify"><font face="Tahoma" size="2">Spinning by the Gauteng Spinners, where driver&rsquo;s exit their cars while the machine is doing crazy wheel-spinning donuts </font></div></li><li><div align="justify"><font face="Tahoma" size="2">Freestyle motocross high-flying jinks </font></div></li><li><div align="justify"><font face="Tahoma" size="2">Trips around a parking lot course in a Ferrari, courtesy of Forza Racing </font></div></li><li><div align="justify"><font face="Tahoma" size="2">Massages and facials for the lady visitors, courtesy of Mangwanani Spa </font></div></li><li><div align="justify"><font face="Tahoma" size="2">Body-painting, done on show-goers by a nationally-famous airbrush wizard </font></div></li><li><div align="justify"><font face="Tahoma" size="2">Kiddies activities, including bungi- trampolining and face-painting</font></div></li><li><div align="justify"><font face="Tahoma" size="2">Visitors will be able to vote on the The Custom Bike of the Year </font></div></li><li><div align="justify"><font face="Tahoma" size="2">And countless competitions at the show, where <strong>no less than four custom cars can be won by 2009 show-goers! </strong></font></div></li></ul><p align="justify"><br /><br /><font face="Tahoma" size="2">The response to this year&rsquo;s show has been phenomenal,&rdquo; says Graeme Ayerst, &ldquo;to the point where I&rsquo;ve had exhibitors phoning me and begging to be on the show. With 280 vehicles it&rsquo;s going to be bigger and better than ever, and to me it&rsquo;s proof that when the going gets tough, as we&rsquo;ve experienced in the economy in the early part of 2009, it&rsquo;s the tough guys, the ones with the passion, that really get going.&rdquo; </font></p></p></strong></p><!-- Parsing Time: 0ms-->
