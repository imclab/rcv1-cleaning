<h1 class="headline">Toilet Humor</h1>
<h2 class="subHeadline">Dingleberries</h2>
<div id="byline"><p>by Jonathan Padget<br />Published on <a href="/archive/?ad=12/05/2002">December 5, 2002</a>, 12:00am | <a href="#commentInstructions">Comments</a>
</p>
</div><div id="content"><!-- HEADLINE SUB_HEADLINE BYLINE SIDEBAR -->

<!-- BEGIN CODE FOR PICTURE 1 -->
<table align="right" width="250" cellpadding="15" cellspacing="0" border="0"><tr valign="top" align="center"><td>
<img src="/articles/attachments/2002-12-05_arts_and_entertainment_228_213.jpg" border="1"><br />
<strong>Girls Just Wanna Have Fun: LaForce, Murray and Painter</strong> <br /><font size="-1">(Photo by Ian Armstrong )</font>
</td></tr></table>
<!-- END CODE FOR PICTURE 1 --> 


<p>There's a lot to be said for taking a taboo subject and
examining it exhaustively. In the case of <strong>Dingleberries</strong>,
the uneasy topic is the bathroom and the bodily functions -- mostly of the
bowel-related variety -- carried out there. By the time Cherry Red Productions
tears through fourteen short toilet-themed plays, doggone it if you don't feel
like talking about shit is an easier thing to do. </p>
<p>At the very least, using the bathroom is one of the most
fundamental, universal human experiences there is, so why not put it out there
as theatrical fodder and see what happens? The writers represented in <em>Dingleberries</em> have certainly come
through with consistently amusing, and some downright wickedly funny, takes on
what we do in the loo, and a game cast, some quite talented, tackles the
scenarios with abandon. </p>
<p>Highlights include Andrea Thorne's <em>Worm Girl</em>, featuring Glee Murray in a bizarrely hysterical
personification of an ultimate pariah in the high school girls room pecking
order. For sheer linguistic skill, give props to Erica Hoffman, whose <em>The Rectum, by Edgar Allan Poo</em>
(performed with dead-on deadpan by Richard Renfield) finds not a single Poe
phrase that can't be matched with a precise, prurient parallel. </p>
<p>The revulsion factor gets ratcheted up for the evening's
pinnacle of hilarity, David Andrew's <em>Kernels
of Corn, Chunks of Carrot</em>, which mercilessly skewers the scat fetishist
subculture. There's nothing so priceless as the sight of Tony Greenberg as a
sullen-faced man "dining" alone in a room full of enthusiastic
poo-eaters at a scat convention because his excrement is deemed unsuitable for
sharing. </p>
<p>Gross? Yes. But nonetheless funny, and a perfect antidote
when you find the forced cheer of the holiday season too much to bear. </p>
<p><em>Through December 28th
at Warehouse Next Door, 1017 7th Street NW. Tickets are $15. Call 202-298-9077.
Visit <a href="http://www.cherryredproductions.com" target="_blank">www.cherryredproductions.com</a></em>. </p>
