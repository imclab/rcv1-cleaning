						<p>&ldquo;I am so excited to be hosting the Oscars for the second time,&rdquo; DeGeneres said in a statement; she also had the job in 2007. &ldquo;You know what they say &mdash; the third time&rsquo;s the charm.&rdquo;</p><p>Known for her cheery demeanor and love of practical jokes, DeGeneres has a style that  sharply contrasts with MacFarlane&rsquo;s often controversial frat-boy shtick. During this spring&rsquo;s ceremony, MacFarlane described <a data-xslt="_http" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B005LAIIKI?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B005LAIIKI&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=washingtonpost-20">&ldquo;Django Unchained&rdquo;</a> as &ldquo;the story of a man fighting to get back his woman, who&rsquo;s been subjected to unspeakable violence &mdash; or, as Chris Brown and Rihanna call it, a date movie.&rdquo; He also put on an elaborate chorus number about actresses in nude scenes, titled &ldquo;We Saw Your Boobs.&rdquo;</p><p>DeGeneres also breaks the cycle of three years of headline-making host choices, starting with the unfortunate pairing in 2011 of James Franco and Anne Hathaway. The next year,  producer Brett Ratner stepped down after a fury over homophobic comments he made, with his host, Eddie Murphy, abruptly quitting, as well. </p><p>&ldquo;I think this year there really may have been a desire not to make an inflammatory choice and to let the news about the Oscars be the movies,&rdquo; said Mark Harris, author of <a data-xslt="_http" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0143115030?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=0143115030&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=washingtonpost-20">&ldquo;Pictures at a Revolution: Five Movies and the Birth of the New Hollywood,&rdquo;</a> who covers the Oscars for Grantland.com.</p><p>Plus, having a female host could make up for the thrashing that MacFarlane received for his jokes about women in Hollywood (&ldquo;Lot of beautiful women here tonight. And for those of you who gave themselves the flu two weeks ago to &lsquo;get there,&rsquo; it paid off.&rdquo;), which many derided as <a data-xslt="_http" href="http://www.newyorker.com/online/blogs/closeread/2013/02/seth-macfarlane-and-the-oscars-hostile-ugly-sexist-night.html">misogynistic</a>. </p><p>&ldquo;I would really not underestimate the degree to which the academy took seriously the backlash to MacFarlane from women last year,&rdquo; Harris said. &ldquo;It wasn&rsquo;t just bloggers or journalists, it was a number of women in the industry who publicly said, &lsquo;I have a problem with the movie year being represented by this tone.&rsquo;<span>.&thinsp;.&thinsp;.</span> [The academy] really listens to that kind of stuff.&rdquo;</p><p>The MacFarlane-led broadcast didn&rsquo;t hurt for ratings despite (or perhaps because of) the controversy: About <a data-xslt="_http" href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/tv-column/post/tv-critics-may-have-hated-the-oscars-but-40-million-viewers-tuned-in/2013/02/25/5bf6172a-7fa9-11e2-a350-49866afab584_blog.html">40&nbsp;million people</a> tuned in to that ceremony, in which <a data-xslt="_http" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00AHTYGRW?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B00AHTYGRW&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=washingtonpost-20">&ldquo;Argo&rdquo;</a> won best picture, making it the most-watched entertainment program in almost three years. When DeGeneres hosted, the same number watched <a data-xslt="_http" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000M341QE?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B000M341QE&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=washingtonpost-20">&ldquo;The Departed&rdquo;</a> take home the top prize.</p><p>Given the viewers, some don&rsquo;t think the academy, or ABC, which broadcasts the show, would deem MacFarlane a &ldquo;failure.&rdquo; But the executives are probably eager for the &ldquo;comfortable but fun&rdquo; choice of DeGeneres, said Dave Karger, chief correspondent for Fandango, because the show can easily capi&shy;tal&shy;ize on her huge following on TV and Twitter.</p><p>And the diversity factor is always crucial. &ldquo;I think the fact that you have an openly gay host this year, it&rsquo;s really keeping with what is shaping up to be a very diverse list of potential nominees this year,&rdquo; said Karger, pointing to an &ldquo;unprecedented&rdquo; number of African American potential best actor nominees, including Forest Whitaker in &ldquo;<a data-xslt="_http" href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/magazine/the-butler-movie-forest-oprah-and-me/2013/07/24/7e38d738-d386-11e2-a73e-826d299ff459_story.html">The Butler</a>&rdquo; and Michael B. Jordan in &ldquo;<a data-xslt="_http" href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/goingoutguide/movies/fruitvale-station-humanizes-a-man-behind-the-headlines/2013/07/17/587e9284-eee9-11e2-bed3-b9b6fe264871_story.html">Fruitvale Station</a>.&rdquo;</p><p>The Oscars, airing March 2 on ABC, will be produced for the second year in a row by <a data-xslt="_http" href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/tv-column/post/smash-execs-to-produce-the-academy-awards/2012/08/23/52534788-ed50-11e1-a80b-9f898562d010_blog.html">Craig Zadan and Neil Meron</a> (the minds behind NBC&rsquo;s <a data-xslt="_http" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00CWX1HKU?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B00CWX1HKU&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=washingtonpost-20">&ldquo;Smash&rdquo;</a>), who will have to brainstorm new ways to make headlines with a much safer host.</p><p>&ldquo;It&rsquo;s safe to say Ellen DeGeneres won&rsquo;t be singing, &lsquo;We Saw Your Boobs,&rsquo;&thinsp;&rdquo; Karger said.</p><p>

		var maxPhotoHeightpx = maxPhotoHeight + "px";
		var minPhotoWidthpx  = maxPhotoWidth + "px";
		var blankImageUrl = "http://img.wpdigital.net/wp-srv/images/spacer.gif";

		var results = data.results;

		//we want to only show full rows.  It was decided partial rows look like an error.
		var countResultsToShow = results.length <= itemsPerRow ? results.length : (results.length - (results.length % itemsPerRow));
		//alert("1countResultsToShow=" + countResultsToShow +', totalresults=' + results.length);

		var arrayOfHtmlDivs = new Array(countResultsToShow);

		for(var i=0; i< countResultsToShow; i++){
			var item = data.results[i];
			if(item == null){
				continue;
			}

			var paramTid = (item.url.indexOf('?') == -1) ? "?tid=video_carousel" : "&tid=video_carousel";	
			var url = item.url + paramTid + "_" + recipe;
			var headline = item.headline;
			var photo = item.photo;

			//find the photo path, but we need fallback code in case there isn't one
			var photoPath = blankImageUrl;
			if(photo != undefined && photo != null && photo.width >= maxPhotoWidth && photo.path != undefined && photo.path != null){
				photoPath = photo.path;
			}

			//construct the div, by concatenating the array
			var rgDiv=['<div class="',getClassesForDiv(i%itemsPerRow),'"><a href="',url,'" style="position:relative"><div style="position:relative"><span class="tool Video-play"></span></div><img style="min-width:',minPhotoWidthpx,';max-height:',maxPhotoHeightpx,'" width="',photoWidth+"",'" height="',photoHeight + "",'" border="0" src="',photoPath,'" alt="',headline,'" title="',headline,'" class="" style="display: inline;"></a><h3><a href="',url,'">',headline,'</a></h3></div>'];				
			arrayOfHtmlDivs[i] = rgDiv.join('');
		}

		var rgFinal = new Array();
		var iRow = 0;
		for(var i=0; i<countResultsToShow; i++){ 
			var rgRowStart = ['<div class="story',(i==0 ? ' on' : ''),'" rel="',iRow+"",'" style="',(i==0 ? '' : 'display: none; '),'position: static; visibility: visible; height: 0px; margin-left:2px">'];
			var rowStart = rgRowStart.join('');

			rgFinal.push(rowStart);
			for(var j=0; j<itemsPerRow; j++){
				if(i<data.results.length){
					rgFinal.push(arrayOfHtmlDivs[i]);
				}
				i++;
			}
			i--; 

			var rowEnd = '<div class="clear"></div></div>';
			rgFinal.push(rowEnd);

			iRow++;
		}

		var rowOfVideos = rgFinal.join('');

		var htmlToSet = getStartOfHtml() + rowOfVideos + "</div></div>";	  
		//$("#storiesConnector").html(rowOfVideos);
		var jCarousel = $("#recommended-videos-module").after(htmlToSet);
		//jCarousel.find('.carousel-control-prev').click(function(){alert("Clicked prev");});
		//$(jCarousel).find('.carousel-control-prev').click(function(){alert("Clicked prev2");});
		$('#btn-prev').click(function(){
			//alert("button prev was clicked");
            moveCarousel(findRowContainer(this), -1);
		});

		$('#btn-next').click(function(){
			//alert("button next was clicked");
            moveCarousel(findRowContainer(this), 1);
		});
	});

    function findRowContainer(clicked) {
        return $(clicked).parents('.panel.postMost').find('.stories-container');
    }

    function findCurrentRow(rowContainer) {
        return rowContainer.children('.on').first();
    }

    function findIndexLastRow(rowContainer){
        var rowCount = rowContainer.children().length;
        return rowCount == 0 ? 0 : rowCount - 1;
    }

    function moveCarousel(rowContainer, delta) {
        //moves the carousel to the next or previous row, depending on delta
        //plan:
        //  Find current Row
        //  Figure out which row-to-select
        //  if the row-to-select != current Row
		//      disable all buttons
		//      fade out current row
		//      make row-to-select the current-row
		//      fade in row-to-select
		//      enable buttons based on current-row

        //find current row
        var curRow = findCurrentRow(rowContainer);
        
        var indexLastRow = findIndexLastRow(rowContainer);

        //figure out row to select
        var indexCurRow = parseInt(curRow.attr("rel"));
        var indexRowToSelect = indexCurRow + delta;
        if(indexRowToSelect < 0) {
            indexRowToSelect = 0;
        }
        else if (indexRowToSelect > indexLastRow){
            indexRowToSelect = indexLastRow;
        }

        //only proceed if there is a possible change
        if(indexCurRow != indexRowToSelect) {
            //disable all buttons
            $('#btn-prev').addClass("deactivated");
	        $('#btn-next').addClass("deactivated");

            //fade out current row
            curRow.fadeOut(100, function(){

                curRow.hide();
                //make row-to-select the current row

               	//remove class 'on'
	            rowContainer.children('.on').removeClass('on')
	            //add class 'on'
                var rowNext = $(rowContainer.children()[indexRowToSelect]);
	            rowNext.addClass('on');
                //fade in the new row
                rowNext.fadeIn(100, function() {
                    rowNext.show();

                    //alert("index row to select=" + indexRowToSelect + ", indexLastRow=" + indexLastRow);
                    //enable buttons based on current row
                    var enablePrev = indexRowToSelect != 0;
                    var enableNext = indexRowToSelect != indexLastRow;

                    if(enablePrev) {
                        $('#btn-prev').removeClass("deactivated");
                    }
                    if(enableNext) {
	                    $('#btn-next').removeClass("deactivated");
                    }
                });
            });
        }
    }
}
