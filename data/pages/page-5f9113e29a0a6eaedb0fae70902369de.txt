                                    <h2><span>Best Answer</span> - Chosen by Voters</h2>
                              <div class="content">If it is a Manufacturer Certified Pre-Owned, such a &#039;Cadillac Certified&#039;, then it has gone through an extensive inspection process and also will have a factory extension of the the warranty, in this case it would be 6 years or 100k miles from ORIGINAL date of purchase and is good at any Cadillac dealership. Each manufacturer has different warranty guidelines, so you would have to see what they offer in regards to the warranty. Often, they also have lower interest rates than non Certified Cars.<br>
<br>
The requirements to Factory Certify also state that the vehicle must still be under the Factory Warranty before the Dealership can have it certified. This is why a used car&#039;s trade value changes drastically when its factory warranty has expired.<br>
<br>
If it is a third party &#039;Certification&#039;, then you would have to ask the sales associate the process they use to certify a vehicle as well as if the warranty is good at other dealerships. Be sure to get it in writing. There is a major difference between &quot;Factory Certified&quot; and simpy &quot;Certified&quot; I hope this helps and you can email with questions.<br>
