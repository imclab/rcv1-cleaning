<div class="article-title">Sengai: Zen and Humor</div>
<div class="meta-clear"><div class="clear"></div><div class="meta">By: <a href="http://metropolis.co.jp/author/?auth=C.B. Liddell" target="_top">C.B. Liddell</a> | Oct 14, 2010 | Issue: <a href="http://metropolis.co.jp/issue/?issue=864" target="_top">864</a> | <a href="http://metropolis.co.jp/arts/artifacts/sengai-zen-and-humor/#comments" title="Comment on Sengai: Zen and Humor"><span class="dsq-postid" rel="7977 http://metropolis.co.jp/arts/?p=7977">One Comment</span></a> | 2,212 views</div><!-- #meta-->

							<div class="entry-content">
		<div id="attachment_7976" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 410px"><img class="size-full wp-image-7976" title="864-A&amp;E-Artifacts" src="http://metropolis.co.jp/arts/files/2010/10/864-AE-Artifacts.jpg" alt="" width="400" height="296" /><p class="wp-caption-text">Idemitsu Museum of Art</p></div>
<p>Sengai (1750-1837) was the head abbot of Shofuku-ji, a Zen Buddhist temple in Kyushu, but it’s his post-retirement career for which he’s now famous. After stepping down as chief priest, Sengai took up ink painting, turning out rough, doodle-like brushworks, often with verses or proverbs. These were done at the request of local people, including children. To commemorate the 260th anniversary of the artist’s birth, the Idemitsu Museum of Art presents <strong>Sengai: Zen and Humor</strong>, featuring about 100 works. While the artist’s most famous painting “Circle, Triangle, and Square” is believed to show the three fundamental forms of the universe, most other works avoid reductionist esotericism in favor of a warm, down-to-earth humor. Sengai’s depictions of monks, animals and figures from legend exude a carefree spirit that suggests he was enjoying his retirement to the full.</p>
<p><strong>Idemitsu Museum of Arts Sengai: Zen and Humor.<br />
Ink painting, calligraphy. Until Nov 3, free (MS and under)/¥700 (HS, univ)/¥1,000 (adult). 9F Teigeki Bldg, 3-1-1 Marunouchi, Chiyoda-ku. Open Tue-Sun 10am-5pm (Fri until 7pm), closed Mon. Nearest stn: Yurakucho or Hibiya. <a href="http://www.idemitsu.co.jp/museum" target="_blank">www.idemitsu.co.jp/museum</a></strong></p>
