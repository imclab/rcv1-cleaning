</div>

<div class="element" id="element625010_303925">

    <p>Work is widely recognizable by broad audience OR has achieved national/international distinction; winner of Oscar, Tony, Emmy, Grammy award</p>
<ul>
    <li>Ruby Mercer, AB ’27, opera star<br />
    <em><span style="font-size: 10px;">See more: Ruby Mercer <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruby_Mercer">(Wikipedia)</a></span></em></li>
    <br />
    <li>Sammy Kaye, BSCE ’32, Big Band leader<br />
    <em><span style="font-size: 10px;">See more: Sammy Kaye <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sammy_Kaye">(Wikipedia)</a></span></em></li>
    <br />
    <li>Irving R. Shulman, AB ’37, author of novel version of “West Side Story” and the screenplay for “Rebel Without a Cause”</li>
    <br />
    <li>Richard O. Linke, BSJ ’41, TV Producer and personal agent to Andy Griffith, Jim Nabors, Ken Berry, Jerry van Dyke, Frankie Avalon, Bobby Vinton, Forrest Tucker and Ronny Schell</li>
    <br />
    <li>Lee Rich, BBA ’44 and HON ’82, film producer (“Rain Man,” “A Fish Called Wanda,” “Moonstruck,” “An Officer and a Gentleman”) </li>
    <br />
    <li>Herman Leonard, BFA ’47, internationally renowned photographer known for “Images of Jazz” collection<br />
    <em><span style="font-size: 10px;">See more: Herman Leonard <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herman_Leonard">(Wikipedia)</a></span></em></li>
    <br />
    <li>Bill McCutcheon, BFA ’48 and HON ’90, 1988 Tony Award winner as best featured actor in a musical for “Anything Goes”; played Wally on “Sesame Street”<br />
    <em><span style="font-size: 10px;">See more: Bill McCutcheon <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bill_McCutcheon">(Wikipedia)</a></span></em></li>
    <br />
    <li>David Hostetler, MFA ’49, sculptor known for wood and bronze female forms and emeritus professor of art at Ohio University<br />
    <em><span style="font-size: 10px;">See more: David Hostetler <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Hostetler">(Wikipedia)</a></span></em></li>
    <br />
    <li>Robert Forsythe, BFA ’50, architect who designed the Pro Football Hall of Fame in Canton</li>
    <br />
    <li>Jim Dine, BFA ’57, pop artist whose work is in the collections of the Guggenheim Museum and the Museum of Modern Art<br />
    <em><span style="font-size: 10px;">See more: Jim Dine <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jim_Dine">(Wikipedia)</a></span></em></li>
    <br />
    <li>Harvey Breverman, MFA ’60, painter and printmaker whose works are in the collections of the Museum of Modern Art, Whitney Museum and the British Museum</li>
    <br />
    <li>Ken Ehrlich, BSJ ’64, producer of more than 21 Grammy Awards telecasts and the MTV Music Awards</li>
    <br />
    <li>Mel Harris, MA ’65, PhD ’71, president and chief operating officer, Sony Pictures Entertainment</li>
    <br />
    <li>David Sackeroff, BFA ’66, production designer (“Seinfeld,” “Home Improvement”)</li>
    <br />
    <li>Linda Georgian, BSEd ’68, former Psychic Friends Network host and “psychic to the stars” whose clients have included Liza Minelli, Bob Hope and Regis Philbin, </li>
    <br />
    <li>Terrence McDonnell, BSC ’70, multiple Emmy winner as writer (“Jeopardy!”) and producer (“Win Ben Stein’s Money”)</li>
    <br />
    <li>Betty Thomas, BFA ’69, Emmy Award-winning actress (“Hill Street Blues”); movie director (“The Brady Bunch Movie,” “Dr. Dolittle,” “28 Days”)<br />
    <em><span style="font-size: 10px;">See more: Betty Thomas <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Betty_Thomas">(Wikipedia)</a> <a target="blank" href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0858525/bio">(IMDb)</a></span></em></li>
    <br />
    <li>David Hackel, BSC ’71, TV producer (“Dear John,” “Wings,” “Becker”)</li>
    <br />
    <li>Ed Lachman, BFA ’71, cinematographer (“Erin Brockovich”); Oscar nominee (“Far From Heaven”)</li>
    <br />

    <br />

    <li>Jonathan Freeman, BFA ’72, Broadway actor and the voice of Jafar in Disney’s “Aladdin”<br />
