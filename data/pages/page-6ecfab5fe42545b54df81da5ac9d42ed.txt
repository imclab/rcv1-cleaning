	<h1>
		FINE ARTS</h1>
	<p>
		The fine arts department of the Minnesota State Fair presents an annual art exhibition that is the result of a juried competition. Participation is open to all living residents of Minnesota, and each may submit one work for consideration. Only those works selected by the jurors are displayed in the Fine Arts Center during the State Fair. In 2012, a total of 2,267 pieces were submitted, and 365 works were accepted.</p>
	<ul>
		<li>
			<h4>
				<strong>State Fair Fine Arts Exhibition:</strong></h4>
			<strong>2012 People&rsquo;s Choice Award:</strong> &ldquo;Am I Your Pride and Joy&quot; by Daniel Volenec<br />
			<strong>2012 Kids&rsquo; Choice Award:</strong> &ldquo;Bless the Beasts and Children&rdquo; by Attila Ray Dabasi</li>
	</ul>
	<p>
		<br />
		<strong>Registration</strong> for the Minnesota State Fair Fine Arts Competition is <strong>required</strong>. Eligible artists must register their work. Registration must be completed online no later than 4:30 p.m. on Monday, July 1, 2013. <strong>Registration absolutely closes July 1 at 4:30 p.m.</strong><br />
		<br />
		<strong><font color="red">IMPORTANT: ENTRIES WILL NOT BE RECEIVED IF NOT REGISTERED. </font></strong></p>
	<h2>
		<strong>GENERAL INFORMATION AND RULES FOR ENTRY:</strong></h2>
	<p>
		The Fine Arts Rules and Regulations will be available for download April 1. Please be sure to read the rules and regulations document before beginning the registration process.</p>
	<p>
		<strong>JURY PROCESS: <span style="color: rgb(255, 0, 0);"><u>There are now two phases of the jury process.</u></span></strong> In Phase 1, jurors will first review submitted digital images to determine which works will be delivered for Phase 2. Phase 2 will be an in-person jury review to determine the works to be accepted for the 2013 Fine Arts Exhibition at the Minnesota State Fair. Please take care in photographing your work. The jury will consist of recognized individuals in their respective media. All artists will be notifies via email of the decisions of the jurors.</p>
	<p>
		<strong>Phase 1 submission process:</strong> We now require each entrant to submit a digital image(s) of their work. Images should be in JPEG format. We recommend the shortest dimension of the submitted image be 1000 pixels. Artists MUST submit <u>AT LEAST ONE DIGITAL IMAGE</u> of the entry. Only one image is required, but entrants are allowed up to THREE total images to present the <strong>ONE</strong> work. If images of more than one work are submitted, entrant will be disqualified.</p>
	<p style="margin-left: 40px;">
		For three dimensional art (generally classes 2, 6, 7), each image may be used to present a different view of the object in space.</p>
	<p style="margin-left: 40px;">
		For two dimensional art (generally classes 1, 3, 4, 5, 8), the first image must be a full, frontal view, while the other two may be details of the work.</p>
	<p>
		<strong>DIGITAL PROCESS:</strong> As digital technology continues to expand and influence the way many artists work, not simply photographers, we are changing our policy regarding entries that display digital process. Acceptance of these entries is expanded to all classes. Please note that this applies to the finished work itself, not the process of entering the submission. For instance, digitally photographing an oil painting for submission does not constitute &#39;digital process&#39;. However, if an oil painting includes a digitally printed photo as a collage element, that does constitute an element of &#39;digital process&#39;. During registration, for identification purposes and to insure we are best serving our participants, entrants will be asked to indicate if the submitted work includes digital process, and to describe that element.</p>
	<p>
		<strong>WEB PRESENCE:</strong> We recommend all artists establish a web presence. Participation in the Fine Arts Exhibition is an opportunity to make a lasting connection with viewers, many of whom might be interested in seeing more work. There are several options for creating a web page to display art and resume information, many are free (for example: www.mnartists.org).</p>
	<p>
		<strong>ELIGIBILITY:</strong> Original works by living residents of Minnesota executed since January 1, 2011. Work of students eligible only if executed without the help of the instructor. Works that have been accepted and shown in a previous Minnesota State Fair Fine Arts Exhibition are ineligible.</p>
	<p>
		<strong>NOTE:</strong> Students in grades K-12 who are looking to enter artwork and photographs should do so in our <a href="/competition/edu.html">education department</a> competitions. Although the fine arts competition is open to any Minnesota artist, regardless of age, it is a highly competitive contest and we strongly recommend students enter the education department competition, where their work will be judged solely against their peers.</p>
	<p>
		<strong><font color="red">PLEASE NOTE: Any work accepted in the fine arts competition listed FOR SALE by the exhibitor will be sold through the Minnesota State Fair at a 20 percent commission. </font></strong>All accepted entries will remain on exhibit during the run of the State Fair even if sold.</p>
	<h2>
		IMPORTANT DATES</h2>
	<p>
		<strong>REGISTRATION</strong> is <strong>required</strong> for all fine arts divisions and will be available beginning April 1. <strong><em>Entries will not be received if not previously registered</em></strong>. Registration must be completed online or postmarked by 4:30 p.m. on Monday, July 1. Online registration absolutely closes July 1 at 4:30 p.m.</p>
	<p>
		<strong>PHASE 1:</strong> From July 10-14, jurors will review submitted digital images to determine which works will be delivered for Phase 2. All artists will be notifies <u>via email</u> of the decisions of the jurors on July 15-16.</p>
	<p>
		<strong>PHASE 2:</strong> Entries selected for Phase 2 will be received on Saturday and Sunday, July 27 and 28 from 10 a.m.-8 p.m. All Phase 2 artists will be notified <u>via email</u> of the decisions of the jurors on July 31-Aug. 1.</p>
	<p>
		<strong>DELIVERY OF PHASE 2 ENTRIES:</strong> Received at the Fine Arts Center, corner of Randall and Cosgrove (on the State Fairgrounds) Saturday, and Sunday, July&nbsp; 27 &amp; 28, 10 a.m.&ndash;8 p.m.</p>
	<p>
		<strong>RETURN OF ENTRIES:</strong> <strong>Unaccepted work</strong> must be picked up from the Fine Arts Center Saturday or Sunday, Aug. 3 &amp; 4 from 10 a.m.&ndash;8 p.m. <strong>Accepted work</strong> must be picked up from the Fine Arts Center Tuesday or Wednesday, Sept. 3 &amp; 4, 10 a.m.&ndash;7 p.m.</p>
	<p>
		<strong>FIRST GLANCE EVENT:</strong> Tuesday, Aug. 20, 5-7 p.m. The Minnesota State Fair Foundation 501(c)(3) invites artists, friends and family to support the future of the arts at the Minnesota State Fair. This is your chance to get a first glance at the selected works and celebrate art at the fair with arts enthusiasts. Light refreshments available. Tickets will be available for purchase in July at <a href="http://www.msffoundation.org/pages/events/first_glance_fundraiser.html">www.msffoundation.org</a> or by calling (651) 288-4323.</p>
	<p>
		<strong>PREVIEW NIGHT:</strong> Tuesday, Aug. 20, 7-9:30 p.m. at the Fine Arts Center, for participating and non-participating artists and their friends.</p>
	<p>
		For further information, please <a href="mailto:competition@mnstatefair.org?Subject=Fine%20Arts%20Competition">email us</a> or call us directly at (651) 288-4417.</p>

	<h2>
		Salon 300:</h2>
