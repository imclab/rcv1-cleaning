                                  <h1>Associate of Fine Arts Degree: Art</h1>
                                                    </div>
                          <div id="node-1128" class="node node-type-degree-nontrad">
	<div class="node-inner">
						<div class="content">
							<p>The Associate of Fine Arts (AFA) degree in Art is designed for students pursuing the fine arts, but does not transfer
to other colleges and universities the way the Associate of Arts
(AA) degree does.</p>				<p>The AFA in Art is accepted by The Evergreen State College
and some other colleges with transfer agreements similar to those of the AA degree.
Students seeking a Bachelor of Arts or a Bachelor of Fine Arts should consult
the <abbr title="North Seattle Community College">NSCC</abbr> <a href="https://northseattle.edu/transfer/degrees/associate-arts-degree">AA degree requirements</a>, their art advisor and the university in which they
intend to enroll about equivalency requirements.</p>									<div class="objectives">
						<h2>Learning Outcomes</h2>
						<ul>
<li>Use
form, organization, structure and tools appropriate to the discipline</li>
<li>Understand
and use the language and vocabulary of the discipline</li>
<li>Make
aesthetic judgments and decisions</li>
<li>Understand
the history and traditions of the discipline</li>
<li>Connect
the arts with the cultures and societies that they are a part of</li>
<li>Integrate
the arts into one’s life as a member of the community</li>
<li>Function
as a citizen and lifelong learner through attitudes, skills, and knowledge
gained in courses in areas of knowledge outside of one’s area of focus.</li>
</ul>					</div>

<div class="tab_body" id="tabs-3"><p>If you have transferred to <abbr title="North Seattle Community College">NSCC</abbr> from another school and you wish to have credits from other colleges apply to your AFA or CFA, you must obtain the “Incoming Transcript Request” form at the Admissions Office front counter and send it to the appropriate colleges. Send a separate request to each college involved. In approximately two weeks, check with Credentials to see if your transcripts have arrived.</p>
<p>As soon as your transcripts have arrived, complete the “Application for Evaluation of Transfer Credits” from the front counter of the Admissions Office. Be sure to indicate the program for which you are requesting transfer credit. Once the credentials evaluators have received your transcripts and your application for evaluation of transfer credits, they will evaluate your credits on a first-come, first-served basis. The evaluators will send the “Transfer Credit Evaluation” form, which lists specific courses that apply to your program, to the Arts, Humanities and ABE Division dean and to the vice-president of instruction, who will decide whether or not to accept the credits presented toward your degree or certificate.</p>

												<h2>Requirements</h2>
<p>Candidates for the Associate
of Fine Arts degree in Art must complete a minimum of 100 credits of college transfer-level courses (numbered 100 or greater) with a GPA of 2.0 or higher. At least 15 credits must be earned at <abbr title="North Seattle Community College">NSCC</abbr>. The last quarter must be completed
at <abbr title="North Seattle Community College">NSCC</abbr>.</p>
<h3>Required Courses</h3>
<p>The AFA in Art requires successful completion of the following specific courses:</p>
<ul>
<li><span class="course-overlay">ENGL&AMP;101</span> and <span class="course-overlay">ENGL&AMP;102</span></li>
<li>One of the following courses:
<ul>
<li><span class="course-overlay">MATH&AMP;107</span> (or any higher-level MATH)
</li>
<li><span class="course-overlay">BUS116</span></li>
<li><span class="course-overlay">ACCT&AMP;101</span> (or any higher-level ACCT) </li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<p>There are also 45 required ART credits and a minimum of 25 ART electives. Some of the art courses are offered only once a year:</p>
<ul>
<li>Fall quarters: <span class="course-overlay">ART251</span></li>
<li>Winter quarters: <span class="course-overlay">ART252</span>, <span class="course-overlay">ART112</span>, <span class="course-overlay">ART205</span>, <span class="course-overlay">ART206</span>, <span class="course-overlay">ART207</span></li>
<li>Spring quarters: <span class="course-overlay">ART253</span>, <span class="course-overlay">ART101</span>, <span class="course-overlay">ART112</span>, <span class="course-overlay">ART205</span>, <span class="course-overlay">ART206</span>, <span class="course-overlay">ART207</span></li>
</ul>
<p>Students must submit a portfolio of work of at least eight pieces for evaluation during the final quarter. The art faculty will provide exact details on portfolio preparation and will review and vote on acceptability toward the degree.</p>
<p>All studio art courses must be taken in sequence. However, art history courses may be taken in any order. Substitutions may be made only with the approval of the art faculty and the dean. Requests for such substitutions must be made on a <em>Request for Waiver</em> form. Contact your advisor for the required form. Please note, it may be necessary to take courses out of sequence based 
on the quarterly schedule when you begin your coursework.</p>
