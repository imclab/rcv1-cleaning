                <td valign="top" colspan="2">                    <p>COMPLETED PARTIAL RESTORATION</p>
<p>1964 Chevrolet Impala SS, convertible</p>
<p> </p>
<p> </p>
<p><a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/11.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>
<img width="150" height="113" src="http://www.parderby.com/plugins/content/fboxbot/thumbs/11_150x113_625d57f42e540c920cdf94733955c187.jpg" alt="11" />
</a> <a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/33.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>
<img width="150" height="200" src="http://www.parderby.com/plugins/content/fboxbot/thumbs/33_150x200_621b9fe04a9d8b7d480a3c9a81dd356d.jpg" alt="33" />
</a><a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/22.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>
<img width="150" height="113" src="http://www.parderby.com/plugins/content/fboxbot/thumbs/22_150x113_2aafca9fc2fcecbd19bbda43034d4e79.jpg" alt="22" />
</a></p>
<p>When this project rolled in the door we could see evidence of poor workmanship under the recent paint job on the main body. Since the front clip was still assembled, we fit it to the body.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/44.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>
<img width="150" height="113" src="http://www.parderby.com/plugins/content/fboxbot/thumbs/44_150x113_2ad58a15f651497a7a92491ea4ee62ff.jpg" alt="44" />
</a> <a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/88.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>
<img width="150" height="113" src="http://www.parderby.com/plugins/content/fboxbot/thumbs/88_150x113_3a3e880e3d2450fd927976053a1b33f4.jpg" alt="88" />
</a> <a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/99.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>
<img width="150" height="113" src="http://www.parderby.com/plugins/content/fboxbot/thumbs/99_150x113_24d4c6b95324dac194448ec2e77ed847.jpg" alt="99" />
</a></p>
<p>We removed thick body filler from the deck lid by warming it with torch and scraping. The body panels were media blasted to remove the bad paint and show the true car we would be dealing with: Small rust holes and over-worked quarter panels were revealed.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/66.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>
<img width="150" height="113" src="http://www.parderby.com/plugins/content/fboxbot/thumbs/66_150x113_dcb11f6cf1621879e8578f67a3801772.jpg" alt="66" />
</a> <a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/77.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>
<img width="150" height="113" src="http://www.parderby.com/plugins/content/fboxbot/thumbs/77_150x113_18e24aaaff21ef43ca2aab286c355e40.jpg" alt="77" />
</a> <a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/55.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>
<img width="150" height="113" src="http://www.parderby.com/plugins/content/fboxbot/thumbs/55_150x113_71dc9a0b609ea47696eaada4099eb188.jpg" alt="55" />
</a></p>
<p>Fenders, hood and small body parts were stripped and found to be in fair condition.</p>
<p> </p>
<p> </p>
<p><a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/222.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>

</a> <a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/111.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>

</a></p>
<p><a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/444.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>
<img width="150" height="113" src="http://www.parderby.com/plugins/content/fboxbot/thumbs/444_150x113_f9f3b5279a6a015271e6d12870650161.jpg" alt="444" />

<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>
<img width="150" height="113" src="http://www.parderby.com/plugins/content/fboxbot/thumbs/555_150x113_e0d566b714e353230ceb1c122e048e79.jpg" alt="555" />

</a></p>
<p>The rear trunk sill and tail light panels were fitted before removing the rear quarters to give reference for welding-in  the new quarter panels.</p>
<p>At this time no one is reproducing convertible quarter panel sheetmetal, but Dynacorn's hardtop panels can be trimmed to fit, as we have done to this car.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/643.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>
<img width="150" height="113" src="http://www.parderby.com/plugins/content/fboxbot/thumbs/643_150x113_c09e88f5e4ceb7fe5f3064d2eb6ac9af.jpg" alt="643" />
</a> <a href="/images/stories/1964_Chevy_Impala/641.jpg" target="_blank">
<span class="frontbox"  name="fbox_20" title="."></span>

<p>Rusty sheetmetal replaced... Check</p>
<p>Right side body work roughed-in... Check</p>
