		<div class="article-content" itemprop="articleBody">
		  <p>If you live in North America, you have an array of muscle cars to choose from. That&#8217;s not the case everywhere else in the world, as some markets only get Ford Mustangs with four cylinders, let alone something as hair-brained as a Dodge Challenger SRT. Then there&#8217;s the 2013 <a title="Hyundai" href="http://blogs.automotive.com/manufacturer/hyundai/">Hyundai</a> Genesis Coupe 2.0T R-Spec, a respectable alternative for those who want a muscle car, sans the Detroit muscle. We recently spent a week with Hyundai&#8217;s first-ever rear-wheel drive <a title="sports car" href="http://blogs.automotive.com/vehicle-types/sports/">sports car</a> and found many things we enjoyed, and a few features we could live without. However, the turbocharged 2.0-liter inline-4 engine and its 274 horsepower made us forget about those things once we got the Genesis Coupe up and going.</p>
<p>The question still remains though: Can the 2013 Hyundai Genesis Coupe 2.0T R-Spec hang with the big boys from Detroit? After a week-long stay with us, we&#8217;re hesitant to say the Genesis Coupe deserves a spot with the Mustang, <a target="_blank" title="Chevrolet" href="http://blogs.automotive.com/manufacturer/chevrolet/">Chevrolet</a> Camaro, and Dodge Challenger but that doesn&#8217;t mean it will never get there. It&#8217;s heading in the right direction,but there are a still kinks Hyundai needs to work out before taking on the rest of the world. For $27,515 out the door though, the 2013 Hyundai Genesis Coupe 2.0T R-Spec is a solid purchase. Check out what we had to say about Hyundai&#8217;s first whack at a sports car in our <a title="2013 Hyundai Genesis Coupe Quick Drive" href="http://www.automotive.com/hyundai/genesis-coupe/2013/quick-drive/index.html" target="_blank">2013 Hyundai Genesis Coupe Quick Drive</a> and then make a decision for yourself.</p>		</div>
