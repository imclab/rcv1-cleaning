<h1>Cars For Sale<br/></h1>

<p>This subject is something I know about inside and out. When I see <i>cars for sale</i> I see dollar signs. Anyone can make money with cars. The trick is to make the maximum amount per deal. </p><p>This
 of course starts with the most basic sale principal of all time. Buy 
low and sell high. Yes this is easier said then done. I know people that
 have turned this into an art form. I wrote an article about my mentor 
and his unique <a href="http://www.auto-facts.org/used-car-business.html" target="_blank" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">used car business</a>. I enjoyed a unique perspective that helped me learn from his <span style="text-decoration: none;">mistakes</span>
 and his success. </p><p>I also learned from others. I am currently 
writing a book on cars for sale focusing on the buying low part. This 
book will take some time but I wanted to share some tips with you Today.
 Lets start with the product since the success of any business is directly related to the quality of the product. When first getting started only buy desirable cars. A good example, 
the 99 Infiniti Q45 above. It only comes 1 way (Loaded). </p><p>Buyers 
will over look high mileage and many other things to wrap themselves in 
luxury! But you have to get the car cheap. How I started buying low was 
the mass low-ball offer on private cars for sale. A car buying trick
 I learned from a late night infomercial real-estate guru. Interesting 
sidebar: Many rich and famous people built their success on the back of 
buying and selling cars because you have to start somewhere.
</p>
<h2>Best Time to Buy a Used Car<br/></h2>
<div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockLeft"><img src="http://www.auto-facts.org/images/makemoneywithcars.jpg" width="128" alt="The car to make money with" title="The car to make money with"></div>
<p>Unlike real estate people with <i>private cars for sale</i> are 
almost always motivated sellers. Your job is to find out just how 
motivated they are. You know from your own experience, when you have 
cars for sale, you always want to get rid of that old car fast.</p><p>People
 want to move on and that old car sitting around stops this from 
happening. You can use this to your advantage by being patient and 
realizing the seller needs you. However, to maximize this advantage you 
will need cash in hand. The power of a cash now offer can make sellers 
jump at the stack of bills and forget the original asking price.
</p>

motivated of these automobile sellers. It works like this; you go

then pick out the most popular models loaded with options (selling 
features) at the lowest prices. </p><p>Circle all the good ads and build up a big list. If you need help on determining what a <span style="text-decoration: none;"><em>good price</em></span> for a specific model car or truck is you can look up unlimited book values for free at

<p>After purifying the list make the calls and go see the vehicles. You want to make sure the car "has eye". To turn a profit selling cars the inventory must have extreme curb appeal. "Eye" refers to the pleasant feeling someone gets deep inside when they're looking at something that brings an involuntary positive emotional response generating the words "I want".<br/></p><p>Luxury
 Infiniti, Acura, Lexus and sporty automobiles even with high miles sell
 much easier for this reason. Then make the insulting low ball offers. Thousands below
 the asking price. You must be firm and direct. Most people will say no 
way. Leave them your name and number and tell them to call you if they 
get stuck and need the money. </p><p>You'll be surprised at how many 
calls you will get. Just do this over and over until you find the 
ultimate deal. Don't hesitate on walking away from a deal or making a 
counter offer even lower to cover any <i>auto repair</i> costs. Before 
the money changes hands check the vehicle out thoroughly. More on the 
subject on my how to buy used cars page. See how to check a vehicle with
 a <a href="http://www.auto-facts.org/usedcars.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">used car inspection guide</a>.
