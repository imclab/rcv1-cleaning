<p>If your baby is tall, and you want the convertible car seat that will last the longest, you have to look beyond the stated limits on each car seat&#8217;s box.  </p>
<div style="float: left; margin: 10px;"><img src="http://mommieslike.com/images/carseats/2smallcarseats.jpg"/></div>
<p>The vast majority of the time, children outgrow car seats by height rather than by weight.  However, it gets even more confusing than that!  Kids generally outgrow their car seats by &#8216;seated shoulder height&#8217; rather than &#8216;total height&#8217;.  What does that mean?</p>
<p>When a child is sitting on a car seat, her &#8216;seated shoulder height&#8217; is her height measured while she is sitting down until the top of her shoulder.  So it is entirely possible that two children will have the exact same &#8216;total height&#8217; while standing, yet, have different &#8216;seated shoulder heights&#8217; because they are proportioned differently!  Child A may have a long torso and short legs, while Child B would have a short torso and long legs!  Child A&#8217;s &#8216;seated shoulder height&#8217; would be greater than that of Child B, even though their &#8216;total heights&#8217; may be identical!</p>
<p>Therefore, when you are looking for a convertible car seat to last a long time, one of the most important factors to consider is the &#8216;seated shoulder height limit&#8217;.</p>
