    <p>Hyundai&rsquo;s Genesis coupe will receive the same 333-hp, 291-lb-ft 3.8-liter V-6 engine that the company recently announced is <a href="/news/2012-hyundai-genesis-sedan-50-r-spec-official-photos-and-info-hyundai-genesis-news-car-and-driver" target="_self">headed for the Genesis sedan</a>. The revised powerplant&mdash;which features direct injection&mdash;offers a good bump in power over the outgoing V-6 engine, which made 306 hp and 266 lb-ft. At 333 hp, the Genesis coupe will top its six-cylinder pony-car rivals; the Chevy Camaro offers 312 hp, while the Ford Mustang and Dodge Challenger each pack 305-hp sixes under their hoods.</p><p>Although Hyundai will almost definitely continue to offer a traditional six-speed manual gearbox when this engine arrives in the Genesis coupe, the plan for the V-6&rsquo;s two-pedal option isn&rsquo;t yet clear. While the six-speed auto could hang around, the most likely transmission is an eight-speed automatic of the torque-converter variety. Developed in-house by Hyundai, this is the transmission that will be mated to the same V-6 in the Genesis sedan.</p><p>Based on the heavy use of camouflage in the accompanying spy photos, we also anticipate that a modest cosmetic face lift will arrive at the same time, sharpening the looks of V-6 <i>and</i> four-cylinder Genesis coupe models. (Speaking of which, in spite of the outstanding 274-hp turbo four offered in the Sonata turbo, the four-cylinder Genesis coupe will probably soldier on with its current 210-hp engine.)</p><p>There is a distinct possibility that this car, seen testing in the snow, is packing all-wheel drive, which company CEO John Krafcik has said is <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/hyundai-equus-genesis-and-genesis-coupe-to-receive-all-wheel-drive/" target="_self">headed for the Genesis coupe</a> at some point. </p><p>With available all-wheel drive, the more-powerful V-6 engine and a new automatic gearbox on the way, it&rsquo;s clear Hyundai wants people to take it seriously in this segment. It&rsquo;s rearming at the right time: In addition to a slew of American rear drivers, the long-baking <a href="/news/toyota-ft-86-ii-concept-news-car-and-driver" target="_self">Subaru/Toyota rear-wheel-drive coupe</a> is just about to come out of the oven. We just hope Hyundai has budgeted some development time to sort out the Genesis coupe&rsquo;s tooth-chipping ride. </p><p>&#160;</p>
