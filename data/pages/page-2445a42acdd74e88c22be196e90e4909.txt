<p>The Associate in Fine Arts (A.F.A.) offers the first two years of concentrated study in studio courses for students planning to transfer to a&nbsp;visual arts, theatre, or music baccalaureate program. As a fine arts major, the student may choose to concentrate in either theater, art or&nbsp;music.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>What will students learn if they study Fine Arts?&nbsp;<br /></strong>The Associate of Fine Arts Degree (AFA) prepares students for transfer to Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA) programs at four-year institutions. Just as the Associates in Arts (AA) degree is composed of the first two years of study toward the Bachelor of Arts (BA) degree, the Associate of Fine Arts (AFA) is composed of the first two years of concentrated study in studio courses appropriate to the BFA in art. This includes coursework in drawing, design, painting, sculpture, printmaking, ceramics, and mixed media.</p>
<p>The Associate of Fine Arts degree (AFA) will provide a well –rounded education with a generous range of liberal arts courses required by four-year BFA programs. Specifically, the AFA degree contains a series of general education “core” requirements, which all AFA students must complete.<span style="text-decoration: underline;">&nbsp;</span><span style="text-decoration: underline;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p>In terms of academic requirements, the major difference between the AA degree and the AFA is that the AFA requires more “hands-on” studio courses while reducing the number of courses in math and science.&nbsp; The AA degree requires&nbsp;<b>four </b>courses in math&nbsp;<b>and/or </b>science. The AFA degree, however, requires only&nbsp;<b>two </b>courses in math&nbsp;<b>and/or</b> science for graduation.</p>
<p><strong>Are there any requirements that must be satisfied&nbsp;before taking courses in the major? </strong><br />Algebra I is a prerequisite for all majors. Algebra I competency may&nbsp;be verified with a passing score on the College’s placement test or&nbsp;completion of the appropriate course.</p>
<p><strong>How long will it take to complete this degree? </strong><br />Once students complete developmental coursework (if needed), the&nbsp;degree can be completed in two years of full-time study. They can&nbsp;shorten the amount of time by taking courses in the summer and&nbsp;winter sessions.</p>
