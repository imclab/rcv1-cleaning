					<h1><a href="http://autoeclectic.com/forza-motorsport-4-car-culture-in-a-console-3/" rel="bookmark" >Forza Motorsport 4: Car Culture in a Console</a></h1>
                    <p class="meta">
                        Posted by <a href="http://autoeclectic.com/author/p-stevenson/" title="Posts by P. Stevenson" rel="author">P. Stevenson</a>                    </p>
			    	<div class="gallery"><a rel="gallery" href="http://autoeclectic.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_2010_Mosler_MT900S_1.jpg" title="Forza Motorsport 4: Car Culture in a Console"><img width="545" height="306" src="http://autoeclectic.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_2010_Mosler_MT900S_1.jpg" class=" wp-post-image" alt="Forza Motorsport 4: Car Culture in a Console" title="Forza Motorsport 4: Car Culture in a Console" style="" /></a></div>                       
						                                            
<div class="tweetmeme_button" style="float: right; margin-left: 10px;"><a class="tm_button" rel="&amp;style=normal&amp;b=2" href="http://autoeclectic.com/forza-motorsport-4-car-culture-in-a-console-3/" ></a></div>
<p><a href="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_2010_Mosler_MT900S_1.jpg" ><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3936" title="FM4_2010_Mosler_MT900S_" src="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_2010_Mosler_MT900S_1.jpg" alt="" width="610" height="343" /></a></p>
<p>Forza Motorsport 4 is soon to release for sale to the public and Auto Eclectic has had the chance to get behind the wheel of the game. Aimed to be the best racing game on sale today take a quick read of what we think of it as well as a few quotes on what other publications have to say&#8230;..</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_TG_1993_McLaren_F1_3.jpg" ><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3941" title="FM4_TG_1993_McLaren_F1_3" src="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_TG_1993_McLaren_F1_3.jpg" alt="" width="610" height="343" /></a></p>
<h2>Graphics</h2>
<p>This is the first aspect of the game you will experience the minute to pop in the disc. Amazing does not begin to describe what the designers at Turn 10 Studios have developed. Here is what Ray Wert of Jalopnik.com had to say:</p>
<div>
<div>
<blockquote><p>The result is so mind-numbingly beautiful that while I was playing the game at my office, people would walk by, stop, turn around and then ask me if it was a game or — frankly, they didn&#8217;t know what else it could be. It looks that good.</p>
</blockquote>
<div>
<blockquote>
<div><a href="http://jalopnik.com/5847234/forza-motorsport-4-first-drive" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://jalopnik.com']);" target="_blank">Forza Motorsport 4: First Drive</a> <img src="http://cache.jalopnik.com/assets/v10.jalopnik.com/img/apple-touch-icon.png" alt="" width="16" border="0" /><a href="http://jalopnik.com/5847234/forza-motorsport-4-first-drive" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://jalopnik.com']);" target="_blank">jalopnik.com</a> , October 6, 2011 at 13:50</div>
</blockquote>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Forza 4 has thrown out the entire Forza 3 graphics engine and created a new system. Unlike the boys from Gran Turismo, all of Forza&#8217;s 500 plus cars look absolutely stunning.</p>
<p><a href="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_2002_Ferrari_Enzo.jpg" ><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3974" title="FM4_2002_Ferrari_Enzo" src="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_2002_Ferrari_Enzo.jpg" alt="" width="610" height="343" /></a></p>
<p>Forza 4 offers a chance for you to explore these graphics up close and personal in the Autovista mode. This virtual showroom allows you to not only look at but truely examine some of the worlds most amazing cars. This mode includes introductions by Top Gear&#8217;s Jeremy Clarkson for each car. Using Kinect you can walk around the car, open the doors with your hand and even bend down to get a closer look.</p>
<p>So the cars are pretty, but what about the tracks? If the boys at Turn 10 could have found a way to pump out the smell of burnt rubber and race fuel; the game could almost be just as realistic as actually being in the car. The details at tracks like Top Gears test track have you expecting the Stig to walk out from the tires on the follow thru.</p>
<p><a><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3940" title="FM4_TG_1993_McLaren_F1_4" src="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_TG_1993_McLaren_F1_4.jpg" alt="" width="610" height="343" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Tires, Tires, Tires&#8230;&#8230;.why you say? Well the tires connect the car to the track and as a result they play a major part in the physics of the game. In real life tire technology is changing all the time as a result the data from Forza 3 was highly out of date. So Forza 4 partnered with Pirelli and designed a system to import all of their tire testing data right into the game. The results are A-W-E-S-O-M-E! I actually played Forza 3 and Gran Turismo 5 before playing Forza 4 just to see the differences. The results are day and night. There is so much feedback from the tires it actually feels real. Having slid my share of cars around I can say that these cars actually behave like a real car should. This means Forza 4 is a drifter&#8217;s delight. You want to power slide the new McLaren MP4-12C on the Top Gear test track, of course you do and now you can! If you start smoking the tires expect the back-end to get loose a lot quicker as the tires heat up and get greasy.</p>
<div>
<div>
<blockquote><p>in terms of handling models the boundaries have become as blurry as a Zonda&#8217;s tachometer needle &#8211; comparing Forza 4 and GT5&#8242;s dynamics is a bit like choosing between Ferrari and Porsche.</p>
</blockquote>
<div>
<blockquote>
<div><a href="http://www.computerandvideogames.com/321412/reviews/forza-motorsport-4-review/?cid=OTC-RSS&amp;attr=CVG-Reviews-RSS&amp;utm_source=twitterfeed&amp;utm_medium=twitter" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.computerandvideogames.com']);" target="_blank">Xbox Review: Forza Motorsport 4 Review &#8211; ComputerAndVideoGames.com</a> <img src="http://cdn.static.computerandvideogames.com/images/favicons/cvg-favicon.gif" alt="" width="16" border="0" /><a href="http://www.computerandvideogames.com/321412/reviews/forza-motorsport-4-review/?cid=OTC-RSS&amp;attr=CVG-Reviews-RSS&amp;utm_source=twitterfeed&amp;utm_medium=twitter" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.computerandvideogames.com']);" target="_blank">computerandvideogames.com</a> , October 6, 2011 at 13:54</div>
</blockquote>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_PreviewTour_5.jpg" ><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3966" title="FM4_PreviewTour_5" src="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_PreviewTour_5.jpg" alt="" width="610" height="343" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The start of Forza 4 drops you right into the new World Tour Mode. This career mode is addicting, traveling to different tracks around the world with the option of several races at each track. As you win races and gain experience points you are offered a choice of vehicle rewards. For a gear head like myself these decisions can be extremely hard. I mean I had to decide between a BMW 2002 Turbo and a 71&#8242; Nissan Skyline GTR, could it get anymore difficult? This kind of choice is designed to convert gamers into gear heads. The creative director for Forza 4 Dan Greenawalt, a car guy himself, designed Forza 4 to help create the next generation of gear heads.</p>
<div>
<div>
<blockquote><p>Forza 4 captures the cult of cars better than any racing game before it. It&#8217;s a game that understands what makes a 20-year-old high-performance pickup truck just as important to some people as a brand new Lexus LF-A.</p>
</blockquote>
<div>
<blockquote>
<div><a href="http://xbox360.ign.com/articles/119/1198556p1.html?utm_campaign=twposts&amp;utm_source=twitter" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://xbox360.ign.com']);" target="_blank">Forza Motorsport 4 Review &#8211; Xbox 360 Review at IGN</a> <img src="http://media.ignimgs.com/media/ign/favicon.ico" alt="" width="16" border="0" /><a href="http://xbox360.ign.com/articles/119/1198556p1.html?utm_campaign=twposts&amp;utm_source=twitter" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://xbox360.ign.com']);" target="_blank">xbox360.ign.com</a> , October 6, 2011 at 11:57</div>
</blockquote>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_2012_BMW_M5_F1_3.jpg" ><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3969" title="FM4_2012_BMW_M5_F1_3" src="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_2012_BMW_M5_F1_3.jpg" alt="" width="610" height="343" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>In the community section of the game you can create your own car club, which may sound kind of well&#8230;..lame. This is until you find out this means you can share all of the car in your garages with each other. Just think about all the different types of cars you can drive without having to spend your hard-earned credits. Especially when you only need a car for one race or series. Also with all the customization options the possibilities are endless.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_PreviewTour_1.jpg" ><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3944" title="FM4_PreviewTour_1" src="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_PreviewTour_1.jpg" alt="" width="610" height="343" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Another game mode is Rivals, in which you can compete against anyone online or off in games ranging from autocross to car bowling. The back and forth chess match of rivals shows you when your opponent has beaten your time or score. Allowing users around the world to compete even while their opponent is sleeping.</p>
<p><a href="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_TG_2010_Spyker_C8_Laviolette_LM85.jpg" ><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3938" title="FM4_TG_2010_Spyker_C8_Laviolette_LM85" src="http://autoeclectic.com//wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FM4_TG_2010_Spyker_C8_Laviolette_LM85.jpg" alt="" width="610" height="343" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>In closing, I am going to go play some more and I advise you to go get your own copy as soon as possible. You will not regret it.</p>
<div>
<div>
<blockquote><p>The world of Forza Motorsport – there’s no clever storyline, no rise to fame through a faceless character, just you, few hundred cars and the open road. This is more than a game, it’s an experience and one than can be tailored to any play style and level of skill.</p>
</blockquote>
<div>
