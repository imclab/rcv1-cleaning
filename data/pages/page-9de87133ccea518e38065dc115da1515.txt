        	<span class="paragraph-0">
				<p>Whether you are planning to spend Valentine's Day with a special
someone or if that special someone happens to be a giant tub of Ben
&amp; Jerry's, Mane Entertainment (ME) will help you get into the
spirit of the holiday. LMU students are invited to join ME in The
Living Room for a Valentine's Day Dinner Show Wednesday, Feb. 8 at
8:30 p.m.</p>
			</span>
        	<span class="paragraph-1">
				<p>After a successful performance during the Thanksgiving Dinner
Show, Mission IMPROVable was invited back to LMU's campus for the
Valentine's Day event. The comedy troupe is part of the Westside
Comedy Theater located in Santa Monica.</p>
			</span><div class="p402_hide"><div id="in-story">
    
	<div id="tncms-region-ads-in-story" class="tncms-region-ads"></div>
    
</div>
</div>
                    <p>ME Special Events Manager Sharon Aguilera said the comedy troupe
is reliable for lots of laughs. "Mission IMPROVable always gives us
a really good performance, and we're trying to break the idea of a
traditional Valentine's Day," said Aguilera.</p>
                
                    <p>Along with jokes, students can also enjoy a fine Italian dinner,
free of charge. Even though the food source is yet to be
determined, ME guarantees it will be delicious. "Who doesn't like
free food?" said Tiffany Hunter, the signature events manager of
ME.</p>
                
                    <p>Sophomore English major Erin Callier said she's anticipating
good food and fun. "I'm looking forward to this event. I love
Italian food! There's no such thing as forever alone when you have
garlic balls!"</p>
                
                    <p>Hunter also said the Dinner Show is open to all students who
want to have a good time, regardless of the event's attachment to
Valentine's Day. "Whether [students] bring a date or just a
‘friend-date,' ME wants to make sure students are having a good
time on campus," Hunter said.</p>
                
                    <p>And despite whether Cupid's bow has struck or not, Hunter also
said the event will be enjoyable for all students.</p>
                
                    <p>"What's not to love about funny people? I think it really
doesn't matter whether you're in a relationship or not. The night
is more about being around other students and having a good time,"
said Hunter.</p>
                
                    <p>The event promises to put a cheerful spin on Valentine's Day,
according to Evanne Drucker, showcase events manager at ME. "Aside
from the free food, I think [the event] provides a more
lighthearted tone to view Valentine's Day. I just think it's a fun
little break from the possible heartbreak and lovey-dovey madness
of Valentine's Day."</p>
                
                    <p>Facebook relationship statuses notwithstanding, ME invites all
LMU students to attend. Doors open at 8 p.m., and there is a
100-student limit. For more information, check out Mane
Entertainment on Facebook or visit the Mane Entertainment office in
Malone 105.</p>
