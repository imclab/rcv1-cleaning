The black exploitation film was not only thriving but also enjoying crossover appeal.</p><p>But probably no one in Hollywood was prepared for the martial arts mania that erupted the summer of 1973 when Warner Bros. released the kung fu epic "Enter the Dragon," starring the legendary Bruce Lee, who died at 32 shortly before the U.S opening. It was a real cultural moment. And the posters are an incredibly important part of the way these movies were publicized. The art is incredibly dynamic, full of action."</p><p>The 80 pieces in the exhibition &mdash; Chin's collection numbers more than 800 &mdash; includes U.S. and international posters from martial arts films such as 1978's "Enter the Fat Dragon" with Sammo Hung.
