<div class='focusText' style='color: black; padding-top: 5px; padding-bottom:0px; margin-bottom: 0px;'>
	Convertible Top Installation
</div>

<p>
Many convertible owners have determined that they can install their own convertible top. This
is not for everyone, but over half of our customers have done do-it-yourself top installation.
There is a significant savings as the typical shop installation for a new convertible top is billed at
8 hours.
</p>

<p>
Your car's page may display an available installation manual for that convertible top. If your convertible top is a classic, or is an American convertible prior to about 2005 then it will require stapling and gluing. There is never sewing involved. Stapling of a convertible top is best done with an air driven staple gun (which can
be rented) or via our heavy duty electric staple gun. Modern tops are doing away with staples and often with glue work. These often require the use of a common pop rivet gun and the removal and replacement of straps, screws, bolts and convertible top frame seals.<p>Please select your convertible top and you can then click on a link to its page. There you will find a lot of information that we hope you will read, or print out for later reading.
</p>

<p>
If you want to select a convertible top shop to get a quote for installing a top that you deliver to
them, then please contact us at our support email address. We will either have a convertible top
shop with which we have good experience, or we will email you advice on how to find and select
a cooperative local convertible top shop.
</p>

<div class='focusText' style='color: black; padding-top: 8px; padding-bottom:0px; margin-bottom: 0px;'>
	Convertible Top Accessories
</div>

<p>
There are important components of a convertible top installation that are not part of the actual
convertible top. The important ones are the top hold down cables, the frame bow tacking strips,
the convertible top pads (or webbing), the top's rear well liner and possibly other items.
</p>

<p>
We have assembled, for many convertibles, top installation packages. In these convertible top
packages there will usually be hold down cables and, where often required, new top bow tacking
strips. If available for your convertible an instruction manual will be included for free in that top
package. In the following sections we'll describe some important convertible top accessories.
</p>

<div class='focusText' style='color: black; padding-top: 8px; padding-bottom:0px; margin-bottom: 0px;'>
	Hold Down Cables
</div>

<p>
Most tops are held to the folding top frame, along the sides, with special cables. Replacement
hold down cables are recommended when the top frame has been frequently raised and lowered.
</p>

<p>
Some tops have rear base attachment cables (Jaguar, Volkswagen) but most do not. And a few
tops have short rear side cables for the corner flaps of the convertible top.
</p>

<div class='focusText' style='color: black; padding-top: 8px; padding-bottom:0px; margin-bottom: 0px;'>
	Tacking Strips
</div>

<p>
Some tops, notably the BMW and some very recent modular top assemblies, require no stapling
in the installation of the convertible top. Almost all American tops prior to 2005, and other
classic two part top designs, require that staples be driven through fabric into the tack strips
which are embedded in the top frame. Tack strips are not difficult to remove and replace. Tack
strips are most often employed in both the header bow (the convertible top frame bow that is
over the windshield) and the rear top bow (the top frame bow that is over the rear window.)
</p>

<p>
Most cars with tacking strips also employ complex rear "trim stick" assemblies. These are also
tacking strips, but they are embedded along the rear belt line of the body of the car. The base
of the top is stapled there. Replacement trim sticks are separate from the tacking strips and are
much less often required for convertible top replacement.
</p>

<div class='focusText' style='color: black; padding-top: 8px; padding-bottom:0px; margin-bottom: 0px;'>
	Convertible Top Pads
</div>

<p>
Many convertible tops have, under them, convertible top pads. These may be reinforced nylon
webbing or foam and fabric assemblies. New pads are often required but only after the top has
been in use for significant time or has been raised and lowered many times. We include pads
in our packages as it is a good idea for the do-it-yourself installer to perform a conservative
restoration.
</p>

<div class='focusText' style='color: black; padding-top: 8px; padding-bottom:0px; margin-bottom: 0px;'>
	Installation Manuals
</div>

<p>
These are our own published top installation manuals. Most of them have been through
revisions based on our own and even customer experiences and inputs during their convertible
top installations. Convertible top installation manuals include illustrations and step by step
instructions. For example, our classic two part top manual is profusely illustrated and has been
through four significant edition changes. This manual applies to almost any American top and
many foreign tops as well. A summary of this manual appears on our site. This convertible top
manual is a summary intended to orient the shopper in deciding upon do-it yourself installation.
The complete manual is for sale and its purchase price is deductible from any follow up order of
a convertible top. Look for this top installation summary at the Help link at the top of this page.
</p>

<div class='focusText' style='color: black; padding-top: 8px; padding-bottom:0px; margin-bottom: 0px;'>
	Latches
</div>

<p>
The two convertible top latches at the front of most top frames are being reproduced for some
cars. If you do not see them listed for your car please inquire at our support email address.
</p>

<div class='focusText' style='color: black; padding-top: 8px; padding-bottom:0px; margin-bottom: 0px;'>
	Weatherstripping Seals
</div>

<p>
Most weatherstrip seals are not part of the top itself but are attached to the folding framework. We
do list many replacement rubber seals for convertible top frames. If a seal is a permanent part of
the top then it is manufactured into our replacement top.
</p>

<div class='focusText' style='color: black; padding-top: 8px; padding-bottom:0px; margin-bottom: 0px;'>
	Hydraulic System
</div>

<p>
While most convertible tops are manually operated, many have hydraulic motors and pistons that
allow the top to be raised and lowered with a switch. We can service most motors which involves
rewinding the armatures and fields of the top hydraulic motor pump. The motor rebuild service is
described at the site. Just click on "Hydraulic Motor Pump Rebuild" from the menu above.
</p>

<p>
There is also a choice on the menu for Hydraulic Cylinders and this takes you to other hydraulic
parts for your convertible top system. Where available these parts and service should also be
listed at your car's page.
</p>
<div class='focusText' style='color: black; padding-top: 8px; padding-bottom:0px; margin-bottom: 0px;'>
	Raggtopp Cleaner & Protectant
</div>

<P>

RAGGTOPP Canvas/Vinyl Convertible Care Kit contains one bottle of RAGGTOPP Cleaner and one bottle of RAGGTOPP Protectant to safely clean and protect fabric convertible tops per manufacturer's recommendation RAGGTOPP Protectant was especially developed for the true convertible top professional and enthusiast to protect and maintain their fabric, vinyl soft tops and plastic windows from harmful elements The number one factor in deteriorating convertible tops is acid rain, along with U.V. rays, smog, tree sap, bird droppings, salt, tar , dirt, grease, and grit. All these elements contribute to the weakening and failure of convertible tops. RAGGTOPP Protectant is the future of convertible top protection. Over three years of product testing has produced a polymer formula that creates a superior protective barrier that micro-bonds to the convertible top and protects it from the elements of nature and man. <P>
