<h1 id="article_title">Integrating Art and Language Arts through Children's Literature.</h1>

  Debi Englebaugh. Westport, CT: Teacher Ideas Press/Greenwood

$25.00.

<BR><BR>  This book explores the interesting concept of integrating art
activities that relate to well-known examples of <a onmouseover="t_i(1)" onmouseout="t_o(1)" class=tip href="http://encyclopedia2.thefreedictionary.com/children's+literature">children's
literature</a><span ID=Tp1 class=hint> <span class=hw>children's literature,</span>&nbsp;writing whose primary audience is children.
<br><br>See also children's book illustration. 
<b>The Beginnings of Children's Literature</b><br>
<br><br>The earliest of what came to be regarded as children's literature was first meant for adults. </span>. Directed primarily to students in grades one through three,
the book begins with explanations of more than fifty art techniques that
may be integrated with almost any of the books listed in part two of the
publication.

<BR><BR>  These art techniques cover the usual range of art activities for
use at the primary grade levels, along with several more challenging
activities such as <a onmouseover="t_i(2)" onmouseout="t_o(2)" class=tip href="http://encyclopedia2.thefreedictionary.com/batik">batik</a><span ID=Tp2 class=hint> <span class=hw>batik</span>&nbsp;<span >(b&#601;t&#275;k`)</span>, method of decorating fabrics practiced for centuries by the natives of Indonesia. It consists of applying a design to the surface of the cloth by using melted wax. </span>, suminogashi, and the use of a computer scanner
to create images. Directions are minimal with a list of needed supplies
<a onmouseover="t_i(3)" onmouseout="t_o(3)" class=tip href="http://www.thefreedictionary.com/relating+to">relating to</a><span ID=Tp3 class=hint> <span class=hw>relating to</span>&nbsp;relate <i>prep</i> &#x2192; <span lang=fr>concernant</span><br><br><span class=hw>relating to</span>&nbsp;relate <i>prep</i> &#x2192; <span lang=de>bezüglich <i>+gen</i></span>, <span lang=de>mit Bezug auf <i>+acc</i></span>&nbsp;</span>&nbsp;sparse step-by-step directions. These technique listings are
followed by suggestions for integrating lessons applying the techniques
to the content from more than 140 favorite children's books, most
published within the past decade. A sampling of books includes
<a onmouseover="t_i(4)" onmouseout="t_o(4)" class=tip href="http://encyclopedia2.thefreedictionary.com/Charlotte's+Web">Charlotte's Web</a><span ID=Tp4 class=hint> <i>Charlotte&#x2019;s Web</i><br><br>story of a spider who saves a young girl&#x2019;s pet pig. [Am. Lit.: E. B. White <i>Charlotte&#x2019;s Web</i>]<br><br>See : Insect </span>, Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire, Harold and
the Purple Crayon, and Tar Beach. Author's represented include Eric
Carle, Lois Ehlert, <a onmouseover="t_i(5)" onmouseout="t_o(5)" class=tip href="http://encyclopedia2.thefreedictionary.com/Leo">Leo</a><span ID=Tp5 class=hint> <b>Leo, in astronomy</b><br><span class=hw>Leo</span>&nbsp;[Lat.,=the lion], northern constellation lying S of Ursa Major and on the ecliptic (apparent path of the sun through the heavens) between Cancer and Virgo; it is one of the constellations of the zodiac. </span>&nbsp;Lionni, Faith Ringgold, Shel Silverstein, <a onmouseover="t_i(6)" onmouseout="t_o(6)" class=tip rel=nofollow href="http://encyclopedia.thefreedictionary.com/J.+K.+Rowling">J. K.
Rowling</a><span ID=Tp6 class=hint> <b>Joanne "Jo" Murray </b> née <b>Rowling</b> OBE (born 31 July 1965),<sup>[2]</sup> who writes under the pen name <b>J. K. Rowling</b>,<sup>[3]</sup> is an English writer and author of the <em>Harry Potter</em> fantasy series. </span>, Tomie DePaola, and many more.

<BR><BR>  There is no reference to the book's contents, only the title
and author, a suggested art technique, a list of supplies and a sequence
of how-to instructions, usually listing no more than two or three
suggested procedures. Drawings related to the activity are sparsely
interspersed throughout the book. An interesting idea and resource that
would have benefited from more supportive words and illustrations.
