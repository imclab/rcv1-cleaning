                            <h1 class="title">Japanese Car Plates</h1>
              <div class="tabs"></div>
                                          <div class="node">
  <div class="picture">
  </div>

  
      <span class="submitted">Submitted by <a href="/user/1" title="View user profile.">admin</a> on Wed, 07/30/2008 - 10:59</span> <span class="taxonomy"><ul class="links inline"><li class="taxonomy_term_11 first"><a href="/taxonomy/term/11" rel="tag" title="Articles not ranked by language skill; mostly cultural lessons">All Levels</a></li>
<li class="taxonomy_term_2 last"><a href="/taxonomy/term/2" rel="tag" title="">Culture</a></li>
</ul></span>
  
  <div class="content">
<p align="center">Cars in Japan have license plates. :) In Japanese they are called <em>namba- pure-to </em>.&nbsp;&nbsp; <br>
  <img src="/node/gif/plate.jpg" height="335" width="509"></p>
<p align="left">The top left has a screw.&nbsp; Next to the screw is the <em>ken </em> (prefecture)'s name. In this case ç¦äº <em>fukui </em>.&nbsp; According to Dean, a surfer kind enough to drop me a note, the numbers to the right may be the class of vehicle, small car, big car, taxi, truck, etc. for example, in Osaka Taxi is 500. After the 2nd screw the bottom left has a hiragana, in this case ã¿ .&nbsp; This along with the numbers to the right are the car's number.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </p>
<ul>
  <li>
    <p align="left">There are two main types - Yellow plates (see above) and White plates (Same as above only white - It doesn't hurt to use your imagination once in a while!) </p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">YELLOW PLATES - smaller cars (called <em>kei ji dou sha </em> in Japanese)&nbsp; </p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">WHITE PLATES - bigger cars (if it has a more powerful engine it is probably a white plate)&nbsp;&nbsp; </p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">Needless to say, Yellow plates are much cheaper than White plates.&nbsp; <em>shaken </em> is also much cheaper.&nbsp; ( <em>shaken </em> is the mandatory car inspection every 2 years. - I hope to do more on this later!) </p>
  </li>
</ul></div>
