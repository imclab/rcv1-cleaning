			<h1>Buying and selling</h1>


			<p class="meta">Updated: 6 November 2012</p><p>There's more to buying or selling a  vehicle than exchanging the keys and money. Follow these easy steps to avoid  getting caught with other people's unpaid licence fees and fines - whether  you're the seller or the buyer.</p>

<p>The quickest and easiest way to notify us that you have bought or sold a vehicle is to do it online through our transaction centre.</p>
    <p class="explore-page">On  this page:</p>
    <ul class="anchors">
      <li><a href="#changing">Have you bought or sold a vehicle?</a></li>
      <li><a href="#tipsbuyers">Advice  for buyers</a></li>
      <li><a href="#tipssellers">Advice  for sellers </a></li>
    </ul>
		</div><!-- intro-subsub -->

		<div class="content">
			<h2><strong><a name="changing" id="changing"></a>Have you bought or sold a vehicle?</strong></h2>
<p>Both the buyer and seller are required to notify us immediately when a vehicle is bought and sold.</p>
<div class="content-block">
<h3>Save time - do it online</h3>
<p>The quickest and easiest way to notify us that you have bought or sold a vehicle is to do it <a href="/transact/index.html">online through our transaction centre</a>.</p>
</div>
<h3><strong>If  you're selling </strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li>Complete the <strong>Notice of person selling/disposing of motor vehicle form</strong> (MR13A)  <a href="https://transact.nzta.govt.nz/transactions/NoticeOfDisposal/entry.aspx">online</a> or post it to our <a href="/about/contact/our-offices.html#trc">Palmerston North office</a>.</li>
  <li>Ask the buyer to give you a transfer receipt to show they are registered in respect of the vehicle before you hand over the vehicle. </li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>If  you're buying </strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li>Complete the <strong>Notice by person acquiring motor vehicle form</strong> (MR13B) and <a href="/transact/index.html">notify us of your acquisition online</a> or <a href="/vehicle/registration-licensing/where.html">visit one of our agents</a>.</li>
  <li>You will be required to <a href="/vehicle/registration-licensing/owner.html#identity">provide  ID</a> and pay the notice of acquisition fee of $9.00 (including GST).</li>
  <li>Request a transfer receipt, which you may need to give to the seller when you pick up the vehicle to show you are now  registered in respect of the vehicle. </li>
</ul>
<p>For more information, see <a href="/resources/factsheets/41/buying-and-selling.html">Factsheet 41: Buying and selling: your legal obligations</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Note:</strong> If  your vehicle weighs 6000kg or over, you will also need to apply for a transport  service licence (if you do not have one already). Some other vehicles under  6000kg also require a transport service licence. For more information, see <a href="/resources/factsheets/47/47.html">Factsheet 47: Transport service licences</a>.</p>
<p>See more on how to <a href="/resources/factsheets/41/buying-and-selling.html">notify a change of registered person</a>.</p>
<h3><strong>Personalised  plates</strong></h3>
<p>If you're buying a vehicle with  personalised plates and you wish to have those plates, you'll need to negotiate  this separately with the seller and complete a transfer agreement. </p>
<p>Find out more about <a href="http://www.plates.co.nz/service/faqs/">personalised plates</a> (external link). </p>
<p class="backtotop"><a href="#page">top</a></p>
<h2><a name="tipsbuyers" id="tipsbuyers"></a>Advice for buyers </h2>
<h3><strong>Do:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li>Notify your acquisition of the vehicle <strong>immediately</strong> after purchasing the vehicle.</li>
  <li>remember to  take suitable <a href="/vehicle/registration-licensing/owner.html#identity">identification</a>  to the <a href="/vehicle/registration-licensing/where.html">licensing agent</a>.</li>
  <li>remember to ask the agent for a transfer receipt, so you can show the seller that you are now registered in respect of the vehicle. Alternatively, complete the <a href="/transact/index.html">notification of acquisition online</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Protect yourself</strong> by asking: </h3>
<ul>
  <li>Is any money  owed on the vehicle? You can search the <a href="http://www.ppsr.govt.nz/cms">Personal Property  Securities Register</a> (external link) </li>
  <li>Has the  vehicle had an independent vehicle inspection? </li>
  <li>Has the  odometer been tampered with? </li>
  <li>Are there any  outstanding <a href="/vehicle/registration-licensing/ruc.html">road user charges</a>  (RUC) payable? </li>
  <li>Does the  vehicle have a current <a href="/vehicle/registration-licensing/ruc.html">RUC licence</a>?  </li>
  <li>Does the  vehicle have a current licence? </li>
</ul>
<p class="backtotop"><a href="#page">top</a></p>
<h2><a name="tipssellers" id="tipssellers"></a>Advice for sellers </h2>
<h3><strong>Do:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li>Make sure  you've paid all outstanding licence fees.</li>
  <li>Notify the sale of the vehicle  immediately.  If you  don't, you may receive the new registered person's:
    <ul>
      <li>speed camera  fines </li>
      <li>parking  tickets </li>
      <li>vehicle  licensing fees for up to 12 months </li>
      <li>fines for not  displaying a vehicle licensing label</li>
      <li>fines for not  displaying a current warrant of fitness.</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Don't:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li>Rely on the  buyer to notify their acquisition.</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Protect  yourself</strong></h3>
<p>Wait for the buyer to show you proof that they've notified their acquisition before handing over the vehicle.  This proof could be the receipted  customer section of the <strong>Notice by person acquiring motor vehicle form</strong> (MR13B)   issued by one  of our <a href="/vehicle/registration-licensing/where.html">licensing agents</a>.</p>
<img src="/vehicle/registration-licensing/img/ownership-label.gif" width="289" height="142" alt="Change of ownership card. " />
<br /><br />
<h2><strong>Want  more advice?</strong></h2>
<p>Visit the <a href="http://www.consumeraffairs.govt.nz/for-consumers/motor-vehicles">Ministry  of Consumer Affairs website</a> for more buying advice. </p>
<p>Also check: </p>
<ul>
  <li><a href="http://www.rightcar.govt.nz">Rightcar</a>   to see which cars offer the best in safety, economy and environmental features.</li>
  <li> <a href="/vehicle/choosing/tips.html">Buying a used car</a>  for tips on what to look for.</li>
  <li> <a href="/vehicle/choosing/rate/index.html">How does your vehicle rate?</a>  for the latest available vehicle safety  ratings.</li>
</ul>
