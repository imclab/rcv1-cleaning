<h1>New Camaro Convertible<br/></h1>
<!-- start: shared_blocks.33546700#below-h1 -->


<!-- end: shared_blocks.33546700#below-h1 -->
<p>The new Camaro convertible what a beautiful car. If this rear wheel 
drive American sports car does not get your blood pumping its time to 
see a doctor. Hats off to GM for taking the time to strengthen the body 
before they removed the roof. Examples of the stronger design are in the
 <span style="text-decoration: none;">video review</span> of the <i>2013 ZL1 rag top model</i> below. </p><p>Although
 this means 300 more pounds added to the already heavy car it is a 
necessary evil. But don't worry it still turns a low 13 second quarter 
mile in the SS trim. The 2014 Camaro will 
continue to steal some retro styling from its grandfather the 1969 
Camaro rs. One of the only muscle cars that when I see it I think about 
emptying out my bank account to own it.
</p>
<div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockCenter"><img src="http://www.auto-facts.org/images/nocamaro.jpg" width="480" alt="new camaro convertable image" title="new camaro convertable image"></div>
<h1>New Camaro<br/></h1>

<p>But before I talk about the new <span style="font-weight: normal;">Chevrolet Camaro</span>. I 
would like to tell you a story about my first brand-new car. It was a 
1977 Chevrolet Camaro rs. My dream car, she was beautiful two-tone black
 and silver. The interior had bucket seats and a floor shift console 
with full instrumentation. 
</p><p>The 1977 Chevrolet Camaro had some 
slightly different styling from the years previous. Eventually I decked out my new 
Camaro rs with wire mags and gold pinstripes all hand done of course and
 very much in style at the time. The wire mags were very hard to 
clean. I remember taking a toothbrush and a bucket of soapy water and 
spending a couple of hours, every few months on just cleaning the 
wheels.</p>
<div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockRight"><img src="http://www.auto-facts.org/images/77-RS.jpg" width="259" alt="77 RS Camaro" title="77 RS Camaro"></div>
<p>She turned heads wherever she went. I was in love, and I have been 
hooked on Camaro's ever since. Of course there was the Z28, maybe to 
some the flashiest model, but there was something so clean so sleek 
about my 1977 RS.</p><p>I had my <b>Camaro rs</b> for 10 years. This is 
the longest I've ever owned one single car. Although her operation was 
not completely trouble-free, She never left me stranded and always got 
me home. After a decade of driving the car it was finally time to say 
goodbye. An offer that I could not refuse sealed the deal. I will never 
forget the day me and my first new car, parted company.
</p>
<div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockRight"><img src="http://www.auto-facts.org/images/camaroconcept.jpg" width="350" alt="image of camaro concept car" title="image of camaro concept car"></div>
<p>I have been waiting ever since to fall in love again with another car, that time is now. The <i>new Camaro convertible</i>
 is absolutely stunning. Buyers will have their choice of hard top 
version or a rag top special edition that is now available for 2013-2014
 And of course the retro styling is included. </p><p>The interior is 
very sharp and just like my 1977 Camaro rs it comes with bucket seats 
and a floor-mounted console shifter. Round retro gauges are sleek and 
set into square moldings. The gauges feature white faces with red meters
 (well the one we saw at the car show).
</p>
<div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockCenter"><img src="http://www.auto-facts.org/images/medcamaro.jpg" width="480" alt="Camaro rag top" title="Camaro rag top"></div>
<p>The body accents are bright metal for things as the exhaust tips, and the fuel filler door. The <i>new Camaro convertible</i> is the proper mix of clean lines and style. Any clutter is thankfully omitted. The
 entry level will offer a V-6 but not the lame one of the past. The 3.6 Liter is 318 HP but you can move up to a 556 HP 6.0 L V8. My 
husband says a four-wheel independent suspension with a multi-link rear
 end will be included. </p><p>This must be a good thing? All this adds up to one thing. I love This new hot car from Chevrolet, just like I loved my 1978 <span style="font-weight: normal;">RS</span>.
 It has been long wait but it appears that the real deal is on its way. I
 will guarantee you that when the new Camaro convertible is released I 
will be in line at my local Chevrolet dealership to take the first test 
drive.
