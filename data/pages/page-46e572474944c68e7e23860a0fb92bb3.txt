		<h2>The National Academy of Television Arts & Sciences Announces the 40th Annual Daytime Entertainment Emmy Award Nominations</h2>
		
		
		
		
		<p><b>Press release from NATAS, May 1, 2013:</b></p><p>New York -- The National Academy of Television Arts &amp; Sciences (NATAS) today announced the nominees for the 40th Annual Daytime Entertainment Emmy Awards.</p><p>The Daytime Entertainment Emmy Awards recognize outstanding achievement in all fields of daytime television production and are presented to individuals and programs broadcast from 2:00 a.m.-6:00 p.m. during the 2012 calendar year.  This year&rsquo;s gala will return to the prestigious Beverly Hilton Hotel in Beverly Hills, CA and be telecast on the Turner Broadcast Networks, HLN on Sunday evening, June 16, 2013.</p><p>The Daytime Entertainment Creative Arts Emmy Awards gala, which honors the crafts behind the many shows that grace the Daytime genre will return to the Westin Bonaventure Hotel in Los Angeles on Friday, June 14, 2013.</p><p>&ldquo;These exciting nominations officially launch the 40th Anniversary of the Daytime Emmy Awards,&rdquo; said David Michaels, Senior Executive Director, Daytime.  &ldquo;This will be a true celebration of the 40 years of Daytime Television focusing on the past, the present and the future of Daytime.  Congratulations to all the nominees!&rdquo;</p><p>This year&rsquo;s Daytime Entertainment Emmy Award for Lifetime Achievement Award will be presented to two Game Show genre icons, Monty Hall and Bob Stewart.</p><p>&ldquo;Aside from all of the excitement around our 40th anniversary,&rdquo; said Malachy Wienges, Chairman, NATAS, &ldquo;we are honoring two giants in Game Show history.  So many of us grew up watching Monty Hall excite us with what was behind Door # 1, #2 or #3 and found ourselves addicted to the challenge and imagination of Bob Stewart&rsquo;s classic &ldquo;To Tell the Truth,&rdquo; &ldquo;Password&rdquo; &ldquo;Pyramid&rdquo; and others.  The high-water mark these two pioneers have made in Daytime television is unparalleled and the entire Emmy Award community stands in appreciation for the immeasurable contribution they have made to the Game Show genre.&rdquo;<br /> <br />The 40th Annual Daytime Emmy Awards is a presentation of the National Academy of Television Arts &amp; Sciences in cooperation with the Academy of Television Arts and Sciences (ATAS).</p><p>A breakdown of the awards by network, as compiled by the independent accounting firm of Lutz &amp; Carr, LLP, follows.</p><p><b>The 40th Annual Daytime Entertainment Emmy Award Nominations</b></p><p><b>NETWORK TOTALS</b></p><p>CBS	49<br />	PBS	44<br />	ABC	38<br />	SYNDICATED	37<br />	NICKELODEON	36<br />	NBC	25<br />	THE HUB	17<br />	CARTOON NETWORK	11<br />	FOOD NETWORK	10<br />	DISNEY CHANNEL	6<br />	DISNEY XD	4<br />	MTV	3<br />	Youtube	3<br />	Cooking Channel	2<br />	Daystar Television Network	2<br />	Discovery Channel	2<br />	Hallmark	2<br />	MeTV	2<br />	Sprout	2<br />	American Public Television	1<br />	Blueworldtv.com	1<br />	CNN	1<br />	Facebook	1<br />	Fuse	1<br />	Lifetime	1<br />	MTVU	1<br />	Nick Toons	1<br />	Oprah Winfrey Network	1<br />	Veria Living	1</p><p></p><p><b>MULTIPLE NOMINATIONS</b></p><p>The Young and the Restless	23<br />	General Hospital	19<br />	Sesame Street	17<br />	Days of Our Lives	17<br />	The Bold and the Beautiful	15<br />	The Ellen DeGeneres Show	10<br />	R.L.
