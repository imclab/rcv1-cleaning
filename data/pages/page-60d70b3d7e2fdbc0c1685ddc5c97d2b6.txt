<ul type="disk">
    <p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">An &quot;Up&quot;</font></b> is someone who walks on the car  
    lot. &nbsp;A customer!</li></p>
	
    <p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">A &quot;Beback&quot;</font></b> is a customer who leaves without    
    buying, and comes back at a later date.</li></p>
	
    <p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">A &quot;Laydown&quot;</font></b> is a customer who
    buys at whatever price the salesperson quotes.</li></p>

    <p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">A &quot;Roach&quot;</font></b> is how car salespeople refer
    to people with bad credit!</li></p>
	
    <p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">A &quot;Get-Me-Done&quot;</font></b> is somebody
    who has borderline credit at best, and will take almost   
    any vehicle at any terms just to get financed.</li></p>

    <p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">The &quot;Bump&quot;</font></b> is where the Sales Manager    
    sends the salesperson back out to get a higher price, or 
    to <b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;Bump&quot;</font></b> the customer for more money!</li></p> 

	<p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">A &quot;Lowball&quot;</font></b> is an unrealistically low price that the
    salesperson gives the customer before the customer
    leaves to shop price at another dealer.</li></p>
	
    <p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;Upside Down&quot;</font></b> is when a person owes more money on their trade-in then the vehicle is worth.</li></p>

<p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;Negative Equity&quot;</font></b> is related to <b>Upside Down</b> above. &nbsp;It is the amount that you still owe on your car loan after subtracting how much you are actually getting for your vehicle.</li></p>

<p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;Positive Equity&quot;</font></b> &nbsp;This is when your trade-in is actually worth more than the amount you owe on it. &nbsp;Needless to say, this doesn't happen very often!
</li></p>

    <p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;Croak and Choke&quot;</font></b> pertains to the Finance Manager selling Credit Life Insurance and Disability Insurance on the car loan!</li></p>

    <p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">To &quot;Brick&quot;</font></b> somebody, or to <b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;Spot Deliver!&quot;</font></b> &nbsp;Car salespeople are so worried that you'll change your mind once you buy a vehicle, that they want you to take it <b>NOW!</b>  &nbsp;Very often, the dealer will have the customer sign bank papers before the loan is officially approved, just to get the customer down the road.</li></p>

    <p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;Buyers are Liars!&quot;</font></b>  &nbsp;Every car salesperson is instilled with this belief!</li></p>

<p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;The T.O.&quot;</font></b>  &nbsp;If one salesperson can't close you he or she will <b>T.O.</b> you or turn you over to a Manager or a Closer, who in turn might <b>T.O.</b> you to someone else, and someone else again until you buy.</li></p>

<p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;A Packed or Loaded Payment!&quot;</font></b>  &nbsp;Packing or loading payments is a slang term used to describe a practice used by the auto industry to get customers to agree to purchase additional products, such as credit insurance, service contracts, chemical protectants, and security devices, without revealing their true impact on their monthly payments.</p>
  
<p><b>Packing</b> is played out when a customer finances their vehicle through the dealer. &nbsp;A customer agrees to a purchase the car  at a monthly payment that is much higher than what is needed to cover the price of the vehicle. &nbsp;That creates a <b>"pack"</b> or room in the payment to add in the optional products....of course without the customer knowing he's actually paying more for the extra stuff!</li></p>

<p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;Buyers Remorse!&quot;</font></b> &nbsp;This is when a car buyer starts  having second thoughts about the car purchase. &nbsp;Once the euphoria of getting a new or different vehicle wears off, a person begins wondering if they got ripped-off, did they do the right thing, can they really afford the payments and on and on it goes. &nbsp;There are countless reasons a person second-guesses themselves.</p> 

<p>Car sales people call this <b>&quot;Buyers Remorse&quot; or &quot;Coming Out of the Ether!&quot;</b> &nbsp;This is the sales person's worst nightmare, because this is when the buyer starts looking for ways to back out of the car deal.</li></p>

<p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;A Today Buyer!&quot;</font></b> &nbsp;This is a customer who is prepared to purchase a vehicle the same day they enter the dealership. &nbsp;The large majority of buyers are <b>not</b> in this category. &nbsp;Most people want to think it over and look around before making a decision.</p>

<p>However, it is the car sales person's job to turn you into a <b>&quot;Today Buyer!&quot;</b> &nbsp;They are trained in techniques that do just that. &nbsp;They have a <b><a href="http://www.insidercarsecrets.com/dealer-selling-system.html">Selling System</a></b> in place that is designed to take control of you, culminating in you taking delivery of your new or used vehicle before you leave the dealership. &nbsp;They will go to great lengths to put you in that vehicle <b>&quot;today&quot;</b> including doing a <b><a href="http://www.insidercarsecrets.com/spot_delivery.html">Spot Delivery!</a></b> &nbsp;<font color="#FF0000"><b>BEWARE!</b></font></li></p>

<p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;ACV&quot;</font></b> &nbsp;This stands for <b>Actual Cash Value.</b> &nbsp;This is what dealers use when referring to the amount of money they are actually putting in a trade. They have to differentiate this number from what they tell the customer. Very often the customer is led to believe that he is getting more for his trade than what the <b>ACV</b> is!
</li></p>

<p><li><b><font color="#0000FF">&quot;Quarterback&quot; </font></b> &nbsp;Car salespeople hate it when you bring someone
with you to to the car dealership to help you negotiate a deal. &nbsp;For example a young woman may bring her father to help her. &nbsp;Or she may bring another relative or even worse...a boyfriend. &nbsp;They call these people <b>&quot;quarterbacks!&quot;</b>
</li></p>
