<div id="ansContent" class="answr">Spark plugs are some of the most commonly replaced parts on automobiles, many times as a preventive measure and sometimes as the root cause of a larger problem. Spark plugs are often quite similar in size and shape, keeping the potential for confusion and improper installation quite high. Even something as simple as a misaligned plug installation can have serious consequences for other critical <a class="more">.</a> <div class="moresent" style="display:none;"> parts and the engine as a whole. All of these aspects highlight the need to employ a reference source at every practical step, whether it is in the form of a parts number cross-reference or a visual representation chart. Many of the manufacturers and third party providers have electronic catalog files online that allow users to search by part numbers or application. There are also options for obtaining shop charts featuring the most common and confused types of plugs for a handy comparison prior to installation. The many automotive parts chains in operation around the country are also excellent resources, with several, such as www.NapaOnline.com, offering a parts look-up from any Internet connection. This eliminates the need for calls or physical visits to the store locations for confirmation. The consequences of a mismatched or misaligned plug range from lost compression and increased engine deposits to a total failure of the engine. There are some steps that can be taken to rectify the damage, involving boring and re-tapping the holes or replacing the heads, both of which are costly, time-consuming and easily avoided. Some examples of online resources for spark plug cross-reference and spark plug cross-reference charts include www.SparkPlug-CrossReference.com and www.SparkPlugs.com, both providing instant online part number searches.
