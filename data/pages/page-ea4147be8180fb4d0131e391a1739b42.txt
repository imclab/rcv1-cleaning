    $('ul.tabs').each(function () {

        // For each set of tabs, we want to keep track of
        // which tab is active and it's associated content
        var $active, $content, $links = $(this).find('a');

        // If the location.hash matches one of the links, use that as the active tab.
        // If no match is found, use the first link as the initial active tab.
        $active = $($links.filter('[href="' + location.hash + '"]')[0] || $links[0]);
        $active.addClass('active');
        $content = $($active.attr('href'));

        // Hide the remaining content
        $links.not($active).each(function () {
            $($(this).attr('href')).hide();
        });

        // Bind the click event handler
        $(this).on('click', 'a', function (e) {
            // Make the old tab inactive.
            $active.parent().removeClass('active');
            $content.hide();

            // Update the variables with the new link and content
            $active = $(this);
            $content = $($(this).attr('href'));

            // Make the tab active.
            $active.parent().addClass('active');
            $content.show();

            // Prevent the anchor's default click action
            e.preventDefault();
        });
    });</script>

    <span class="author"><a href="/Blog/author/Brenda-Priddy.aspx">Brenda Priddy</a></span> on <span class="pubDate" itemprop="datePublished">07.18.2012 13:19</span>
    <div class="text">
        <p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Wrapped in camouflage head-to-toe, we photographed the 2013 Toyota Rav4 earlier today. But even covered, these photos show the next RAV4 is going to change quite a bit from its current form and move closer to the crossover mainstream.</p>
<p>When the Toyota RAV4 first went on sale in 1996 (read: <a title="History of the Crossover" href="http://www.automedia.com/History_of_the_Crossover/dsm20100201hc/1" target="_blank">History of the Crossover</a>), it was a quirky little SUV powered by a tiny four-cylinder engine. Each successive generation of the RAV4, however, has grown larger and heavier. Today, even a third row of seats is available. The next iteration of the crossover, however, will need to compete with the new Ford Kuga, the Mazda CX-5, as well as the venerable, updated-for-2012 <a title="2012 Honda CR-V" href="http://www.automedia.com/2012_Honda_CR-V_First_Drive/rts20111101hc/1" target="_blank">Honda CR-V</a>. That means it will need to retain a roomy cabin <em>and</em> deliver the fuel economy buyers expect.</p>
<p>Based on these spy photos, don&rsquo;t be surprised if the new RAV4 arrives on a modified version of the current model&rsquo;s platform. That&rsquo;s because in addition to some drastic stylistic changes, it looks like a size increase may be in store for the 2013 as well. Although most of the car in the photos is covered up, the sweeping roofline and fast windshield make it clear that the new RAV4 will abandon its off-road pretensions and embrace its crossover roots.  <a href="/Blog/post/2013-Toyota-RAV4-SPIED-Sneak-Peek-of-a-Visually-Larger-Redesigned-RAV4.aspx" >more</a>

    <span class="font18 bold"><a href="/Blog/post/2013-Acura-RDX-Redesigned-Premium-Crossover-SUV-Gets-New-Look-and-V6-Power.aspx" data-role="ajax-nav">2013 Acura RDX: Redesigned Premium Crossover SUV Gets New Look and V6 Power</a></span><br/>

    <span class="author"><a href="/Blog/author/Gary-Witzenburg.aspx">Gary Witzenburg</a></span> on <span class="pubDate" itemprop="datePublished">07.09.2012 00:06</span>
    <div class="text">
        <p>When Acura sent the RDX out in 2007 to battle the <a title="2012 Lexus RX" href="http://www.automedia.com/New_Cars/lexus_rx_2012" target="_blank">Lexus RX</a> and <a title="2012 Infiniti EX" href="http://www.automedia.com/New_Cars/infiniti_ex_2012" target="_blank">Infiniti EX</a>&mdash;both V6-powered&mdash;it chose to go with a turbocharged four-cylinder. It was willing and eager, but not as smooth and refined as those rival V6s.</p>
<p>Now comes this 2013 Acura RDX with a slicker body, a new six-speed automatic, a new all-wheel-drive system and, yes, a standard 273-hp 3.5-liter V6. It also boasts a longer wheelbase, increased track widths, new Amplitude Reactive Dampers, and more energy-efficient motion-adaptive electronic power steering.</p>
<p>Smoother front and rear fascias, a toned-down Acura bird-beak grille, wider wheel flares and a more tapered rear roofline improve the RDX&rsquo;s look and make it more aerodynamically slick, while new underbody panels significantly smooth airflow underneath. The redesigned cabin is more upscale and luxurious and Active Noise Control makes it a quieter environment. There&rsquo;s more leg- and shoulder room, more cargo capacity, larger door and rear hatch openings, a new (available) power liftgate and a host of upscale features.</p>
<p>The 2013 Acura RDX rides on four-wheel independent suspension with MacPherson struts front and multi-links in back. Its longer wheelbase, increased track widths, a lower center of gravity and new Amplitude Reactive Dampers help provide both better handling and a more supple ride, while new motion adaptive electronic power steering replace the previous hydraulic system.  <a href="/Blog/post/2013-Acura-RDX-Redesigned-Premium-Crossover-SUV-Gets-New-Look-and-V6-Power.aspx" >more</a>

    <span class="font18 bold"><a href="/Blog/post/First-Drive-Review-2013-Ford-Escape.aspx" data-role="ajax-nav">First Drive Review: 2013 Ford Escape</a></span><br/>

    <span class="author"><a href="/Blog/author/Gary-Witzenburg.aspx">Gary Witzenburg</a></span> on <span class="pubDate" itemprop="datePublished">05.02.2012 21:31</span>
    <div class="text">
        <p>EPA (Environmental Protection Agency) fuel economy of the <a title="2013 Ford Escape towing capacity" href="http://www.automedia.com/Blog/post/2013-Ford-Escape-%E2%80%93-Sleek-Powerful-and-Ready-To-Play-With-More-Tow-Capacity-Than-Competitors.aspx" target="_blank">2013 Ford Escape</a>, depending on which models, engines, and drivetrains you compare, is up to five mpg better than the 2012&rsquo;s. The base front-wheel-drive (FWD) S model is powered by a 168-hp 2.5-liter four-cylinder that delivers 22 city and 31 highway mpg, while the smaller but more powerful (178 hp) 1.6-liter EcoBoost four-cylinder is good for 23/33 with FWD. The highest-performance choice is a 240-horsepower 2.0-liter EcoBoost four-cylinder rated at 22/30 mpg.</p>
<p>The 1.6-liter EcoBoost engine is standard in SE and SEL models, while the top-level 2013 Ford Escape Titanium comes with the 2.0-liter.&nbsp;There is no V-6, since Ford&rsquo;s EcoBoost combination of turbocharging, direct fuel injection, and twin independent variable camshaft timing (Ti-VCT) makes more cylinders and larger displacement unnecessary, even for light towing (up to 3,500 lbs.) All three engines drive through smooth six-speed SelectShift automatics that can be manually shifted through a shifter-mounted switch, and an optional new &ldquo;intelligent&rdquo; 4WD system offers better off-road traction than before.</p>
<p>The 2013 Ford Escape looks like a taller Focus because it essentially is. Built on Ford&rsquo;s global compact platform, it&rsquo;s about the same length as a <a title="2012 Ford Focus test drive review" href="http://www.automedia.com/2012_Ford_Focus/rts20110301ff/1" target="_blank">Focus</a> sedan but 10 inches wider and nearly nine inches taller on a 1.6-in.-longer wheelbase. It leads with the wide-mouthed, mustachioed Focus face with large blackout sections flanking the central opening and wrap-over headlamps atop the fenders. Broad shoulders connect the tops of the head- and taillamps along the base of the six-window greenhouse, while sculpted character creases flow front to rear at door-handle and fender-flare levels.</p>
<p>It&rsquo;s also 10 percent aero-sleeker than the boxy old Escape to boost highway fuel economy, and active grille shutters close on models with the 2.5-liter base and 1.6-liter EcoBoost engines to further improve aerodynamics at cruising speeds.  <a href="/Blog/post/First-Drive-Review-2013-Ford-Escape.aspx" >more</a>

    <span class="font18 bold"><a href="/Blog/post/Quick-Spin-Test-Drive-2012-Honda-CR-V.aspx" data-role="ajax-nav">Quick-Spin Test Drive: 2012 Honda CR-V</a></span><br/>

    <span class="author"><a href="/Blog/author/Ben-Lewis.aspx">Ben Lewis</a></span> on <span class="pubDate" itemprop="datePublished">04.09.2012 22:48</span>
    <div class="text">
        <p class="MsoNormal">The Honda CR-V has always been one of our favorite vehicles. Fun, frugal, and well thought out, it&rsquo;s simply the Honda take on the way small <a title="Crossover Research Center" href="http://www.automedia.com/New_Cars/Crossover">crossover</a> SUVs should be. And we appreciate that. Where some competitors have tried to go the rugged off-road direction, and others the more powerful, turbocharged or V6 way, the CR-V was always happy to stay your smart, efficient friend for on-road adventuring.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">So with an all-new CR-V for 2012, we wondered if Honda had turned the tables to take the CR-V in a new direction?</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Nah. Honda&rsquo;s smart. It already had a very popular vehicle, so it just looked at ways to make good even better.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">The looks are certainly a step up. The redesigned <a title="2012 Honda CR-V" href="http://www.automedia.com/2012_Honda_CR-V_First_Drive/rts20111101hc/1">2012 Honda CR-V</a> is one of the first to show off Honda&rsquo;s new design direction, and it&rsquo;s more aero, muscular and aggressive. The lines make it appear larger than it is (actually about the same size as the previous generation), and the rear tailgate treatment reminds us of Volvo&rsquo;s XC crossover. Sitting on (our EX model&rsquo;s) handsome 17-inch alloy wheels, we think the new exterior design is a fresh, upscale look that promises to age well.  <a href="/Blog/post/Quick-Spin-Test-Drive-2012-Honda-CR-V.aspx" >more</a>

    <span class="font18 bold"><a href="/Blog/post/New-2013-Subaru-XV-Crosstrek-Compact-Crossover-SUV-2012-New-York-Auto-Show.aspx" data-role="ajax-nav">New 2013 Subaru XV Crosstrek Compact Crossover SUV: 2012 New York Auto Show</a></span><br/>

    <span class="author"><a href="/Blog/author/James-M-Flammang.aspx">James M. Flammang</a></span> on <span class="pubDate" itemprop="datePublished">04.01.2012 21:28</span>
    <div class="text">
        <p class="MsoNormal">Subaru is introducing a brand-new model for 2013, which makes its world debut at the <a title="new auto show photo gallery" href="http://www.automedia.com/Auto-Shows/New_York_International_Auto_Show">New York Auto Show</a>. Billed as a &ldquo;fun to drive&rdquo; compact crossover vehicle, the 2013 Subaru XV Crosstrek is based on the subcompact <a title="2012 Subaru Impreza Review" href="http://www.automedia.com/2012_Subaru_Impreza/rts20120101si/1">Subaru Impreza</a> platform. Significant body and chassis revisions have been made, according to Subaru, resulting in a &ldquo;go-anywhere stance and attitude.&rdquo; Premium and step-up Limited editions will be offered.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Black side- and wheel-arch cladding, rocker spoiler and rails contrast with the XV&rsquo;s body color. Raised ride height mates with an 8.7-inch ground clearance, to help in clearing obstacles. Up front is a unique grille, matched by distinctive front and rear bumpers, as well as 17-inch wheels. A roof spoiler and dark-tint privacy glass will be standard.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><p class="center"><object width="425" height="350"><param name="movie" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/9AAFlSwnsOM"></param><param name="wmode" value="transparent"></param><embed src="http://www.youtube.com/v/9AAFlSwnsOM" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" wmode="transparent" width="425" height="350"></embed></object></p></p>
<p class="MsoNormal">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Under the XV Crosstrek&rsquo;s hood, a 2.0-liter &ldquo;boxer&rdquo; (horizontally-opposed) four-cylinder engine develops 148 horsepower. When the XV goes on sale in fall 2012, buyers may choose a five-speed manual gearbox or Subaru&rsquo;s Lineartronic continuously variable transmission (CVT). The manual gearbox incorporates Subaru&rsquo;s Incline Start Assist feature, which briefly holds the vehicle while the driver coordinates brake and clutch action to pull away from a stop. The CVT includes a paddle-selected manual mode with six preset ratios, to simulate operation of a conventional transmission.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Subaru estimates fuel economy at 23 mpg in city driving and 33 mpg on the highway, with a 15.9-gallon fuel tank. An ECO gauge is standard, to help monitor mileage.  <a href="/Blog/post/New-2013-Subaru-XV-Crosstrek-Compact-Crossover-SUV-2012-New-York-Auto-Show.aspx" >more</a>
