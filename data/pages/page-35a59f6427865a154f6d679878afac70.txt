<div id="WNStoryBody" class=" withRelated"><span id="WNStoryDateline">HUNTSVILLE, AL (WAFF) -
			</span><p>The streets of downtown Huntsville looked empty Saturday early evening&nbsp;in comparison to Thursday and Friday night for the kickoff the Arts and Entertainment districts.<br /><br />"We saw just a mass amount of people coming out and having a great time and staying out too. Our restaurant was full, every table," said Zachary Mayhall,&nbsp;manager at "Sam and Greg&#39;s.<br /><br />There&#39;s no denying that the entertainment districts are good for business. Mayhall said he knows that&nbsp;firsthand, "This past Thursday was our record breaking day, the most money we&#39;ve made in a single day."<br /><br />But now the question is: will the momentum remain?&nbsp;Mayhall said there&#39;s no doubt it will.</p>
<p>"I certainly think we can. I think this is only the beginning. It&#39;s only going to get bigger. Without a doubt," said Mayhall.<br /><br />Mayhall said he&nbsp;believes the key to keeping the districts fresh will depend on the ability to grow</p>
<p>"I think we should try and make it grow and have more local artists, more people coming out to buy art," he said.<br /><br />"It&#39;s really cool to see things start to take off and grow and a bigger demand for live music and art sold on the street corner. It just feels like the community is really coming together," said Mayhall.</p>
<p>Huntsville Mayor Tommy Battle said in 90 days they will&nbsp;take a look at the district and see if any changes need to be made.</p>
